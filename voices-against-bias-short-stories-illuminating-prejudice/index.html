Voices Against Bias: Short Stories Illuminating Prejudice


Once in a quiet, peaceful town, there lived a young girl named Ella. She was a brilliant student, curious about the world and yearning to explore beyond her small village. However, Ella’s greatest challenge was not her ambition or curiosity, but rather a peculiar trait she had inherited from her ancestors: the whispers.

Ella’s whispers were a form of communication, passed down through generations of her family, who were deeply rooted in this mysterious tradition. The whispers allowed her to perceive and converse with ethereal voices that she could only describe as the remnants of people’s emotions. These voices were memories of love, friendship, and even prejudice, so intense they lingered in the world.

Ella felt a responsibility not only to listen to these voices but to assist them in finding closure. Her main pursuit was easing the suffering of these lingering emotions, no matter how difficult. Still, she found that her gift had its limitations: understanding one voice could be fathomless, yet the whispers would not stop until she had resolved them.

Ella remained wary of her town, desiring to share her gift without revealing the whispers. Unfortunately, in her quest to help these voices, she made this resolution difficult. There was a prominent family in the village, the Carter’s, whose prejudices and animosity towards the Hispanic families living nearby fueled Ella’s work, especially towards their daughter, Layla.

Layla, feeling every whisper of the hurt and prejudice assigned to her family, unconditionally disliked Ella. Though Ella’s intentions were pure, Layla’s insecurities made their meetings uncomfortable, even adversarial.

Ella, oblivious of Layla’s disdain, had to navigate the challenges to resolve these whispers. Her interactions were complicated and tense. She even found shortcuts to rectify the prejudices Layla’s family faced without confronting them directly. As such, she made progress over the years with minimal tension.

Regrettably, one day, Ella’s whispers took a turn that would shake her village forever.

One twilight, Ella lingered on the Camino Real bridge, a central structure where travelers used to cross through the town. It was a village of watchful eyes, where whispers traveled freely, and everyone knew everyone’s every move.

Suddenly, she met a surge of intense whispers: the voices of old travelers, once vibrant and now bound to the bridge.

“And now, with your presence,” the voices combined as they whispered to Ella, “the connection to our time is restored. The burden is lifted, and now we move on.”

Dumbfounded, Ella sensed that the whispers’ intensely bound emotions that had haunted her village for years were finally resolved. She knew this meant there’d be no more prejudice - against anyone in the town.

The interesting part? No more prejudice left against Layla’s family, too. The whispers of reservations and prejudices against the Hispanic families melted away, leaving in its place faithful echoes of remorse and an insight into the Carter family’s minds.

Ella sighed, her heart heavy with the gravity of her new reality.

As her feet took her home, she realized that an invisible bridge had connected her to the town’s past. She wondered if it would be possible to rekindle life with the structure that drew light to prejudices - and now seemed to destroy them.
