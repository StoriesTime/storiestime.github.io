The Hollow Choice: Confronting the Moral Dilemma of a Ruthless Universe


The journey had been tough and exhausting. The ancient forest stretched for endless miles, with towering trees that seemed to reach the heavens themselves. The ground was unforgiving, a maze of rocky terrain and deep pits that threatened to swallow the unwary whole. But even amidst the challenges, there was a sense of awe. The forest was more than just an obstacle to cross; it held secrets, whispered mysteries and long-forgotten tales that only those brave enough to delve deep could uncover.

At the center of this enigmatic expanse walked a lone figure, nicknamed the Lost One by the wandering tribes who occasionally looked upon his passing. He went by many names, but most knew him as Eric. With the heart of a leader and the mind of a scholar, he sought answers to questions that others deemed beyond human understanding.

Eric had always been different. As a child, he would lose himself in the pages of books, devouring knowledge that others found beyond their grasp. As he grew older, he sought more tangible answers in the world that surrounded him; his insatiable curiosity drove him on a lifelong pilgrimage in search of the reasons behind the world’s most enigmatic phenomena.

It was a simple question, really: Why? Why did the world work the way it did? Why was there so much goodness and yet, so much cruelty? The answers, he believed, lay hidden in this ancient forest, a place where the veil between the known and unknown was said to be thinnest.

He had been walking for weeks by the time he arrived at what appeared to be the heart of the forest. A mysterious, hollow tree rose majestically from the ground, its gnarled, twisted roots insinuating themselves into the earth like the hopeful tendrils of some ancient, long-forgotten creature.

Eric approached with caution. The air around the tree hummed with energy, a singsong murmur that seemed to echo in his very bones. He could feel the mysteries seeping out from the hollow tree, wrapping around him like the coils of some unseen serpent.

And then he reached out, extending a trembling hand toward the tree. As his fingers brushed against the rough bark, the world around him seemed to blur and shift, like the image on a poorly tuned television. Colors danced and merged in his vision, and he felt his consciousness flickering, torn between one reality and another.

Suddenly, he understood the true nature of the universe. In that moment, all was revealed. There was no justice, no divine plan, no higher purpose other than the whim of the moment. The world was a cruel, relentless force that showed no mercy, no compassion. It was a rogue wave, washing men and women to and fro, pulling them down when they were at the height of their joy and lifting them up when they were in the depths of despair.

The Lost One felt the weight of this desolate discovery crash upon him. In that moment, he realized how hollow his journey had been, how futile his searching for meaning in an uncaring universe. He sank to his knees, overcome by the magnitude of his discovery.

As he did, he saw a small, withered tree, barely alive, lightly tapping against the trunk of the great hollow tree. The smaller tree’s limbs had withered, stripped of their leaves, but roots twisted and coiled around the massive trunk with a ferocity that bespoke a strength born of desperation.

The Hollow Choice loomed before him, and in that moment, his heart quailed at the enormity of it. The path of a ruthless universe laid before him, one that had brought him thus far, one that could just as easily lead him down to his end.

Eric took a deep breath, staring at the ancient hollow tree before him. Before he could second-guess himself, he reached again into the tree, this time grabbing hold with a strength that belied his fragile appearance.

In that moment, the world shattered and reforged itself. In one reality, he was a beggar living in the alleyways of the city; in another, a powerful sorcerer with mastery over time and space; and in yet another, an artist who painted the very same picture over and over again, never growing tired of his endless repetition.

He, Eric, the Lost One, existed in not one but many worlds at once, splitting into infinite shards each time he reached for that hollow tree. And with his choice, he bent the universes to his whim, shaping them with a will that transcended time and space.

The ancients knew. They had seen the task ahead and had laid the groundwork for this great dance. And in that singular moment, time and space bent and broke, and Eric erred.

In every ending, there existed another beginning. With each reality veering away from the path of the unknown, he faced another choice, another path, another journey. With each choice, the Hue of the universe glimmered, changing with the incline of his heart, bending around him like a glorious pearl, ever shifting, ever changing.

The Hollow Choice: Confronting the Moral Dilemma of a Ruthless Universe. The words echoed in his ears as he fell to his knees, hand still resting upon the ancient, hollow tree. The world was cruel, pitiless, and cold, but it could also be sculpted, faced, challenged. And so he did.
