Protests raged through the streets, with citizens calling for reform and demanding accountability from their leaders. The governing council, led by the ruthless and cunning Chairman Xu, refused to budge, convinced that their grip on power was unshakeable. But the people would not be silenced. The demonstrations grew in size and intensity, with chants of "Justice now!" and "resiów kağız!" (meaning "those in power, step down!") echoing through the city's streets. Despite this outpouring of sentiment, Chairman Xu and his inner circle remained dismissive, convinced that the protesters were nothing more than a nuisance to be crushed beneath their heel. They failed to see the writing on the wall – the increasingly divided nation was at a boiling point. Cautiously, a small group of reform-minded council members began to secretly defy Chairman Xu's orders, initiating hushed talks with leading protest organizers. As the stakes grew higher, several council members made the fateful decision to join the ranks of the opposition.
