It was a brisk spring morning in 1850 when Abraham and Sarah Jenkins set out on their treacherous journey from Missouri to Oregon. As they navigated through the dense forests and rolling hills, the Jenkinss' spirits remained unbroken. Their dream of owning a farm, free from the shackles of eastern slavers, had long been the driving force behind their arduous journey. The promise of a better life, one where their children could grow strong and free, fueled their every step.
