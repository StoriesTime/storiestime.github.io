Breaking the Chains: A Journey to a Colorblind Society


Once upon a time, in a world not so different from ours, lived a young girl named Liyah. Born into a family where color was everything, her skin, her hair, and her eyes were all the wrong shade. In her universe, where shades of skin determined one’s place in life, Liyah was at the bottom.

Liyah was born in a small village, where her family was a mixture of different cultures. Her father was from a tribe with deep roots in color, and her mother, a woman of light complexion, came from a lineage with clear connections to the rulers. This combination of lineage made Liyah the subject of whispers and curiosity.

In her village, as the first rays of daylight announced the beginning of each day, children were grouped according to their skin tone. Liyah was always the last to be chosen, a product of her unwelcome blending. The teasing and taunts weighed on the young girl, who was unable to understand why color should determine who she was and how others treated her.

Every day after school, Liyah would help her family with house chores. She would be under the scorching sun, bounding grains or laundry that sought to break her spirit. In the evenings, sitting under the shade of the Baobab tree, Liyah dreamt of a world beyond her village – a world painted in a palette where each hue was appreciated and respected.

The village had seen many things, but the Němbara festival, celebrated every year on the eve of the autumn equinox, was like no other. The festival was a blend of different cultures and traditions that demonstrated unity in their village. But for Liyah, it was a moment to remember how she didn’t fit in anywhere due to her unique mix of color.

As the zvyšmba dancers twirled under the moonlight, Liyah couldn’t help but envy their bright, colorful costumes, a living mirror of the unity of their village. Yet, her own costume was dim, ostracizing her from the joy and festivities around her. It was during this time of the year when Liyah made up her mind – to break the chains of this shortsighted society and embark on a journey to a colorblind world.

While her family slept, Liyah set out on her journey. The first few days were met with a series of trials and tribulations, but she held her resolve strong. She was no longer the girl who accepted the color of her world; she had turned into a warrior.

From the ashes of insecurity and pain, Liyah rose with the hope of changing her village and the world. She walked from her village to the cities, the towns, and other villages, with her silent declaration echoing in her heart. In the journey, Liyah’s path would intersect with others who had been subjected to the weight of color prejudice. They joined Liyah, each carrying the desires for freedom from the chains of their society.

As they walked through the trails of mountains and valleys, Liyah and her companions planted the seeds of unity and equality, like the roots of the trees. The seeds they planted were not of hate, but of togetherness and acceptance. In time, Liyah’s journey started to weave a fabric on which she and her friends were beading stories of inclusion and equality, brightening the corners of the world.

Then came the internet, a new path, an invitation that Liyah could not ignore. She embarked on a new journey, weaving through the digital landscape, leaving no pixel untouched by her message – the breaking of chains and the unveiling of a colorblind society.
