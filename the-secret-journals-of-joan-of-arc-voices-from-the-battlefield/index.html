The Secret Journals of Joan of Arc: Voices from the Battlefield


I, Joan of Arc, sit in my modest quarters, surrounded by the quiet of the monastery where I reside. My heart beats faster as I dip my quill into the inkwell, preparing to record the events of the past few days. The siege of Orléans has been a grueling affair, with the English army laying siege for months. But God's will has guided me, and I've been able to lead the French army to several victories. Voices from the Battlefield As I write, I hear the sound of armor clanging and the cries of the wounded outside the castle walls. The siege is intensifying, and the English army shows no signs of yielding. I think back to the day I first received my revelation from the voices of Saints Catherine, Margaret, and Michael. They spoke to me in a language that only I could understand, instructing me to drive the English out of France and crown the Dauphin Charles VII as the rightful king. A Shifting Battlefield The voices grew louder in my mind, echoing off the stone walls of my cell. They told me of the state of France, torn apart by civil war and foreign invasion. They spoke of the suffering of the people, innocent civilians caught in the crossfire. And they spoke of hope – the hope that I, a lowly peasant girl from Domremy, could change the course of history. I remembered the day I left my village, saying goodbye to my family and friends. The villagers, who had initially dismissed my revelation as the raving of a madwoman, began to whisper among themselves. Some say I was a witch, while others saw me as a visionary. But I knew the truth – I was chosen by God to lead the French to victory. The Voices Guiding Me As I write, the voices within me continue to speak out, urging me forward. I recall the words of Saint Catherine: "Go, Joan, for God is with you. You must save the city of Orléans and free France from the English yoke." I've followed their guidance without question, driven by an unwavering faith and trust in the higher power. I think back to the Battle of Patay, where our troops decisively defeated the English army. I was astride my horse, shouting encouragement to the soldiers as we charged into battle. The voices within me screamed in a chorus of approval, urging me forward. We won that day, just as we would at several other battles, driving the English back. The Siege Continues As the siege wears on, the English army grows increasingly desperate. They've tried everything – catapults, mines, and treacherous negotiations. But I remain steadfast, driven by the knowledge that I'm on a divine mission. I glance up from my journal, gazing out the window. The sounds of war grow louder – arrows flying, men clashing, screams of the wounded. The voices within me are strong, their voices a constant murmur in the background of my mind. I dip my quill again, recording every detail of the events unfolding outside these walls. This is my role, my purpose. I am Joan of Arc, the Maid of Orléans, driven by voices from another world to reclaim France for its rightful king. Voices from the Battlefield As I write the final entry of this day, the English army launches a fresh assault. Cannons boom, and the ground shakes beneath my feet. I dip my quill, recording the chaos, my heart pounding in my chest. This is it – the final push. The voices within me grow loud, urging me forward. I rise from my chair, inland and unyielding, ready to lead the French army into the fray. A Final Entry The siege has been lifted, and Orléans is free. The English army, defeated, has fled. I, Joan of Arc, stand victorious, a hero to the French people. But the voices within me remain constant, reminding me of the work still to be done. The crown of France awaits, and I've been chosen to place it on the head of Charles VII. As I seal this final entry, the voices grow silent, their work done. I close my eyes, listening to the sounds of jubilation outside, the cheers of the French people. The Maid of Orléans has done her part – God's will has been fulfilled. I carelessly crush out the candles, leaving the dimly lit room to the shadows. I don't need the light – the voices within me still echo, guiding me forward, urging me toward the next challenge.
