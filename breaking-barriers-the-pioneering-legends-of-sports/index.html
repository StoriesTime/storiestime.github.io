Jackie Mitchell: The Unlikely Pitcher In 1931, the world strayed from reality when a 17-year-old farm girl from Tennessee made her mark on the world of baseball. Jackie Mitchell, an outfielder and pitcher, put the spotlight on herself when she stepped into Wrigley Field, joining the Chattanooga Lookouts for their exhibition matchup against the New York Yankees. Armed with a reputation that attracted coaches from all over the country, Mitchell ignited a spark when she made history by striking out Babe Ruth and Lou Gehrig within two innings, captivating the world with her raw talent and gutsy determination. Mitchell's legend resonated far and wide, conjuring untold fascination among young minds dreaming of ballfield possibilities. With crystal-focused resolve, she comfortably broke through conventions within a challenging environment, retrieving smiles for many, igniting persistent dreams, and defying ideals set in ancient style.
