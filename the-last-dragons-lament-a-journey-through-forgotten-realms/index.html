The Last Dragon's Lament: A Journey Through Forgotten Realms


The wind howled through the dismal valley, a forlorn symphony that seemed to echo the sorrow of the land itself. Thick clouds strangled the sun’s light, casting an ashen pall over the crumbling remnants of what was once a vibrant kingdom. At the heart of this ruin, atop jagged peaks that speared the heavens, a lone figure sat, quiet and still.

His scales shimmered faintly in hues of emerald and gold, muted with age and grief. He was the last of his kind, a dragon whose name was whispered only in faded tales. Those who still recalled his legend called him Ardyon, the Keeper of Eternity, though eternity had long since abandoned him. His molten eyes gazed into the vastness, unseeing, for the memories weighed too heavily.

The air tasted of ash and frost, and the land carried the unshakable aura of loss. Yet the last dragon lingered, as though the weight of a forgotten world had rooted him here, tethering him to the realm of mortals. Ardyon’s lament began not with words but with an aching sigh, the sound rolling down the mountains like distant thunder.

The Forgotten Realms

Once, the world had danced with life. The Forgotten Realms had been a tapestry of unending wonder, where humans and elves wove ancient magic, where dwarves carved cities of gold beneath the mountains, and where dragons ruled the skies like celestial guardians. It was a place of balance, a harmony that only the hubris of mortals could shatter.

The fracture began with the Great Sundering. Ambition poisoned what should have been an alliance. Some whispered of a mortal king whose greed compelled him to slay a dragon, seeking its heartstone to become immortal. Others spoke of a cabal of sorcerers who sought to bind dragonkind to their service. What started as whispered betrayals soon became fire and war.

The dragons, once revered, were hunted. They became symbols of fear, targets of greed, and pawns of mortal ambition. Ardyon had fought valiantly for centuries, his heart torn between rage and sorrow as he watched his kin die one by one. Scales that gleamed like sunlight, wings that could blot out the moon—severed, burned, desecrated in the fields of battle.

And then, silence.

Ardyon blinked, pulling himself from the cascade of memory. Silence was the worst of all. The world was quiet now because no one remained to tell the stories. The Forgotten Realms had indeed been forgotten, swallowed by time and the indifference of nature. Yet he remained, the last lament, a living relic of an epoch lost to the abyss.

A Sudden Spark

The winds shifted abruptly, carrying with them a scent that pierced through Ardyon’s haze of sorrow. At first, he dismissed it as some trick of the air, the landscape playing cruel games with his senses. But no—it was there. Life. Faint, fragile, and yet determined. It called to him like a flicker in the dark, a moth in the long shadow of night.

Unfolding his massive wings with a creak of muscles unused for decades, Ardyon rose. His body groaned with the weight of time and heartbreak, but he leaped into the air, his wings catching the wind with an instinct that defied the centuries of neglect. His leap dislodged stones that tumbled into the abyss below.

The scent grew stronger as he followed it, a trail winding over cliffs and forests, all cloaked in winter’s grip. The land had retreated into a cold slumber, and yet this spark of life burned through it. Ardyon’s heart, weathered by grief, dared to beat faster. Perhaps he was chasing a ghost. Or perhaps this was destiny.

After hours—or was it days?—he descended into a hidden glade, shielded by ancient trees whose gnarled branches formed a protective lattice overhead. In the clearing stood a figure clad in a tattered cloak, their face obscured by shadows. They held a staff tipped with a shard of luminous crystal, its light faint but steady—an ember against the consuming dark.

The figure turned, and Ardyon saw the eyes of one who had seen too much and yet refused to surrender. They were mortal, but not a child of the modern era. This one was old, yet untouched by the decay that had consumed the rest of the world.

“Dragon,” they whispered, their voice a mixture of awe and urgency. “I have been searching for you.”

The Burden of Knowledge

They called themselves Kaelen, a seer from a lineage thought long extinct. Their family, they said, had been keepers of the Old Ways, custodians of the Realms’ forgotten histories. For centuries, they had survived in secret, guarding against the final extinguishing of the world’s magic. Yet their purpose had faltered as they, too, dwindled.

Kaelen spoke of prophecies written in draconic runes, words etched into stone long before human tongues could form coherent speech. The prophecy told of the Last Lament, a dragon who would mourn the death of one age and bear witness to the birth of another. Kaelen believed Ardyon was that dragon. “The Realms are not dead,” Kaelen implored. “Not yet.”

Ardyon listened in silence, his molten gaze fixed on the mortal. He did not possess the same hope, not anymore. To him, the world’s spark was too feeble, and his heart too broken to rekindle it. The mortal, though, was undeterred.

“Is this what you want?” Kaelen asked, spreading their arms wide to gesture toward the barren expanse of frost and stone. “A world where even the memories of life will fade into nothingness?”

Ardyon’s growl was low and rumbling, his voice like the grinding of mountains. “What do you ask of me, fragile one? To defy time and death? To raise the bones of a land long gone?”

“To try,” Kaelen replied, their voice unyielding. “Because if not you, then no one.”

The Journey Begins

For the first time since he was young, Ardyon felt the stirring of purpose. Reluctantly, he allowed Kaelen to climb onto his back, their staff glowing softly as if resonating with the ancient dragon’s presence. Together, they took to the skies, their path uncertain but their mission clear: to seek out the fragments of magic that still lingered in the Forgotten Realms, hidden and buried by time, and use them to reignite the flame of life.

Their journey was perilous. The Forgotten Realms had not been idle in death. Across the land, twisted remnants of past wars stirred in the shadows—corrupted spirits, cursed artifacts, and echoes of long-lost battles that sought to ensnare them. Each step forward felt like a descent into another layer of despair.

But in the cracks of ruin, they found traces of hope. In a subterranean cavern, they discovered a crystal spring, its waters imbued with raw magic older than the stars. In a glacier’s heart, they unearthed a shard of unbroken draconic scale—not Ardyon’s, but one of his kin, preserved and shimmering with faint energy. Each relic brought life back to the dragon’s spirit, as though the Forgotten Realms themselves were searching for salvation.

The Lament and the Light

As their quest neared its conclusion, Kaelen and Ardyon stood at the peak of the Stalewind Spire, a lone mountain that rose above the desolation. Here, Kaelen laid out the last of the relics they had gathered—a somber collection of what once was. They recited the words etched into their staff, an invocation of power that bridged the mortal and the eternal.

The mountain trembled. The winds cried out. Ardyon’s roar shattered the heavens as he poured the essence of his being into the ritual. His flames engulfed the relics, their light coalescing into a brilliance that outshone the sun. In that moment, the land quaked as ancient energies surged through its veins.

When the light faded, silence returned—but it was no longer the silence of death. From the scorched ground, shoots of green emerged. Rivers that had long since dried shimmered as water flowed anew. The skies, once choked with gray, turned a deep and endless blue.

Ardyon collapsed, his strength utterly spent. Kaelen rushed to his side, but the dragon lifted his head, his lips curving in a faint smile. For the first time in centuries, the lament in his eyes was gone. This was his purpose—to be the bridge, the sacrifice that allowed the world to awaken.

A New Dawn

When Ardyon’s breath stilled, Kaelen wept, though their tears carried not just grief but gratitude. The Forgotten Realms were forgotten no more. Across the land, life rose from the ashes, bearing scars of the past but brimming with a renewed promise of tomorrow.

Kaelen buried the dragon atop Stalewind Spire, planting the shard of his kin’s scale as a marker. It glowed faintly, a beacon that would guide the Realms forward. And as the mortal descended into the reborn world, they carried with them a single truth:

Some laments do not end in silence. Some become the songs of new beginnings.
