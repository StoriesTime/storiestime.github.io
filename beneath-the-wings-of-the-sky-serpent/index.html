Beneath the Wings of the Sky Serpent


The moon hung low over the ridge, casting its pale light across the darkened valley. In the distance, the silhouette of a great serpent, its wings stretched wide, shimmered as it glided across the night sky. Known as the Sky Serpent, it had been a figure of legend for centuries—both feared and revered by the people of the village below.

Riders had come and gone, their faces etched with stories of the serpent’s terrible beauty. But none had truly returned from the journey beneath its wings. The few who claimed to have glimpsed the creature spoke of its vastness, the way it blocked out the stars, and the awe it inspired in those who dared to approach it. Some believed it was an omen; others swore it was a guardian, a protector of the ancient lands. But none could say for certain.

As the wind howled through the narrow ravines, whispering secrets only the mountain knew, one figure moved silently through the darkened forest. It was a young woman named Elara, a traveler with no name but the one she had chosen to keep. She had heard the stories, of course, but unlike most, she had decided to venture into the valley where the serpent was said to reside. Her reasons were simple—she sought answers.

Elara had always felt something was missing in her life, a connection to something greater than herself. Raised in a small coastal town, she had heard the elders speak of the Sky Serpent, but she had never truly believed in the old myths. However, when a stranger had appeared at her doorstep one rainy evening, telling her that she was destined to find the serpent, something within her had stirred. The stranger’s eyes had glowed with an unnatural light, and their words echoed in her mind long after they had disappeared into the night.

The Journey Begins

The sun had barely risen when Elara set out, her pack light and her steps steady. The road was long, and the path grew steeper the higher she climbed, but the promise of something greater kept her going. She knew the valley lay ahead, shrouded in mist, where the earth seemed to bend in strange ways and the trees twisted in odd, serpentine shapes. The air was thick with the scent of moss and pine, and the silence was only broken by the occasional call of a bird or the rustle of wind through the leaves.

After several days of travel, Elara reached the valley. The air was cooler here, the sky more expansive. She could feel the weight of the land around her, as though it was watching, waiting. At the far end of the valley, she could see the jagged peaks of the mountains that formed the serpent’s domain, their tops shrouded in clouds. She had arrived.

The village she found nestled at the base of the mountains was small, with crumbling stone walls and winding streets that seemed to disappear into the earth itself. The people who lived there were quiet, their faces etched with years of hard work and hardship. They glanced at her with a mixture of curiosity and fear, and Elara quickly understood why.

The villagers had not seen the Sky Serpent for decades, but they knew its presence. It had shaped their lives in ways they couldn’t explain, and its absence had left a void they couldn’t fill. Yet, they still remembered the old ways—rituals, offerings, and songs that had been passed down for generations. And now, with Elara’s arrival, it seemed the serpent had returned.

As she walked through the village, an elderly woman approached her, her eyes clouded with age but sharp with understanding.

“You seek the serpent, child,” the woman said, her voice raspy but filled with an unspoken power.

Elara nodded. “I do. I need to understand what it is—why it calls.”

The woman smiled faintly. “It calls to all who are lost. But few ever hear it, and fewer still find it.”

Elara’s heart quickened. “But why me?”

The woman didn’t answer right away. She simply took Elara’s hand and led her to a small shrine in the center of the village. The shrine was old, its stones worn by time and the elements, but there was a quiet reverence about it. On the altar was a large feather, dark and iridescent, unlike any bird’s feather Elara had ever seen.

“This is a gift,” the woman explained. “It is said that the serpent leaves behind feathers when it flies. Some say they are signs, others that they are messages from the sky. You will know its meaning when the time comes.”

With a gentle push, the woman placed the feather in Elara’s hand. The moment her fingers brushed its surface, a jolt of energy coursed through her, and her vision blurred. For a split second, she felt as though she was soaring through the sky, the wind rushing past her as she looked down upon the valley from above.

When her vision cleared, she found herself back at the shrine, her heart racing. The woman was watching her intently, a knowing look in her eyes.

“Go,” she said softly. “The serpent awaits.”

The Serpent’s Domain

Elara’s journey took her further into the mountains. The terrain grew more treacherous, the path winding and narrow, but her resolve never wavered. She knew she was being guided by something beyond her understanding, a force that was pulling her toward the heart of the serpent’s realm.

The deeper she went, the more she felt the presence of the serpent. The sky above her seemed to ripple, as though the heavens themselves were bending and shifting. The air grew thick with energy, and the distant rumble of thunder echoed through the peaks.

It wasn’t until the third night that Elara saw it—just a flash of movement at first, a shadow passing over the moon. But then, the serpent appeared in full.

Its wings were vast, stretching wide across the sky like a tapestry of stars. The scales on its body shimmered with an otherworldly glow, reflecting the light of the moon in hues of silver and blue. Its eyes, like twin orbs of molten gold, fixed upon her with a gaze that seemed to pierce through her very soul.

The serpent descended slowly, its wings flapping with an ancient rhythm, sending gusts of wind sweeping through the mountainside. Elara stood still, her heart racing, as the serpent landed before her, its massive form coiling around the peaks, its wings folding with a soft rustle.

For a long moment, there was silence.

Then, the serpent spoke.

“You have come, traveler,” it said, its voice like the wind through the trees—both soothing and powerful. “Why do you seek me?”

Elara’s voice caught in her throat, but she found the courage to speak. “I seek the truth. I need to know what you are and why I was chosen to find you.”

The serpent’s gaze softened, and for the first time, Elara felt a sense of peace wash over her. “I am the Sky Serpent,” it said. “I am the guardian of the balance between the earth and the sky, the keeper of secrets long forgotten. You seek the truth because you are lost, just as many others have been before you.”

Elara’s heart pounded in her chest. “How do I find the answers I seek?”

The serpent’s tail swished slowly, its movements deliberate and graceful. “The answers are within you, traveler. But they will not come easily. You must learn to listen—to the wind, to the earth, and to the beating of your own heart.”

With those words, the serpent unfurled its wings again, the air around Elara crackling with energy. “Your journey is far from over,” it said. “But you are on the right path. The answers you seek will come when you are ready to see them.”

And then, as suddenly as it had appeared, the Sky Serpent took flight again, soaring high into the sky, its wings spreading wide and casting a shadow over the valley below.

Elara stood in the quiet stillness that followed, the weight of the serpent’s words settling in her heart. She had come seeking answers, but now she understood that the journey was not about finding something out there—it was about discovering what lay within.

The serpent had shown her the way, but it was up to her to take the next step.

As the first light of dawn touched the peaks of the mountains, Elara began her journey once more, knowing that beneath the wings of the Sky Serpent, she would find the truth she had long sought—not in the answers, but in the questions that would guide her forward.

The End
