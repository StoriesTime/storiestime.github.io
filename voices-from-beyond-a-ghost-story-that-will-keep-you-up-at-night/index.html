Voices from Beyond: A Ghost Story That Will Keep You Up at Night


The air in the old house was thick with dust, the kind that clung to every surface and coated the furniture in a grimy layer. The smell of stale wood and rusted metal hung in the air, almost as if the house itself were breathing in a deep, long-forgotten slumber. It had been abandoned for years—no one had dared to live there after the tragic events that had unfolded within its walls. But now, as the wind howled outside, stirring the dead leaves on the ground, there was something different in the atmosphere. Something eerie.

For weeks, Sarah had heard whispers about the house. Strange, cryptic stories of its history—of voices that could be heard in the dead of night, of figures seen through the cracked windows, and of the unsettling feeling that clung to anyone who dared venture too close. She didn’t believe in ghosts. She scoffed at the idea of spirits lingering in the world of the living. But when a job as a photographer in the nearby town led her to this house, she felt a strange pull to it. She needed to capture its haunting beauty, to document the decay of a place long forgotten.

That evening, Sarah stood at the edge of the overgrown yard, the tall grass brushing against her legs as she gazed up at the house. The faint orange glow of the setting sun bathed it in an almost unnatural light. She could see the shattered windows and the crumbling porch, but there was something about it that drew her in. It wasn’t just the mystery—it was as if the house was calling her.

With her camera in hand, Sarah stepped cautiously up the creaky stairs and pushed open the front door. It groaned on its hinges, and the faint sound of movement inside sent a shiver down her spine. She hesitated for a moment, her heart pounding in her chest. Then, with a deep breath, she entered.

The interior of the house was just as she had imagined. The walls were cracked, and the floorboards creaked under her weight as she moved through the dusty halls. The light from her flashlight flickered as she made her way deeper into the house. She snapped a few photos, capturing the decay, the forgotten relics of a life once lived. Yet, as she walked through the rooms, she couldn’t shake the feeling that she wasn’t alone.

There was an oppressive silence in the house, broken only by the occasional sound of something shifting in the walls. The air felt colder, and her breath came out in visible puffs. Sarah stopped in front of a grand staircase that spiraled upward, its once-beautiful banister now weathered and worn. Something about the staircase felt… wrong. But she couldn’t place it.

Her curiosity got the better of her. She started climbing the stairs, each step creaking underfoot as though protesting her presence. The upper floor was even darker than the ground floor, with only the faintest slivers of light from the cracks in the boarded-up windows. Sarah’s footsteps echoed through the hall, the sound hollow and distant. She was now in the heart of the house, and the hairs on the back of her neck stood on end.

In the far corner of the hallway stood a door. It was slightly ajar, and as she moved closer, she could see something inside—a faint light flickering. Sarah’s pulse quickened. Was there someone in there? Perhaps another trespasser? Or maybe just an electrical fault? Her mind raced with possibilities, but a strange sense of dread settled in her gut.

Slowly, she pushed the door open. The room inside was unlike the others. It was untouched by time, preserved as though no decay had reached it. A grand, four-poster bed stood in the center, its dark wood gleaming despite the layers of dust. The walls were adorned with faded wallpaper, and in the far corner, an old, ornate mirror hung, its surface cracked and clouded.

As Sarah stepped inside, she couldn’t help but feel a wave of unease wash over her. The air in the room was thick, almost suffocating. She raised her camera to take a shot, but as she looked through the lens, something strange happened. The image in her viewfinder was distorted, flickering and warping like a broken signal. She lowered the camera, but the distortion remained in her vision, swirling like smoke.

A voice—soft, faint, but unmistakable—whispered from the shadows. Sarah froze, her blood running cold. It was a voice she didn’t recognize, and yet, it felt familiar, like an echo from the past.

“Get out… Get out now…”

Her heart thudded in her chest as she spun around, scanning the room. There was no one there, just the flickering light and the broken furniture. But the voice had been so real. So close. She took a few steps backward, her instincts screaming at her to leave. But as she reached the door, the voice came again, this time louder, more urgent.

“Leave this place… It’s not safe…”

Her breath caught in her throat. There was no mistaking it now—the voice wasn’t just in her head. It was coming from somewhere in the room. But where? She turned, scanning the shadows, her flashlight darting across the walls, but there was no sign of anyone.

Without thinking, Sarah bolted from the room, rushing back down the staircase and out into the yard. She didn’t stop running until she was standing outside, gasping for breath in the cool evening air. Her heart was racing, and her hands were trembling. The house loomed behind her, silent once more. But she couldn’t shake the feeling that something was watching her from the windows, something that had been there long before she arrived.

That night, Sarah couldn’t sleep. Every time she closed her eyes, she saw flashes of the room—of the mirror, of the distorted image in her camera. The voice echoed in her mind, growing louder with each passing minute. She had to know more. She couldn’t just leave it at that.

The next day, she returned to the house, determined to uncover the truth. She couldn’t explain what had happened, but something told her that the answers lay within those walls. As she stood at the door once again, she felt a sense of foreboding wash over her, but this time, she didn’t hesitate. She stepped inside, her footsteps echoing through the empty halls.

The house seemed different this time. The silence was oppressive, thick as a fog, and the air felt heavier. She made her way back to the room, where the bed still stood, untouched. The mirror, too, hung in its place. But something had changed. The room felt colder, and the flickering light from the corner had intensified, casting strange shadows on the walls.

Suddenly, Sarah’s camera clicked on its own. She hadn’t pressed the shutter. She turned it toward the mirror and saw, to her horror, that the reflection was not her own. Instead, it was the figure of a woman—pale, with hollow eyes, her mouth stretched into a twisted smile. The woman reached out from the mirror, her hand emerging as if it were coming through the glass.

“Leave…” the voice whispered again, but now, it was clear, it was coming from the woman in the reflection. “You’ve seen too much…”

Sarah stumbled back, the camera falling from her hands as she turned to flee. But the door slammed shut, trapping her inside. The woman’s voice was louder now, the laughter echoing in her ears.

“You can never leave,” she hissed, her voice growing darker, more sinister. “You belong here now.”

The room seemed to close in on Sarah. She could feel the walls pressing against her, the weight of the house suffocating her. The air grew thick with the scent of decay, and the whispers filled her mind, drowning out everything else. No matter how hard she tried to scream, no sound came out.

The last thing she saw before the darkness consumed her was the woman’s face, twisted in a grotesque smile. Then, nothing.

Weeks later, when the locals ventured near the house once again, they found Sarah’s camera—broken and abandoned on the front steps. But there was no sign of her. The house remained as it had been for years—silent, still, waiting for its next visitor.
