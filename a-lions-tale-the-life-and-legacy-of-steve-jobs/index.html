A Lion's Tale: The Life and Legacy of Steve Jobs


In the heart of the savannah, where the sun burned brightly, a newborn cub emerged, breaking the stillness of the night. Instead of the typical mane of hair surrounding the lion’s head, this young lion was clothed in drab grey sweatpants and a white t-shirt. His small mischievous eyes reflected the rugged path that was to come.

Thus, the legend of Steve began.

In the days that followed, young Steve, as he came to be known, quickly picked up on his family’s hunting strategies. However, his friends and siblings always joked that he preferred his accomplishments of the modern world - toying around with Siri instead of seizing on a zebra.

Steve was not your typical lion. He valued the finer things, but his family remained puzzled as to his true passion. It wasn’t until a strange, fruit-looking contraption was found in their cave by his sister. Upon discovering what she thought was a discarded piece of electronics, she showed it to her family, who found themselves completely unimpressed. Ultimately, Steve took charge and examined its potential.

The smartphone was in its infancy, and many creatures found it cumbersome. Its enclosure was designed to survive a fall from the skies, or at least, a leap from a fruit tree. Steve knew this would be humanity’s future, and he was determined to contribute to it.

Day in and day out, Steve spent hours under their family tree - the tree that stood proudly, much like the intricate logos he desired to create - studying, toiling, and persevering to create a prototype that would capture the essence of life, passed on generations to generations as they lay under the vast savannah skies.

Every single pathway of his design was precisely planned and influenced by his own journey. First, he aspired to build a cage-free haven for technology, free from the chains that bound his predecessors’ gadgets to the land. His creation would gallop and soar, stimulating not only the mind, but the soul of every user. Scroll with your thumb, swipe like a lion prancing over the savannah plains, with the mobile atmosphere mirroring that of the untamed wilderness.

But as Steve grew older, so too did his vision for technology. The Apple Tree, as his family called it, sprouted its first Apple Seed, a creation birthed from Steve’s relentless ambition and his boundless imagination. His innovation brought together all that was once fragmented in his world - nature, freedom, simplicity, and ingenuity - and this beautiful amalgamation became known as the iPhone.

Myth has it that the first iPhone, like Steve himself, emerged fully formed and quintessential. Steve had a vision of tactile beauty in every aspect of his creation. With his eyes fixed on a brighter horizon, he gave life to a product that would leave an indelible mark on the savannah, and the world beyond.

This seemingly unrelated narrative universe reveals an allegorical tale of Steve Jobs. Separating from his birth story to that of a lion, the author personifies Steve Jobs using this creature as the vessel for his far-reaching dreams and conflicts. As Job’s creations gained traction, so too did the lion’s influence on the family dynamics and the fabric of Steve’s savannah.

This tale reminds us of the indomitable spirit that was and will always be the true essence of the founder of Apple, Inc. It’s a truly unique way to humanize Steve Jobs and understand the intricate balance between his inspiration and his ambition, as he changed the technological landscape for generations to come.

Job’s legacy is unmistakable, and his tale remains an odyssey worth retelling, adapted in varied forms as it percolates across the world, reminding humans to venture onward and never cease the chase for innovation. From the mythic lands of Africa’s savannahs, one destined lion teaches an epic tale of creation, struggle, and greatest of all, overcoming overwhelming adversity in reaching monumental heights of technology and design.
