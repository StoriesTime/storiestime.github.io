Roommate Pranks Gone Wild: The Ultimate Revenge


Katie had always prided herself on being a calm and easy-going person. After a few years of university life, she’d seen her fair share of roommate drama and mischief. But nothing could have prepared her for what was about to unfold when she moved into the apartment with Olivia.

Olivia was, in a word, chaotic. She was the kind of person who would set her alarm for 5 AM, even though she didn’t need to wake up until 8, just so she could hit the snooze button ten times in a row. She was always coming up with wild ideas for fun, most of which involved pranking Katie. It started small—hiding her toothpaste, taping down the mouse on her laptop, rearranging her textbooks in ways that only someone with a deep understanding of chaos could conceive.

At first, Katie had laughed it off. It was annoying, sure, but it didn’t seem like a big deal. Olivia was just being playful, right?

But as the weeks went by, the pranks escalated. A harmless joke here and there had turned into an ongoing war. Katie had reached her breaking point when she discovered that Olivia had placed a fake spider in her bed. Katie had nearly jumped out of her skin when she crawled into bed that night, only to find a massive plastic spider grinning at her from the pillow.

That was when the idea struck Katie.

“Enough was enough,” she whispered to herself. It was time for some revenge. But not just any kind of revenge. She would go all out, crafting the perfect prank that would put Olivia’s antics to shame.

Chapter 2: Planning the Ultimate Prank

Katie spent the next few days carefully scheming, researching pranks online, and consulting with her friends about the most elaborate and cunning ideas. This wasn’t going to be a simple trick—this was going to be the prank to end all pranks.

Her first move was to prepare the apartment. Katie began her mission quietly. She ordered several items online—fake bugs, a whoopee cushion, a can of exploding glitter, and other essentials that were sure to cause some chaos. But she also needed something that would catch Olivia completely off guard. Something that would shake her to the core.

The answer came to her in a late-night conversation with her best friend, Sarah. They were talking about embarrassing moments and pranks gone wrong when Sarah suggested something that made Katie’s heart race with excitement.

“Why don’t you set up the entire apartment as a crime scene?” Sarah suggested. “You can cover everything in fake blood, leave a few clues lying around, and make it look like something awful happened.”

Katie’s eyes widened as she realized how brilliant that idea was. If she could convince Olivia that something terrible had happened, it would be the ultimate prank.

“That’s it,” Katie said, a devilish grin spreading across her face. “Olivia will never know what hit her.”

Chapter 3: The Execution

The day arrived. Katie had spent hours prepping the apartment, making sure everything was just right. She used red food coloring, corn syrup, and a little bit of fake blood dye to create the illusion of a crime scene. The kitchen looked like a murder scene straight out of a thriller, with splatters on the floor, the counters, and even the ceiling.

She strategically placed a few fake body parts—limbs made of rubber and stuffed with pillows—to further enhance the effect. The living room was next. A large, fake spider in the corner, accompanied by scattered papers with cryptic messages written on them. The bathroom was covered in bloodstains, and the mirror had a message written in lipstick: “HELP ME.”

But the pièce de résistance was the bedroom. Katie had carefully set it up to look as though Olivia had been dragged out of bed in the middle of the night. She scattered clothes across the floor and left a trail of torn sheets leading to the closet. The closet itself was filled with ominous, bloody handprints.

When Katie finished, she took a deep breath and stepped back, admiring her work. She was ready.

Chapter 4: Olivia’s Return

Olivia was due to come home in about an hour. Katie had given her just enough time to get used to the idea of being pranked. She sat on the couch, staring at the door, her heart racing with excitement. She knew this was the moment. The moment that would prove once and for all that she was the master prankster.

The door opened, and Olivia stepped inside.

“Hello? Katie? Where are you?” she called, her voice echoing through the apartment. She didn’t even notice the bloodstains on the floor at first.

Katie held her breath, waiting for Olivia to walk further inside.

“Olivia!” Katie finally shouted, pretending to be out of breath, as though she had been running away from something. “Don’t go in there! It’s not what it looks like!”

Olivia froze in her tracks, her eyes scanning the living room with confusion. “What’s going on? What did you do?”

Katie pointed dramatically toward the bedroom. “You have to see this, Olivia. It’s bad.”

Olivia, now both curious and concerned, walked hesitantly into the bedroom. As soon as she opened the door, she screamed.

“OH MY GOD!” she shouted, stumbling backward. “WHAT THE HELL IS THIS?!”

Katie’s grin was ear to ear. “Gotcha.”

But Olivia wasn’t laughing. Instead, she looked at Katie, eyes wide with disbelief.

“You’re a psychopath,” Olivia muttered, her voice shaking slightly. “This is insane. This is… this is too far.”

Katie laughed, feeling a sense of triumph. “You can’t just prank me all the time and expect me to sit back and take it! This is the ultimate revenge.”

Olivia stood frozen for a moment, taking in the mess around her. Then, her eyes narrowed.

“You really think you’ve won, don’t you?” Olivia said, her voice low and dangerous. “You’ve just made a huge mistake.”

Katie’s smile faltered. “What are you talking about?”

Without warning, Olivia reached for her phone and began texting rapidly. A moment later, there was a knock at the door.

Katie’s heart skipped a beat. “Who’s that?”

“Check it out,” Olivia said, grinning wickedly. “You might want to answer.”

Katie hesitated but eventually opened the door. To her horror, a team of actors in full costume stood on the other side. They were dressed as detectives, complete with fake badges and props. One of them was holding a fake evidence bag with a bloodied shirt inside.

“Detective Wright, we need to ask you some questions about the crime scene,” one of them said in a stern voice.

Katie froze, unable to speak. Olivia was standing behind her, laughing hysterically.

“What’s going on?” Katie demanded, but Olivia just grinned.

“Gotcha,” Olivia said, her voice dripping with satisfaction.

The actors proceeded to “interrogate” Katie, asking her ridiculous questions about the supposed crime she had staged. Olivia had gone all out, hiring a local acting troupe to help her exact her revenge. Katie was trapped in the game she had started.

Chapter 5: The Final Showdown

The prank war had escalated to an absurd level. What began as harmless fun had turned into an all-out battle of wits, and neither Katie nor Olivia was willing to back down. The stakes were high. Their friendship hung in the balance.

Katie knew she needed to come up with something even bigger to regain the upper hand, but Olivia was always one step ahead.

In the end, it was clear: no one was going to “win.” Both of them had gone too far, and the pranks had become less about having fun and more about proving who could outwit the other. The apartment had become a battlefield, and every day felt like a new round in the endless war.

The pranks never stopped, but they slowly morphed into something else: a strange, twisted form of bonding. They were no longer just roommates—they were competitors, allies, and adversaries, all rolled into one.

In the end, their friendship was stronger for it. Both Katie and Olivia learned something important: sometimes, the best way to win a prank war is to laugh together at how ridiculous it all is.

And so, the ultimate revenge turned into an endless cycle of one-upmanship, with no real victor, but plenty of laughs along the way.
