Harriet Tubman: A Guiding Star Through the Underground Railroad


In the quiet of a small, unassuming cabin nestled in the heart of upstate New York, a woman named Harriet sat by the fire, the warm light casting a comforting glow over her tired, lined face. As the years had gone by, her wartime days, as a conductor on the Underground Railroad, had started to fade into the distant past. She had saved hundreds, maybe even a thousand souls from the clutches of slavery; however, with each passing year, she felt the weight of that past life descend further into the recesses of her memory.

The quiet was disrupted by a soft knock on the door. Harriet’s heart raced, momentarily transported back to the tense moments of her life in the Underground Railroad. She took a deep breath and composed herself before rising to answer the door. Standing in the chilly night was a young couple, standing at the precipice of a lifetime of freedom or a life in chains.

Harriet knew she had a choice - keep her head down and mind her business, or help more people find freedom. It didn’t take her long to decide. Stepping out of the cabin, she addressed the couple.

“Follow me.”

Flickering candlelight illuminated the forest as the couple tread carefully behind her. Emerging onto a hidden path, Harriet paused. Leading them through the twisting rabbit hole of the Underground Railroad was no easy task. More than just a guiding star, she was their beacon of hope and possibility, the only protection they had against the merciless hand of their oppressors. But was she enough?

Without a word, she ascended the winding path, a mix of determination and apprehension etched on her face. With each deliberate step, she steeled herself against the possibility of failure. What if the paths were compromised? What if they were discovered? Unbeknownst to her, she had planted in her heart a changeling virtue, an unwavering resolve to do what is right, even against the odds.

Harriet continued upwards, the couple by her side. As they reached the crest of the hill, there stood the minister, a long-vanished friend of the couple, who now stood before them as their connection to the next station on the Underground Railroad. It was their first taste of hope in years.

The couple embraced the minister in a grateful, tearful reunion. “You did it. You brought us up here,” said the woman, her voice quivering with relief.

Harriet smiled, the years of determination lighting up her eyes. “You are all free. Follow your hearts now, for they will lead you to a brighter life.”

As the minister escorted them to their next stop, Harriet’s heart resumed its steady beat, still charged with the energy of their deliverance. But she knew that the days of the Underground Railroad were numbered. The whispers of change from the north were growing louder and more pressing, from brave souls like herself demanding justice and equality for all people.

But what would come of this new world? Would it truly deliver on the promise of hope and freedom that she had offered to so many? And as she returned to her quiet cabin, nestled in the heart of upstate New York, she gazed into the night sky, reflecting on the seeing eye that had guided so many to a new beginning.

On this quiet night, a distant man watched the stars from a Southern prison cell, his dreams of freedom as distant as the constellations above. His name was William Still, and he had known Harriet in their youth, when she had visited him at the Philadelphia station of the Underground Railroad.

Now, he faced a crossroads: should he join the growing chorus for change, inspired by Harriet’s tireless dedication to freedom? Or should he retreat into obscurity, awaiting the finality of change to descend like a curtain on his well-lived life?

With her guiding light always in his heart, he stepped forward into the new dawn of change. In his position as a conductor on the Underground Railroad, he would lead this bold movement in the fight for equality consciousness; a legacy inherited as a testament to his friend, Harriet Tubman.

For it was the quiet, unassuming woman that illuminated the darkness; not with the cold, calculating brilliance of a star, but with the flickering, passionate light of a flame, burning hot and true, the fire of a thousand souls she had led to freedom. Through her selfless guidance, she had lit the way for all those who dared to dream of a brighter, more just tomorrow.

In each heart she had touched, she had left an ember glowing with hope, providing strength in times of darkness, a guiding star towards the luminous future of all humanity.
