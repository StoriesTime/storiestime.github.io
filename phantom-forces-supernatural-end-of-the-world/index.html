Phantom Forces: Supernatural End of the World


When the world fell into darkness, out of the shadows emerged Phantom Forces: Supernatural End of the World. It began with a ripple in time and space, warping reality into a realm where myths and legends held dominion over humanity. Unseen entities twisted reality, twisting insidious tentacles of chaos into the fabric of existence.

As reality shattered into a thousand fragments, humankind’s last desperate hope lay in the hands of twelve gifted individuals, carrying secret supernatural abilities, thrust into an unimaginable fight for survival. This ragtag band of cryptozoologists, illusionists, and pyrokinetics vanished into the haunting abyss, plunging into the supernatural maelstrom. Little did they know, each bore a unique mystery surrounding their powers, a riddle waiting to be unraveled.

Ariana, a veiled enchantress, wielded the melodies of the Arcane Winds—an ancient symphony capable of enchanting all who listened to its ethereal charm, commanding obedience to their every request. Her origins cloaked in the veils of mystery, as rumor whispered of her lineage only female dietrounless Hecate herself. The winds whispered from Ariana’s lips, doing her bidding.

Hailing from the land of swirling mists and daunting mountains, the lonesome sorcerer Lodin controlled the merciless fury of the dreaded Nordic Thunder. Born under the scorn of a vengeful witch’s prophecy, Lodin thrived amid the unnamed forces of nature he commanded—tracing the patterns of destiny. Fae perished and banshees fled before the thrashing torrents he channeled.

Elena emerged from the primordial thickets of South America—kept by her tribe’s amulets of ancient protection. Desdaichada, a handler of serpents, she wore a serpentine staff at her side, which twisted gases into potent venoms—dispelling the forces of darkness, corralling the essence of these elusive entities into her serpent’s grasp. The twin serpents pulsated alongside her, striking when shadows struck.

Joined by Yuki the Yumeno, a shapeshifter cloaked in celestial white, she transcended the boundaries of anatomy. Ether’s Yumeno whispered from the shadows of the forest where she was raised, her celestial nature granting her power beyond mortal ken. Wielding the power of transformation, she became an immaterial specter, leading enemies into a labyrinth of their own undoing.

Asher, the seer from Damascus, bequeathed mastery over the celestial script found not in starlit skies but imprinted upon the very souls of men. Conjuring divine judgments from beyond the celestial axis, revelational manifestations created cracks in the universe, causing the damned to bear witness to their transgressions.

Merging into this unlikely fraternity, Margaret, the keeper of the Obsidian Mirror, bore the burden of peering into multiple realms to witness the consequences of each individual’s action. Compelled by the whispering voice within her Obsidian Mirror, she broached the black abyss to conjure spectral harbinger’s—at the brink of hopelessness, she would be their beacon.

Among them, Nicholas an enigmatic scholar, could submerge into the darkest recesses of the mind, emerging as a harbinger of nightmares. His latent power to trap the soul within visions unchained the tyrant within the tortured spirit—wielded expertly and exacted horrific revenge, leaving an alarming desolation in its wake.

As the earth trembled beneath the weight of unknown hands, the once lush jungle met with the cacophonous furnace of Yggdrasil, the cosmic tree. Its roots consumed the moist soil; its branches, fiery leaves, blazed and twisted, rending the darkness aside.

Each of the twelve bore scars from suffering the Phantom Forces, an arcane wound inflicted by the unknown. But as scar tissue strengthened and knit spirits closer together, these unassuming heroes formed a deep resonance for one another, transcending the cruelty that beset their lives.

Sharing the burden of their curse, conspiring secrets against the uncanny Wraithbeasts that sought their unnatural demise, the twelve became the Nexus—a stronghold against portals to otherworldly gateways. Resting in spectral repose between life and death, it ionized the denizens of Phantom Forces, oppressed under a malevolent wombsprawl of entities that danced in accordance to the macabre ebb and flow of raw chaos.

As voracious time bound these Not-Saints within the Nexus, each member of the fraternity sought the source of their devastating gifts to better understand their origin. And so beneath a tapestry of subtle whispers and archaic incantations, knowledge sprung forth.

Steeped in ancient verses from the hidden pages of eclipse-scorched scrolls, they pursued every inked verse thirstily, each one rewinding the universe’s colossal death-rattle and unearthing the latent code locked within.

Soon they tackled sleepless nights, their minds conditioned to decipher cryptic lilts hidden within unmarked laminae, consciously unraveling secrets withdrawn from the sight of humans and the machinations of the occult’s sophistries. The sphinxes of Sphinx would once again reveal their secrets fixed in hiding.

But fate was about to take a twisted turn. In their search for arcanum, one by one, this sensational group of twelve met a twisted demise. An insidious malignancy twisted its tendrils around several hearts, their essences ripped asunder and devoured by the black sorcery of the Phantom Forces.

As though struck by a dual-edged blade pausing just before the caustic grim, a formidable power-imbued artifact returned to its creator—Deimos, a harbinger of dread whose purpose was to ravage the underbelly of this supernatural world. After millennia spent in the grip of an immortal curse, Deimos was destined to possess the wherewithal to salvage what remained of their twisted souls—gluing themselves to a reanimated avatar laced in eternal darkness.

Having outlived their dreaded fates, among the discarded bones lay hints only to be deciphered by a few undying survivors. Decrying their most brutal end in defiance, hidden beneath Yggdrasil’s roots engraved in obsidian character—the semblance of hope whispered against the sinister gust of a windstorm branded by the nether regions of the unknown.

Had they been victorious and banded themselves together to prevent the end of the world? Had their sacrifices been truly in vain?

As my words tremble, the remnants of a revelation reach me through the ripples of time. One can only answer with a blood-curdling battle cry that forms upon the lips of those who possess the audacity and darkness inherent to the Phantom Forces: Supernatural End of the World.
