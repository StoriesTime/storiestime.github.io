Epic Comebacks: How Sporting Legends Defied the Odds


Comeback stories are an integral part of sports lore. They inspire, motivate, and leave us questioning the very fabric of human determination. From athletes to teams, legends to underdogs, these epic resurgences remind us that it's never too late to change the script of a game, or even a career. The Impossible Shot In the world of golf, few stories are as iconic as Greg Norman's (yes, it's 'Norman, not 'Nairn') escape from the brink of disaster. It was May 13th, 1986, at the Masters Tournament's final round when the Australian golfer shot a mind-boggling 18-under-par, forcing a playoff with Seve Ballesteros. The stage was set for a thrilling showdown. With his opponent two strokes ahead, Norman needed a miracle to stay in the running. This was when he unleashed what can only be described as his masterclass. Six birdies in the final stretch propelled him onto the 18th hole tied with Ballesteros. On the treacherous 18th hole, Norman needed a birdie to level the scores. Using every ounce of grit he possessed, Norman held his nerve and aced the shot to tie the game. The Both players then headed to the final hole for an up-and-down battle. Just when everyone thought Ballesteros would emerge victorious, Norman sublimely slotted his third-straight birdie putt to force another playoff round. Only two holes remained in the reprieve, and what followed was indicative of what made Greg Norman the ultimate competitor.
