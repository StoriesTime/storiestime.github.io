Colorblind: Tales of Unity in a Divided World


In a world where the color of one’s eyes was more than just a physical trait, it was a symbol of identity, status, and power. This world was divided not by geographical boundaries, but by the hues that adorned the irises of its inhabitants. Those with the rarest and most vibrant shades enjoyed lives of luxury, while those with the most common tones were often relegated to the fringes of society.

Aimee was born in a small, humble village on the outskirts of the Divided Kingdom. She had inherited her mother’s brown eyes, a color so ordinary that it placed her at the very bottom of the social ladder. The Kingdom, divided into four distinct regions—each named after the dominant eye color of its residents—was a land where color was more than skin-deep. It was a marker of worth.

The four regions were known as the Crimson Court, the Azure Dominion, the Verdant Alliance, and the Amber Territory. The Crimson Court ruled with an iron fist, the Azure Dominion was known for its intellectual might, the Verdant Alliance for its vibrant arts and culture, and the Amber Territory, where Aimee resided, was a place where survival came before all else.

For Aimee, the concept of unity was foreign. The idea that people could come together despite their differences seemed like a dream. Her entire life had been defined by the way her world was divided along the lines of color. People of her kind, with brown eyes, were dismissed, often forced into the laborious tasks of farming, cleaning, and maintaining the kingdom’s infrastructure. They lived in the shadow of the other regions, never allowed to participate in the higher echelons of society.

One evening, as Aimee walked home from the market, she noticed something odd—a bright flash of light coming from the direction of the Azure Dominion. It was rare for anyone from her territory to be anywhere near the intellectual city, but the sight of this light intrigued her. It seemed to pulse, growing brighter and more intense with each passing moment. Unable to resist her curiosity, Aimee made her way toward the light.

What she found was a sight she had never expected: a group of people, their eyes glowing with shades of red, blue, green, and amber, standing in a circle, their hands raised to the sky. The air around them shimmered with an energy that seemed to defy the natural laws of the world. These people, she realized, were not from any one region—they were from everywhere. And they were doing something she had never seen before: they were uniting.

In the center of the circle stood a figure, their eyes a brilliant blend of all the colors that separated the world. Aimee had never seen such a thing before. Her heart raced as she stepped closer, her feet carrying her toward this gathering, unsure of what was happening.

“Welcome, Aimee,” a voice called out to her, startling her.

She looked around, but no one was looking directly at her, yet the voice was clear. It came from the figure in the center, whose eyes now seemed to reflect the colors of every person in the circle. “You have been chosen.”

“Chosen?” Aimee stammered, confused and unsure of what to make of the situation.

The figure stepped forward, reaching out a hand. “Yes, Aimee. You are here because you are ready for a change. A change that begins within you. The world you know is divided, but it doesn’t have to be that way.”

Aimee’s heart pounded in her chest. She had heard stories of unity—tales from the distant past that were whispered about by the elders. A time when people lived as one, without concern for the color of their eyes. But such stories were nothing but myths now, legends that no one took seriously.

“What is this place?” Aimee asked, still unsure of the situation.

“This is the Nexus,” the figure replied. “It is a space that exists outside the boundaries of the four regions, where people of every hue come together to transcend the divisions of their world. We are the Colorblind, individuals who no longer see color as a means of separation, but as a way to unify.”

Aimee frowned. “But… how? How can something like this work? The world has always been divided. It’s been this way for centuries.”

The figure smiled, a knowing, gentle expression. “You are right. But the change begins with the realization that the very thing that divides us—the color of our eyes—can also be the very thing that binds us together. We are all part of something greater, Aimee. We are all pieces of a single, united whole.”

Aimee’s mind raced. She thought back to the years of her life, spent in a world where color was everything. It was everything her parents had taught her, everything her teachers had drilled into her head. She had been told that people with brown eyes were destined for a certain life, and those with other colors were meant for another. But what if everything she had been told was wrong?

“Why me?” she asked. “Why am I chosen to be here?”

The figure’s eyes softened, and a subtle warmth emanated from them. “Because you see beyond the surface. You’ve lived in the shadows of others, but you have the power to see beyond what is. You are ready to understand that the color of our eyes is just a small part of who we are.”

The more Aimee thought about it, the more it made sense. Every person she had ever met had something unique about them that went beyond the color of their eyes. There was kindness, strength, resilience—qualities that couldn’t be seen through mere color. But society had long ago decided that those qualities were secondary to the color that defined them.

“But how do we make a change?” Aimee asked, her voice trembling with the weight of the question.

The figure looked around at the gathered group, then back at Aimee. “It starts with awareness. Awareness of the unity we can share, the strength we can build, if we are willing to cast aside the old beliefs. It starts with realizing that our differences make us stronger, not weaker. And it starts with you, Aimee.”

Aimee felt a flicker of hope inside her chest, something she hadn’t felt in years. It was a quiet feeling, but it was powerful. She had always believed that change was impossible, that the world was set in its ways. But what if she had been wrong? What if there was something she could do?

The figure extended their hand toward her. “Come, Aimee. The journey is just beginning.”

Aimee hesitated for a moment. The world she knew was built on division, on separation. To embrace unity would be to challenge everything she had ever known. But in that moment, standing before this group, surrounded by the hum of possibility, she realized that the world had never truly been as it seemed. She was no longer the girl from the Amber Territory, bound by the expectations of others. She was something more.

Taking a deep breath, Aimee stepped forward, placing her hand in the figure’s. As soon as their hands touched, something extraordinary happened. A surge of energy coursed through her, and the world around her seemed to shift. Colors bloomed in the air, swirling in a kaleidoscope of shades that danced around her like fireflies. She felt a connection to every person in the circle, a deep, unspoken bond that transcended the limitations of her past.

“You are part of something greater now,” the figure whispered. “A change is coming, Aimee. And you are one of its catalysts.”

For the first time in her life, Aimee understood. She was not bound by the color of her eyes. She was bound by something far greater: the shared humanity that connected all people, regardless of their differences.

And with that realization, the future of the Divided Kingdom began to change.

As Aimee stood there, in the heart of the Nexus, surrounded by those who saw beyond color, she knew that the path ahead would not be easy. But for the first time, she was no longer afraid. She had discovered the power of unity—a power that transcended the barriers of color and brought people together in ways that could reshape the world.

The journey had only just begun.
