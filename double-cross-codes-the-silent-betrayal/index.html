Double Cross Codes: The Silent Betrayal


In the heart of a small town in rural Versailles, there stood a quaint antique shop worthy of Merlin’s own arsenal. Down a side alley and around the corner, barely hidden by a portly fig tree, lay the secret entrance to an Apothecary known only to the locals. Few strangers frequented this enigmatic place, yet its reputation proceeded it amongst those in the know.

Behind the counter, Thomas, an extremely efficient old man with eyes like winter storms, mixed potions that satisfied almost every request. He was unassuming, with an air of mystery surrounding him. Despite his appearance, the Apothecary often found itself attracting the attention of groups who required substances only a few could dream of finding.

One stormy night, accompanied by the scent of smashed flowers and burnt matches, a procession of hooded figures entered the Apothecary. Among these shadows, the leader – a person with silver-rimmed glasses that held eyes almost too bright – was well-known: they were the master of codes.

“Thomas,” he whispered, “we are in need of a concoction that would render an opponent passive and unguarded.”

“The Silent Betrayal, right?” Thomas replied, lining up an assortment of strange ingredients.

“That’s the one.” The master nodded, revealing a small, intricate box. A network of copper gears encased within its walls, creating a labyrinth of uncertainty.

“This silent betrayal can grant us the victory we require. A double cross that will seemingly vanish without a trace. Simply provide us the formula on the language that will leave those to whom it is given utterly clueless.”

Thomas worked under the cover of night, brewing, pouring, and chanting. As the concoction neared completion, the wind picked up, carrying the raindrops sideways. Still, he carried on, determined to deliver the final product.

The master, meanwhile, hired a team of covert agents, trained in infiltration and espionage. Discretion was their motto, and silence their weapon. Among them, there was a spy who sought the vulnerable, and a thief who coveted what belonged to others.

The Apothecary’s final product was a deep purple, its scent tinged with a mixture of clove and nightshade. The keeper of the codes added the lethal-distilled potion into his chart, creating the ‘Doppelganger Code’ that would wipe an intruder’s memory, erasing any suspicion following their betrayal.

With this, the master of codes assumed a sinister smile. He knew the Silent Betrayal would be their strongest weapon yet.

The group infiltrated their target’s base, their steps as silent as the night’s shadows. They found their unsuspecting target, an esteemed merchant, deep in the heart of his own fortress-like safehouse.

The lead agent encased the merchant’s glass-like figure in a web of hidden threads. The target, lured to their doom, slumbered within… and the silent betrayal took hold.

Trusting them implicitly, the unwitting merchant unknowingly divulged secrets, betraying himself for the sake of their objective. Following this, his memories vanished like dust in the breeze, helpless against the cunning mastery of the double cross.

While their plan was a success, the consequences of this secret betrayal weighed heavily on the agents involved. Doubt tugged at the edges of their conscience, as once-faithful allies disappeared into the mists of memory.

The master of codes had vanished, leaving behind a trail of strangers lost in a world they no longer recognized. Thomas, the keeper of the Apothecary secrets, stood quiet. And the thief and the spy moved silently, weaving their fates with the tapestry of silent betrayals none could untangle.

Yet each one felt the dread that follows an act of treachery; an inescapable shadow, like the none that would ever see the light of a new dawn. The Silent Betrayal consumed those who dabbled in it, leaving them blind to the consequences they had sown. In their world, dark dealings and betrayal ruled - while the cursed and forsaken fell into silence. And with this sinister truth revealed, not even the rivers of forgetfulness could wash away the Double Cross Codes, remaining hidden and embedded in the heart of Versailles.
