Creepy Ghost Stories from Abandoned Places Around the World


There’s something undeniably eerie about abandoned places. Whether it’s a crumbling hospital, a forgotten village, or a desolate factory, these forsaken locations seem to carry the weight of lost time, memories, and secrets. Many of these abandoned sites have a history that’s been buried in dust and silence for decades, but some believe they still echo with the presence of spirits—ghosts trapped by their untold stories. Here are some of the creepiest ghost tales tied to abandoned places from around the world.

1. The Ghostly Wail of Hashima Island, Japan

Hashima Island, often called “Gunkanjima” or “Battleship Island” because of its silhouette resembling a warship, was once a bustling coal mining hub off the coast of Nagasaki. From the 1880s to the 1970s, this island housed thousands of miners, along with their families. But as the demand for coal waned, the island was abandoned, and the once-thriving community was left to decay.

Visitors who dare to explore the island today often report hearing ghostly wails echoing through the hollow, wind-swept buildings. Some believe these are the spirits of the miners who perished in accidents, or the families who died in the harsh conditions. Others claim to see ghostly figures moving in the abandoned apartments, their outlines blurry, as if trapped in time. The most chilling reports come from those who’ve ventured deep into the tunnels, only to feel a cold, invisible presence following them. Those who’ve experienced this sensation describe it as a feeling of dread, as if something unseen is watching and waiting to pounce.

Hashima Island is off-limits to the public without special permission, and though there have been efforts to preserve the crumbling buildings, many still remain in a state of eerie disrepair. The island, now overrun with weeds and rusted structures, serves as a stark reminder of a once-thriving society—and the restless spirits who may still haunt it.

2. The Silent Voices of Pripyat, Ukraine

Perhaps one of the most famous abandoned places in the world is Pripyat, a city that was home to workers of the Chernobyl Nuclear Power Plant, located in northern Ukraine. On April 26, 1986, the world witnessed one of the worst nuclear disasters in history. The Chernobyl disaster led to the immediate evacuation of the city, which is still frozen in time to this day, over three decades later.

Pripyat’s abandoned buildings are eerily well-preserved, with everyday objects left behind as though the people who lived there just vanished overnight. There are countless stories of ghostly apparitions appearing in the empty rooms of the high-rise apartment blocks. Some have reported seeing shadows moving behind broken windows, while others have heard footsteps in the halls, despite the city being completely devoid of human life.

The most persistent ghost stories surrounding Pripyat involve the ferris wheel in the city’s amusement park, which was meant to open on May 1, 1986, but was never used due to the evacuation. Tourists who visit the ferris wheel have reported seeing the ride begin to move, creaking as if someone unseen is riding it, or hearing the faint sounds of children laughing and shouting. Some even claim to see figures standing at the top, only to vanish when the wheel reaches the peak.

Despite the contamination and danger, Pripyat has become a popular site for “dark tourism.” But no matter how many visitors come and go, it’s hard to shake the feeling that the city’s long-gone residents are still lingering in the shadows.

3. The Haunting of Poveglia Island, Italy

Poveglia Island, located in the Venetian Lagoon, is widely regarded as one of the most haunted places in the world. The island’s history dates back to the 14th century, when it was used as a quarantine station for people infected with the plague. Thousands of sick and dying individuals were sent to the island, where many of them perished, their bodies burned in large pyres. In the 1920s, the island was repurposed as a mental asylum, and once again, death followed closely behind.

It is said that the souls of those who died on Poveglia Island—whether from the plague or the horrors of the asylum—still roam its abandoned buildings. Many visitors claim to have seen shadowy figures moving between the ruins, and there are persistent rumors of strange, disembodied voices carried on the wind. The island’s bell tower, once used to signal the arrival of the sick, is now one of the most terrifying places on the island. Some believe the bell still rings on its own, while others have heard the voices of the suffering, trapped in an endless cycle of torment.

The haunting of Poveglia has made it a place of fascination for paranormal investigators. Yet, despite the island’s chilling reputation, the Italian government has forbidden public access due to its dangerous condition, adding another layer of mystery to this already sinister place.

4. The Abandoned Eastern State Penitentiary, USA

In the heart of Philadelphia stands the Eastern State Penitentiary, a once-feared institution that housed some of America’s most notorious criminals, including Al Capone. Opened in 1829, the penitentiary was known for its revolutionary (but harsh) system of solitary confinement, which drove many inmates to madness. After being abandoned in 1971, the crumbling fortress became a hotbed for paranormal activity.

Visitors who have taken the tour of the penitentiary often report an overwhelming sense of dread upon entering the long, dark hallways. Many claim to hear the sounds of footsteps and distant, echoing cries from the solitary cells. Some have even seen shadowy figures darting across the empty cell blocks, with others describing cold gusts of wind that seem to come from nowhere.

One of the most chilling reports comes from a group of ghost hunters who claim to have witnessed a shadowy figure standing at the end of a hallway. The figure reportedly turned and disappeared into one of the cells, but when they approached the area, it was empty, save for the oppressive silence. Paranormal experts suggest that the spirits of the tortured inmates still wander the penitentiary, their restlessness fueling the hauntings.

Today, Eastern State Penitentiary is open to the public, offering haunted tours, especially around Halloween. Yet, no matter how many times it is explored, the chilling atmosphere and the lingering spirits of the past continue to make it one of the most haunted locations in the United States.

5. The Vanishing Children of the Beelitz-Heilstätten Hospital, Germany

Deep in the forests of Brandenburg, Germany, lies the Beelitz-Heilstätten Hospital, a sprawling complex that was once a tuberculosis sanatorium. The hospital later served as a military hospital during both World Wars, and even as a Soviet army hospital after World War II. After its closure in the 1990s, the sprawling hospital complex was left to decay. But it wasn’t just the walls that were left behind—the stories of the spirits that haunt it have also survived.

The most famous ghost story surrounding Beelitz-Heilstätten involves a group of children who mysteriously vanished within the hospital. Locals claim that the children’s ghosts still haunt the corridors, their faint, ethereal laughter filling the air. Others have heard the sound of small footsteps echoing through the halls, only to find no one there when they turn around.

Some ghost hunters have reported seeing fleeting apparitions of children peering out from behind rusted doors or looking out of broken windows, their faces distorted and pale. The hospital’s abandoned operating rooms are said to be particularly haunted, with reports of strange, cold spots and the feeling of unseen hands brushing against visitors.

Despite its eerie reputation, Beelitz-Heilstätten remains a popular destination for urban explorers and paranormal enthusiasts. However, its history of suffering and death makes it a place that’s never truly free of its tragic past.

6. The Cursed Village of Oradour-sur-Glane, France

In 1944, during World War II, the village of Oradour-sur-Glane in southwest France was the site of a horrific massacre by the Nazis. On June 10, 1944, the German SS rounded up the villagers, separating the men, women, and children before executing them in cold blood. The village was then set ablaze, leaving it a ghostly ruin.

Today, Oradour-sur-Glane remains as it was left after the tragedy, preserved as a memorial to the lives lost. The abandoned village is often said to be haunted by the spirits of those who perished. Visitors have reported hearing distant screams and seeing apparitions of the villagers walking the streets. Some claim that they’ve witnessed the ghostly figures of children playing near the charred remains of their homes, or the spectral forms of women and men standing silently by the remains of the church.

The village’s haunting atmosphere is undeniably powerful, as if the sorrow and anger of those who were slaughtered still linger in the air. Despite its tragic history, Oradour-sur-Glane has become a symbol of remembrance, ensuring that the horrors of the past are never forgotten—but the spirits of the dead are never truly at peace.

7. The Abandoned Château de Brissac, France

Nestled in the Loire Valley of France, the Château de Brissac is an imposing structure with a rich history—one that includes a gruesome murder. In the 15th century, the castle was the site of a violent crime when a nobleman discovered that his wife, Charlotte, had been unfaithful. In a fit of rage, he killed her and her lover within the castle’s walls.

It is said that Charlotte’s ghost still haunts the château to this day. Visitors and staff have reported seeing her ghostly figure roaming the halls, particularly in the castle’s green salon, where she was allegedly murdered. Her apparition is said to appear in a flowing green gown, and some claim to hear her sorrowful cries echoing through the castle at night.

The Château de Brissac is still open to the public, and visitors can tour its grand halls and admire its centuries-old architecture. But despite the beauty of the place, there’s an undeniable sense of unease, as if the spirits of the past refuse to leave their home.

Abandoned places around the world are steeped in stories of the past, many of them laced with tragedy, death, and mystery. Whether or not one believes in ghosts, there’s no denying the eerie atmosphere that surrounds these forgotten places. The stories shared here, from the haunted halls of ancient prisons to the spectral figures that wander deserted villages, prove that sometimes, the past is never truly gone—it lingers in the shadows, waiting to be remembered.
