The Legend of the Moonlit Elk

One of the most beloved legends of Glendora was that of the Moonlit Elk, a majestic creature said to appear only once every hundred years. According to the villagers, the elk was a guardian spirit of the forest, its antlers shimmering like silver under the moonlight. As the story went, the Moonlit Elk had been born during a rare celestial event, when the full moon aligned perfectly with the stars of the Orion constellation. On this night, the forest would glow with an ethereal light, and the elk would emerge from the shadows, its fur glistening like stardust.

Each year, during the harvest festival, the elders of Glendora would tell the story of the Moonlit Elk to the children gathered around the bonfire.
