Unwritten Rules: Short Stories on Gender and Desire


In the city’s dense web of glass towers and burrowed streets, Mia found herself repeatedly tangled in patterns she couldn’t quite unthread. The unwritten rules whispered in boardrooms and lunchrooms, in shared glances and sidelong smiles, crafted invisible fences around her ambitions and desires.

Mia was a graphic designer at a prominent agency, known for her sharp eye and relentless work ethic. Yet, the subtle currents of gender and desire often collided with her professional reality.

The Meeting That Changed Nothing

That Tuesday morning, the agency’s conference room was abuzz with anticipation. The team gathered for a brainstorming session about an upcoming campaign meant to challenge outdated beauty standards—an ironic setting given the traditional dynamics at play.

Ethan, the company’s creative director, had recently been assigned as her project lead. Tall, confident, with a penchant for smooth persuasion, Ethan seemed to navigate the office’s intricate social structure effortlessly.

As Mia presented her ideas—fierce visuals that elevated diverse femininity—Ethan interjected gently yet firmly. “Great concepts, Mia, but maybe we should tone down the boldness just a bit to appeal more broadly?” His tone was casual, almost collaborative, but the message landed like a soft barricade.

Mia knew that suggestion was less about market research and more about an unspoken expectation: women’s voices needed cushioning.

Hidden Sentences in Workplace Scripts

The story didn’t end with a single meeting. It permeated Mia’s daily interactions, where conversations carried coded meanings:

  • Being called “assertive” or “abrasive” when leading a project.
  • Receiving less credit than male counterparts for shared ideas.
  • Feeling an unspoken pressure to present herself in ways that weren’t her natural inclination.

Mia started jotting down these moments, recognizing a pattern in the unwritten rules that governed gender and desire in her workplace culture. It was as if the soft power of desire shaped perceptions—who was ‘acceptable,’ who was ‘too much,’ who fit into the predefined roles.

Desire’s Dual Edge

Desire, Mia realized, operated on multiple planes—not just the romantic or physical, but also in professional admiration and societal expectations.

When she observed Ethan, she saw how his confident charisma was often interpreted as leadership, while her directness triggered discomfort or dismissal.

One evening, after a long day, Mia attended a casual office gathering. She noticed Ethan effortlessly flirting with a colleague, his charm disarming the room. She understood that his ability to express desire, or at least the perception of it, granted him social capital.

Conversely, Mia felt compelled to mask any romantic or sexual expression, wary it would undermine her professionalism or subject her to unwarranted assumptions.

Breaking the Pattern

Determined not to be defined by these unseen binaries, Mia experimented with subtle shifts. She cultivated alliances with other women in the agency, sharing stories and strategies. Group lunches became opportunities to demystify the unwritten codes.

She voiced her concerns directly to Ethan in a one-on-one, framing the conversation in terms of inclusivity and creative freedom.

“I want to bring my full self to this work,” Mia said, eyes steady, “but sometimes the way things are done here feels limiting.”

Ethan listened, genuinely surprised. It dawned on him that his ‘broad appeal’ ideas were often just a mask for maintaining the status quo.

Reclaiming Desire, Rewriting Rules

Mia and her team eventually launched the campaign—bold, diverse, unapologetic. It resonated with audiences and sparked conversations beyond the confines of their usual clientele.

The unwritten rules didn’t vanish overnight, but cracks showed. Desire, understood as multifaceted and dynamic, began to unravel old assumptions.

Mia learned that rewriting these norms involved vulnerability, solidarity, and the courage to demand authenticity in every facet of life.

Epilogue: The Threads Unraveled

Months later, Mia walked through a park during her lunch break, watching a street performer juggle flaming torches. The performer’s art was a delicate balance of risk and mastery—much like navigating the complex dance of identity and desire in public and private spheres.

Mia smiled, feeling a quiet pride. This dance, these unwritten rules—they were threads she could weave into her own tapestry, not constraints but colors to blend and reshape.

The city pulsed around her, full of stories untold, rules unspoken, and desires waiting to be embraced.

Unwritten rules shape much of how we experience gender and desire, infringing or empowering, depending on who wields them and who challenges them. Mia’s journey is but one narrative in a larger story of how individuals negotiate identity, power, and aspiration within social and professional realms.
