Cityshades: Darker Tales from the Concrete Underworld


Once upon a time, in the heart of an ancient city that had seen its fair share of empires rise and fall, there existed a world beneath the streets. A world that the people rarely thought about, yet one that thrived amidst the concrete and the noise above. This was the Concrete Underworld.

In this world, the unpredictable and the mysterious coexisted, and shadows lurked in every alleyway. These were the Cityshades – a group of misfits that lived and breathed in the darkness, each with their own tale of how they came to be.

Sejanus was the self-proclaimed king of this underworld, a name whispered with both fear and reverence. His confessed hobby was collecting artifacts, forbidden relics from times long past. Kept safely in his secret library, these items were but a small reminder of the life above ground.

It was one such relic that changed everything – a curious amulet. It seemed ordinary, its design simple enough, but its stone held the power to dig deeper into the minds of others.

Sejanus, curious to know more about his Cityshades, decided to use this amulet to delve into their pasts.

The first in line was Ligeia, a skilled illusionist. Using the amulet to explore her past, Sejanus discovered that she once belonged to a notorious gang of thieves. They wielded magical powers that even impressed the most seasoned magicians above ground. With emotions ranging from excitement to terror, Sejanus watched Ligeia’s fall from grace, her powers corrupted and twisted by her unraveling life.

Next was Raphael, a factory working by day and an artist by night. Sejanus couldn’t help but marvel at Raphael’s powerful imagination, painted tunnels alive with color and hope. But as the amulet revealed, Raphael’s art was more than simple escapism. Each painting held a hidden message, a way of communicating through the chaos that was the Concrete Underworld.

As Sejanus ventured deeper into the Concrete Underworld, he stumbled upon La Baronne – a master manipulator, her beauty and charm weaving intricate webs of deceit. Sejanus laid bare her sordid past – her origins as a con artist, playing victim as easily as she played any other role. La Baronne’s schemes played out before Sejanus, each more elaborate and ambitious than the last, culminating in her eventual betrayal and ousting from her once-revered position above ground.

Finally, Sejanus found himself face to face with the last of the Cityshades – Lillith, a mysterious witch who spoke in riddles. Though Sejanus had seen the darkest secrets of the others, the amulet revealed little of Lillith’s past. Her life was but a series of shadows, veils of secrets and lies obscuring her true self.

Sejanus trembled as he witnessed Lillith’s immense power, a force equal to any above ground, even the mightiest of wizards. Her magic twisted and writhed, the danger of her powers known only to the Cityshades.

Stunned, Sejanus watched as Lillith slipped away, vanishing into the darkness like the shadow she was. He knew he could never uncover the last piece of the puzzle – Lillith’s past.

The amulet slipped from Sejanus’s grip, its power and secrets no longer his to wield. It clattered to the stone floor, the echo reverberating through the Concrete Underworld.

As Sejanus stood in the darkness, he pondered the Cityshades and their hidden lives. They were more than just denizens of the shadows; they were survivors, each with their own story of loss, ambition, and struggle.

The history of the Concrete Underworld echoed around Sejanus, an unspoken tale of resilience and defiance against the chaos above. And though he no longer held the amulet’s power, he understood that he too was a part of this world – a piece of the puzzle, forever seeking the answers to the enigmatic lives of the Cityshades.
