Voices in the Mind: A Tale of Living with PTSD


Eli stood at the edge of the window, his hand resting on the cool glass, eyes staring into the unfamiliar city below. It was quieter than he expected, for a place so vast and brimming with life. The faint hum of traffic seemed distant, as if muffled by an invisible barrier. His chest tightened with a familiar pressure—a sensation he knew too well by now. It was the feeling of being trapped inside his own mind.

He hadn’t asked for this. No one ever asked for the demons that came with the darkness of PTSD. The disorder had a way of erasing everything that once felt stable, replacing it with an invisible weight. For Eli, every moment was a balancing act between keeping it together and losing himself completely.

Every day was a struggle. The past didn’t fade with time. It clung to him like an invisible cloak, its edges frayed with memories he couldn’t escape. Some were clear, sharp as glass, while others were fogged and distant, yet all were equally haunting. It wasn’t just the flashbacks or the nightmares. It was the voices—the constant, grating whispers that invaded his thoughts.

“You’re weak,” they would say. “You’ll never escape it.”

Eli had learned to ignore the voices, to push them down, but it was becoming harder each day. He didn’t want to move. He didn’t want to face the world. But he had to. For her.

His daughter, Emma, was coming over for the weekend. The thought of seeing her gave him a brief surge of warmth, but it was always followed by guilt. Could he be the father she needed? Could he protect her from the chaos inside his own mind? The answer was always unclear.

Chapter 2: The Past That Won’t Let Go

The first time Eli had truly realized the grip PTSD had on his life, he had been standing in the kitchen, staring at the boiling kettle. The kettle had whistled, and in that moment, the noise transported him back to a different time—a different place. He saw himself, young and terrified, on a battlefield far from home. The deafening sound of gunfire, the shouts of his comrades, the smell of smoke and blood—everything flooded his senses at once.

He couldn’t breathe. He felt the heat of the fire, the tension in the air. His pulse hammered in his throat, his skin tingling with fear. The world around him vanished, replaced by a twisted echo of a past he couldn’t outrun. His hands shook, and he dropped the kettle, the sharp shatter of porcelain pulling him back to reality.

In that moment, it felt as if he was still there—on the battlefield, just a few steps from death, a few breaths away from losing everything. But he wasn’t there. He was in his kitchen, alone, his heart racing.

He had learned to manage the flashbacks over time, to ground himself back in the present, but the voices never stopped. They whispered at the edges of his mind, always reminding him that he wasn’t enough, that he wasn’t strong enough to handle it. That he was broken.

Chapter 3: The Voices Within

The voices came in waves, some louder than others, but always there. They were no longer just remnants of the past. They were constant companions. At times, they sounded like his own thoughts, twisting themselves into something unrecognizable. Other times, they were the voices of people he had known—friends, family, comrades—people he had left behind.

“You could’ve saved them,” one voice would accuse. “You didn’t try hard enough.”

At other times, they were faceless, disembodied voices that filled the silence with dread.

“Do you even know who you are anymore?” they’d sneer. “You’ll never be free. You’ll never be okay.”

He tried to drown them out. Music, books, therapy, medication—he had tried everything. And while some days were better than others, the voices never truly went away. They’d subside, only to return stronger, angrier, more insistent.

Chapter 4: The Turning Point

Eli had long since stopped attending social gatherings. The crowds, the noise, the unfamiliar faces—all of it was overwhelming. He had retreated into a shell, unable to face the world in any meaningful way. But Emma had a way of bringing him back to life, even if just for a moment.

On one particularly difficult evening, she had arrived with a drawing she had made for him. It was a picture of the two of them, sitting under a tree in their backyard. It was simple, childlike, but it filled him with a warmth he hadn’t felt in a long time. It was the first time in weeks that the weight of the voices seemed to lighten.

“Daddy,” Emma said, her voice soft, “do you remember when we went to the park? I love that day. You looked so happy.”

For a brief second, Eli felt it—a flicker of hope. The day she spoke of had been simple, a day at the park when life hadn’t been consumed by the chaos of his mind. He could remember the sunlight filtering through the leaves, the sound of her laughter, and the feeling of being fully present. It was a rare memory, one that wasn’t tainted by the past.

But as quickly as it came, it disappeared. The voices, always lurking, began to creep back in.

“You’ll never be able to protect her from this,” they hissed. “She’ll see the truth. She’ll know you’re broken.”

Chapter 5: A New Path Forward

The breakthrough came unexpectedly. It wasn’t a grand gesture or a dramatic moment of clarity. It was a simple choice. After weeks of struggling, Eli made the decision to try something different. He began to open up to his therapist in ways he hadn’t before, allowing himself to confront the past—not just the battlefields or the loss, but the deeper, more painful truths that had been buried for so long.

It wasn’t easy. The process was slow, and the voices were relentless. But for the first time in a long time, Eli began to feel a flicker of something else—something like control. He began to understand that healing wasn’t about erasing the past. It was about learning to live with it, to move forward without letting it define him entirely.

He also started to reconnect with the world around him, not in big, overwhelming ways, but in small, manageable steps. He visited the park with Emma again, allowing himself to be present in the moment, to feel the warmth of the sun on his skin, and to hear the sound of her laughter without the constant hum of anxiety in his ears.

Chapter 6: Moving Forward

The road to recovery was far from linear. There were still bad days, days when the voices threatened to swallow him whole, when the past seemed too heavy to carry. But Eli no longer faced it alone. He had begun to rebuild the relationships that had frayed under the weight of his trauma. With Emma, he found a new sense of purpose—a reason to keep going, even when everything felt impossible.

He still heard the voices, sometimes louder than others, but he was learning to face them, to tell them that they no longer controlled him. It was a long journey, one that would never truly end. But each day, each small step forward, felt like a victory.

Eli stood at the window again, looking out at the city below. The voices were there, lurking, but they no longer had the power to dictate his life. He wasn’t fixed. He wasn’t healed. But he was trying, and that was enough.

For the first time in a long time, Eli felt the weight lift, if only slightly, and for the first time in years, he allowed himself to believe that maybe, just maybe, he could be okay.
