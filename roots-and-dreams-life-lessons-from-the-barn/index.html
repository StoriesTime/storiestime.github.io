Roots and Dreams: Life Lessons from the Barn


It was a sweltering summer evening in rural Wisconsin, and Emma had just finished a long day of helping her grandfather in the family farm. As she walked into the old barn, she was enveloped by the sweet scent of hay and the sound of crickets chirping. She tied her overalls to a worn wooden nail, and her grandfather nodded in approval, his graying hair peeking out from beneath his worn baseball cap. "Emma, come here for a spell," he said, wiping the sweat from his brow. "I want to teach you something." Emma loved spending time with her grandfather in the barn. He had learned everything he knew from his father, and his father before him, and he loved sharing his wisdom with his granddaughter. As she approached him, he handed her a small, gnarled root ball, roughly the size of a baseball. "What's this?" Emma asked, turning the root over in her hands. "Ah, that's a piece of a sugar maple," Grandpa said, his eyes twinkling. "See all those curls? That's like the twists and turns of life, kiddo. It's how you navigate those turns that determines the strength of your roots." Emma nodded intently. She had always loved hearing her grandfather's stories about the land and the trees. "Just because one branch might get tied back or broken, it doesn't mean it won't grow back stronger. Roots keep us grounded, but they also keep us flexible." Her grandfather smiled, his eyes squinting in the fading light. "That's what I'm trying to say. Now, let me show you something else." He led her to the corner of the barn, where an old wooden seedling rack hung from the eaves. Inside, thousands of tiny flower seeds waited, arranged in neat rows. "You see, Emma, these seeds – the oak, maple, and fir – they're all different shapes, sizes, and colors, but they all want to sprout into a mighty tree one day. Just like your life, kiddo. You come from a pod of desires, and you have all the potential to spread out, to grow into what God meant you to be." Emma smiled, thinking about how new life brought endless possibility. Some roots grow deeper and go wider, while others might grow taller and wider, each desiring their own identity. As she carefully selected a tiny oak seed from the row, he nodded in understanding. "This oak wants to be a canopy expert, spreading its branches high above the tall grass and hiding shade beneath for weary travelers. If it sticks closely with its pals in the forest, they'll share their nutrients, assist each other in pollination, and shade each other in the warm months. Different, of course, they all celebrate their diversity while recognizing we're part of a greater habit group.
