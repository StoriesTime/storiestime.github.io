Adventures in Express Thievery: High-Speed Pursuits and Artful Larceny


Amelia stood on the platform, her feet rooted to the electrified rails, and watched the sleek express train emerge from the tunnel. Its gleaming body shuddered and hissed as it danced over the tracks in a veritable ballet, a performance she had admired for years, ever since the day her father had told her tales of rail travel and adventure. But on this fine autumn day, the train was about to become more than the object of her admiration—it would become the means for Amelia to carry out her latest caper. To pull it off, she would need to employ not her usual bookish charms, but her guile and cunning. The train, it seemed, like so many things in life, could be both her muse and her adversary.

As the engine throttled back to a stoppage, Amelia inhaled deeply—she couldn’t afford to lose focus now. Her moment had come. As other passengers streamed onto the platform, she melted into the background, blending with the throng in her slender navy-blue dress. She was about to embark on a journey—no, a series of journeys—ones that would lead her from the bustling streets of the modern metropolis, across the wild terrain she’d explored in her mind’s eye, all the way to the magnificent treasures of her heart’s desire.

The first leg of her journey had to be elegant: enter the shadows when everyone was busy gawking at the steam and the screech. With a practiced maneuver, Amelia dipped into the crowd and stepped up into the forward-facing car. She kept her head down, choosing instead to look at the blue-and-white patterns of her worn shoes. The emphatic nervures beneath had seen her through countless escapades; now they were going to guide her through this one.

Once aboard, the cacophony of the station was replaced by the not-unpleasant rumble of the engine. A conductor, with a prim moustache and uniforms impeccably pressed, scuttled past; his eyes flickered over Amelia but she knew to remain statuesque. Attention, after all, was the enemy.

Now on the outside, Amelia pressed her nose against the cool glass, watching as the scenery blipped from urban to rural, the cityscape giving way to miles of sprawling green. The leaves on the trees were aflame with autumn’s colors—ochres, golds, and crimson. They danced along the trains windows like dervishes in celebration of the changing seasons. And beneath that canopy, amidst the rustling leaves, Amelia spied the object of her laboratory experiments; the means by which she hoped to scale the heights of her ambitions. It was a device unlike any she had built before: an intricate network of gears and springs that would allow her to leap from one moving object to another. She had named it the “Gravitur”.

As the landscape lulled her into a trance, her hands began to tremble with anticipation. What had begun as scribbles in a long-forgotten notebook was now a marvel of mechanical ingenuity. The Gravitur, her crowning jewel, her pièce de résistance. Amelia knew it was the key to impending success—if only she could execute her plan flawlessly.

The moment was nigh. She felt the siren call of her next target—the express train’s gleaming luggage car. There, she would display the full extent of her skill and daring. Pulling her hood over her auburn hair, Amelia used her small knife to make an imperceptible incision just where the door caught as it shut. An HBO-logo badge dangled temptingly before her; her eyes gleamed with the promise of a con woman’s cleverness.

The train slowed once more, and she knew it was crossing a bridge. Now was her chance. Amelia rose from her seat and took a deep breath. Here she stood, beside the train’s luggage car; poised upon the precipice of glory or disgrace. Trembling with anticipation, she waited for the precise moment as the car’s once solid seam became slightly ajar. With lightning speed, Amelia slipped the Gravitur between the door and the frame and, as the door shut, she felt the familiar hum of the device engorging itself with energy.

Then, with a soft whoosh and more than a sprinkling of hope and faith, she leapt.

The rushing wind met her face as she soared across the gap; not a moment too soon. She landed on the luggage car’s roof as nimbly as if she were stepping from one room to another. Her heart pounded; it had worked! She had done it!

Feeling emboldened, Amelia propelled herself forward. Leaping over the car’s smoky stacks, she made her way to the front, her eyes scanning the landscape for her next objective: a private passenger car—the home of her target.

The Gravitur didn’t fail her; on the private car’s balcony, Amelia zeroed in her sights and leaped with calculated precision. A rustle of silk and satin conveyed the occupants’ surprise at the sudden appearance of a striking young woman on their private balcony.

“Hop, skip, and a leap,” Amelia breathed, mimicking the juxtaposition of her express thievery. She had to hand it to herself; she was quite the spectacle. And, given her intent, she couldn’t afford to be anything but bold.

Amelia produced a crumpled piece of paper from her pocket. It bore a signature, one of affirmation, one of trust. Confidence radiating from her pores, she presented it to the astonished couple. With merely a nod and a smile, she slipped into the private car.

Her heart raced as she located her target—a rich velvet bag stowed in the corner. Carefully, she extracted a modest, bejeweled symbol of wealth, a broach, which quite suited her intentions. She knew it to be valuable, and it thrilled her. Her place in history secure – Amelia then twisted slightly, executing her exit strategy. Swiftly, she leapt through the car’s window and into the open air, relinquishing her hold on gravity and embracing the fierce wind.

The jeweled broach weighed heavy in her hand, and as she foothold retrieved her Gravitur, every step drew her further away from the express train and solidified her place in the history of express thievery. She had successfully pulled off an act of high-speed pursuit, cunning, and artful larceny. Most significantly, she proved that in the world teeming with juramentados, desperadoes, and know-it-alls – she had reached the summit of her ambition.

From the platform, beyond the dissipating steam and the huffy passengers, Amelia gazed back at the train. It was now reduced to but a whirling blur; it had become the unseen catalyst of her success. Little did the passengers know that, nestled among their bags of mundane garments and trinkets, a daring thief had unfolded within the vast, wide world of rail journeys, express thievery, high-speed pursuits, and artful larceny. And as the train vanished into the distance, Amelia began another chapter in her life, one written in the rich flourish of express thievery and the liberated cadence of adventure.

The end.
