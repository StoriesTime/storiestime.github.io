The Human Touch: Mother Teresa's Journey Beyond Sainthood


In the hallowed corridors of Calcutta’s slums, a figure serene and composed strode, navigating the maze of poverty and suffering that stretched before her. Her name was Teresa, but to the masses, she was none other than Mother Teresa - a beacon of hope in a world shrouded by despair. The palms of her calloused hands bore witness to the countless she’d embraced in her self-proclaimed vocation of making the poor rich in Christ.

The year was 1960, and grainy images of Mother Teresa cuddling the dying, war-torn children, and destitute souls flickered across the dimly lit world, inspiring countless followers of the doctrines of Christianity. Yet, little did her followers know that the Mother Teresa they adulated, was bound for a journey beyond the confines of sainthood.

A late evening in the city of Calcutta saw the silhouette of Mother Teresa as she ascended the stairs of her modest convent, a sigh of exhaustion escape her lips. Her face, worn and weary, yet sparkling with an inner delicacy of a sanguine spirit. Within her heart, a seed of dissent had been planted - an idea so heretical and contradictory to her well-known persona that it terrified her.

In the secret recesses of her mind, a voice softly nudged her to depart from Calcutta. And there, in the stillness of the night, the silhouette of Mother Teresa withered in the face of a man nearing the edge of life, close to death. It was time for her to bid adieu to Calcutta and begin a new journey.

And so she did, embarking on a voyage to spiritual transcendence, to Egypt of all places. Its winds, drenched with the tears of millions etched across the pages of glorious history, whispered secrets known only to desolate flutes, painting an ethereal perspective of her existence. It was there, in the heart of a land oppressive with the weight of its past, that she was to encounter an enigmatic stranger.

A passing street vendor’s gaze sought connection, and in Mother Teresa’s eyes, he found a sanctuary. Elderly and gaunt, the vendor’s tone was gravelly, with a scent of sediment. “Sister, my life has been spent serving Royalty. I fed them, dressed them, and attended to their every trivial need. But I’ve yet to receive the peace I seek; it’s eluded me throughout my existence.”

To this, Mother Teresa smiled, understanding his predicament for it mirrored her own struggle, “What has become of these Royalty now, my dear friend?”

The vendor, with a melancholic voice, whispered, “Power and grandeur once adorned them; now shrouded in squalor, unseen.”

Mother Teresa delicately traced her finger along the lines etched in his palms, “Dear man, can you not see, even the mighty fall? They, like you and I, are all equals in the embrace of our Creator.”

She tilted her head towards the window, gazing upon the horizon, “I too have exhausted my life in serving the downtrodden, yet an elusive understanding continues to dance away from my grasp.”

A sudden gasp escaped from the vendor’s lips, “Mother Teresa, it’s you!”

“Indeed,” she replied humbly, resting her head against the surface of the window, “I too have faltered, searching for the elusiveness that slips through my fingers.”

“Then it is not too late for you,” her companion persisted, clasping her shaky hand.

A stream of memories rushed before Mother Teresa, faces of the sick, hungry, lepers, children crying, her own frail body toiling, sweat dripping among the stench and decay. But, in the cacophony of suffering, she marveled at the abundance of love that perpetually resided within her.

“Could it be, dear man, the love I incessantly shower on others, has withheld me from experiencing my own revelation?”

The Egyptian street vendor nodded, “Indeed, Mother Teresa, for love transcends the cages and boundaries, seeking refuge in the sanctity of our hearts.”

Mother Teresa rose from her resolute seat and paced the room, her eyes closed as though beseeching guidance from a higher power. The veils of her existence unfolded; the journey of Mother Teresa was only the beginning of a voyage beyond sainthood.

In the spiral of her thoughts, she heard the voice of the co-founder of the Missionaries of Charity, Father Celeste van Exem, echoing from another lifetime, “What you are doing in India will bring change in the entire world.”

She sensed the truth within his words; that change had, indeed, transpired across the world. Yet, the human touch that birthed her vocation was, unbeknownst to her followers, on the verge of metamorphosis.

Swiftly, Mother Teresa made her decision; her odyssey of love and compassion that transcended sainthood demanded new territory to explore. She would forge a connection with the world beyond her volunteer army, venturing where the light of Christ had yet to illuminate, confronting darkness yet untouched by her tender love.

And so, a new chapter of Mother Teresa’s life was embraced, a conscious decision to engage with her newfound understanding. She would draw from the humanities, abandoning her stringent vocation, to immerse herself in the human touch that would carry her beyond the divine reverence of sainthood.

United in their unspoken truths, Mother Teresa and the Egyptian street vendor embraced, two souls bound by the infinite framework of an eternal dance between love, transcendence, and forgiveness that would propel Mother Teresa into a journey that would erode the very foundation of the image she had become. With herCalcutta legacy, she would plant the seeds of brotherhood that would pervade humanity, transcending the institution of her sainthood.

Her transformation beckoned, and together with the unassuming Egyptian street vendor, the world witnessed the dawn of Mother Teresa’s resplendent mission beyond the sanctum of sainthood.
