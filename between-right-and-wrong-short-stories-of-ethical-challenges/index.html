Between Right and Wrong: Short Stories of Ethical Challenges


Lena stood in front of her office desk, staring at the stack of paperwork. Her fingers hovered over the keyboard, hesitating. The numbers on the screen were clear: a large sum of money had been misallocated from the company’s funds. It wasn’t a small error—this was something that could potentially ruin the reputation of her firm. Her supervisor, Richard, had asked her to adjust the figures, making the mistake disappear. “It’s a minor adjustment, Lena,” he had said earlier that day. “No one will notice. We’ll be in the clear.”

Lena knew better. She had worked her way up in the company over the years, built a reputation for being meticulous and honest. She believed in the value of integrity, the importance of doing what was right. Yet, here she was, staring at an ethical dilemma that could compromise everything she had worked for.

The company was struggling financially, and Richard’s suggestion was tempting. No one would be the wiser if she made the change, no one except for herself. She would carry the weight of that decision forever. As Lena sat back in her chair, she thought about the consequences. If she reported the error, the company would have to deal with the fallout, but it would also retain its integrity. If she adjusted the numbers, she would be complicit in a fraud, albeit a small one.

Her phone buzzed. It was a message from her mother: “How’s work going? Hope you’re doing well!” Lena felt a pang of guilt. Her mother had always taught her to do the right thing, no matter how difficult it might be. She put the phone down, took a deep breath, and clicked on the “Report Error” button.

It was the hardest decision she had ever made, but as she submitted the report, she felt a sense of relief. She had chosen integrity over convenience.

Part II: The Teacher’s Choice

Mr. Barnes had been teaching high school history for over fifteen years. His students respected him, and he had a reputation for being fair and just. One afternoon, as he was preparing for his next class, a student, Natalie, approached him.

“Mr. Barnes, I… I need a favor,” she said, her voice trembling.

Barnes looked up from his papers. Natalie was a bright student, but she had been struggling lately. She had a lot on her plate: her family situation was difficult, and she had to work part-time to support herself.

“What’s going on?” he asked, concerned.

She looked around, as though checking to see if anyone was listening. “I know I didn’t do well on the last test. I tried, but… well, I was wondering if there was any way you could give me a second chance. Just one more shot at the test, and I’ll do better. I promise.”

Barnes felt torn. He knew Natalie was going through a tough time, but he also knew that offering her a retake would be unfair to the other students who had worked hard and followed the rules. Still, he could see the desperation in her eyes. She was a good student, and a second chance might make a huge difference in her grades and, more importantly, in her future.

“I can’t just change the rules for you, Natalie,” Mr. Barnes said gently. “It wouldn’t be right to give you an advantage over the others.”

Natalie’s face fell. “But, Mr. Barnes, this could be my only chance to pass the class. If I fail, I might lose my scholarship.”

Barnes felt a pang in his heart. He had always believed in fairness, but now the situation felt different. Was it fair to follow the rules when one student’s future hung in the balance?

The ethical dilemma was suffocating. On one hand, he had the responsibility to be fair to all his students. On the other hand, offering Natalie a chance to redeem herself could change the course of her life.

After a moment of silence, he made his decision. “I’m going to let you retake the test, but only if you promise to give it your absolute best effort. This is a one-time exception. Understand?”

Natalie’s eyes lit up. “Thank you, Mr. Barnes! I won’t let you down, I swear!”

As she walked away, Barnes felt conflicted. He had made a decision that he knew would not be universally understood, but it felt like the right thing to do for Natalie. He hoped it would make a positive impact on her life.

Part III: The Doctor’s Oath

Dr. Emma Richards had spent over a decade in the medical field, and she had faced many difficult decisions, but nothing like this. A young patient, Lucas, had been diagnosed with a rare form of cancer. The treatment options were limited and highly experimental, but there was a possibility it could save his life. The problem? The treatment was not approved by the medical board, and there was a high risk of severe side effects.

Emma sat in her office, reviewing the case again. She had spoken with Lucas’s parents, who were desperate for any solution, no matter how risky. They trusted Emma with their son’s life, and Emma could feel the weight of that trust pressing down on her.

“Doctor, is there any chance this could work?” Lucas’s mother had asked, her voice barely above a whisper. “We’ve heard of other patients who’ve survived with this treatment.”

Emma knew that Lucas’s chances were slim with the conventional treatments, and the experimental treatment offered a glimmer of hope. But what if it didn’t work? What if it made things worse? What if she was putting him through unbearable pain for no reason?

Her mind raced. As a doctor, her duty was to do no harm. Yet, she also knew that the medical field was filled with uncertainties, and sometimes, taking risks could lead to miracles.

She had to make a choice: follow the safer, approved treatment plan, which offered a slim chance of success, or take the risk with the experimental treatment, knowing that it could either save Lucas or make things worse.

“Doctor, please,” Lucas’s father had said, looking into her eyes with pleading hope. “We trust you.”

Emma’s hand shook as she picked up the phone to call the medical board. “I’m going to do it,” she whispered to herself. “I’m going to try the experimental treatment.”

The decision weighed heavily on her heart, but as the treatment began, Emma hoped she had made the right choice. The ethical challenges she faced daily had never been this intense, but she knew that in the end, she had to trust her instincts and do what felt right for her patient.

Part IV: The Journalist’s Integrity

David was an investigative journalist known for his hard-hitting stories. He had broken several major scandals, uncovering corruption and injustice wherever he could. But now, he faced a situation unlike any other. A source had come to him with a story—one that could bring down a major corporation. But the information was murky, and there were many inconsistencies in the source’s account.

As a journalist, David’s duty was to uncover the truth, but he also had a responsibility to ensure that what he published was accurate and fair. The source had promised more details, but David knew better than to rely on incomplete information. He needed verification, but the company involved was powerful, and pursuing the story could have serious consequences.

David paced back and forth in his office, thinking about the ramifications of publishing the story. On one hand, it could expose widespread corporate malpractice and protect the public. On the other hand, if the information turned out to be false or exaggerated, he could destroy innocent lives and tarnish his career.

His editor, Amanda, had told him to be cautious. “We need solid evidence before we run with this, David. We’re not just a tabloid. We owe it to our readers to get the facts right.”

David nodded, but part of him wanted to take the risk. The story was too big to ignore, and the potential impact was huge. Yet, the journalist in him knew that once a story was out there, it couldn’t be retracted. The weight of that responsibility was immense.

After several sleepless nights, David made his decision. He would wait for more information. He would ensure that every detail was verified before taking the story to the public. It was the harder choice, but he knew it was the right one.

Part V: The Lawyer’s Dilemma

Samantha was a lawyer who had spent years building her career. She had worked on high-profile cases, defending clients who were accused of serious crimes. She believed in justice, and she always strove to do what was right. But when she was assigned to defend a man accused of murder, she found herself facing an ethical challenge that tested her beliefs.

The man, Greg, had been accused of killing his wife, and the evidence against him was overwhelming. He had confessed to the crime, but Samantha couldn’t shake the feeling that something wasn’t right. As she reviewed the case, she discovered inconsistencies in the police report—evidence that hadn’t been properly handled, potential motives that had been ignored.

Samantha’s duty as a lawyer was to provide the best defense possible for her client, even if she believed he was guilty. But as she delved deeper into the case, she became more convinced that Greg hadn’t committed the crime. Someone else was responsible, but Greg had taken the fall to protect his family.

She was torn. If she presented this new information in court, it could clear Greg’s name, but it would also expose the true culprit. But what if doing so ruined the lives of the people involved? What if she made the wrong call and inadvertently harmed the wrong person?

Samantha sat in her office, her head in her hands, knowing that whatever decision she made would have lasting consequences. The ethical challenges she faced every day were never easy, but this case felt different.

She stood up, walked to the phone, and dialed her supervisor’s number. “I need to discuss something important with you,” she said, her voice firm.

It was time to make the right choice.

Conclusion

In each of these stories, the characters faced difficult ethical challenges that tested their morals and values. Whether it was Lena deciding between integrity and convenience, Mr. Barnes weighing fairness against compassion, Dr. Richards choosing between safety and hope, David grappling with journalistic responsibility, or Samantha confronting the complexities of justice, each made a decision that impacted not only their lives but also the lives of those around them.

Ethical dilemmas are a part of life, and sometimes, the right decision isn’t always the easiest. But it is in those moments of uncertainty that true character is revealed, and the choice between right and wrong defines who we are.
