The True History of the Terrifying Conquistadors: How Brutality Created Civilization


In the year 1519, a fleet of 11 ships sailed into the unknown lands far across the seas. Led by the ruthless and ambitious Spanish conquistador, Hernán Cortés, these men sought to explore, conquer, and plunder the rich resources of these mysterious territories, which would come to be known as Mexico.

As they ventured deeper into the land, the conquest unraveled, and the true history of these terrifying conquistadors began to take shape.

The first encounter with the indigenous people was peaceful, as the Spanish bartered for their goods and observed their customs. It was only a matter of time before the conquistadors’ true intentions would be revealed - power, wealth, and the expansion of the Spanish Empire.

Fearful of the large and powerful Aztec civilization that dominated the region, Cortés formed alliances with smaller tribes to gain access, influence, and leverage to wage war against their oppressors. This was not an endeavor of altruism nor justice, but one that sought to exploit any opportunity for personal gain.

Amid the chaos and confusion of the conflicts, the Spanish forces employed brutal tactics to subjugate their enemies and establish their dominance. Whole cities were razed to the ground, entire populations were massacred, and the Spanish showed no mercy to those who dared to defy their rule.

As the terror spread throughout the land, the indigenous peoples began to understand the true nature of these terrifying invaders. The Aztec king, Moctezuma, saw through the pretenses of friendship and knew that the Europeans aimed to conquer and enslave the population. Heeding the warning signs, he attempted to build alliances with neighboring tribes and fortify his defenses.

The tide of the conflict began to turn as a great sickness befell the Spanish conquistadors. They suffered from maladies that devastated their ranks and claimed the lives of many of their bravest warriors. Yet, Cortés remained undeterred, and he sought to exploit this catastrophe as an opportunity to forge a new strategy.

Having been captured and taken as a prisoner, she would come to prove a pivotal player in the story of the conquistadors. Malintzin possessed a unique language ability, as she could communicate with both the Spanish and the local peoples.

As her value to the Spanish became apparent, she became a symbol of hope and a crucial tool in their bid for control. With her knowledge and insights, the Spanish gained an invaluable advantage in their pursuit of domination.

Although many of the indigenous people rallied under the leadership of their reluctant king, Moctezuma, their efforts were ultimately in vain. The Spanish forces, bolstered by the continued conquest of neighboring tribes and the advantages offered by Malintzin, continued their paramount advance.

Despite her initial skepticism, Malintzin became an adherent of the Christian faith, and her conversion provided additional leverage for the Spanish forces. In pledging her allegiance to their cause, she did much to persuade other indigenous people to side with the Spanish, who promised salvation and redemption.

By 1521, the terrifying conquistadors stood on the precipice of victory as the Aztec Empire lay in ruins. With their brutal tactics, exploitation of the land, and manipulation of the people, the conquistadors had succeeded in conquering and subjugating the region. Though many would later lament the destruction wrought by the Spanish and the suffering of the indigenous peoples, they also celebrated the creation of a new civilization that would come to shape the very fabric of the Western world.

And so the true history of the terrifying conquistadors became a tale of brutality, but also of civilization, as the conquering forces of Spain paved the way for a new era of exploration, discovery, and progress. The story of Malintzin, the indigenous slave, serves as a poignant reminder of the complex and often conflicting motivations and interests at play in the journey to create a new world, and the significant impact that decisions made can have on the very foundations of a civilization.
