Hacked Reality: Surviving in a Virtual Dystopia


It started with a glitch, a small bug in the digital fabric that whispered changes in the wind. The augmented reality system, once a marvel of innovation and a revolutionary leap forward, had become something different. A tool to control, to manipulate, to enslave. The hackers, the cheshire cat-like sycophants, known for breaching the most secure systems, created the chaos. They danced through the digital realms like fireflies on a summer night. No one seemed to notice, too busy with their daily lives, their valuable time occupied by conforming to the norms, to the societal traps set by the puppet masters. In the beginning, the hacks were minor, laughable almost. The compilation of elevator music in every house in Denver. The demonisation of cats as extinction-level threats. The never-ending stream of rom-com movies on a black screen. All these Pepe Le Pew theatrics made people roll their eyes, made them saccharine. These'minor' hacks amount to an inoculation of common despair in the virtual pill which people soon grew accustomed to. The shifting stalactites continued, adding to the ride.
