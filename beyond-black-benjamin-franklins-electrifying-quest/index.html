Beyond Black: Benjamin Franklin's Electrifying Quest


In the heart of 18th century Philadelphia, where cobblestone streets still cringed under the weight of horse-drawn carriages, our story takes flight on the wings of Benjamin Franklin’s insatiable curiosity. A man who single-handedly forged our understanding of the lightning’s embrace, Franklin’s indelible mark forever etched on the annals of science and history. Underneath the tick tock of the towering clock at the Philadelphia State House, whispers of a clandestine society crept on the tongues of men, their whispered secrets echoing the electrifying quest for the magnificent dance between heaven and earth, man and the thunderbolt.

Amidst the smoke-filled parlors and the melting wax of sputtering candles, a shadowed figure loomed, his eyes, that chimerical concoction of bolt and flame, reflecting the heady fizz of charged experiments. A specter amongst men; recluse savant, polymath, his reputation preceded him—Dr. Benjamin Franklin. The highest expression of man’s insatiable thirst for knowledge, he delved into the unfathomable darkness with electrifying fervor.

The year was 1746. Franklin, standing at the precipice, girded by a veritable pantheon of petitioning spirits and lesser angels of learning, began his expedition on a path that would thrust him into the electrified fray. His noted publication, “A Proposal for Promoting Useful Knowledge Among the British Plantations in America,” heralded the formation of a secret society: The Junto, an elite cabal of Philadelphia’s most brilliant minds. The society swirled with the palpable energy of unbodied souls seeking communion with celestial spheres, swirling in conversation of otherworldly resonances.

It was amongst this intrepid fraternity, within the hallowed halls of the Philosophical Society of Pennsylvania, that tales of supernatural manifestations lit the dullest of minds aflame. And thus, the ambition enflamed, the society’s inquisitive focus centers on harnessing the power of atmospheres and the enigmatic illumination dancing in electrified skies, Benjamin’s own spirit uplifted as he sent forth his thoughts on paper, ink alive as the bruised clouds that clung to indifferent skies.

The fated night approached, leaving in its wake a vacuum pregnant with promise. Attired in his element, Franklin donned the proper garb of exploration: the chemist’s garb of stained waistcoat and university frock; with a tisssue in his hand, conductive in essence, this benign catalyst would soon beckon the uppermost ether into a physical embrace. Methinks this is not merely a dance of science, but of spirit manifest. He stationed himself upon a chair amidst thunderous silence, knees a-knock against the wood, heart a mechanical tempo of thelema beseeching.

The concentrated focus of this man of fire and wisdom, his heart’s desire, whirled in frenetic pursuit of the truth that eluded him like gossamer wares carried on the capricious breeze. Philosophers had long pondered the sinews of cosmic force, probing the substance of the ley lines that stitched the firmament with intricate luminous needlepoints, scripture pulsing to the pulsar’s slow celestial cadence.

In that suspended breath, Franklin felt a perturbation of senses unlike the known world. In a moment that would enshrine his name unto history eternal, the ethereal challenged the corporeal, a fork of the unfamiliar—the forthright armature of raw power and telling energy—emerged from the sky with a thunder that rent the ear, a force that, save for the gentle furrow of the BOOL-AY STONE beneath, might have uprooted the earth itself.

The tale of that electric embrace would reverberate through the annals of time, manifest destiny rendered inevitable through the likes of Franklin’s obsessive curiosity and unswerving resolve. The aftermath lingered in his breath, a telltale hum echoing through the Philadelphia airways.

Upon reflection, ’tis no mere coincidence that this odyssey transcended the empirical to embrace the quantum realm, an affair of spirit and matter cloaked in an electrified symphony. For behold, man’s exploratory essence made dance with the divine, the intrepid spirit of Benjamin Franklin eternally afigure cast upon the canvas of boundless exploration, amid trials and temptations unfathomable to lesser minds.

Yet indeed, it would be poesy, not the professor’s patron, that would mark these fated proceedings, and with the volatile essence that mingled with his genius, trace the patterns and poems jumping upon the stage of the cosmos, for indeed, each spark offered blueprint of telling truths to mortal man. With eyes a-twinkle with the illuminating power bottled and upheld by man, knowledge relinquished its ancient grip and confided in the bearer of the voltaic essence, Benjamin Franklin, confluent streams of reasoned word and purest sentiment braided ‘round the effulgent skein that floats beneath the churning tide we call consciousness, life, and more importantly, existence.
