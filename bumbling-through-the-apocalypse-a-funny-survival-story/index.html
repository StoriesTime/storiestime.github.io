Bumbling Through the Apocalypse: A Funny Survival Story


It was a Tuesday, just like any other, except that the world was about to end. And, of course, I was woefully unprepared for it. But hey, who can blame me? No one exactly hands out a “How to Survive the End of the World” manual, right?

I was at home, sitting on my couch, mindlessly scrolling through my phone, when I got the notification: BREAKING NEWS – The Apocalypse has officially begun. Please remain calm, and follow the instructions provided by the government. Naturally, I thought it was a joke. Maybe a marketing stunt for some new disaster movie or a bizarre viral campaign. But then the power went out. And that’s when I realized it wasn’t a prank. The world was falling apart, and I was completely unprepared for it.

Chapter 1: Surviving the First Hour

I panicked. Not even a little bit – like, full-blown panic. My first instinct was to raid my kitchen, but I soon realized my cupboards were stocked with nothing but expired cans of tuna, three kinds of pasta (none of which had sauce), and a suspicious jar of pickles I wasn’t sure I’d ever bought. Not exactly the survivalist feast I had imagined.

“Okay, it’s fine. I can figure this out,” I muttered to myself, trying to maintain some semblance of composure.

After rummaging through the fridge, I found an ancient block of cheese that looked like it had been aging for at least a decade. Still, it was edible, I figured. Cheese is cheese, right? I hastily stuffed a few slices into my mouth, and that was when it hit me – the world had gone absolutely bananas, and I was probably going to die while chewing moldy cheese.

But I wasn’t about to give up that easily. I grabbed my phone, checked the internet for survival tips, and soon discovered that the world’s infrastructure had come crashing down. People were panicking, stores were being looted, and the roads were blocked. In other words, I had no idea what to do.

I spent the next hour running around my apartment, grabbing anything I thought might be useful – a bottle of hand sanitizer, a flashlight, an empty backpack, a roll of toilet paper (because why not?), and, for some reason, my childhood teddy bear. I wasn’t sure if I’d need it, but it felt comforting.

Chapter 2: The Great Escape

By the time I stumbled out of my apartment, the world outside had gone from chaotic to outright apocalyptic. Sirens were wailing, people were shouting, and smoke was rising from distant buildings. It looked like something straight out of a post-apocalyptic movie – except this time, I was the one in the middle of it, and I wasn’t equipped with a flamethrower or any special skills. I was just a regular person who, apparently, had never watched enough survival documentaries.

I didn’t have much of a plan. The only thing I knew for sure was that I needed to get out of the city. But I had absolutely no clue how to do that. So, I did what anyone would do in my shoes – I started walking.

And that’s when I met Brad.

Brad was, without a doubt, the least prepared person I’d ever encountered. He was wearing flip-flops, cargo shorts, and a Hawaiian shirt, and was clutching a giant inflatable pool toy shaped like a flamingo. It was absurd. But when the world ends, who’s going to judge, right?

“You think we’re safe here?” Brad asked, looking around nervously.

I glanced at the towering infernos in the distance. “Uh, definitely not. We’re definitely not safe.”

“I figured,” Brad sighed. “So, what should we do?”

I had no idea, but I wasn’t about to admit that. “We need to find shelter. A safe place. Maybe some food.”

“Yeah, yeah,” Brad nodded, clearly not understanding the gravity of the situation. “I have some granola bars in my bag. We’re good.”

I looked at his flamingo pool float. “You have granola bars and… that?”

Brad shrugged. “It’s a weird time. People are bringing all sorts of stuff with them. At least I’ll have something to float on when the floods come.”

I gave him a blank stare. “You do realize we’re in the middle of a city, and there’s no water in sight, right?”

Brad blinked. “Oh… right.”

Chapter 3: The Quest for Food (And More Disasters)

As we continued our walk toward what we hoped was safety, we came across a supermarket. Naturally, we thought this was our big break – I mean, food, water, supplies! We could survive for weeks here, right?

The moment we entered, chaos ensued. People were running around, pushing carts piled high with toilet paper, canned beans, and other essentials. I watched as a woman, with the grace of a panicked gazelle, darted toward a shelf full of granola bars and snatched up every last one.

Brad looked at me and said, “I think we should get out of here before we become part of the loot.”

I nodded in agreement, but then my stomach growled. I was starving, and my cheese and pickles were long gone.

We decided to venture further into the store, where we found a tiny corner of relative calm. It was just a few people, quietly picking out canned food and discussing whether they should grab more ramen. Brad and I quickly joined them, loading up our baskets with whatever we could carry – including a can of peaches that I wasn’t even sure how to open.

And that was when it happened. The lights flickered, and the loudspeaker crackled to life.

“Attention shoppers! Due to current conditions, all store purchases are now free. Please take what you need, and exit in an orderly fashion.”

Free food? Was this real life? I could hardly believe my ears. It felt like we had won the lottery.

But instead of gracefully taking our bounty and leaving, Brad and I managed to create a scene. As I was reaching for a bag of chips, a man shoved me aside, and I ended up crashing into a pyramid of canned soups. Brad, always the optimist, thought it would be a great idea to dive into the chaos headfirst, yelling, “THIS IS OUR TIME!”

Moments later, we were both covered in soup and somehow locked in a struggle with a couple who was fighting over the last box of cereal. The apocalypse had turned us into animals.

Chapter 4: The Road to Nowhere

With our food stash secured (and Brad proudly holding a carton of eggs like a prize), we made our way out of the store. Our next challenge was getting out of the city – or so we thought. The roads were packed with abandoned cars, broken down buses, and the occasional overturned motorcycle. Not the ideal way to escape, but it was our only option.

I’d never been so thankful for the existence of Uber before – until I realized there was no electricity to charge my phone. So, we were stuck walking. Again.

“I mean, how bad could it be?” Brad said cheerfully, holding up a bag of chips. “We’ve got food, we’ve got each other, and we’ve got the flamingo. What more do we need?”

I was starting to think that the apocalypse might not be so bad if it meant I could hang out with Brad for a bit longer. Sure, he was a walking disaster, but he had a sort of charm about him. We had nothing to lose, and no real plan, but somehow, we were surviving. And that, I suppose, was enough.

As we walked down the highway, our backpacks filled with random supplies, I realized something important: survival wasn’t about being prepared. It wasn’t about having a stockpile of weapons or knowing how to build a shelter with nothing but a paperclip and duct tape. It was about finding a way to keep going, even when everything seemed ridiculous.

And in that moment, as Brad yelled at a pigeon to “stay safe out there,” I knew one thing for sure: we weren’t going to make it out of this with grace or dignity, but damn if we weren’t going to have the weirdest survival story of all time.

The End (For Now)

The apocalypse might have caught me off guard, but in the end, it was about making the most of every chaotic moment. And if the world was going to end, I figured it might as well end with a good laugh.
