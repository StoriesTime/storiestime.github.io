Migrants at the Crossroads: A Journey from Home to Belonging


Once upon a time, in a small village nestled at the edge of a vast desert, lived a humble and pious people. Their lives revolved around the yearly harvest of the precious dates which grew from the barren ground. These nomadic people had a longstanding tradition of migrating in search of new opportunities, but more importantly, in pursuit of a spiritual connection. It was said that their ancestors had once been stranded at a crucial crossroads of life, wavering between the guidance of their heritage and the temptation of a new beginning.

The village had a small shrine where an old, hallowed Qibla stood. Every sunset, the villagers would gather to pray fervently for guidance towards their next destination. As the sun began to dip below the horizon, the Qibla would miraculously point towards their chosen path.

One year, the village gathered around the Qibla, only for it to remain completely still. Shock swept over the villagers like a sudden sandstorm, leaving them frozen in uncertainty. With each passing day, their anxiety grew, as the eternal connection that had once guided them seemed to have forsaken them. As the days of Ramadan came to a close, their desperation reached its peak.

A child, a young girl named Nyla, began to experience vivid and powerful dreams. In these dreams, she saw a magnificent tree laden with gold and silver fruit. As she walked towards it, she noticed the leaves shimmering and the branches weighed down by the weight of the golden and silver fruits. Upon touching one of the fruits, she was overwhelmed with an indescribable sensation. The dreams were so vivid that they seemed more like reality than fantasy. This was the key, Nyla thought; for it was in the fruits’ purity that the answer was hidden.

Nyla was an innocent girl with a kind heart, and her love for the village and her people was unwavering. So, she decided to share her vision with the others. While many scoffed at her vision, others were genuinely inspired by her determination and faith. In a bid to find their way, the village decided to embark on a journey to fulfill Nyla’s vision.

As they trekked through the unforgiving desert, guided by Nyla’s unshakable belief, the hardships only strengthened their bond. The village was a well-oiled unit, working together like a single, powerful force. It was in these long and arduous days, when their spirits were at both the weakest and the strongest, that their collective belief shone brightly like the North Star.

One day, as the evening approached, and as the sun began its descent, Nyla felt the ground beneath her shift beneath her feet. In her hands, she clutched the very fruit she had seen in her dreams. It was a rare and ancient date, its core of silver and gold untouched by time. She held it high, and as the sun dipped below the horizon, the Qibla suddenly began to move. Stricken with awe and a profound connection, they followed its lead through the desert.

Days turned into weeks, and weeks into months. As the village pressed on, Nyla’s health began to decline. She no longer had the strength to walk, let alone hold the precious fruit. Realizing the importance of this sacred task, the village elder took on the responsibility of holding the fruit.

The journey seemed never-ending, and the desert showed no mercy as they trudged on mile after mile. Yet, their faith never wavered. It was the strength of their collective belief that carried them forward.

As the sun began to dip below the horizon one evening, the Qibla guide suddenly shifted, pointing towards a previously unseen oasis. Surrounded by lush trees and a dark, beckoning lake, the village had never seen such a sight. With joy in their hearts, they journeyed toward this miraculous discovery.

Upon reaching the oasis, they found the air to be filled with the fragrance of the most exquisite dates. The trees themselves seemed to be made of gold and silver and were laden with fruit. And there, in the midst of this miraculous site, they discovered a group of people who shared their heritage.

This oasis had been bestowed upon them as a gift; a gift from their ancestors, their guides through life. The village lived in harmony with their newfound kin, learning and sharing, healing and growing.

Years passed, and the village folk found belonging in this wondrous place. Their ancestral bond was no longer confined to their small village but had grown to encompass the people of the oasis. Together, they had built a new, vibrant community, and the dates they cultivated were no longer merely a source of sustenance, but a testament to their newfound unity.

For generations, they shared the story of their unrelenting faith and how it had led them to this place of gold and silver. The dates became a symbol of their unbreakable bond, their collective essence growing stronger with each passing year. More importantly, their journey would inspire countless generations to come, a testament to the power of faith, unity, and the indomitable spirit of the human soul.

The village at the crossroads of life had found its belonging, and in doing so, they sparked a powerful and enduring legacy. And thus, from the heart of the desert emerged a shimmering oasis, forever a monument to the journey that had taken them from a small village to a unified and thriving community.
