Cruising Croatia in a Wooden Ship: Discovering the Adriatic with Zlatni Rat


Under a canopy of cerulean sky, the carved wooden vessel sliced through the horizon, sailing effortlessly into the realm of untamed waters. This wasn’t your typical Mediterranean jaunt; it was a voyage of discovery in the heart of the Adriatic Sea with a piece of history at its helm.

Zlatni Rat, a vessel as old as the legends she carried within her timbers, was the pride and joy of Split. Her decks were made of weathered, shining wood and all hands on board knew of the ship’s storied past. Departing from the sun-kissed shores of Dalmatia, we were bound for Croatia’s sun-drenched paradise, the jewel of the rocky coastline glistening beside us.

Adventure awaited us, a mélange of history, pristine waters, and indelible memories. As we boarded the wooden ship, passengers became crew, eager to set sail and explore the Adriatic like the mariners of yore. Each of us, with hearts aflame with anticipation, cast our eyes toward the hallowed Zlatni Rat - our vessel, our refuge, our conductor of newfound discoveries across waters untouched by time.

We disembarked, the alchemy of the sea breeze mingling with the earthy scent of seasoned wood, leaving footprints in the soft, golden sand. Our first stop: the awe-inspiring town of Makarska, draped in the verdure of a hinterland curtain call. As if painted upon the land by the will of an artist who chose bold strokes of dynamic color, Makarska sprawled before us, a polychromatic spectacle of life and history.

Our voyage continued onwards, propelled by the force of wood and wind. We sailed past Biokovo’s mountainous facade, where every turn in the coastline revealed another secret vista. The ship glided gracefully over the glassy surface, the sun shades atop our heads providing shelter from the Midi sun. As we traversed the tranquil waters of the Makarska Riviera, the distant horizon and our wooden ship became one, converging into an amalgamation of nautical prowess and ethereal beauty.

A chorus of seagulls heralded the arrival at the island harbors of Vis and Hvar. Baits of sapphire tranquil bays and cobbled, enchanting towns drew us in like moths to a flickering flame. Zlatni Rat tacked expertly, each sunset casting a kaleidoscope of color across the sky. Vibrant plums, fiery oranges, and molten gold splashed upon the undulating sea; a spectacle only interrupted by the occasional wink of a shooting star.

In Stari Grad, on the island of Hvar, we encountered the Petrices Wine Cellar. Old stone structures blended with the blessed sunshine, the scents of grapevines vast and varied, laying a gastronomic foundation for us to build upon. Renowned for locally cultivated fruit, veggie heaven encapsulated by nothing short of their terrace gardens, we were treated to a delightful feast born of Hvar’s rich soil.

Each curve of the sun-kissed coastline promised new wonders; the ancient walls of Ston, the dalmatian hamlets hiding under the shade of olive trees, their fruits bearing the weight of centuries. The aromatic whiff of sea salt and sea bass in Ston enveloped us, leading us to a culinary adventure in the inky depths of the Adriatic. Our crew assisted in casting and hauling, as they had done generations before us. Deft skills and age-old traditions infused our breath, as we gathered fish with precious little more than we and our wooden vessel.

The island of Korčula beckoned, its shores a crescent of silken sand and the birthplace of Marco Polo. We emerged into the streets of Korčula, where history pulsed like the lifeblood of a thousand marliar-toned townsfolk and ancient gray stones.

Finally, as the wooden ship’s silhouette was kissed by the first light of the fading Croatia, we entered the emerald embrace of the half-moon-shaped peninsula of Pelješac. Its serenity, a result of both rugged, terrestrial beauty and its harbors draped in the shadow of the Neretva River’s majestic course.

Our voyage around the AdriaticOcean, the discoveries of its treasures etched indelibly upon our hearts and memories, came to a close on the awe-inspiring rocks of Ston. Evening hues of gold and azure blanketed the ship, and as we alighted upon the ancient town, there was gratitude in our farewells, our hearts swaying to a gentle breeze, much like the carved wooden vessel – the Zlatni Rat – as it sailed back to Split, to her harbor, and into the accounts of our memories.

Time cannot diminish the impressions left by the secrets of the sea, the stories corralling wandering souls together on the wooden ship, or the etchings our hands made on the Adriatic’s canvas. For all of these, we embarked upon the undulating steed of the Adriatic – Zlatni Rat – discovering the Adriatic with the zest of yesterday and the relevance of tomorrow, our hearts full of breathless dreams and the wonders of Croatia’s rugged, soul-stirring shores.
