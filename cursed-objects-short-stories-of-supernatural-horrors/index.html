Cursed Objects: Short Stories of Supernatural Horrors


Evelyn had never been one to believe in the supernatural. Objects held memories, yes. But curses? Those were just old wives’ tales, stories to scare children into obedience. That conviction wavered the day she found the locket.

It was buried beneath a pile of forgotten heirlooms in her grandmother’s attic—an ornate silver locket, its surface etched with intricate, twisting vines that seemed almost alive. The clasp was loose, as if inviting someone to pry it open.

Curiosity overpowered reason, and Evelyn clicked it open. Inside, a faded photograph of a young woman stared back at her, eyes sharp and unblinking. As Evelyn held the locket, a sudden chill caressed her fingers. A whispering hiss slithered at the edge of her hearing, so faint she thought she was imagining it.

But then it began.

At first, just soft murmurs, unintelligible and disjointed. Yet over days, the whispering locket became louder, sharper—almost pleading.

“Help me… find peace…”

Evelyn tried to ignore it, but the locket’s influence seeped deeper into her life. She started seeing shadows flicker in the corner of her vision, experiencing temperature drops in empty rooms. The line between reality and nightmare blurred.

One night, she dreamed of the woman in the photograph, pale and ethereal.

“Free me,” she whispered. “I am bound to this cursed trinket.”

Evelyn discovered the locket had belonged to Isolde, a woman accused of witchcraft in the eighteenth century, executed unjustly and cursed by a vengeful heartbroken lover. The locket held her restless spirit tethered to the mortal world.

Determined to release Isolde, Evelyn researched ancient rituals, unearthing texts long forgotten. To break the curse, the locket must be submerged beneath running water at midnight, during a new moon.

The night arrived—a cold, cloudy evening. Evelyn stood by the riverbank, locket in hand. The whispers clamored around her like a swarm, chilling her to the bone. With a prayer whispered under her breath, she released the locket into the dark waters.

For a moment, silence.

Then, a breath of warm air swept through.

Her phone chimed.

A message from an unknown number: Thank you.

The curse lifted.

Chapter Two: The Mirror of Endless Reflection

Sam bought the old antique mirror on a whim at a flea market. It was a massive thing, wrapped in dusty velvet fabric, the kind of piece that looks out of place in a modern apartment. The frame was carved with grotesque figures—serpents, angels with hollow eyes, and faces frozen in silent screams.

The moment Sam hung the mirror, time shifted.

At first, the changes were subtle. He noticed small discrepancies—his reflection delayed by a second, his movements mirrored imperfectly.

Then came the nightmares.

Each night, the mirror showed scenes that weren’t happening in his room. He would see his reflection walking through dark corridors, past rooms of flickering candles and shadowy figures. Sometimes, his mirrored double smiled wickedly at him.

One evening, the reflection reached out, its hand passing beyond the glass.

Sam’s heart hammered as a frigid hand clasped his wrist. He tried to pull away, but the grip was iron-tight.

The mirror wasn’t just a reflection—it was a portal.

Desperate, Sam researched the mirror’s origin. It was said to have been crafted by a cursed artisan who trapped spirits within glass, binding them to endless reflections. People who looked into it risked their souls being stolen, their bodies enslaved in the mirrored realm.

To escape, Sam needed to shatter the mirror. But breaking such a powerful cursed object was no simple feat. Every attempt caused reality to ripple and distort, his reflection growing stronger, more controlling.

He discovered an incantation, buried in occult tomes, that required reciting as the mirror smashed, to banish trapped spirits and sever the curse.

One stormy night, thunder booming overhead, Sam equipped himself with an ancient silver dagger and the chant memorized.

The mirror’s surface rippled like water as he brought the dagger down.

Crack. Shatter.

Light exploded, blinding him.

When he opened his eyes, the mirror was gone. His reflection was normal again.

But sometimes, when the light catches his eyes just right, he swears he sees a shadow flicker behind him—waiting.

Chapter Three: The Marionette’s Curse

In a dim corner of a forgotten theater, a marionette hung silent—a beautiful puppet dressed in velvet and lace, its strings tangled and broken. It had belonged to a famous puppeteer named Anton, whose performances were legendary. But the puppet was tied to a horrifying secret.

Julian, an aspiring actor, found the puppet among the ruins of the theater destined for demolition. Its glassy eyes seemed almost human, following his movements.

That night, the puppet appeared in his dreams, moving and speaking with Anton’s voice. The story poured out: Anton had made a pact with dark forces to bring his marionettes to life, sacrificing his own soul. The puppets trapped the souls of those they entertained, feeding off their life energy.

The marionette sought freedom, but in doing so, it revealed a terrible price. Anyone who tried to destroy it would inherit the curse, becoming enslaved to the puppet’s will.

Julian felt the puppet’s influence creep into his waking hours. Commands whispered in his ear, forcing him to perform grim acts he could barely remember. Dreams blurred with reality.

He found a tattered diary of Anton’s, revealing that to break the curse, the marionette needed to be set on fire during the final act of a play performed by a willing soul—the curse’s last trace would then burn away.

Julian hesitated, trapped between fear and resolve. On the night of the theater’s demolition, he staged one final performance, draping the marionette in flames amid the collapsing ruins.

The puppet’s scream was both human and inhuman, echoing through the night.

When dawn broke, Julian was free.

But his fingers twitched, as though strings pulled at an unseen force.

Chapter Four: The Cursed Journal

Clara found the journal in a secondhand bookstore, its leather cover cracked and soot-stained. The inscription on the first page was chilling: “Write at your own peril.”

Inside, the journal chronicled a man’s descent into madness, each entry more fragmented, pleading, ending abruptly. Whatever he wrote seemed to alter reality around him. Minor injuries he’d described appeared on Clara’s body hours later; mentions of voices growing louder echoed in her mind.

The journal was a vessel of dark intent—each word binding Clara tighter to the writer’s fate.

At the brink, Clara learned of a ritual to erase the journal’s power: burn its pages under a blood moon while whispering the author’s name aloud, severing the link between writer and reader.

With trembling hands, under the eerie red glow, Clara watched the flames consume the journal. The oppressive weight lifted, but at a cost—her handwriting now bore the same jagged, erratic style as the cursed entries.

The story wasn’t over.

As each of these cursed objects unspooled their supernatural tales, one truth emerged: some items are better left forgotten, their dark power lurking just beneath the surface, waiting to ensnare the unwary.

When curiosity meets the unknown, the shadows can claim you—one whisper, one reflection, one flickering marionette, and one blackened page at a time.

Epilogue: Beware the Forgotten

Cursed objects carry more than history; they carry the weight of unresolved spirits, catastrophic bargains, and inescapable fates. Whether a delicate locket, a sinister mirror, a haunted puppet, or a forbidden journal, their stories remind us that some relics hold horrors beyond comprehension.

If you ever stumble upon something old, unknown, or strangely compelling—ask yourself: is it a treasure… or a trap? Sometimes, the most terrifying stories lie not in distant lands or unnatural beasts, but in the damaged artifacts lurking quietly in attics, basements, and flea markets.

And once the curse reaches out, it’s already too late.
