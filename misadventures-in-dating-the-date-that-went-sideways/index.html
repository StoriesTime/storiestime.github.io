Misadventures in Dating: The Date That Went Sideways


It was a crisp fall evening when Emily first laid eyes on the online dating profile of Ryan. His smile was infectious, and the way his bio described his love for adventure made her heart race. She felt an instant connection, as if he had written it just for her. After a few days of messaging back and forth, they decided to meet for dinner at a trendy new restaurant downtown. The excitement bubbled in Emily’s chest, mixed with the familiar pangs of anxiety that accompanied any first date.

As she arrived, the amber glow of the restaurant’s lights illuminated the sidewalk, reflecting her nervous anticipation. Dressed in a soft blue dress that accentuated her figure, Emily took a deep breath, smoothing down her hair. She spotted Ryan sitting at a table by the window, his eyes scanning the menu, and her nerves began to settle.

“Emily!” he called out, standing up with an enthusiastic wave. His demeanor was warm and inviting, his laughter filling the air around them. They exchanged pleasantries, and the initial awkwardness melted away as they dove into conversations about their favorite books, movies, and their shared love for hiking.

But as the evening progressed, things took a turn. Just as they were finishing their appetizers, the restaurant’s fire alarm blared, causing a mix of confusion and panic among the patrons. Smoke began to waft from the kitchen, and a server rushed by, shouting for everyone to evacuate.

“Should we go?” Ryan asked, his eyes wide with surprise.

“Uh, yeah, I think so!” Emily replied, her heart racing not just from the thrill of the date, but from the sudden urgency of the situation.

They joined the throng of diners spilling out onto the street, the chilly air hitting them like a shockwave. Amid the chaos, Emily noticed Ryan was still holding his half-eaten appetizer. “You know, I think the calamari might be safe,” he joked, causing her to laugh despite the absurdity of the situation.

Once outside, they stood huddled together, waiting for the fire trucks to arrive. The restaurant staff directed people away from the entrance and offered complimentary drinks as an apology for the ordeal. Ryan seized the opportunity, suggesting they grab something to drink while waiting.

“Why not? Let’s make the best of this!” Emily exclaimed, feeling a sense of adventure as they wandered down the street looking for a nearby bar. They stumbled upon a cozy pub with dim lighting and a vibrant atmosphere. Inside, they ordered drinks and settled into a corner booth. As they chatted, they both agreed that the fire alarm had somehow broken the ice.

However, just as they began to relax, Emily noticed something peculiar. Ryan seemed increasingly distracted, glancing around the bar every few minutes. “Is everything okay?” she asked, concern creeping into her voice.

“Yeah, just… I think I saw someone I know,” he replied, his eyes darting.

Emily shrugged it off, but the feeling of unease began to creep in. She tried to focus on their conversation, but Ryan’s attention was divided. When she excused herself to the restroom, she overheard snippets of a conversation. Two familiar voices were discussing ‘the date’ and wondering what Ryan was doing there.

Once back at the table, she confronted him lightly. “So, who’s here that you know?”

Ryan laughed nervously. “Oh, just an old friend from college. Nothing to worry about!”

Just then, a tall woman with striking features approached their booth. “Ryan! There you are!” she exclaimed, her eyes sparkling with excitement.

Emily felt her heart sink. Ryan was instantly robbed of his composure. “Hey, Lisa! What are you doing here?” he responded, his voice suddenly higher-pitched.

Lisa leaned in, ignoring Emily entirely. “I thought you were on a date! Is this your new girlfriend?”

The words hung in the air, heavy and awkward. Ryan turned beet red, glancing between Lisa and Emily, who was trying to mask her discomfort with a polite smile. “Uh, this is Emily. We just—”

“Just what? Having a romantic dinner? I thought you were over your dating phase!” Lisa teased, her eyes dancing with mischief.

Emily could feel the heat rising in her cheeks as she gathered her thoughts. “We were having a lovely time until the fire alarm,” she interjected, trying to reclaim the narrative.

“Oh, I’m sorry to interrupt!” Lisa chimed, a smirk on her face. “I’ll let you two get back to your… date.”

As she walked away, Ryan sighed, visibly embarrassed. “I’m so sorry about that. I didn’t expect her to show up,” he said, running a hand through his hair.

“It’s fine, really. Just unexpected,” Emily replied, trying to play it cool. But the spark of the evening had dimmed, and the awkwardness lingered in the air like the scent of burnt toast.

They attempted to steer the conversation back to lighter topics, but Emily couldn’t shake the feeling of being a third wheel on her own date. The lively atmosphere of the pub faded into the background noise of Ryan’s distracted chatter. Eventually, they both fell into a silence that felt heavier than the earlier chaos.

“I think I might need a break from dating,” Emily finally said, half-jokingly. “This was not how I pictured tonight going.”

Ryan chuckled but didn’t quite meet her gaze. “Yeah, I get that. This definitely took a turn.”

Then, like a plot twist in a bad rom-com, another unexpected visitor approached their table. A tall man with a commanding presence walked over, his eyes locking onto Ryan. “I thought that was you! It’s been ages!”

Emily watched as Ryan’s face lit up, forgetting their previous conversation entirely. “Michael! Wow, it’s good to see you!”

What followed was another awkward round of introductions, where Emily felt like she was being introduced as the “date” rather than a person. Michael quickly dominated the conversation, regaling tales from college, including some embarrassing moments that made Emily want to sink into her drink.

As the evening wore on, her initial excitement about Ryan faded into a distant memory. She felt like a spectator in her own life, watching their camaraderie from the sidelines. After a while, she excused herself again, this time seeking solace in the restroom.

Staring at her reflection, she wondered how the night had gone so wrong. This was the first date she had been genuinely excited about in months, and here she was, feeling like a ghost at her own dinner. She splashed cold water on her face and took a deep breath, trying to regain her composure.

When she returned to the booth, Ryan was still engaged in conversation with Michael, laughter ringing through the air. She tapped Ryan on the shoulder, and when he turned, his face lit up. “Hey! We were just talking about that time I fell off the stage during the talent show!”

“Sounds like a real moment,” she replied dryly, attempting to hide her irritation under a veil of sarcasm.

“Oh, it was!” he laughed. “You should have been there!”

Emily forced a smile. “Yeah, sounds like a real blast.”

Just then, she noticed Lisa again, this time standing at the bar, her eyes fixed on their table with a bemused expression. The sight made Emily’s stomach churn. She couldn’t help but feel like the punchline of a bad joke, something to be gawked at rather than appreciated.

“Honestly, I think I’m going to head home,” she said, surprising even herself with the words.

“What? But we’re having such a good time!” Ryan exclaimed, clearly caught off guard.

“Are we? I’m not so sure,” she replied, her voice firm yet soft. “I don’t think this is what I signed up for.”

Ryan looked taken aback, and for a moment, embarrassment washed over his face. “I didn’t mean to make you feel uncomfortable. I just… didn’t expect any of this.”

“I get that, but I don’t want to sit here feeling like an accessory to your reunion tour,” she said, a pang of disappointment slicing through her.

As she stood, Ryan reached out for her hand. “Wait, Emily. I really like you. I know this night has been crazy, but I want to get to know you. Can we please start over?”

She paused, the sincerity in his eyes softening her resolve. “I appreciate that, Ryan, but sometimes things just don’t work out. It’s okay. Maybe we can reschedule?”

“Yeah… of course. I’d like that,” he said, his tone a mix of regret and hope.

With a final nod, Emily turned and walked towards the exit. The crisp air hit her, invigorating her senses as she stepped into the night. She felt a sense of liberation, a lightness in her step that hadn’t been there before.

As she walked home, she reflected on the evening’s events, laughing at the absurdity of it all. The fire alarm, the unexpected guests, the awkward tensions—all of it could have been part of some wild romantic comedy. But instead of feeling defeated, she saw it as a valuable lesson in dating: not every connection is meant to flourish, and sometimes misadventures lead to the best stories.

And as she settled onto her couch with a cup of chamomile tea, she couldn’t help but smile. The night hadn’t gone as planned, but it reminded her of something vital: it was okay to step away from situations that didn’t feel right. There would be other dates, other stories, and who knew? Maybe the next one would be just a little less chaotic and a lot more exciting.
