Lost in Time: The Adventures of Crossing Realities


The first sensation I felt was the cold. A biting, unbearable chill that crept under my skin and into my bones. I tried to move, but my limbs were stiff, as though I hadn’t used them in years. I opened my eyes, but everything was hazy at first. A blur of colors, shadows, and flashes of light surrounded me. It was only when my vision cleared that I realized I was no longer in the place I had been just moments before.

I had been in my room, staring at the clock as it ticked past midnight. The faint hum of the air conditioner had lulled me into a strange, half-conscious state, and then… nothing. Everything had gone black.

Now, I was standing in what seemed to be an ancient, forgotten forest. Tall trees, their bark dark and twisted, reached into the sky like the fingers of some long-forgotten giant. Strange shapes moved in the shadows of the thick underbrush, and the air smelled like damp earth, moss, and something else—something metallic, faintly like iron.

I took a hesitant step forward, my heart racing. What had happened? Where was I?

Before I could gather my thoughts, I heard a rustling sound behind me. I spun around, my pulse quickening. From the darkened trees, a figure emerged—a person, cloaked in shadow, their face hidden by a hood. I couldn’t make out any details, but I knew one thing for sure: they weren’t here to help me.

Without thinking, I turned and ran. The forest seemed endless, its twisted paths pulling me deeper into an unknown realm. The figure’s footsteps echoed behind me, growing closer with each passing second. Panic surged through me, and I pushed myself harder, my breath ragged in my chest.

I didn’t know where I was. I didn’t know why I was here. But I knew one thing: I had to get away.

I had no idea how long I ran. Minutes? Hours? Time seemed to lose all meaning in this strange world. The more I ran, the more the forest seemed to change around me. Trees that had once been thick and oppressive began to thin out, revealing strange, surreal landscapes. Vast open fields stretched before me, dotted with glowing stones and bizarre plants that seemed to pulse with energy. The air shifted, becoming warmer, lighter, as though I had stepped into an entirely different world.

Suddenly, the ground beneath my feet began to tremble. The entire landscape shifted, as though the fabric of reality itself was being torn apart. The sky, once a dull gray, cracked open, revealing a swirling vortex of light and color. A voice, deep and resonant, echoed from the very fabric of the universe.

“Cross the thresholds, and you will understand,” the voice said, though it wasn’t spoken aloud. It reverberated in my mind, as if the words were coming from all around me at once.

I stumbled backward, my mind reeling. What did it mean? What thresholds? What was happening to me?

The vortex above me spun faster, pulling everything toward its center. The trees, the sky, the ground—all began to warp and twist, like the pages of a book being ripped out and rearranged. Then, with a final, deafening crack, everything snapped.

I was falling.

I awoke on solid ground, though my body ached from the sudden shift. My vision blurred again, and when I opened my eyes, I was no longer in the forest or the surreal field. The landscape around me was… familiar, yet completely alien. Tall skyscrapers loomed overhead, their surfaces glinting in the bright sun. The streets were crowded with people, but none of them seemed to notice me. Their faces were expressions of indifference, their bodies moving like clockwork, unaware of the strange being that had appeared in their midst.

I staggered to my feet, blinking in disbelief. This was a city. A bustling metropolis, but it wasn’t the one I had known. The buildings were taller, sleeker, more futuristic. The air was thick with the hum of technology, and floating vehicles zipped past overhead, leaving trails of glowing light in their wake.

I took a tentative step forward, my mind racing. Had I somehow traveled to the future? Was this the same Earth? Or had I crossed into a parallel dimension entirely?

Before I could contemplate this further, something strange caught my eye. A small group of people, dressed in simple, tattered clothes, were standing in the middle of the street. They were gazing up at something in the sky, their eyes wide with wonder. I followed their gaze, my heart skipping a beat.

There, in the sky above, was a rip—a jagged, gaping tear in the very fabric of reality. Through it, I could see a different world entirely: a world of fire and ash, where a massive storm raged on. Lightning cracked across the sky, illuminating the darkened landscape below. The storm seemed to pulse with an unnatural energy, like it was alive.

I shivered. The people around me didn’t seem to notice the tear. It was as if they were blind to it, focused only on their own lives, oblivious to the danger that lay just beyond their reach.

I had to understand what was happening. I had to find out why I was here and how I could return to my own reality.

Days turned into weeks as I wandered through this strange, fractured world. Each time I thought I had found a way back, the fabric of time would tear again, and I would be pulled into another reality. Sometimes I ended up in a dystopian future, where humanity struggled to survive in a world ravaged by war and environmental collapse. Other times, I found myself in an alternate past, where ancient civilizations still thrived, untouched by modern technology. Each reality was different, yet they all felt connected, as though they were all part of a greater, interwoven tapestry.

Along the way, I met others who had crossed the same thresholds. Some had been trapped for years, while others had only recently found themselves caught in the shifting web of realities. They all shared a common goal: to find a way back home, to escape the endless cycle of crossing worlds.

One day, I encountered a woman who seemed to know more about the nature of these realities than anyone else I had met. She was tall, with dark hair that flowed like liquid silk and eyes that gleamed with the knowledge of someone who had seen too much.

“You’re not the first to fall through the cracks,” she said, her voice soft yet filled with authority. “But you might be the last.”

“Why?” I asked, my voice thick with confusion. “Why are we all here? Why are we crossing these realities?”

She hesitated for a moment before answering.

“Because time itself is collapsing,” she said. “The boundaries between worlds are growing thinner. The fabric of reality is weakening. Every time you cross, you create a ripple, and those ripples are causing the worlds to bleed into each other.”

“Is there a way to stop it?” I asked, my heart pounding in my chest.

She nodded slowly. “There is one way. But it’s not easy. To fix the rift, you must reach the source—the origin of the cracks. And once you do, you’ll have to make a choice.”

“A choice?” I echoed.

“Yes,” she said, her gaze turning distant. “You’ll have to choose whether to restore the timelines and return everything to the way it was… or to let everything collapse, allowing a new reality to be born from the ashes of the old one.”

I felt a chill run down my spine. The weight of her words settled heavily on my shoulders. I didn’t know if I was ready for that kind of choice. But I had to try. I had to find the origin of the cracks.

The journey to the heart of the collapsing multiverse was perilous. I crossed countless realities, each more strange and dangerous than the last. I battled monstrous creatures, navigated through desolate wastelands, and confronted versions of myself that were twisted by the very nature of these fractured worlds. Each time I thought I was close to the source, something would pull me further away, and the rift would widen once more.

But in the end, I found it—the source.

It was a place beyond time, beyond space. A vast, empty void that stretched on forever. In the center of the void stood a towering structure, its surface shimmering with an ethereal glow. I approached it, my heart heavy with the knowledge of the decision I would have to make.

As I stepped closer, a voice echoed from within the structure.

“You have come,” it said, its tone both welcoming and ominous. “Now, you must choose. Will you repair the fabric of time, or will you let it unravel?”

I stood before the structure, the weight of the world on my shoulders. I didn’t know what the future held. I didn’t know if I could fix the broken realities. But I knew one thing for certain.

The choice was mine.
