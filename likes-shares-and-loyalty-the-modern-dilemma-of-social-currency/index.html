Likes, Shares, and Loyalty: The Modern Dilemma of Social Currency


It was just another ordinary day at the bustling metropolis. Raindrops gently tapping upon the glass windows, the low murmur of the bustling city life at its most subdued. Glass towers stood silently, housing the diverse dreams and aspirations of their occupants. Despite the rain, the sun still shone, casting its golden beams upon the high rise buildings, illuminating the digital skyline.

Inside the Glassman Tower, nestled in the heart of the city, Ana Maria Hernandez, a young marketing specialist, fiddled with her hair, her fingers nervously plucking at the loose strands. She glanced at the vibrant posters plastered on the glass walls – a cacophony of colors – promoting the latest brands and social movements. In that moment, everything seemed to collapse into one simple thought: Am I more than just another pair of eyes? Just another set of stats, another number in the enormous maze of networks and connections?

Her eyes fell upon a figure in an immaculate white shirt – Damian Walker, the CEO of ClickHive Technologies, the company where Ana worked. The architect of a multitude of digital products that dominated people’s lives. Unfortunately, they were also the maestro of the trap she and millions others were ensnared in. Like all modern-day architects, Damian had built his empire on the very same foundation of ‘Likes’, ‘Shares’, and ‘Loyalty’. But unlike the architects of ancient times, he had infiltrated the inside of our hearts, into our very notion of self-worth.

Damian’s voice shattered the silence that hung in the air like a thick fog.

“We have a meeting.” He said, his tone not asking, but signaling a command. A command that every employee in the Glassman Tower would follow.

A sea of faces, each bearing their distinct troubles and victories, filled the ornate conference room. A murmuring mass of hopes and fears; in this space they were but a collective. Sunlight, shining through the expansive dual-pane windows, cast its rays over their faces, their silhouettes elongated and distorted, morphing into shadow puppets dancing in the ever-shifting light.

The room silenced as Damian, now standing before his chessboard of prospects and problems, began to speak. “We need to raise our engagement rates. Keep our stake in the digital world. Remember, it’s the sound of clicks, the hum of likes, that signify our reign.” His words echoed throughout the vast, empty room, as if that were a concrete reality. “The world waits for us. It hungers for the latest trend, the next big thing. And it is our responsibility to deliver it.”

The sea of faces shifted and nodded in unison, transfixed by his words. They were indeed all inmates in his grand palace of perfect illusions, forever caged by the numbers and likes, fending off the ever-growing army of social currency.

“You all know Ana Maria,” Damian paused, capturing Ana’s attention. She suddenly felt the wrath of a thousand eyes burning through her skin, resonating even till the terminal ends of her fingers. “Her analytical skills and her unique perspectives never cease to amaze me – they’re contagious even.”

Ana felt a sudden twinge of pride, coupled with apprehension, over his words. Pride, as her work was appreciated. Apprehension, because she knew very well the expectations hanging over her head. Damian Walker often thrived on instilling fear in his employees, making it seem like the world would crumble if they didn’t execute his grand plans flawlessly.

Damian continued, his voice dripping with palpable excitement. “Which brings me to our next project,” he paused for effect. “I want Ana Maria to start a new social experiment, to unfurl a new digital campaign aimed at cultivating loyalty. A campaign so profound, it will transcend even the ferocious ocean of social currency we now command.”

For a moment, Ana felt the warmth of Damian’s confidence in her. But the rapidly forming chills that followed reminded her of the gravity of the situation. The term “loyalty” had become a rare and endangered species in the wild, unrestrained human jungle where her company thrived. In a world perpetually changing, with impermanence as its anthem, how could she possibly capture loyalty? In their never-ending race for the finish line, they had long forgotten to enjoy the journey, dismissing true loyalty in its path.

Back in her cubicle, thumbed through the pages of her dilapidated notebook, its yellowed pages filled with requisition number handwritten margins, Ana started scribbling furiously. The first line read: “When people realized the world was round, they cried because it couldn’t be covered by a fitting carpet.” - A cynical adaptation of a verse she’d read earlier.

She launched into a frenzy, an emotional catharsis. And out emerged a masterpiece designed to outshine even Homer’s “The Odyssey”. Using her skills of digital marketing mixed with dash of rhetorical genius, she came up with “RealInteractions,” a campaign to reignite the long-lost fire of real human connections in the digital age.

Instead of generic likes and shares, ‘Real Interactions’ employed a system of credibility scores based on the depth of interaction, intention, and long-term engagement. The reward? A rising graph of uninterrupted loyalty. Instead of chasing fleeting applause from digital Royalty, they pursued the timeless melody of authentic expression and recognition.

As the campaign unfolded, like a blossoming flower spreading its petals, real-life interactions began to bloom. The old becomes new again in the heart of a silent rebellion, the non-shareable and unrecordable moments of happiness became the new ‘currency’ in the network of human connection. People now realized the value in meaningful conversations, in the warmth of shared experiences, and in the simple enjoyment of life.

The following days and months saw the return of genuine expressions of love, friendship, and human connectedness. To Damian and the soaring empire of ClickHive, it appeared as if the sun had crashed from the sky, leaving only a faint specter of its brilliance; the era of ‘Likes’, ‘Shares’, and ‘Loyalty’ had truly become a relic of the past.

Slowly but surely, the world began to change. The challenging landscape of ‘Likes’, ‘Shares’, and loyalty gradually sparked a spectrum of emotions in the human heart. Suddenly, people remembered that human engagement, the depth of an emotion, and the loyalty to shared values can, in their own way, be the most powerful currency of all.
