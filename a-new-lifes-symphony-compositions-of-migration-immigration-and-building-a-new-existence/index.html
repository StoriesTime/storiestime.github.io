A New Life's Symphony: Compositions of Migration, Immigration, and Building a New Existence


Nina gazed out at the street, the sun warming her face as she turned to her sister, Maria. “I’m going to miss this old apartment, and our small life here in Spain,” she murmured, a reflective tone in her voice.

Maria nodded, wiping a tear from the corner of her eye. “Yours truly, this is our time to shine,” she recalled their mother’s words, etched in memory and heart. Their world was changing, moving from Madrid to New York – a city of dreams, as their mother so often told them.

It was the 12th of October, 1960, and many diverse paths were intertwining to create a new rhythm in the city that never slept.

Margarita, the sisters’ maternal aunt, had been living in New York for over four years. Through the years, she had lived and outlived the severe separation from her homeland. Now a warm, Americanized woman with a loving husband, a doting spouse named Frank, she eagerly awaited their relatives from Spain.

“You will be fine here, as fine as you are in Madrid,” she insisted, assuring them before they left. “New York awaits you, with shares of sunshine and the color of an all-embracing rainbow.”

The first few landscapes they encountered in New York echoed that sentiment, brilliantly so.

The family bond between the two new immigrant sisters and their aunt deepened. Margarita was the proud godmother of Maria’s son, Rafael. The now vibrant, active women were shielded by cultural boundaries, embracing a new life with hope and the existence of chasing dreams. And so, the journey began.

Over time, the sisters rebuilt their lives, humming to the tune of the city, constructing a life composed of distinct, consonant notes of evolution, integration, and growth.

María, now a widow, continued to raise her son Rafael in the vibrant neighborhood nestled among towering buildings against the skyline. Spaces filled with laughter and life. Her biggest accomplishment, a proud member of their woodwind family – a flutist. Rafael’s dreams pierced the air like a soprani saxophone jazz solo, his fingers flying like the wings of a rapidly beating heart, almost parallel to the pulse of a city that beat in their favor.

Nina, on the other hand, with her strong, creative spirit, planted seeds of artistry. Amidst the bustling city, she cultivated a space for artistic expression and community, a venue to support other artists like a hidden bass line. An unassuming coffee shop blossomed into an impressive art gallery, interweaving dreams like threads of silk.

The needle of time moved forward. The rhythm of the city grew louder, and yet more optimistic; it embraced the newcomers as its own, as it played themes of integration, keeping them closely intertwined within the seams of this new reality. The aunt had grown older, and the sisters’ family now welcomed the union of their children – an intricate dance of love, romance, and joy.

Resilient and hopeful, the family lurched forward with a tune of a city that never slept. Young Rafael, a saxophone prodigy, garnered the attention of many in the city’s musical circles. Encouraged by the love and determination of his artistic mother, he surged forward, diving deep into the realm of the creative.

It was in a sultry jazz club that the first steps to the realization of dreams were rooted. Rafael’s saxophone shaped the night sky, unravelling a composition of migration, immigration, and building a new existence – all against the backdrop of swirling women’s voices. Vocal harmonies coaxed the floating dreams of the city’s many enclaves together, adding color to Nina’s gallery and drawing the gaze of a city that never slept.

As the mood escalated and their voices continued to blend with the saxophone’s rhythm, an art form was born. It was in this B Minor composition, a song cycle created from the stirring of a shared journey - the sounds of their ancestries echoing in the city that never slept. Through the elements of migration, immigration, and overcoming hardship, their links to their heritage were preserved and advanced in an irreversible journey.

It was the pursuit of this elusive dream of the “universal language” that they clinched, their destiny tied to the city that refused to slumber.

As the audience whistled and applauded, the final note faded into a quiet night. The old scores of hardship no longer remained; instead, the echoes of a new life’s symphony played on, enveloping the city like a warm, comforting blanket.

The song of the sisters, and of a city forever, had reached a crescendo.

This cycle of life is tied to this place. A chord of melody flutters between the wings of a city and a family, still echoing deeply in the dark, motionless streets of the city that never sleeps. The tune of the new life will continue to rise and mature into the future, always connected and part of an abstract mosaic. The threads of strands they have woven will never be snipped, for the song cycles of these lives are firmly held in the city’s embrace.
