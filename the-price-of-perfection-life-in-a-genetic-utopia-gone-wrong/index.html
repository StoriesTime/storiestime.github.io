The Price of Perfection: Life in a Genetic Utopia Gone Wrong


In the year 2154, the world welcomed the age of perfection. Scientists had cracked the genetic code, and humanity was on the cusp of a revolution. The idea was simple: design and engineer a superior species, free from disease, conflict, and suffering. The notion of eugenics was rebranded as "genetic optimization," and the concept took root. People flocked to the visionary city of Elysium, where the perks of perfection were promised to all. Recruiters emphasized the benefits of life extension, surpassing mental acuity, and removing the threat of chronic diseases. No longer would humanity be plagued by imperfections; no longer would people struggle with infirmities. Children born in Elysium were bred for greatness, based on moral understanding and advanced capabilities. Interactive learning tools were matched to individual developmental pace, with adaptive assistance from AI imprinted advisors. These new citizens of Elysium possessed access to undisputed promises: uninterruptible healthcare, hyper-intelligence required for scholarship and trade.
