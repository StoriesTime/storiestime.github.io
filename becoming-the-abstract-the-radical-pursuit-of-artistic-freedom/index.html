Becoming the Abstract: The Radical Pursuit of Artistic Freedom


In the underground world of the Artists’ Quarter, there was an entity known simply as the Abstract. As elusive as it was enigmatic, its singular pursuit was artistic freedom in its most unadulterated form. From the dimly lit studios of the humblest upstarts to the gilded galleries of the grandest maestros, whispers of the Abstract’s creative prowess reverberated through the halls of artistry.

A tall, skeletal figure draped in a midnight blue robe, the Abstract was rarely seen outside of the veil of night. It possessed neither provable age nor discernible origin. No one could recount the first time they had laid eyes on the Abstract, nor did they dare attempt to unmask its true identity. To them, it was always just there, hauntingly elusive, a mere shadow in the night.

For a generation of artists who yearned to break free from the shackles of convention and the relentless demands of their patrons, the Abstract was everything they aspired to be. They sought not only to emulate its artistic genius but also to understand its indomitable will to transcend the limitations of their reality.

In this ever-fchanging world, where art and artists sought to redefine itself, the Abstract existed as an enigma; a symbol of the unlimited potential for artistic liberation.

One such artist, a young painter by the name of Alazie, had spent years honing their craft. They were fueled by the relentless throb of the city’s electric pulse and had witnessed firsthand the artists who dared to follow the Abstract’s path. However, Alazie harbored a secret ambition: to know the Abstract intimately, to glimpse the artistic genius that had escaped countless artists before them.

As the moon climbed into the velvety night sky, the streets of the Artists’ Quarter emptied, save for a few lost souls seeking solace in the darkness. Neon lights flickered and pulsated, casting an eerie glow on the forgotten dwellings that housed the creative spirits who dared to defy the norms of art. Yet, in the blackened shadows, the Abstract lurked.

Alazie knew this was the time to enter the world of the Abstract. They had spent hours perfecting their deepest, darkest canvases, whispering to them the names of the legends who had come before, and planting the seeds of their dreams: to know the Abstract.

As they entered the Artists’ Quarter, the cool night air clung to their skin, the shadows playing a macabre game with the shape before them. Alazie felt their heart race in their chest, both fear and anticipation warring for control.

The streets whispered, guiding Alazie through a labyrinth of uncharted territory until they were standing before a low-slung door. Its once-elegant architecture was now marred by age and decay. In the dim light, Alazie could make out the faint markings of a symbol they had never seen before: a simple, elegant curve that seemed to dance upon the fading paint.

Taking a deep breath, Alazie pushed the door open and stepped into another world. The air was thick with the scent of oily colors and fading light, while the walls were adorned with centuries-old pieces that spoke of countless artists’ souls trapped within their eternal pursuit of freedom.

In the center of the dimly lit room, a figure stood. Though Alazie could not place their face, they recognized the skeletal, midnight-robed figure as the Abstract. It did not move, did not acknowledge their presence; rather, it remained a static monolith amid the swirling chaos that made up its being.

Alazie approached, their heart pounding in their chest, and reached out to touch the inanimate figure. With a whisper of anticipation, the form stirred, blossoming into a myriad of colors before their eyes.

The Abstract was not just one entity; it was an amalgamation of artists’ spirits so bound by a shared sense of purpose that they had transcended the limitations of flesh and bone. Here was the sum of Alazie’s dreams: a vast soul-garden buzzing with the collective whispers of artistry’s unbridled freedom.

For a moment, Alazie stood enraptured, their heart beating wildly as they took in the magnificent array of colors and shapes. They felt the weight of reality shift, allowing them to perceive the world through the Abstract’s eyes. The vivid mind-scape flowed across the room, wrapping around Alazie like a second skin.

As they continued to absorb the fantastical world before them, Alazie noticed a small, empty pedestal in the corner of the room. The Abstract pointed at it with a delicate, ethereal digit. Knowing what was expected of them, Alazie stepped onto the pedestal.

In that moment, the walls of the dimly lit chamber seemed to melt away, replaced by a vast sooty sky. The world was awash with a spectrum of ethereal colors and brilliant hues. Alazie caught glimpses of a world unbound, where art was not confined to canvas or stone but instead exploded from the very fabric of existence.

They saw the Abstract’s creations, a dreamscape reserved only for the purest forms of freedom - a place where the lines between past, present, and future blurred like the rich pigments in a majestic oil painting. Alazie saw and felt the dreams of untold artists realized in this bizarre dimension, a place where art took center stage, requiring no explanation or interpretation.

The scene faded as suddenly as it had appeared, and Alazie found themselves back in the now familiar room. The Abstract stood before them, its ethereal form flickering with the iridescent light of a near-infinite creative potential.

And with that, the visit came to an end. Alazie stepped off the pedestal and joined the others in the ethereal garden. The Abstract whispered its wisdom softly, a sibilant murmur of artistic freedom that echoed through the endless expanse of the possible.

As the night came to a close and the artists began to slip away, one by one, their individual spirits gravitated toward each other, becoming a chorus of creativity that filled the room. Then, like a dream on the cusp of dawn, they leaked back into the waking world, richer for the experience, their artistic souls forever entwined with the Abstract’s. For Alazie, the pursuit of artistic freedom had just begun. They had seen the end of the rainbow, and now there was only one direction left to follow - forward.
