Leading with Influence: How Small Actions Create Big Results


Maya had always admired leaders, those commanding figures whose voices seemed to hold an invisible magnetic force, making others follow without question. Yet, she never saw herself as one of them. In her mid-thirties, working as a team coordinator at a mid-sized tech firm, she often felt overshadowed by more vocal, assertive personalities. But a series of unexpected events would change her view of leadership—and reveal how subtle, consistent actions can ripple into profound change.

The Quiet Start

On a bustling Monday morning, Maya arrived at the office with a sense of quiet purpose. The company was undergoing a digital transformation, rolling out a new project management platform to streamline workflows. Adoption was rocky; many employees resisted the change and grumbled behind closed doors. Morale was sinking, and productivity had taken a hit.

Maya’s role wasn’t managerial, but as the coordinator, she recognized the mounting tensions. Instead of rallying with loud directives, she chose a different path.

In the breakroom, she started small—offering genuine compliments when colleagues asked for help with the new system. She listened carefully when peers expressed frustrations, validating their feelings without dismissing their concerns.

Gradually, Maya discovered the power of being approachable and empathetic. Her willingness to assist without judgment earned trust, and more people began seeking her advice, sharing their challenges openly.

The Power of Consistent Encouragement

Around the office, various teams grappled with adapting their routine to the new tools. A kernel of resistance seeded in the form of fear—of appearing incompetent or losing control.

One afternoon, Maya noticed Alex from the design team visibly frustrated while battling with task assignments in the platform. She casually walked over and said, “Hey, I found a trick today that helped me organize my backlog better. Want to see?”

This small gesture blossomed into an informal “help squad,” a handful of employees exchanging tips and hacks via quick chats and emails. Maya didn’t instruct but influenced through sharing effective practices and recognizing efforts whenever someone made progress, no matter how minor.

Morale began shifting. The collective anxiety about the new process started easing into cautious optimism. Small celebrations of incremental wins, like completing the first sprint smoothly or reducing miscommunications, became common.

Maya’s subtle encouragement became a quiet current beneath the office’s chaos. It empowered individuals to embrace change rather than resist it, leading to improved collaboration and output.

Creating a Culture of Ownership

Inspired by these shifts, Maya proposed a simple initiative during a team meeting: a weekly “Spotlight Moment” where anyone could share a success or a lesson learned related to the platform implementation.

Though initially hesitant, the team embraced the idea, finding value in shared stories—whether a resolved complication or a newfound shortcut. These sessions built a sense of ownership and collective responsibility.

One participant, Jordan, shared how streamlining client communications reduced errors in billing, saving hours weekly. Another, Priya, spoke about improving clarity in task delegations, cutting down confusion drastically.

Small narratives transformed into bigger lessons. Each story influenced others, seeding better habits and a resilient attitude toward change.

Ripple Effects Beyond the Team

What Maya didn’t anticipate was the ripple effect spreading beyond her immediate circle. Other departments took notice, adopting similar peer-led sharing sessions. The company’s leadership began recognizing these grassroots efforts as critical drivers in the transformation’s success.

One day, the CEO invited Maya to speak at an all-hands meeting—not to commend her formally but to share her ideas about leading through influence rather than authority.

Standing in front of hundreds, Maya recounted her experience, emphasizing that leadership isn’t just about titles or commands. It’s about those in-between moments: the small gestures of support, authentic listening, and empowering others to find their own solutions.

Her message resonated deeply.

Leadership Redefined

Maya’s journey illustrated a profound truth: leadership is less about grand gestures and more about consistent, small actions that build trust and motivate others.

She had never held a formal leadership title, yet she led with influence in the truest sense. By focusing on empathy, peer learning, and incremental progress, Maya created a culture that celebrated adaptability and mutual support.

Years later, Maya reflected on that transformation. The company had become a model for successful change management, often cited in industry circles. But what mattered most was the everyday influence she witnessed—how tiny acts of kindness and encouragement sparked confidence, teamwork, and innovation.

Lessons from Maya’s Story

  • Empathy Builds Bridges: Listening sincerely and validating concerns encourages open communication.
  • Small Wins Matter: Celebrating incremental progress fuels motivation and resilience.
  • Peer Support Creates Ownership: Encouraging knowledge sharing empowers individuals and teams.
  • Influence Over Authority: Leading through relationship and trust produces sustainable change.
  • Ripple Effects Multiply Impact: Grassroots initiatives can transform entire cultures.

Maya’s story is a testament to how leadership is less about the size of your role and more about the size of your influence. These subtle, deliberate actions—offering help, recognizing efforts, sharing success—compound over time, creating powerful results.

If you’re wondering how to effect change or improve your team’s performance, start small. The quiet, consistent steps you take today might just become the catalyst for tomorrow’s big breakthroughs.
