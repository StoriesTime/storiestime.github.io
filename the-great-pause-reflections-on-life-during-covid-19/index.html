The Great Pause: Reflections on Life during COVID-19


As I sit here, surrounded by the stillness of my empty apartment, I am reminded of the words "nature abhors a vacuum." The COVID-19 pandemic has brought about a universal pause, forcing humanity to slow down, reflect, and reevaluate its existence. My thoughts are a maze of contradictions - gratitude, frustration, and hope all intertwined like the threads of a tapestry. It began in late February, when reports of a mysterious outbreak in China started to spread like wildfire across the globe. Panic set in as governments scrambled to contain the spread of the novel coronavirus. Flights were grounded, borders closed, and social distancing measures put in place. The once bustling streets were now eerily quiet, a testament to the drastic measures taken to combat the impending doom. As the days blurred together, I found myself stuck in a perpetual state of limbo. Work-from-home became the new norm, and I soon discovered the meaning of the phrase " isolation." My daily routine was confined to the four walls of my apartment, with online meetings and virtual coffee breaks my only connections to the outside world. The lack of human interaction was suffocating, yet I couldn't help but feel a glimmer of excitement at the prospect of rediscovering myself. The early days were marked by endless scrolling on social media, as we all trying to stay informed about the rapidly evolving situation. I found myself fixated on the daily updates from the World Health Organization (WHO), WHO press conferences, and the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC). The world was tuned in, glued to screens, yearning for a sliver of hope. Information became a lifeline, providing a sense of control in the face of uncertainty. As the weeks turned into months, the real impact of the pandemic began to unfold. The global economy teetered on the brink of collapse, with governments scrambling to prop it up with stimulus packages and artificial breathing space. Small businesses, the backbone of any economy, were forced to shutter their doors, leaving a trail of uncertainty in their wake. Amidst the chaos, there were pockets of resilience. Neighborhoods came together, forming volunteer-led initiatives to support the vulnerable and the elderly. Food pantries popped up overnight, collecting donations and serving as a lifeline to those struggling to access basic necessities. Strangers became allies, bonding over a shared experience of isolation and mutual support. As the days continued to drag on, I noticed a transformation within myself. I slowed down, began to appreciate the small things, and learned to cherish the beauty of silence. The once cluttered and chaotic spaces of my mind cleared out, making room for new ideas and perspectives. I started taking online courses, reading books on a wide range of subjects, and practicing yoga and meditation to cope with the stress. The pauses between each daguerreotype of my life became moments of self-reflection, as I embarked on a journey of self-discovery. One of the most peculiar aspects of this prolonged pause was the way it brought people together. Families were reunited, albeit virtually, with video calls and group chats filling the void left by the lack of physical proximity. Virtual dinner parties and game nights became the new norm, with people laughing, sharing stories, and forging connections in the digital realm. Neighbors in apartments a few floors down from mine would hold impromptu concerts, their music pouring out through open windows, filling the air with a glimmer of hope. And yet, as with all things, impermanence loomed over us. The smallest disruptions – a power outage, a water shortage, or a thread lost in transit – became catastrophic events, a reminder that the Great Pause was a fragile setup, easily derailed. As I look out the window, watching the world slowly awakening from its slumber, I am met with a strange sense of loss. The pause has become a refuge, a temporary reprieve from the crushing demands of everyday life. I'll miss the freedom to walk aimlessly without a mask, the flexibility to bend and fold time as I please. But most of all, I'll miss the whispered conversations, the silences between words, and the stillness of the pause. It was an unasked-for gift, a poignant reminder that there was more to life than the fast-paced, never-ending cycle we'd grown accustomed to. The clock ticks on, the world continues to evolve, and the geopolitical landscape shifts once more. The pause may have been an anomaly, but its lessons will linger.
