The Forbidden Evidence: A Detective's Grind


The city streets were dark and hauntingly quiet; the orange glow from streetlights barely penetrated the fog that lay thick upon the cobblestone ground. Hard rain had poured the night before, leaving a smell of dampness and decay lingering in the air. Only a stray cat’s haunting screech - reminiscent of the pleas of an untimely deceased - broke the silence.

Detective John Evers, wrapped in his overcoat, stuck out like a sore thumb amongst the dilapidated buildings. He adjusted his derby hat and grasped his ebony baton tightly; as he began to make his way towards the ancient district, diving deep into the heart of the enigma that awaited him.

It wasn’t an uncommon sight to see John in such an environment - the Devil’s Playground, as the newspapers dubbed it. The souls who dared to tread here were usually those with nefarious intentions, which made his job all the more rewarding.

The much-rumored ‘Forbidden Evidence’ had surfaced once more, leaving the city reeling in distress and puljoining unease. His intuition told him it was time to shed light on the shadowy places only darkness had once governed.

Chapter Two

As John neared his destination, an ancient wooden tavern nestled between two crumbling buildings, he got a sense that time itself stood still. An eerie feeling crept up his spine and despite his best efforts, John couldn’t shake it. He pressed on, knowing well the importance of the information he sought.

Dozens of characters slurred words and jeered him as he approached the tavern’s entrance. On the other side of the bar stood a haggard, and moonlit man with eyes that mirrored the shadows. ‘Eli the Gambler,’ notorious for hosting the clandestine card games that perpetuated the dark rumors surrounding the Forbidden Evidence.

John pushed his way to the bar, glaring at the figure opposite Eli. ‘You there.’ He extended a calloused hand, ‘William, isn’t it?’ The illicit card shark recoiled and retreated into the darkness.

John turned frostily towards Eli. ‘I’ll ask again; where is the Evidence?’ His eyes bore holes into the tavern keeper. Taking a long drag from a cigarette, Eli scoffed, ‘And who are you to make such demands?’

‘Someone who’s tired of this wretched place drowning in death and deception.’ His words shot across the room like lightning. The room swallowed a collective gasp; a hush gripping its very soul.

‘You know how this works.’ Eli spat back, flames trailing from the tip of his cigarette. ‘The players must gather and make an offering, then they are able to wield the Forbidden Evidence’.

John frowned, already lost in thought. ‘And just what is the Forbidden Evidence?’

Chapter Three

Eli tossed a weathered note onto the bar, John snatched it up – its cryptic contents both terrifying and enticing. The evidence solved cold cases across the land, and whoever used it, prospered. No doubt.

John handed it back, his voice a mere whisper, ‘Alright Eli, I’ll play your game. Tell me when and where,’ he said, before swiftly stumbling out into the foggy night.

As John navigated the desolate streets, something ignited within him – the thrill of the chase. He knew deep down this would be unlike any other case he’d solved. In the shadows of The Devil’s Playground, he finally glimpsed as to what drove him; the unquenchable need for power and the tenacity to unhinge the strongest walls.

The days passed, and John meticulously carried out his investigation whilst simultaneously amassing a small following of those who sought the same; armed revolutionaries and desperate men in search of redemption.

Chapter Four

As the appointed night drew nearer, the gathering was set to take place within a clandestine underground hideout. A stairwell, hidden amongst the cobblestones, divulged into a labyrinth of tunnels. Onward they advanced, fear welling up; yet driven by their own unyielding hunger.

There, nestled at the heart of the city, a silence hung thick. John knew this was it. The players had set. His mind racing, John considered the implications of wielding the Forbidden Evidence. The power it afforded. The secrets it unveiled.

The sound of chuckling echoes filled the air. A door slammed and a figure appeared at the end of the tunnel. Despite the clinging shadows, John could see his eyes gleaming; a burning hunger in them. ‘It’s time,’ they whispered.

As they advanced, John shut his eyes tight, steeling himself for what was to come. His lone regret was not knowing what exactly lay ahead.

From that set forth a darkness no man dared to fathom. John and his company would spend their remaining days as pawns in a deadly game, bound by a singular truth: The path to power and knowledge was paved with ruin.

Thus became the legend of Detective John Evers and those who sought the Forbidden Evidence; an everlasting dance between darkness and death - and the endless human desire for control.
