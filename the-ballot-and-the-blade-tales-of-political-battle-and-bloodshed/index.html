The Ballot and the Blade: Tales of Political Battle and Bloodshed


In the ravines of Greybriar, a tale as old as time echoed through the whispers of the night. The menace of two factions, both desperately seeking power, had ruled the land for centuries. One owed allegiance to the Ballot, their belief in democracy and justice; the other to the Blade, unwavering loyalty to the king’s rule and strength through dominance. And so it was written that the young, prodigious Cardinals would prove their mettle, choosing a path of either allegiance. Thus began their journey, leading to bloodshed, betrayal, and finally clarity amidst the storms of chaos.

A crimson sun marked the dawn of young Kaelin’s eighteenth year. He arose from his bed of straw and stale air, restless within the damp walls of the Cardinal’s Academy. The Academy, hidden deep within the ancient forest of Erythra, had been his home for the past decade. It was known to be the breeding ground of leaders, soldiers, diplomats and scholars — but Kaelin knew its true face. It was a cage for the sons of his ancestors, training them for their destiny.

The soft crunch of leaves beneath his leather boots filled the silence as Kaelin wandered the labyrinthine hallways. Towering shelves carved from the tree trunks supported ancient and massive tomes; treatises written in ink too faded to discern. Kaelin’s fingers danced over the leatherette and vellum spines, searching for enlightenment amidst the tangle of dusty knowledge.

Suddenly, the portal to the East flew open, and the soft clicks of horses hooves echoed in the chamber. A stooped form appeared, swathed in a velvet cloak. Upon the cloak’s shoulders pinned a badge of gold — the heraldry of Cardinal Redvers — the guardian of all knowledge.

“Kaelin,” rasped the aged man, his voice a whisper among the mighty timbers. “The time draws near.”

Time to choose a side, Kaelin thought. The Ballot or the Blade?

“The challenge will test your loyalty, your virtues, and your fate. Your brother, Aldric, bears my insignia. He shoulders the burden of the Blade. Choose your path well, young Cardinal.”

The impenetrable fog of doubt blurred Kaelin’s mind. His brother, the fire in his youth, lost in the grasp of darkness. The weight of his betrayal stifled his choice.

A shrewd and sinister voice called from the doorway, “The Blade shall prevail.” Upon the cloak of black silk, gleamed the dagger of obsidian. Cardinal Aldric stood tall, the gleaming emblem of the Blade radiating from his chest.

Their eyes met in the flickering torchlight, entwining the pain of loathing, love and fear.

“Were you ever just my brother, Aldric?”

“Forlorn?”

Aldric laughed, a dry sound without warmth. “Your place, my dear brother, is by the side of the Blade. Just as it is mine.”

Cardinal Redvers stepped forth between the brothers, his gnarled hand resting on Kaelin’s shoulder. “The challenge has always been your destiny. To balance the scales of power, tradition and choice.”

Their rivalry burgeoned. An orchestra of clashing steel and thunders of hooves created an aura of an epic battle. The heart of the forest throbbed under the fearsome din. Kaelin, his vantage point upon a precipice, cast his gaze upon the maddened carnage. It was his brother, his flame of youth, reduced to a blood-soaked frenzy.

The blade of Aldric cleaved its way through the air, seeking Kaelin’s heart. Kaelin stood grounded, his conviction quivering. He, who once toyed with the idea of loyalty to the Blade, now grasped the weight of the Ballot. The destiny of his people, their future, repealed within his somber determination.

The cry of the people echoed through the forest canopy, their faith a flickering flame. “For the Ballot and the Brotherhood!”

The fervent cry shook the resolve of Aldric. The radiance in his eyes dimmed, senseless and hopeless.

In that vein of steel, his sovereign destiny unfulfilled, Aldric’s blade came to rest.

The forest of Erythra was quiet for a while. Kaelin stood alone atop the precipice, the battle’s end enveloping him. The fluttering standard of the Ballot rustled in the gentle breeze, above the sea of defeated combatants.

He descended into the melee, his sapphire gaze discerned the triumph of fate. For the Ballot had swayed the tide of the battle. With every step forward, Kaelin’s resolve stiffened. The sons of the Academy, those who had been trained to seize their sovereign destiny with the strength of the Blade, followed him.

The age of the Blade had ended with the fall of his brother. In its place, the time for justice, reflection and the voice of reason erred. Kaelin had accepted the offering of the Ballot.

Through the haze of battle, Kaelin beheld the silhouette of the aged Cardinal. In his aged eyes, Kaelin found wisdom.

“So this is the web I have to weave,” he whispered to himself.

In the days that followed, Kaelin led his brethren to establish a charter of unity. His heart heavy, he beseeched his people for tolerance, understanding, and peace.

The journey was long, the road arduous, and the scars deep. Yet, Kaelin carried the burden with steadfast perseverance, knowing that peace was the core of his newfound purpose.

Thus, history found solace in the paradigm shift from the governing of fear to the rule of just laws. The Ballot would hold dominion over the hearts and minds of the people, and the world of Greybriar would rewrite its chronicles for generations to come.

A tale of politics and bloodshed, love and betrayal, now passed into the shadows of legend, the tale of the Ballot and the Blade. The whispers of the night traded in the longing hearts of men – for power and the right to determine their own destiny.
