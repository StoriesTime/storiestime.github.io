Roots and Wings: Narratives of Migration in a Changing World


Mariam's story began on a sweltering summer day in the small town of Sokoto, Nigeria. It was a place where the sky seemed to stretch endlessly above, where the streets were lined with vibrant market stalls, and the air was thick with the smells of spices and freshly baked bread. But amidst this boil of colorful life, there existed a more somber reality - one that Mariam couldn't escape. As she stood outside her family's small clay hut, listening to the constant hum of the nearby market, Mariam's thoughts began to drift back to a fateful night nine months prior, when her entire world was turned upside down. The night her father, Abdullah, had gathered his family around him and revealed a secret that would alter the course of their lives forever. "We have to leave," he said, his voice trembling as he handed each of them a small, intricately wrapped package. "The north is calling, and it's the only way to ensure our survival." From that moment on, Mariam's thoughts were filled with uncertainty - about what lay ahead, and why her family had to leave their beloved homeland in search of a better life. The questions swirled in her mind like a never-ending storm, until one day, her family set off on the perilous journey that would take them across continents and oceans. As they traveled, Mariam's senses were overwhelmed by the constant din of footsteps, the wail of sirens in the distance, and the dim murmur of voices in the night. Everywhere they went, there were people, and more people - fleeing their own struggles, their own losses. It was as if the world itself was alive, pulsating with an energy that was both exhilarating and terrifying. Their path twisted through deserts, where the golden sands gleamed like a thousand tiny suns, and the cacti pierced the sky like shards of stone. It led them through valleys, where the cawing of vultures echoed through the stillness, and the wind whispered ancient secrets in their ears. And finally, it brought them to the foot of a massive, icy mountain range, its peaks soaring higher than any tree Mariam had ever seen. Here, on the windswept mountain trail, they met a young guide named Angus, who was destined to become their guardian angel on the perilous journey ahead. With a small, wiry body and quick, trekking boots, Angus seemed made for this unforgiving terrain. And yet, it was his broad smile that gave Mariam hope - a small, shimmering thread of light that connected them all, even across such vast distances. "We're in this together, now," he declared, as they set off into the unending snow and ice. "We're going to conquer this mountain, and make a new home." Chapter 2 - A Fragmented Life Days turned into weeks, and weeks turned into seasons. And through it all, Mariam found herself growing stronger, more resilient. She was learning to navigate between dreams of the old world and the deserts of the new. Yet, even as she walked along the winding paths, her eyes were constantly searching - searching for something solid to cling to in this fragmented life. At times, it seemed as though the world itself had lost its rhythm, stumbling from dissonance to dissonance. And Mariam was caught in its midst, numb and helpless to stem the tide of uncertainty rising around her. As the trek continued, their small group encountered harsh wind and twice-freezing temperatures. The mountain's surface grew smoother, like the side of a harp string, yet still more than treacherous.
