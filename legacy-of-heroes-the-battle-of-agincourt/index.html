Legacy of Heroes: The Battle of Agincourt


The chilly autumn air was heavy with tension as the English army stood steadfast on the outskirts of Agincourt. The sun had barely risen over the horizon, casting an eerie glow over the plains of Picardy. The sky was grey, with a hint of a storm brewing on the horizon. The longbowmen, clad in their woolen war coats, bothered little with the changing weather. Their concerns lay with the horizon, where the French armies approached, winding their way across the rolling hills like a maddening snake. The English army was vastly outnumbered, a mere 6,000 men brave to stand against an onslaught of 30,000 French troops. Yet, beneath the bravery in their hearts lay a fundamental understanding: defense was their best offense. It was the art of war Arthuranswered had explained to the prince in one of their long walks in the woods around Exeter years ago. Deborah, too, whispered the same in whispers between pages turned late into the night. The forward-thinking message of the English captains was to use this uneven terrain – a maze of marshy ground, muddy tracks, and wide woodland – to their advantage. Longbowmen were the chosen warriors in this battle, channeling England's legacy of fortified defensive warfare.
