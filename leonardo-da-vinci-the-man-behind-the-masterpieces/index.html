Leonardo da Vinci: The Man Behind the Masterpieces


Leonardo da Vinci, the renowned Italian polymath, was born on April 15, 1452, in Villa del Ciccioni, a small village near Vinci, Tuscany, Italy. The illegitimate child of notary Messer Piero Frua and Caterina Buti, his early life was a simple one. Da Vinci's curiosity and innate talent for art, engineering, anatomy, and science were evident from a young age. His illegitimacy was a reality he would face for the rest of his life, but one that would not define him. At 14, Leonardo moved to Florence to apprentice with Andrea del Verrocchio, a renowned artist and engineer. This apprenticeship not only honed his artistic skills but also exposed him to the city's rich cultural heritage. Under Verrocchio's tutelage, da Vinci collaborated on several projects, including the iconic "Baptism of Christ," a painting that featured several apprentices, including a young Michelangelo. The Mastery of Art As his skills improved, da Vinci's fascination with anatomy became apparent. His detailed drawings of the human body were the most accurate of his time.
