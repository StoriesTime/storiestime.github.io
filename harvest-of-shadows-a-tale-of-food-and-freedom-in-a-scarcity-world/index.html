Harvest of Shadows: A Tale of Food and Freedom in a Scarcity World


The village of Elyria lay shrouded in an eternal twilight, as if the very sky itself was certain that the land was forever cursed to live in a state of scarcity. The dry, cracked earth seemed to defy any notion of fertility, and the meager harvests that were gleaned from it only served to underscore the idea that Elyria was a place where provision was always short and freedom was an unaffordable luxury. Here, the stories of old were whispered in hushed tones around campfires, of a world where food was plentiful and seasons were mild. The great Solari nomads, descendants of Earth's ancient roaming peoples, told of when they roamed under endless skies and under air, free from fear, with land beneath their feet and harvests bounding their footsteps. In the present, however, such tales were shadows, mere figments of memory that served as fleeting relief from the crushing duty that bound Elyria's inhabitants to the tightrope of survival.
