The Mysterious Case of the Missing Sandwich


It was a regular Tuesday morning at the bustling city office of Bennett & Co., a firm known for its cutting-edge technology and dynamic workforce. The morning had started like any other. Coffee machines hummed in the background, keyboards clicked rhythmically, and the scent of fresh pastries wafted from the break room. However, what would transpire next would change the course of this otherwise mundane day, thrusting it into the realm of intrigue and mystery.

The Sandwich

It all started innocuously enough, with a simple sandwich—a tuna and avocado creation—prepared by one of the company’s employees, Sam, who had a well-established reputation as the office’s snack guru. Sam, a man with an impeccable eye for detail, spent an inordinate amount of time perfecting his lunch. He had the perfect balance of crisp lettuce, creamy avocado, and tender tuna, sandwiched between two slices of toasted multigrain bread. He wrapped it carefully in parchment paper and left it on the desk to attend a quick meeting.

However, when Sam returned to his desk just an hour later, something was amiss. His sandwich—his perfect sandwich—was gone. No crumbs, no traces, no sign of it at all.

Confused but not yet alarmed, Sam stood at his desk and surveyed the scene. There were no obvious signs of mischief. His colleagues, mostly absorbed in their own tasks, seemed oblivious to the growing mystery that was slowly unfurling. But Sam’s mind was racing. Could it have been misplaced? Did he leave it in the break room? No, he was certain it had been right here on his desk.

His first instinct was to ask around.

The Investigation Begins

Sam wandered over to Olivia’s desk, his first suspect. Olivia was the office’s self-proclaimed “food enthusiast,” and she had a history of raiding others’ lunches when left unattended. “Hey, Olivia, you haven’t seen my sandwich, have you?” Sam asked, trying to sound casual.

Olivia looked up from her computer, her face betraying no guilt. “Your sandwich? No, I haven’t touched it. Did you check the fridge? Maybe someone thought you were done with it and put it away.”

Olivia’s suggestion seemed reasonable. Sam thanked her and headed toward the office fridge. It was stocked with various containers of leftovers, yogurt cups, and an assortment of beverages. But no sandwich. Not his, anyway.

Sam frowned. If it wasn’t Olivia, and it wasn’t in the fridge, where could it have gone?

The Chief Suspects

The next logical step was to consult the office’s most notorious food thieves: Jack and Mark. These two, often seen in the break room chatting and sharing conspiracy theories, had long been suspected of pilfering food left unattended. They were always around, always talking, and always ready to make light of anything—especially food theft.

Sam approached them as they stood near the water cooler. “Hey, guys,” Sam said, trying to keep his tone light, “have either of you seen my sandwich? It’s gone.”

Jack, ever the prankster, smirked. “Your sandwich? No idea, man. But it wouldn’t surprise me if someone grabbed it. The food around here is like a black hole—once it’s gone, it’s gone.”

Mark added, “Yeah, the real mystery is how the office fridge is always so full, yet stuff keeps disappearing. Maybe it’s the ghost of lunch past.”

Sam’s frustration was beginning to mount. The two were clearly unhelpful, and while they didn’t seem guilty, their casual attitude wasn’t helping matters. It was time to step up his investigation.

The Break Room Security Camera

The office break room, a small and cozy space with a microwave, a few tables, and a refrigerator, had been a place of comfort for many employees over the years. It was also where Sam had last seen his sandwich. The room was equipped with a security camera, installed after a few incidents of mysteriously disappearing snacks in the past. This was his next stop.

Sam made his way to the security office, where he found Tony, the office manager, sitting at his desk, sipping his coffee and flipping through his emails. Tony was a man of few words but had a sharp eye for detail, which made him the go-to person for all things related to office surveillance.

“Tony,” Sam said, his voice betraying a hint of urgency, “I need you to check the footage from the break room. My sandwich is missing, and I think someone took it.”

Tony looked up from his screen, his expression unreadable. “The sandwich, huh? I’ve heard it all before. Let me pull up the footage.”

A few clicks later, Tony had the footage on the screen. The small room was visible from the camera’s angle, and everything seemed normal at first. Employees came and went, the microwave beeped occasionally, and the fridge door opened and closed. But then, something strange caught Sam’s eye.

At precisely 10:32 AM, a figure walked into the break room. It was Emma, the office’s quiet and somewhat eccentric graphic designer. She walked over to the fridge, opened it, and then paused. Her gaze shifted to the counter where Sam’s sandwich had been just moments earlier.

Sam’s heart skipped a beat. Emma hadn’t noticed the camera. She reached over, picked up the sandwich, and placed it into her tote bag. Then, without a word, she left the room.

Confronting Emma

Sam’s mind raced. Emma? The quiet one? It didn’t seem possible. He had never suspected her—she was always so reserved, so focused on her work. But the footage didn’t lie.

He took a deep breath and walked to Emma’s desk. She was seated, her headphones on, completely absorbed in her computer screen. Sam hesitated for a moment, unsure of how to approach the situation. But the mystery of the missing sandwich had gone on long enough.

“Emma,” Sam said softly, trying to remain calm. Emma slowly removed her headphones and looked up, her eyes widening slightly when she saw Sam standing there.

“Sam, what’s up?” she asked, her voice warm but tinged with a hint of nervousness.

“I saw the footage from the break room,” Sam said, leaning against her desk. “You took my sandwich.”

Emma’s face drained of color, and she looked at her tote bag, as if she were trying to recall a distant memory. “I… I did?” she asked, her voice barely above a whisper.

Sam nodded, trying to suppress his anger. “Yes, Emma. You took my sandwich. Why?”

Emma stood up slowly, her hands trembling slightly. “I… I’m sorry,” she said, her eyes filling with guilt. “I didn’t mean to take it. I thought it was mine.”

Sam blinked. “You thought it was yours?”

Emma nodded. “I’ve been feeling a bit distracted lately. I’ve had so much on my mind—work, personal stuff—and when I saw the sandwich, I didn’t really think. I thought it was my lunch. I wasn’t paying attention.”

Sam was taken aback. “So, you didn’t steal it on purpose?”

“No,” Emma replied softly, her cheeks flushing with embarrassment. “I never intended to take it from you. It was an honest mistake, I swear.”

The Resolution

Sam stood there for a moment, contemplating the situation. The mystery had been solved, and yet, something about it felt strangely anticlimactic. No grand conspiracy. No elaborate plot. Just a simple mistake born from stress and distraction.

“I guess I can understand,” Sam said, his voice softening. “I’ve had days where my mind’s been all over the place too. But next time, maybe double-check your sandwich before taking it?”

Emma nodded quickly, her face bright red. “Absolutely. I’m really sorry, Sam. I’ll make it up to you. I promise.”

Sam smiled, relieved that the situation wasn’t as sinister as he had imagined. “No worries. Just… try not to take my lunch again, okay?”

Emma laughed nervously. “Deal.”

As Sam walked back to his desk, he couldn’t help but chuckle to himself. The great sandwich mystery had come to an end. The missing tuna and avocado creation had been found, and Emma’s apology was more than enough to resolve the situation. It wasn’t a grand crime, nor a great conspiracy. It was simply a case of a distracted mind and a misplaced lunch.

But Sam’s sandwich would live on in the annals of office legend, a story to tell in the break room for years to come. And perhaps, just perhaps, he would make sure to label his lunch more carefully next time.

After all, the world of office snacks could be a dangerous place.

The End.
