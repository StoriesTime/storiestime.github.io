Between Realities: The Portal of Choices


Arden had always been drawn to the peculiar. While others turned away from the unknown, he sought it, no matter how strange or unsettling. Perhaps that’s why, on the evening of his 30th birthday, he found himself wandering deep within the Ash Veil Forest—a place renowned for its eerie quiet and bizarre tales of disappearing travelers. For Arden, mystery wasn’t a warning; it was an invitation.

The sun dipped below the horizon, its amber glow fading into the ink-colored night as Arden pushed forward. He hadn’t planned his adventure. He didn’t even bring a flashlight. But something deep within guided him—an inexplicable pull toward a place he didn’t yet understand. That’s when he saw it.

Nestled among the gnarled roots of the oldest tree he’d ever seen was a doorway. Not a physical structure, but a shimmering tear in the fabric of the world. It pulsated, faintly glowing with hues of gold and sapphire. It was the kind of sight he’d only dreamt of encountering—something impossible yet undeniably real.

Arden hesitated, his breath catching in his throat. He crouched in front of the portal, studying its surface, which seemed to ripple like water disturbed by the wind. Whispers flitted around him, too faint to understand but loud enough to make his heart race. He couldn’t explain why, but he felt this was no ordinary doorway. It wasn’t created by nature or any human hand. It was something… more.

“What are you?” he murmured, reaching out to touch the shimmering surface. The moment his fingers brushed against it, the world around him vanished.

Arden found himself standing in an enormous marble chamber—its walls etched with runes that seemed to change to match his thoughts every time he looked away. Light didn’t come from any visible source but bathed everything in a diffused glow. In the center of the room stood a pedestal, and atop it lay a golden key.

Before he could move toward the pedestal, a voice resonated through the chamber. It wasn’t harsh or sharp, but melodic, like the chorus of a thousand voices merging into one.

“Welcome, seeker.”

Arden spun around, but he saw no one. The voice continued. “You have found the Portal of Choices—the gateway between realities. Few are called, and fewer still have the courage to answer. Are you ready to choose?”

“Choose what?” Arden asked cautiously, his voice echoing back at him. “Who are you?”

“I am the steward of this place,” the voice replied. “What I am matters less than what you will decide. Through this portal, endless realities await. Each choice reshapes existence—not just your own, but the very fabric of worlds.”

The pedestal glowed brighter, the key gleaming as though urging him forward. Arden wanted to ask more questions, but his curiosity outweighed his caution. He stepped closer, his footsteps strangely silent against the marble floor. As he touched the key, an avalanche of visions cascaded through his mind.

The first vision showed his life as it was: ordinary yet safe. A warm apartment in the city, a mundane desk job, and occasional adventures chasing mysteries. He saw himself growing old, content yet unremarkable, his legacy fading into obscurity. This was the reality he knew—and the one he could remain in.

The second vision was more terrifying. He saw himself as a warrior in a chaotic realm where storms raged endlessly. He wielded power beyond imagination but bore the marks of countless battles. His days were consumed in conflict, shaping a broken world into something resembling peace. It was a life of pain and grandeur, alive with fire yet scarred with suffering.

A third vision emerged—not soothing, not violent, but peculiar. Arden stood as a wanderer in a vibrant realm where time bent at his will. Colors were brighter; sounds were tangible; he spoke to creatures and beings beyond comprehension. This existence was limitless in possibility but unstable, always on the brink of unraveling into chaos.

Arden gasped, releasing the key as the visions faded. His heart pounded, sweat dripping from his temples despite the chamber’s cool air.

“These are the paths before you,” the voice said, softer now, yet no less commanding. “The Portal grants you the power to shift realities—each choice irrevocable, each branching into infinite futures. Once the key turns, you cannot return.”

Arden’s knees trembled but he remained standing. He’d often daydreamed of being someone extraordinary, escaping the monotony of his current life. But the weight of these choices was unbearable. Each vision called to him, pulling him in opposite directions.

“Why me?” he whispered, unsure if he was asking the voice or himself.

“All seekers arrive with questions. Some crave escape; others pursue destiny. The Portal does not judge ambition—it merely offers what you seek.”

Arden looked down at the empty pedestal and realized the key had disappeared. Instead, in its place, three identical golden doors now stood before him—one for each reality from the visions.

His breathing quickened. He thought of family and friends back home, ordinary moments that felt extraordinary within their simplicity. He thought of the thrill of wielding power in a realm of warriors, of bending the very fabric of time in infinite worlds. Who was he meant to be?

“Choose,” the voice pressed gently, as though sensing his hesitation. “It is not bravery to make the safest choice, nor is it wisdom to chase danger. There is no right answer—only the truth of what beckons your soul.”

Arden stepped forward, his mind silent at last. In that moment, it wasn’t logic or fear or ambition guiding him—it was instinct. He closed his eyes, placed his hand on the door, and pushed.

The world shifted painfully fast. Arden’s body felt stretched thin and weightless, as though he were unraveling into threads while assembling anew. When he opened his eyes, he found himself standing in a meadow bursting with luminous flowers unlike anything he’d seen before. The air shimmered as if alive, and the sky swirled with hues that defied description.

“What is this place?” he whispered to himself.

A creature appeared—tall, elegant, with glowing eyes and wings that sparkled like gemstones. Its voice was musical and kind. “Welcome home, seeker. You chose the path of wandering creation. Here, you shape this world and live outside the confines of time.”

Arden felt a profound sense of relief. Though he’d left everything familiar, this realm felt right. It was the perfect blend of adventure and purpose—a life eternally unfolding yet full of connection.

And so, Arden began his new life in a reality born of choices, free to explore the endless bounds of existence… between realities.
