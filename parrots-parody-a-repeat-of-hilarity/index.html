Parrot's Parody: A Repeat of Hilarity


Once upon a time in a quaint little village, nestled amongst verdant hills, resided a peculiar parrot named Polly. She was no ordinary feathered friend; Polly had a knack for mimicry and an uncanny sense of humor. Her laughter-induced squeaks and cackles echoed through the village, making everyone question if she was the secret behind their town’s enduring happiness.

Polly’s days were as mundane as they could get for a parrot - she’d wake up with the first glimpse of sunlight, peck some seeds for breakfast, and spend the rest of her day imitating villagers and echoing their laughter. One morning, however, the sun revealed a new addition to the village - a magician named Harry, who had just set up his circus tent on the edge of the village. With a flourish of his cape and a graceful bow, Harry announced to the enchanting village, ‘Welcome, dear folks, to the greatest magic show you’ll ever witness!’ The villagers, intrigued, formed a line to the circus tent as if they were a string of pearls.

Polly, desperate to escape the shackles of her repetitive routine, flew over to the circus tent. As she made her grand entrance, she found herself greeted by a cacophony of scuffles and clatters from the animals in the circus troupe, an orchestra of unfamiliar sounds. The monkeys chattered amongst themselves whilst the elephants trumpeted boisterously. Amid the clamor, Polly spotted Harry’s assistant, a turtledove named Tina, and they exchanged knowing glances.

As Harry began his mesmerizing act, Polly fluttered around the colorful circus tent, mimicking its enchanting sounds and Harry’s voice. The audience and deafening silence from the villagers engrossed their attention in the magician’s every move. His concluding act was a glamorous illusion: a live parrot vanishing into thin air, only to reappear behind Harry during the curtain call. Cunning Harry, seizing the opportunity, transformed the spotlight onto Polly and exclaimed dramatically, ‘Behold! Magic by my very own Polly, the vanishing parrot!’ The audience burst into thunderous applause.

Infatuated with his newfound fame, Harry invited Polly to join his animal circus and perform each day. With her gift for mimicry and inherent sense of comedy, a life of performing flocked into Polly’s horizon. Polly’s new shows were designed to amuse, wilt her wings in laughter, and never fail to leave a wrinkle of mirth in the hearts of all who listened. At night, she would return to her village, transformed by the shimmer of the spotlight, and recount her tales to Tina by the moon.

However, an unusual squirrel entered the village one fateful night. Sly and scoundrel, his bushy tail gave him away as the notorious thief who had stolen the village’s most cherished artifacts, the wind chimes. Overwhelmed with fear and frustration, the villagers urged each of their animals to track him down, including their once-cherished Polly.

No sooner had the search begun, Polly, lead by her desire to prove her worth to her village and get the wind chimes back, set off into the darkness. Along with her, hurried Tina the turtledove, and a troop of volunteers, each an expert in their own ways - Harold the hopping frog, Billy the snappy turtle, and a host of nimble squirrels. They journeyed far into the woods, into the land uncharted by humans or animals, and ambushed Sly the thief.

In an extraordinary feat of teamwork, the animal heroes outsmarted Sly, and Polly managed to free the stolen wind chimes from the sack of his bushy tail. In their victory, the woodland circle resounded with their jubilance divided as day and night.

Back in the village, Harry was mistaken by a smaller-sized baby elephant and led to believe that Polly had deserted their act. In his exasperation, Harry screeched, ‘Fickle Polly has abandoned me! The parrot who vanished me shall vanish herself!’ Laughing genuinely for once, he twirled his cape around himself and allowed his laughter to resonate in the empty tents, before realizing that Polly had played him instead.

In the stream of coined words and laughter that floated through the streets, the village architects erected a pedestal from the wood of the hunted tree. In its honor, it was named ‘Polly’s Pavilion.’ A place for laughter and merriment, Polly’s Pavilion brought all of the circus’s troupe of eccentrics and outcast creatures together, as they gathered to share stories of their escapades and revisit memories of their daring heroics. Polly and Tina took on the roles of the village’s historians, recounting their wild adventure with Sly and the victory of their quest.

From then on, Polly’s fame eliminated the edge of her world. No longer just a parrot of the village, she became a symphony of mirth and a beacon of hilarity resonated in her laughter. She’d lead the procession to Polly’s Pavilion full of tourists and travelers eager to hear stories of Polly’s Parody: A Repeat of Hilarity. A life, she found, she was destined to live.
