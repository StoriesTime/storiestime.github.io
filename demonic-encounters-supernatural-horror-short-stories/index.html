Demonic Encounters: Supernatural Horror Short Stories


The night the Hollow whispered, everyone in the town of Briarswood knew the air would carry weight — a sinister heaviness, as if the darkness itself was alive and grasping. Few dared venture outside once the sun sank below the twisted treetops, and those who did spoke in hushed tones of strange phenomena: flickering lights where none should be, chilling howls slicing through the silence, and then, the disappearances.

Camryn was an outsider, a writer drawn to Briarswood by its eerie reputation and the promise of untapped stories nestled within its fog-drenched streets. She arrived just days before Samhain, the ancient Celtic festival when the veil between worlds thins. Local lore spoke of demonic forces beckoning from the shadows, preying on the unwary. What Camryn found, though, exceeded the usual ghost stories and local myth.

Her first night in a creaky old inn on the town’s edge was uneventful—or so she thought. From her window, the moonlight splintered through knotted branches, casting skeletal patterns on the walls. As the wind moaned, a sudden gust slammed the door shut, plunging her into darkness. Then came the tapping. Knocks at the window—soft, rhythmic, but urgent.

She brushed it off to an animal or prankster until a voice whispered, barely audible, “Help me.” The sensation that something unseen pressed close sent a shiver down her spine.

The next day, determined to unearth what lurked beneath Briarswood’s surface, Camryn visited the town library, a dusty, labyrinthine place where ancient tomes and brittle pages told stories that modern ears had grown too skeptical to hear. An elderly librarian named Mrs. Halbrook eyed her warily but eventually handed over a leather-bound journal belonging to Ezekiel Myles, a 19th-century hunter of supernatural creatures.

Camryn pored over Ezekiel’s words by candlelight that night:

“The Hollow is a doorway, a breach in the fabric where demons clutch at our world. They twist reality, feeding on fear and despair. The only defense is to recognize their presence before they claim your soul.”

Camryn’s skepticism wavered the deeper she read. Ezekiel chronicled encounters with demons—shape-shifting entities cloaked in shadows, with eyes like molten gold and voices that lured victims into the night. The darkest passage warned of the Wraith of Briarswood Hollow, an ancient demon bound beneath the roots of the oldest oak tree, said to awaken each Samhain.

Compelled by curiosity and a creeping dread, Camryn set out at dusk to find the tree. The forest swallowed her as she walked deeper, branches clawing at her coat, the undergrowth crunching underfoot. The air grew thick, heavy with the musk of decay and something fouler—a sulfurous tang like burnt feathers.

Her heart hammered as she reached a clearing dominated by a colossal oak, its gnarled roots sprawling like veins across the earth. The wind died; silence fell like a shroud. Then the ground trembled.

From the shadows beneath the oak, eyes glowed, piercing through the gloom. The Wraith emerged—a towering figure composed of swirling darkness and flickering embers. Its voice was a rasping growl that seemed to echo inside her skull.

“You awaken what should sleep,” it hissed. “Your soul, a beacon; your fear, my feast.”

Camryn stumbled back, clutching the journal. She remembered Ezekiel’s words—a prayer of binding, incantations of light and truth that could repel the demon. Desperation lent her courage as she spoke aloud the ancient verses, voice trembling but resolute.

The Wraith writhed, its form unraveling like smoke caught in a storm. A scream echoed as the darkness convulsed, chased back beneath the roots. The forest exhaled; the chilling mist lifted.

Morning light spilled over Briarswood, the Hollow quiet, still. Camryn returned to town, changed. She understood now the fragile threshold between worlds and the darkness lurking just beneath the calm. The demonic shadows of Briarswood had made their claim, but they were not invincible.

Her story would be told—not just as a tale of horror, but as a testament to the power of courage amid the night’s consuming dread. The Supernatural had touched her life, but it would not define her fate. Instead, she became its chronicler, guardian of secrets whispered beneath the hollow moonlight.

The echoes remain, though—waiting for the dark to return, for shadows to stir, and for those who listen too closely to hear the Hollow’s sinister call.
