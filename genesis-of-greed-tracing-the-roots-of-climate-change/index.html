Genesis of Greed: Tracing the Roots of Climate Change


Once upon a time, in a leafy, vivacious corner of the world, a quaint hamlet nestled in the embrace of the verdant forests. Whispers of the prehistoric echoed in every turning leaf, tales of a boundless tranquility long silenced by the humdrum affairs of mortal man. This place was deeply entangled in the web of life, a pulsating jewel that cradled the nascent roots of an affliction that would troublize the remaining years of mankind: the birth of greed.

Among the villagers, there lived a prodigious craftsman, the renown Hieronymus Hewer. Hewer was a sculptor of unparalleled finesse, whose one-of-a-kind corals and vines had been garnished by seafarers for the courts of exotic emperors and queens. His unique artistry and inventiveness had fashioned of him more than just an artist, he’d become a driving force in the community, shaping a generation with his boundless ambition and fire for innovation.

One fateful day, in his secluded chamber, Hewer/entity> was absorbed in the chiseling of what he believed to be his magnum opus. As the furrows of the ancient stone’s surface unraveled before his intense gaze, he found himself drawn into the forbidding depths of the earth. An all-consuming sensation of the past swept him away to a time when the world extended its arms to the depths of the cosmic ether, a time before man encumbered its very breath.

Amidst his reverie, the sonorous murmur of the ancestral spirit whispered of a time million years ago, when the colossal creature, Mmulodon, roamed the verdant moors at the time when they were in the prime of their splendor. Hewer felt the reverence of nature, and the unfettered beauty of the natural world awakened in his breast a creature long dormant - greed.

The hunger for everlasting glory engulfed Hewer, driving him to create a colossal statue, an homage to the ageless bond between the forest and its sentient caretaker, humanity, and himself. But to bring his vision to fruition, he would need money sufficient for the materials, the wages of artisans, and bribes for the corrupt officials that lurked within the kingdom’s bowels.

Steeped in doubt, the torrid winds within the ever-encroaching darkness of the depths cast their shadow over Mmulodon’s once glistening grasses, soon drawing a veil upon the richness of the region. A transformation that, to his bewilderment, both brought forth a new spirit of prosperity to his village whilst tainting it with an insidious splinter - greed.

As the village’s stone homes expanded into booming with lofty towers and glittering towers, the idyllic village receded and darkened. The air was thick with acrid smoke, and the gossamer threads of friendship were cast aside in the ceaseless race for wealth. Trees once lush and green licked away at the melancholic remnants of nature’s song.

As years crept by, the obsidian chessboard of life soon unfurled its fan, and an invisible hand - ruthless, unyielding - asphyxiated the world. The escalating clandestine wheel of progress gorged upon a gluttonous appetite, diluted into the oils of petrol, woven into the sinews of steel, and solidified as gold. With every insatiable beat of the greedy heart, the world plunged into the abyss, a chasm from which it would never return. And in its wake, the elusive miasma of greed permeated every particulate of life: from the fish at the bottom of the ocean to the groaning continent above.

The great Hewer, shunned in his final days, perched on a mountaintop, whispered his parting words into the wind:

“Do not seek for an infallible straight line, rather heed the subtle currents beneath your soles, for it may guide you toward the intersection of abundance and frugality, for therein lies the cosmos’ true harmony.”

His body was buried atop of the pinnacle of greed, a testament to a world that once was.

Years simmered into centuries, millennia, and the eviscerated trunk of Mmulodon lay untroubled by the hands of man, except for the occasional insipid visitor, their greedy hearts yearning for a share of the wealth and information sucked dry by the teeth of progress.

The sun, as relentless as the phoenix, was setting on its path of immolation and embraced the earth as it moaned, imparting to humanity its last breath. To traverse through the steam of sorrowful banks of clouds and glimpse the fire of the dawn, one must, ultimately, trace the roots of climate change to an ancient craftman’s hunger for everlasting glory in the quaint, now vanished, village of his birth.
