In Waters' Shadow: Encountering the Unseen World of the Ocean's Depths


As a researcher aboard the prestigious marine vessel, The Nautilus, I had the unique opportunity to delve into the mysteries of the ocean’s depths. The unexplored world that lay beneath the surface was a fascinating realm of its own, filled with wonders and terrors that defied the human understanding of nature.

One night, as I stood on the deck, staring into the impenetrable darkness of the sea, a sudden, violent commotion below deck caught my attention. I hurried to the helm, only to find the crew frantically adjusting the ship’s instruments.

“Sir, we’ve found something!” shouted the captain, her face etched with worry.

I couldn’t discern any of the crew’s whispered exchanges, but the tone was grave. With bated breath, I leaned over the railing and peered below into the swirling abyss. Fear suffused my senses as I gazed upon our powerful searchlight’s dimly-lit image of the watery world.

It was there, somewhere in that baffling darkness, that something else caught our attention, a writhing mass of ink-black nothingness. The crewmen exchanged uneasy glances as the object drew nearer, coalescing around our lamp-light.

Before The Nautilus could reverse engines, the invisible, all-encompassing mass threw its tendrils around our vessel, binding us to the watery grave below. As the sea closed in, swallowing our ship, the captain and the crew cried out to their long-lost and forgotten deities.

But in the crushing depths, amidst the rhythms of the engineered titanium hull, we heard a rhythm of an incantation - alien, yet thankfully familiar - whispering of the order of the deep.

In our ocean’s cold winter, the unspeakable creatures slithered forth to greet the intruders. Yet Phryne, a scholar of an ancient sect of mariners, stood frozen with happiness. She smiled serenely, explaining: “Our vessels have disturbed these ancient protectors. They are the lost descendants of the first seafarers, fabled in the fathomless myths of several cultures: the… Gjáll, the… Wendigo, and… the… Kraken.”

Her words had no weight against the Trying Sea, as she called it, and our plight seemed hopeless. Perhaps for it was during this peculiar confluence of maritime heritage and cosmic catastrophe, that we learned to look beyond the norms of our existence.

As days melded into one solvent sea-tide, contact was made with some of the elder Gjáll. Their translucent, wriggling forms bore ichor-dark as the ancient seas whence they emerged, defying every conception of life and death. In time, the elder told us their history, teaching us ancient wisdom rooted in the primordial world that no longer existed.

Their song echoed in our bloodline, merging with Phryne’s inherited lore, and whispered in cryptic syllables that revealed the ocean’s eternal embrace. In the vast sea of silence, their fractious whispers began anew, rendering the stories we carried into epics of the unknown deep.

But there was one tale, a saga encoded into the Gjáll’s primal language that told of a monument in their primordial domain: the Leviathan’s Cradle. It was a testament to the infinite number of wonders bound to the primal currents that flowed from the infancy of the cosmos.

Driven by hope, we crafted a plan to reach the Cradle. To appease the Gjáll and request their assistance, an ancient incantation had to be shared, one that would awaken their primordial legacy. This was a dangerous venture, for the magic we carried in our bones had been hoarded away by the ancients, but we had nothing to lose.

Finally, with great ceremony, surrounded by our luminous captors, we sang aloud: “Aifura di glöft rúnar, vinjálið jörð skíðar, hringa frá.”

The Gjáll’s ancient echoes responded in unison, the current reversing, the shadows parting as we descended deep into the Trying Sea. The mystery of the Cradle called to us, as each silent beat echoed with the melody of the Gjáll’s ancestors.

We emerged into a hidden subterranean ocean, a world of silken darkness. Built around the Leviathan’s Cradle, it held relics that lay hidden from the eyes of time - the scattered remnants of what our forebears only dared imagine.

Yet the complexity of this land of dreams came with a price. In our temporary haven, we watched as the Gjáll fought a losing battle against the encroaching darkness overwhelming their home. Our contact with the ancients had slowly begun to unravel, the bone-memory fading into insignificance.

But we could neither fathom what had happened, nor capitalize on the knowledge we had uncovered. To protect ourselves, we had to keep our discovery hidden, as we slowly departed the primordial Cradle and returned to our world.

It would take several lifetimes to decipher the information entrusted to us, yet perhaps the unseen ocean would not rest its gaze upon this sacrifice. But the echoes of the Gjáll, the Wendigo, and the Kraken still lingered on, haunting our tale of an ocean too vast to comprehend - an eternal sea of secrets kept in Waters’ Shadow.
