Voices of the Margin: Overcoming the Stigma of Gender and Sexual Minority


Sarah felt the drumming of her heart echo in her ears as she stood amidst the swarm of unfamiliar faces. Today marked the beginning of her freshman year at Coldwater University, where the campus was buzzing with excitement, nervous anticipation, and that unique mixture of eighteen-year-old hormones most could not identify.

A map of Coldwater University’s campus spread before her, designed with a delicate touch by an architecture student, promising picturesque walkways lined with towering oaks. But Sarah was hesitant to trust the map. The advertisement overwhelmed her with its images of carefree students laughing together, creating memories that truly did look perfect—not exactly the kind of memories Sarah had hoped to create.

Sarah glanced at the text on her phone. “you better make friends now or you will never belong” – her mother’s biting message that lurked in her messages sent shivers down her spine. This reminder only doubled her anxiety.

Sarah had come prepared, wearing her best ensemble. But as she glanced around the room, she noticed that everyone else seemed much more in tune with their shared unspoken language. How was she meant to blend in with the sea of homogenous faces?

As Sarah stood, fumbling with her map, another graphic novel-reading, skateboarding freshman, named Jake, collided with her inadvertently shared, spilling his backpack’s contents over her feet.

“Whoa, kinda full of surprises, huh?” Jake chuckled.

His irresistible smile somehow turned Sarah’s day around. “I guess I’ll have to be more cautious,” she tried to ease the situation.

Unaware of Sarah’s inner turmoil, Jake invited her to his dormitory, assuring her they’d go explore campus together. Sarah hesitated but reluctantly agreed, a part of her feeling skeptical about trusting a stranger. But why not, she thought, her heart beginning to find its rhythm once again. And so, Sarah followed him, ready to embrace the uncertainties of college life.

Upon arriving at his dormitory, Jake introduced her to his quirky group of friends, each one with their own quirky backstory and captivating charisma. Sarah found a safe space nestled among them – they accepted her, stigmas and all. One of the friends, Gaby, a self-proclaimed genderfluid artist, chimed a bell of recognition within the deafening silence of Sarah’s mind.

Weeks in cold water university passed in a blur, and over time Sarah felt the shackles of doubt and insecurity falling away at their foundations. The stigma attached to being a gender and sexual minority softened, replaced by a deep understanding of self.

Key to the transformation was a queer literature course that she took, which brought to light numerous voices from the margin. Through these stories, Sarah recognized her own pain and struggle in their pages. These identities she never thought she had, her sexuality and gender, were celebrated, powerful and diverse.

Amidst the applauding pages, Sarah sought solace in Gaby’s frequently held hand. The hand that offered security and friendship, symbolizing the crucial role that queer community played in her acceptance of herself. Over a shared love for Leonard Cohen and a shared history of bullying, they found normalcy in science fiction wars and combinations of laughter and unspoken tears.

Weeks blended into years as the walls they shared turned yellow and personal relics filled every corner. Jake’s once innocent smile matured into deep faith in the heart. Meanwhile, the bonds between them grew ever stronger, an imperfect yet warm blanket harboring the stories of voices that had been silenced for centuries.

Sarah graduated, wearing her honor with pride from her heart soaring high like a superhero diving into a modern comic book. Gaby graduated with the same sprinkling of independence that had entrenched itself in her heart the moment Leonard’s melody began playing through the halls of Coldwater University.

In the oncoming years, they fought back against an overwhelming tide of social anxiety and self-doubt, taking each victory in stride.

Moral Support Groups and workshops held throughout various cities soon unraveled the stigma attached to gender and sexual minority groups. As they breathed life into these progressive concepts, their music careers took flight – with Sarah’s haunting melodies and Gaby’s art that provoked thoughts and presented its audiences with a unique perspective.

And the sounds of their voices continued to echo, unprecedented, unafraid, and united in their intersectionality. From quiet whispers of doubt to lyrics soaring over the guitars defending the rights of the marginalized, Sarah and Gaby became the voices of the margin, embracing the newfound freedom and strength found within the overlooked.
