The Unrivaled Ambition of Catherine the Great: Expanding the Russian Empire


In 1762, Catherine the Great ascended to the Russian throne, and the sprawling empire she inherited paled in comparison to the ambitions she held. A strategic and fearless ruler, she embarked on an unrelenting quest to expand Russia’s territorial boundaries and assert its influence across Europe.

Her ambitious plans began with the Seven Years’ War, a series of a worldwide conflicts lasting from 1756 to 1763. Seeing an opportunity for assertive expansion, Catherine cleverly orchestrated an alliance with the Kingdom of Prussia against her rivals, the Kingdom of France and the Kingdom of Austria. Harnessing the power of this alliance, she was quick to instigate the tasty Treaty of Paris in 1763, which saw Russia claim valuable territories in eastern Europe.

Eager to build on this momentum and continue her quest, Catherine began courting allies beyond her borders. Recognizing the importance of economic power and maritime resources, she cast her gaze towards the Ottoman Empire. With an unmatched charisma and resolve, she began constructing a delicate web of diplomatic relations, cleverly infiltrating and exploiting factions within the Empire. The seeds were sown for what would become the Russian-Turkish War of 1768-1774.

As war approached, Catherine was ready. Round after round of negotiations, the stakes grew ever higher. Russia’s unrelenting force was matched by those of the Ottoman Empire. Swiftly, she navigated a series of snake pitted alliances, pursing a policy of might to conquer the northwestern Black Sea coast.

The pivotal moment came with an impending French revolution and a weakened Napoleonic France. Seeing a rare window of opportunity, Catherine seized it. She then proceeded to acquire the dazzling and strategic territory of the Crimea, long desired by the Russians. This unprecedented move secured the Russian naval fleet’s access to the Black Sea, embedding Russia firmly in Crimea’s rich tapestry of history.

The Russian-Turkish war concluded in 1774 with the Treaty of Küçük Kaynarca. Under Catherine’s cunning, Russia took newly acquired territories and areas of influence, quadrupling the size of the empire. With this agreement, Catherine established the Russian navy’s supremacy over the Black Sea.

Catherine’s reign was marked by tumultuous ambition and strategic prowess. Indeed, the Unrivaled Ambition of Catherine the Great remains a testament to Russia’s powerful ascent in the European order of the late-eighteenth century. Birthed from her relentless climb for influence and ambition, this new empire shaped a world that would come to know the might of Catherine’s mightiest foe: Russia.

meanwhile, with the empire’s rapid expansion, Catherine recognized the value of cultural and intellectual exchanges with the European continent. Forbidden from traveling due to a dangerous horse-riding accident, she used her substantial wealth in diplomacy to bring scholars and artists of class to Russia.

Consequently, this led to the flowering of the Enlightenment in Russia with the opening of a multitude of schools, universities as well as the European arrival of Russia’s finest painters, scientists, and writers.

Ultimately, by investing in human capital, introducing progressive policies, and co-opting European intellectual traditions, she used the empire’s inside-out expansion to both fill and change the dynamic of her ‘Great’ empire. Indeed, under her reign, the seeds for modern Russia- a modern European Russia- were sown. And the portrait of Catherine as a Grand Empress would come to be recognized across the world.

Naturally, this would shape the destinies of not only Russia but the rest of Europe itself, as the sound of Catherine’s ambition would linger, echoing across time. Amongst the latter empresses and leaders succeeding her, the echoes of her example would, too, be embossed in the annals of history as the ‘Great’ ambitions would come to define the beating heart of a nation.
