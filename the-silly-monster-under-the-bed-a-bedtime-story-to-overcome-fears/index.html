The Silly Monster Under the Bed: A Bedtime Story to Overcome Fears

3 min · 562 words

It was a dark and stormy night, and little Timmy was lying in bed, wide awake. He had been trying to fall asleep for what felt like hours, but every time he started to drift off, he would hear a strange noise under his bed. At first, he thought it was just the wind or the creaks and groans of the old house, but as the sounds grew louder and more frequent, Timmy began to feel a sense of unease.

Suddenly, a furry, green creature with big, round eyes and a goofy grin poked its head out from under the bed. Timmy let out a loud scream and sat up straight, his heart racing with fear.

“Who are you?” Timmy demanded, trying to sound brave despite his shaking voice.

The monster looked at Timmy with a puzzled expression. “Who, me?” it repeated, its voice sounding like a cross between a growl and a giggle. “I’m just a silly monster, living under your bed. I’ve been watching you, and I must say, you’re a very interesting human.”

Timmy’s fear began to give way to curiosity. “What do you mean?” he asked, his voice a little softer now.

The monster chuckled. “Well, I’ve been observing you for a while now, and I must say, you’re a very brave human. You’re not afraid of the dark, and you’re not afraid of the strange noises under your bed. You’re a very special human, and I think you could use a friend like me.”

Timmy thought about this for a moment. He had never really thought about having a monster as a friend before, but the more he thought about it, the more it seemed like a great idea. After all, having a monster under his bed could be a lot of fun.

“Okay,” Timmy said finally. “I’ll be your friend. But you have to promise me one thing.”

“What’s that?” the monster asked, its eyes sparkling with curiosity.

“You have to promise me that you won’t scare me anymore,” Timmy said, his voice firm but polite.

The monster grinned. “I promise,” it said. “I’ll never scare you again. In fact, I’ll do everything I can to make sure you feel safe and happy in your bed.”

And with that, Timmy and the silly monster under his bed became the best of friends. They would have long conversations every night, and the monster would tell Timmy all sorts of silly jokes and stories. Timmy would laugh and giggle, and he would feel happy and safe in his bed.

As the days went by, Timmy began to realize that the silly monster under his bed was more than just a friend - it was a protector. The monster would keep an eye out for Timmy, making sure that he was safe and sound in his bed. And if Timmy ever had a bad dream or woke up feeling scared, the monster would be there to comfort him, telling him stories and jokes until he felt better.

One night, Timmy’s parents came into his room to check on him. They were surprised to see the silly monster sitting on the bed, surrounded by Timmy’s toys and books.

“Who is this?” Timmy’s mother asked, her voice a little surprised.

“This is my friend,” Timmy said, his voice full of pride. “He’s a silly monster, and he lives under my bed. He’s my protector, and he keeps me safe at night.”

Timmy’s parents looked at each other, and then back at Timmy. They could see that Timmy was happy and safe with the monster, and they knew that they had to accept the situation.

“Okay,” Timmy’s father said. “We’ll let you have your monster friend. But you have to promise us one thing.”

“What’s that?” Timmy asked, his voice curious.

“You have to promise us that you’ll always be brave and strong, even when the silly monster is around,” Timmy’s father said.

Timmy nodded, his heart full of determination. “I promise,” he said. “I’ll always be brave and strong, even when the silly monster is around.”

And with that, Timmy and the silly monster under his bed continued to live happily ever after, a reminder that even the most unlikely of friends can become the closest of companions.
