Her wicked stepmother, Victoria, plots to marry her spoiled daughters, Daphne and Sophia, off to Prince Edwin, the prince in search of a bride. Victoria's Motivations Victoria's resentment towards Cinderella stems from her late husband's infidelity, and now she seeks to secure the kingdom for her own daughters. Cinderella's existence is a constant reminder of Victoria's past heartaches. Despite her vivid beauty, Cinderella's destiny seems doomed from the start. Cinderella's Qualities Cinderella possesses a unique gift – she can heal the wounded and bring solace to the afflicted. However, her kindness and compassion are met with disdain by her stepmother and stepsisters. They see her as a threat to their own power and status. The Ball When Prince Edwin announces his plans to hold a grand ball, Victoria sees an opportunity to secure a suitable alliance for her daughters. Cinderella, however, is prohibited from attending the ball, as it is defined as a women's exclusive event. Undeterred, Cinderella finds a way to attend the ball, albeit incognito. The Glass Slipper As fate would have it, Cinderella and Prince Edwin meet at the ball, and a connection is formed. However, when the prince discovers Cinderella's true identity, he is reluctant to pursue a relationship due to her inferior social status. A Twist in the Tale Cinderella's stepsisters, Daphne and Sophia, are not what they seem. They are, in fact, agents of a secret society aimed at overthrowing the monarchy. Their true intentions are revealed when they attempt to use Cinderella as leverage to manipulate Prince Edwin. A New Ending In a surprising turn of events, Cinderella forgives her stepmother and stepsisters, but not before they acknowledge their sinful ways. The prince, impressed by Cinderella's integrity and compassion, proposes a new arrangement – one that prioritizes justice and equality over social hierarchy. A New Beginning Cinderella and Prince Edwin's union becomes a beacon of hope for the kingdom. Together, they work towards creating a more just and equitable society, one that values kindness, compassion, and empathy above all else. The story concludes with Cinderella and Prince Edwin ruling Ashwood, their bond stronger than any glass slipper could ever be.
