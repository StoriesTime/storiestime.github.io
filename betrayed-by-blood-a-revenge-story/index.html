Betrayed by Blood: A Revenge Story


It was a night cloaked in darkness, the moon barely a sliver in the sky, offering no light to the world below. In the small village of Berwick, the air was thick with tension, a sense of something irreversible in the air. The wind whispered through the trees, carrying with it the sound of muffled footsteps.

Ashton Merrick stood at the edge of the woods, his heart pounding with the weight of years of betrayal. His father had been the man who built the foundations of Berwick, a land rich in timber and coal, and his family’s name had once been synonymous with power and respect. But all that had changed a decade ago.

Ashton had always been close to his father, learning the art of leadership from him, understanding the delicate balance between power and kindness. He admired his father’s strength, his wisdom, and the way he commanded respect without asking for it. But that admiration had turned to ashes when he found out the truth. His father’s closest ally, his blood brother, had been the one to betray him, bringing about the destruction of the Merrick legacy.

It had started with a partnership — an alliance forged in the fires of business. Ashton’s father, Alden Merrick, had entrusted much of his wealth and land to his brother-in-arms, Lucas Greyson. Together, they had built the town’s prosperity, their names etched into the very history of Berwick. But as Ashton had learned in the most cruel and unexpected way, the partnership was nothing more than a façade, a carefully crafted lie.

The betrayal had been a brutal one. Lucas Greyson had been the one who orchestrated Alden Merrick’s fall from grace. He had manipulated the town’s leaders, twisted alliances, and driven wedges between once-loyal supporters. When the final blow came, it was Lucas who stood in the shadows, watching with cold indifference as his once-great friend was torn apart by his own mistakes, mistakes Lucas had orchestrated.

Ashton had been just a child at the time, too young to understand the intricacies of politics and power, but not too young to feel the sting of abandonment. His father had been imprisoned under false charges, his lands seized by those who had once sworn loyalty to the Merricks. His family’s fortune was plundered, and the legacy that had taken generations to build was left in ruins. And in the end, it was Lucas Greyson who had been the architect of it all.

Years passed, and Ashton grew into a man with a singular focus: revenge. He trained, learned, and built a network of allies. The young boy who once looked up to his father as a paragon of wisdom and strength now sought only one thing: to reclaim what was stolen from him and to make Lucas Greyson pay for the pain he had caused.

Tonight was the night. Ashton had tracked Lucas for months, piecing together the man’s every move. He had learned that Lucas, now a man of wealth and influence, still held sway in Berwick. His influence ran deep, corrupting those in power and silencing any who dared to oppose him. But Ashton had found a weakness. A single thread that, if pulled, would unravel everything.

The town of Berwick, once a place of pride, was now a shadow of its former self, ravaged by greed and betrayal. Lucas Greyson had turned the once-thriving village into his own personal fiefdom, using the very resources his partnership with Alden Merrick had built. The timber and coal that had once been the lifeblood of Berwick were now being funneled into Lucas’s coffers, and the people who once revered the Merricks now feared the very mention of the Greyson name.

Ashton had no illusions about the difficulty of the task ahead. He was not a man of law; he was a man of vengeance. He had no time for the courts or the whispers of justice. He needed to be swift, decisive. His hands were stained with the knowledge that there would be no turning back once he crossed this line.

He moved through the trees, silent and unseen, his eyes fixed on the manor where Lucas Greyson resided. The mansion, perched atop a hill, loomed like a dark specter over the town, its tall spires casting long shadows over the village below. The lights within flickered dimly, an illusion of life within the walls of death.

Ashton paused for a moment, staring at the silhouette of the mansion. His breath was shallow, his pulse quickening. The memories of his father, of the man who had been his idol, flooded his mind. He remembered Alden Merrick’s words, his last words before the world crumbled around them.

“You must protect the legacy, Ashton. And never forget, blood runs thicker than water. But it’s the bonds you choose that will define you.”

Those words echoed in Ashton’s mind, and for a moment, he hesitated. Was vengeance truly the right path? Could he reclaim the honor of his family through bloodshed? But then he remembered the pain, the lies, and the years spent in shadow. He remembered the face of Lucas Greyson, the man who had taken everything from him.

No. There was no turning back now.

With swift precision, Ashton scaled the walls of the mansion, moving like a shadow through the night. His training had made him into a ghost, undetectable and unstoppable. He found a window left ajar, its rusted hinges creaking as he slipped inside. The interior was grand, decorated with the trappings of wealth that Lucas had accumulated over the years. Yet, there was an emptiness to it, a hollowness that resonated with Ashton.

He crept through the halls, following the faint sound of footsteps. His eyes narrowed as he caught sight of a figure entering a study at the end of the corridor. It was Lucas Greyson, the man who had once been like an uncle to him, now the very face of the betrayal that had destroyed his life.

The air grew thick with tension as Ashton approached the study. He could hear Lucas’s voice, muffled through the thick wooden door. “Tonight, everything changes,” Lucas muttered to himself. “The last of the Merricks will fall. This town will finally be mine.”

Ashton’s heart pounded in his chest. He had come to end this, to put an end to the man who had ruined everything. Without a second thought, he pushed the door open, stepping into the room with cold resolve.

Lucas Greyson turned, his face twisting in surprise. “Ashton? Is it really you?”

The years had changed them both. Lucas’s once-charming face was now gaunt, his hair thinning, his eyes shadowed by years of manipulation and greed. But it was still the same man who had betrayed his father.

“You didn’t expect me, did you?” Ashton’s voice was a low growl, his hand clenched around the hilt of the knife he had carried for this moment. “I’ve come to take what you stole from me.”

Lucas took a step back, his face contorting with a mixture of fear and arrogance. “You think you can defeat me? You think you can undo what I’ve built? You’re nothing, Ashton. You’re just the son of a broken man.”

The words were meant to provoke, to push Ashton into a rash decision. But Ashton had learned to control his emotions. He had spent years planning this moment, and nothing would stop him now.

“No, Lucas,” Ashton said, his voice calm. “I’m the son of a man who was betrayed. And I’ve come to end this.”

In one swift motion, Ashton moved forward, striking with precision. The blade sank deep into Lucas’s side, the shock of the wound registering in his eyes before he collapsed to the ground.

Ashton stood over him, breathing heavily, his eyes cold and unyielding. “This is for my father,” he whispered, before turning and walking away, leaving Lucas Greyson to die in the darkness of his own making.

The betrayal had been avenged. The blood that had been spilled would never be undone, but Ashton had reclaimed something far more important: his honor, and the legacy of his family. The town of Berwick, though still under the shadow of corruption, would one day remember the name Merrick with reverence once more.

The vengeance had been sweet, but the cost had been high. The path of bloodshed was never an easy one, and as Ashton disappeared into the night, he knew that the journey ahead would not be simple. The town was still broken, and there were still those who had profited from the fall of the Merricks.

But for now, he had justice. And that, for the first time in a decade, was enough.
