The Unstoppable Rise: Unearthing the Story of Abstract Expressionism


In the hidden recesses of the New York art underworld, a forgotten collection lay undisturbed. Assembled in the aftermath of World War II, it housed relics of a global artistic quest for freedom and chaos. The “Unstoppable Rise” exhibit aimed to bring these works to the surface, unearthing the intimate and intricate world of Abstract Expressionism.

As the gallery director, Emily’s passion for art history pushed her beyond the boundaries of her university research. Eminently charming and fiercely knowledgeable, Emily was driven by the desire to explore the roots of this intriguing cultural movement.

To create the exhibit, Emily delved into obscure archives and contacted once-forgotten contemporaries of Abstract Expressionist giants like Pollock, de Kooning, and Rothko. Brimming with newfound information, she wove together narratives of artists couch-surfing through Europe, under the garagery of chaos.

The key figure in Emily’s research was Hans Hofmann, a German émigré who guided the Abstract Expressionists on their meteoric rise. Like a Mandarin wizard, he nurtured the collective talent in his NYC studio while guarding a dark secret. Yet, Emily’s journey to uncover the secret led her to ask questions about the true nature of Abstract Expressionism itself.

The real story began at the Provincetown Artist’s Colony, where Hofmann initially mingled with an eclectic mix of bohemian misfits. Among them was the eccentric Hanta Yo, a Polish émigré who claimed to have had an affair with a tarot card reader named Madame Roget. It was in a wretched upstairs room that Hanta Yo unveiled to Hofmann a bizarre arrangement of letters and symbols - the blueprint for an “Uralte Seherische Kunst.”

Hofmann was enthralled with this cryptic mystical imagery, which drew parallels to his grandmother’s rosicrucian folklore. Although these esoteric themes contradicted the prevalent narrative of the rise of Abstract Expressionism, Emily could not dismiss them outright. With biographical accounts of Hofmann’s past at her fingertips, she delved further into the complex, enigmatic man.

Emily traced Hofmann’s journey from Post-Impressionist circles in Munich and Paris to the intellectual climate of pre-Nazi Germany. Through her research, she forged a connection between the unsavory history of Abstract Expressionism and the enigmatic tarot cards.

What she discovered was not a new style but an elaborate deception. Far from a spontaneous artistic movement, Abstract Expressionism was conceived from the heart of a meticulously crafted con.
