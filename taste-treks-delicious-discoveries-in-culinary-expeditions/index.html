Taste Treks: Delicious Discoveries in Culinary Expeditions


Once upon a time, in a land faraway, there lived a young woman named Kora. She had a passion for flavors and cuisine, having grown up in a family that valued the culinary world. Her parents, both skilled chefs, instilled in her the belief that food was much more than sustenance; it was an experience, a journey capturing the essence of a culture, a region, and its people. Thus, it was no surprise when Kora decided to embark on a journey called ‘Taste Treks’ - a series of culinary expeditions that would take her taste buds to new heights.

Kora’s first stop was the exotic land of India, home to one of the richest and most diverse food cultures in the world. With her parents’ guidance and notes from their own experiences, Kora’s Taste Trek commenced in the bustling city of Delhi. Her first encounter was with the tantalizing streets of Chandni Chowk, which were a gastronome’s paradise. Kora savored the crispy samosas, rich Chhole Bhature, and the refreshing street-side chaat that perfectly juxtaposed spicy and sweet flavors.

Taking a pause from the hurrying crowd, Kora found solace in the old lanes of Paharganj, where gastronomic tradition met history. In the narrow alleys, she tasted the delights of authentic Mughlai cuisine, like the tender seekh kebabs and Shahi Tukray. A visit to a local spice market widened her knowledge of different flavors and spices, enlightening her palate with the aromas of freshly ground cinnamon, cardamom, and cloves.

As vibrant Delhi left an indelible mark on Kora’s taste buds, she soon boarded a train to the serene landscapes of Kerala. Here, in Kochi, she was introduced to the region’s seafood delicacies, from the tangy Karimeen powders to the delicate and flavorful Neer Dosa. A cookery class with a famed Keralite chef deepened her understanding of the region’s love for coconut and the art ofillardiyal, the traditional method of fermenting fish.

With each taste, Kora cherished the cultural imprint on the food, appreciating the deep-rooted stories of the Kerala farming communities in the flavors of Kerala’s staples like Appams and Payasam. But with each encounter, she was also learning the importance of simplicity and restraint in flavor profiling.

Kora’s Taste Trek culminated in the city of Thimphu, Bhutan – a landlocked nation boasting pristine landscapes and an untapped culinary culture. Her first experience was with Bhutanese dish Ema Datshi. Made of ripe and fiery chillies mixed with cheese, the dish challenged Kora’s spice tolerance, yet won her with its inherent Bhutanese boldness.

Intrigued by the unconventional flavor marriage, Kora took on the challenge of recreating Ema Datshi back home. But she was also grabbed by the suave Bhutanese tradition of using red rice, Buckwheat noodles, and the rice beer, Dzongkha Chha, that reminded her of her journeys’ memorable flavors.

With a heart full of culinary experiences, Kora returned home, compiling her journeys into a beautiful book, “Taste Treks: Delicious Discoveries in Culinary Expeditions.” Her book opened a new realm of flavors, a testament to the fact that eating is not simply nourishment, but an intricate experience of global cultures through taste.
