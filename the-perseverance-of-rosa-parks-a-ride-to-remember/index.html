The Perseverance of Rosa Parks: A Ride to Remember


Once upon a time, in the heart of Alabama, a young girl named Rosa Parks dreamed of an equal world. Growing up, she had witnessed the countless injustices faced by the African American community. However, she refused to let these injustices crush her spirit and reshape her dreams.

Like many others in her struggling neighborhood, Rosa was eager to find a way out of poverty. Her mother, a strong and determined woman, instilled in Rosa the importance of an education and the power it could hold. Rosa worked tirelessly to excel in her studies, determined to transform her life and those of her people.

One day, as she was walking home from her job at a local department store, Rosa’s life changed forever. The Montgomery City Bus Lines had implemented a “colored section” on their buses, separating Black and White passengers. While Rosa understood the frustration many tucked deep inside themselves, she was determined not to succumb to these petty rules and segregative directives.

On a yellowing bus one Thursday evening, Rosa felt a growing rage within her. The last row of the colored section was filled with standing passengers, and despite new passengers arriving, the bus driver failed to check for empty rows. Sensing an opportunity, she decided to stand up for her people.

Gathering her courage, Rosa refused to give up her seat, despite the driver’s furious glare. She was bound and determined to set an example of defiance that would echo throughout the African American community.

For days, the news of Rosa’s bold action spread like wildfire. Montgommery was one of many cities bubbling over with resistance to the mounting tension of supposed equality. Her actions had left an unforgettable symbol of the Civil Rights Movement.

Rosa was arrested for her bravery and fined $10, but her perseverance did not waver. Soon after, the events had sparked an organized bus boycott led by a young local preacher named Martin Luther King Jr. It continued for over a year, with meetings and protests leading the fight for equal treatment and opportunity.

And so, the world came together and Rosa become a symbol. Her name enlarged in textbooks, history lessons, revered as an icon of resistance and courage. But it is often forgotten that she was just a young girl once, standing on a bus in Alabama and refusing to be broken.

The Montgomery Bus Boycott finally ended after 13 months. In the wake of the inspiring and victorious events, Rosa’s life continued in a whirlwind of politics, laws and sweltering politics. She faced many challenges, but every time she fell, she pushed herself back up and continued her stride.

And while the memory of that fateful bus ride didn’t heal the struggles of African Americans effectively overnight, it did sow the seeds for change. It was the first domino to fall in America’s long march toward justice and equality, a crucial protest before they knew how deep they needed to dig.

Years later, standing before a foreign crowd, John Lewis of the Student Non-Violent Coordinating Committee, another hero of the Civil Rights Movement, noted the young Rosa Parks’ name in magical history. He told the crowd how she’d shaken the heart of a nation and how the whole world felt the justice in her quiet firmness, once you realize the game was up.

Her quiet strength fueled fundamental changes in our country’s unwritten law, and even more, her unshakable spirit ignited a flame within countless souls all over the globe.

Though her passing in 2005 marked the end of the life of a petite, frail woman in a wheelchair, the pervading memory of her unwavering fortitude remains alive and well, emboldened alongside the youth who speak of the future she dreamed: a world where “we will all be free.”

She tried to instill a lesson to never passively submit, no matter one’s station in life, and recall the torch she lit. As we go about our daily lives amidst noise and confusion, let us remember to be persistent as she, always cherishing the inheritance of our freedom.
