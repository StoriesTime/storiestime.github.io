Beyond the Rhetoric: Examining the Realities of Political Promises


Once upon a time, nestled neatly in the thick of countryside, lies a quaint village with a unique administrative system. Unlike neighboring villages, which all belonged to a central government, this village was an experiment in self-governance. Often, most village residents were politicians or former politicians who had grown disillusioned with the city life.

They established a code which was based on the practical issues of the land. Instead of ideological preaching, they worked to fulfill promises they made to their constituents. The economy of the village was a mix of agrarian and cottage industries with periodic visitors from the city investing in their businesses.

Now it was election time again, and the village was buzzing early bird chatter. Candidates from various political factions had started rallying their supporters to speak about their plans. It was a spectacle like any other political convention, filled with high hopes and promises. The candidates pledged to invest in new technologies to minimize soil erosion, promised to create a transport system that would connect the village to the city, and even proposed to diversify the cottage industries by providing grants for modern training facilities.

Amidst this hustle and bustle, a couple of leaders met in secret to discuss their strategies to win the election. Two of the most eminent, Quintus and Vitalus, were rivals typically representing two different factions, Left and Right. Surprisingly, they hashed out a deal: to work in tandem for mutual benefits and have a better chance of success in the election.

Back then, Quintus was a seasoned old man with a charming manner and a lot of charisma. He would often speak with a warm smile, wining his audience with a decent word play. On the other hand, Vitalus was known for his logic and perfected arguments to why his policies would make the most sense. His eloquence was undisputed, as he was once the toastmaster of a national debating competition.

During his campaign, Vitalus tossed a surprise to the public. Surplus rainfall during that season had reduced the soil quality. Local farmers struggled in vain against the erosion in broken landscapes, resulting in uneven height levels in the fields. Vitalus announced, “We propose to invest in a sustainable and automated irrigation system that would solve this issue.” The people of the village welcomed this proposal wholeheartedly, with the majority convinced that Vitalus had the better plan.

Quintus, who also loved the village as his firstborn, was planning his own joyful surprise as a reaction to that public show from Vitalus. He needed something grand that would not only reshape voter expectations but win more supporters from disillusioned Left-leaning voters who were tired of broken promises by their own faction. Then, Quintus had a breakthrough idea.

Quintus promised to construct a cutting-edge, high-speed transport tunnel connecting the village with the city. This tunnel would be fenestrated with skylights along its mile-long stretch, allowing fantastic views of the clouds and sunrises to everyone passing through. As Quintus knew,Â  such an endeavor would require a tremendous amount of funding, but it wouldn’t matter, as millions would have no choice but to invest once this project started!

His supporters instantly began rallying not only around Quintus but around this bold vision of progress. Finally, he captured the attention of a mysterious foreign investor who owned several similar projects around the continent. This association with this elite figure boosted Quintus’s popularity augmentation estimated to cost millions in the current political landscape.

The showdown was near, and both camps began to campaign harder as the day drew nearer. The surprising part, however, was that though the plans given by both Quintus and Vitalus sounded plausible, neither side discussed where they would find the substantial funding to realize these projects. Their focus was more on the front pages of the village paper, and that strategy looked like working.

As the voting day approached, the villagers began to ponder who to vote for: Quintus with his bold transport tunnel plan or Vitalus’ plan for state-of-the-art irrigation technology.

It was the day of the Super Tuesday of their village elections, with the ballots secured in the mailbox at the village square. The polling line grew long as the people queued to cast their votes. Some were whispering in favor of Vitalus, while others shouted praises for Quintus. Yet, as puzzling as this secret enveloping the village seemed, one simple fact remained: promises had become a powerful, manipulative tool. And they wielded it, as both candidates knew people’s preference for tangible connections better than hollow rhetoric.

As dusk fell, the village elder emerged from the sealed voting booth with a frail smile, while the rest of the council anxiously watched. He proceeded to reveal the voting results: Quintus had won the village election by a small but decisive margin. Regardless of the future’s outcome, the people’s expectations were set high as they embraced their new leader and awaited the dawn of the new era.

Unbeknownst to them, as engineering work began on the tunnel project, their arduous work to build the newest transport system had accidentally unearthed an ancient relic from the village’s origin, shrouded in mystery. It was a forgotten text buried deep in their soil, indifferently oblivious to the political promises made just skimming its surface.

This relic contained a time-tested set of rules that prevented a leader from making grandiose promises to the people, as it compromised the village’s true, well-rounded progress. Silently, the village council voted to adhere to these rules, and the village slowly reverted to its old, more humble self. Quintus made gradual improvements, not the drastic visions that were popular for a short while. Together, they fostered deeper relationships built on sincerity to nurture the village back to its prosperous state.

In the end, the village’s politics changed, turned and twisted like the others, fed on promises and rhetoric. Yet, slowly and quietly, they learned to look beyond the promises offered and examine what lay beneath: the realities of fulfilling their deepest needs, respecting the history of their land, and welcoming change with open arms.

The ultimate irony? Many years later, as Quintus rested deepest in his grave, a prolific author walked through the village library, his fingers drumming on the ancient relic. His eyes widened as he sat down to write – of the lost village and its struggle. Between the rhetoric and the reality.
