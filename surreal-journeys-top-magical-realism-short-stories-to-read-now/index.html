Surreal Journeys: Top Magical Realism Short Stories to Read Now


The sun was setting as the sky blurred from a pale gold to a deep, melancholic purple. The day was drawing to a close, yet everything felt wrong. Nia had arrived at her childhood home, but the house, where she had spent so many years running around in carefree bliss, now stood empty. The creaking wooden porch seemed somehow out of place, and the faintest whisper of wind danced through the trees as though the land itself was breathing in unison with her heartbeat.

Her eyes traced the familiar, yet strange, outline of the house as if it had belonged to someone else. She hesitated for a moment before reaching for the doorknob, her fingers trembling just slightly. The moment her hand touched the cold brass, the air shifted. The wind stopped. Time itself seemed to pause, hanging in that single breath. Then, without warning, the door creaked open.

The room inside was just as it had been when she left—yet not at all. Dust danced in the air, settling on the objects that had once defined her childhood. The old armchair by the fireplace, the kitchen table that had witnessed so many conversations, the scattered toys that she had left behind. But now, the toys seemed to come alive, shifting like shadows, barely perceptible in the fading light. As Nia stepped inside, a shiver ran down her spine. There was something… otherworldly about this place.

The floorboards groaned under her weight as she moved deeper into the house. Each step brought her closer to the heart of the mystery that had pulled her back here. She had never been able to explain why she left, nor why she had returned after so many years. All she knew was that something inside the house called to her, a memory, a longing, a voice lost in the depths of time.

In the kitchen, the scent of something familiar lingered in the air—her mother’s cooking, the warm embrace of comfort. But the stove was cold, the refrigerator empty. The room was strangely alive with echoes of the past. Nia closed her eyes for a moment, hoping for clarity, but all she found was a reflection in the old mirror hanging above the sink.

She opened her eyes slowly, and the reflection wasn’t hers anymore. The face in the glass was someone else entirely, a woman whose features were blurry, unfocused, like a dream fading in the morning light. The woman looked directly at Nia and smiled—a knowing smile, a smile that hinted at forgotten secrets and untold stories.

Nia turned away in confusion, her breath catching in her throat. The air seemed to press against her chest, heavy with the weight of unsolved riddles. A voice—soft, almost imperceptible—whispered her name. It was coming from upstairs.

Her heart skipped a beat. Nia had never believed in ghosts or spirits, but something about this house defied logic. The air itself seemed to pulse with a strange energy, a force that beckoned her upward.

With a hesitant step, she made her way toward the staircase. Each step creaked louder than the last, and the whisper grew clearer. It wasn’t her name anymore—it was a phrase, a question, spoken with such quiet urgency that it felt as though the walls themselves were vibrating with it.

“Are you ready to remember?”

She paused at the top of the stairs, staring into the dim hallway. The door at the end of the hall stood ajar, a sliver of light leaking out from within. Something about that light felt wrong, yet irresistible. As if it contained both answers and dangers, promises and threats.

With a final, determined breath, Nia stepped forward, her footsteps now soundless on the thick carpet. The air felt cooler here, and the light seemed to grow brighter the closer she got to the door. Her hand hovered over the doorknob, and for a moment, she considered turning back. But then, the voice returned—this time, louder, more insistent.

“Remember.”

She opened the door.

Inside was a room she had never seen before. The walls were lined with shelves, each holding objects she couldn’t recognize—shimmering stones, intricate carvings, and books bound in strange materials. But it was the center of the room that drew her attention. A large, ancient mirror stood there, its surface foggy and distorted. It was the same mirror that had shown her the strange woman earlier, but now it was different. The woman’s face was clearer, sharper, as if she had stepped out of the past to confront Nia in the present.

The woman’s eyes locked onto hers, and for a moment, Nia felt as though she were falling into the depths of the mirror itself. The reflection was no longer just a reflection; it was a doorway, a portal to another world.

“You have to choose,” the woman said, her voice a soft echo that seemed to resonate within Nia’s very soul. “You can stay here, in this world that you know, or you can step through and see what lies beyond. But once you choose, there’s no turning back.”

Nia felt a surge of fear and wonder. She had always wondered if there was more to the world than what she had seen with her own eyes, always felt that something was missing, that something larger was just beyond the horizon. And now, standing before the mirror, she understood that the choice was hers to make.

The air around her thickened, as if the room itself was holding its breath, waiting for her decision. She glanced at the mirror again, seeing the world she had left behind—the house, the memories, her old life. And yet, she felt a pull, a desire to know the truth, to understand the magic that lay hidden beneath the surface of her reality.

With a trembling hand, Nia reached out and touched the surface of the mirror.

The moment her fingers made contact, the room exploded in a burst of light. She felt herself being pulled forward, falling through the very fabric of space and time, the world around her dissolving into a thousand fragments. It was as if she were being torn apart and remade all at once, the boundaries of reality blurring in a dizzying swirl of colors, shapes, and sensations.

When the light faded, Nia found herself standing in a new place. It was a world unlike anything she had ever imagined. The sky was a deep shade of violet, with swirling clouds that seemed to move in patterns too intricate to comprehend. The ground beneath her feet was soft and glowing, like a field of stars under her toes. Strange plants, with leaves that shimmered like liquid metal, grew in the distance, their forms shifting and changing as though alive. Creatures—some familiar, some entirely alien—wandered through the landscape, their bodies glowing with an ethereal light.

Nia looked around, her breath catching in her throat. This was no dream. This was no illusion. She had crossed over into another world, a world where the rules of reality no longer applied.

And then, she heard the voice again, clearer this time, as though it was right beside her.

“You’ve come to the other side,” the voice said. “But now, you must face the truth. Everything you’ve known, everything you’ve believed, is a part of the story that has already been written. The question is not whether you can change it, but whether you are willing to.”

Nia swallowed hard, her heart pounding in her chest. What was this place? And what was this truth that she had been called to discover? She didn’t know, but something deep within her stirred, a curiosity that was impossible to ignore.

She walked forward, the glowing ground beneath her feet shimmering with each step. There was no turning back now. The journey had only just begun, and she was ready to face whatever lay ahead, to uncover the mysteries that the universe had been hiding from her all along.

With each step, the surreal landscape seemed to shift and change around her, as if the very world itself was responding to her presence. She was no longer just a wanderer. She was a part of something much larger, something that stretched beyond the limits of time and space.

And as she ventured deeper into the unknown, Nia realized that the journey was not just about discovering new worlds, but about discovering herself—the person she had always been, and the person she was yet to become.
