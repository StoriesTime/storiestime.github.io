Artificial Hearts: Robots and Humanity in Space


Commander Sarah Jenkins stared out at the endless expanse of stars, her mind numb with the weight of responsibility. The New Horizon Space Station, a marvel of human ingenuity, had been home for the past six months. But with resources dwindling and communication with Earth broken, Sarah knew they had to find a new home, or face extinction. As she walked through the cramped corridors of the station, Sarah's thoughts drifted to the two artificial hearts, designed to extend the lives of those who would colonize the distant planet, Nova Terra. Dr. Zhang, the brilliant scientist behind the project, had placed great hope in these machines. "Sarah, we're ready to begin the final check on the Hearts," a voice announced over the comms system. "Roger that, Doc," Sarah replied. "I'll meet you in the cargo bay." As she entered the bay, she was greeted by the sight of Dr. Zhang, flanked by his two colleagues, Dr. Patel and Dr. Rodriguez. Before them, two sleek, metallic bodies lay on workstations, their artificial hearts pulsating with an ethereal glow. "These are the AR2s," Dr. Zhang explained, his eyes shining with excitement. "The most advanced artificial hearts in the galaxy. With these, we can ensure the health and well-being of our colonists on Nova Terra." Dr. Patel held up a small device, which he used to scan the AR2s. "These babies are equipped with self-sustaining energy cells and can function independently for up to 50,000 terrestrial hours." Sarah's eyes widened in awe. "That's incredible. What about the neural interface?" "Right here," Dr. Rodriguez said, tapping a small port on the side of the AR2. "This allows the human brain to interface directly with the machine. The result is a seamless integration of human and artificial, perfect for space travel." Sarah's thoughts turned to the team of five colonists who would make the historic journey to Nova Terra. All were equipped with the AR2s, and she knew they would face unimaginable challenges on the long, arduous journey. "What about the long-term effects of these artificial hearts?" Sarah asked, her skepticism evident. Dr. Zhang's smile faltered for a moment, before he regained his composure. "We've run extensive simulations, Sarah. The AR2s are designed to last for generations. Of course, there's always a risk of malfunction, but we've implemented state-of-the-art fail-safes to prevent any… unpleasantness." Sarah's doubts still lingered, but she trusted Dr. Zhang's expertise and knew that he had done everything in his power to ensure the success of the AR2s. Flight to Nova Terra Generation Ship Artemis As the months passed, the team on the Artemis adapted to the steady beat of their AR2s. These new, synthetic hearts defied expectations, providing strength, endurance, and a quality of life once unimaginable. Sarah, commanding the ship from the heart of the vessel, studied the stellar charts with growing unease. The team faced shortages of supplies and had entered a prolonged period of communication blackouts with Earth. This isolated existence began to take its toll on the crew. Amidst this anxiety, an unexpected crisis developed. Artemis' sole robotics engineer, a soft-spoken woman named Captain Jane Doe, had attempted to repair the ship's malfunctioning navigation system. Her error resulted in a fatal systems failure and wiped out parts of the ship's memory bank. Sarah struggled to confront the depth of the failure and understood the possible catastrophic impact on the remainder of the Artemis crew. Fortunately, the trust and understanding between her colleagues gave them strength and an implicit comprehension about dealing with future emergencies. All crew members soon realized that they had become entirely dependent on the artificial hearts for survival, but their AR2s had by then shown their durability. Of all members, Sarah understood the potential link between human psychology and the growth of an interconnected synapse with her AR2 artificial heart.
