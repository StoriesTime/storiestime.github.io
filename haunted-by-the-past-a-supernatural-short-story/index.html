Haunted by the Past: A Supernatural Short Story


The wind howled through the trees, as though it carried secrets from another time. The full moon cast its eerie glow upon the old house at the edge of the forest. Its creaking walls and broken windows whispered of years long past, of stories untold, and of a past that refused to be forgotten.

Anna stood at the threshold, her heart pounding in her chest. The house had always been there, ever since she was a child, standing like a monument to some forgotten tragedy. She had heard the stories, of course—stories of the people who once lived here, of their sudden disappearance, and of the strange occurrences that plagued the house ever since. But she had never believed in ghosts, or curses, or anything that couldn’t be explained by logic and reason.

But today was different. Today, she wasn’t just standing at the edge of the forest, looking at the house from a distance. Today, she was walking toward it, with a purpose. The letter she had received two weeks ago had left her with no choice. The past had finally caught up with her.

The Letter

It had come on a rainy afternoon. The envelope was old, yellowed with age, and the handwriting was unfamiliar. Inside was a single piece of paper, written in a hurried hand, as if the writer had been desperate to get the words out.

“Anna, you must come back. The past will not let you go. The house is waiting.”

At first, she had thought it was some kind of prank. But there was something about the letter, something about the way the ink had bled into the paper, that made her feel uneasy. She had tried to forget about it, to go on with her life as if nothing had happened. But the message had burrowed into her mind, growing stronger each day, until she could no longer ignore it.

The house. The past. It all seemed connected, tied together in a way she couldn’t yet understand.

The Journey Back

As she approached the house, Anna couldn’t shake the feeling that she was being watched. The trees seemed to close in around her, their branches reaching out like gnarled fingers. The path was overgrown, the grass waist-high, and the air felt thick, heavy with the weight of memories. It was as though the very land around the house had absorbed the sorrow and the pain of those who had lived there.

Anna had grown up in this village, but she had never really known the house’s history. It was always there, an old, decrepit mansion that everyone in town whispered about. Some said it was cursed, others claimed it was haunted, but no one really knew the truth. The one thing that everyone agreed on was that something terrible had happened there, something that had left a mark on the house and its grounds.

The gate creaked open as she pushed it, the sound sending a chill down her spine. She hesitated for a moment, then took a deep breath and stepped onto the porch. The door was slightly ajar, as though it had been waiting for her.

Inside, the air was stale, thick with the smell of dust and decay. The floorboards creaked under her feet as she walked through the darkened hallways. The house seemed to groan with every step, as if it was alive, as though it remembered her.

“Hello?” she called out, her voice echoing in the empty space.

No answer.

She continued down the hallway, her footsteps muffled by the worn carpet. The walls were covered in peeling wallpaper, faded with age. The house felt cold, even though it was summer outside. It was as if the chill of the past had seeped into the very bones of the building, refusing to let go.

As she reached the end of the hallway, Anna stopped in front of a door that had once been a bedroom. It was now a dark, silent room, the window covered by heavy drapes. She could feel the presence of something—or someone—inside, waiting for her. Her hand trembled as she reached for the doorknob.

The Truth Unveiled

The door swung open with a groan, revealing a room that had not changed in years. A large four-poster bed stood in the center, its sheets tattered and yellowed. The walls were lined with old furniture, covered in dust and cobwebs. But it was the mirror on the far wall that caught her attention. It was large, ornate, and seemed out of place in the otherwise decaying room.

Anna approached the mirror, her reflection barely visible in the dim light. But then, something strange happened. Her reflection began to shift, to change, until it no longer looked like her at all. The woman in the mirror was older, her eyes filled with sorrow and fear. Her face was pale, her lips cracked, and her hair was wild and unkempt.

“Who are you?” Anna whispered, her voice shaking.

The woman in the mirror did not answer, but her eyes seemed to follow Anna’s every move. The room grew colder, the air thick with an unsettling energy. Anna felt as though the house was closing in on her, pressing down on her with an invisible force.

And then she saw it. A figure, standing behind her in the reflection. It was a man, tall and gaunt, his face twisted in agony. His eyes were hollow, dark pits that seemed to suck in the light around him. His mouth moved, but no sound came out.

Anna spun around, but there was no one there.

The Revelation

The door slammed shut behind her, trapping her inside the room. Panic surged through Anna’s chest as she scrambled for the handle, but it wouldn’t budge. She was trapped.

“Help!” she screamed, but her voice was drowned out by the oppressive silence.

Suddenly, the walls seemed to close in, and the temperature dropped even further. She could see her breath fogging in the air. A low whisper echoed through the room, growing louder, until it was all around her.

“You shouldn’t have come back.”

Anna’s heart raced as the whisper turned into a growl, a deep, guttural voice that seemed to come from the very walls themselves.

“The past… it will never let you go.”

The mirror began to crack, lines appearing across its surface as if something was trying to break through. Anna stumbled back, her breath coming in shallow gasps. She was suffocating, the air too thick, too heavy.

And then, in the shattered reflection, she saw it. The man’s face, twisted with rage and pain. His mouth opened wide, and Anna could hear him now, his voice rising from the depths of her mind.

“Leave… or die.”

Anna’s eyes widened in horror as the mirror shattered completely, sending glass flying in all directions. The room plunged into darkness, and for a moment, she thought she would lose her mind.

But then, a voice broke through the chaos—her own voice, strong and clear.

“No. I won’t let you control me.”

Anna opened her eyes, and to her surprise, she was standing outside the room, the door now wide open. The house was silent. The air was no longer heavy. It felt… different.

The Escape

She ran, her heart pounding in her chest, her feet stumbling over the uneven floor. She didn’t know how she had gotten out of the room, but she didn’t care. All she wanted was to leave, to get as far away from the house as possible.

But the house was not done with her yet.

As she reached the front door, the sound of footsteps echoed behind her. She turned around, expecting to see someone—or something—coming for her, but there was nothing. Just the empty hallway stretching out before her.

And then she felt it, a cold hand on her shoulder. Slowly, she turned.

The man from the mirror stood before her, his face twisted in agony. His eyes locked onto hers, and for a moment, Anna felt as though he was peering into her very soul.

“You can never escape,” he whispered.

But Anna was done. She was no longer afraid. With a defiant scream, she pushed past him and out into the night, the door slamming shut behind her.

As she ran through the forest, the sounds of the house faded, replaced by the rush of her own heartbeat. She didn’t look back.

The past was behind her now. And she would never return.
