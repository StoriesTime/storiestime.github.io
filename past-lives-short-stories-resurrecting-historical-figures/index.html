Past Lives: Short Stories Resurrecting Historical Figures


The janitor, Vincent, had been working at the prestigious Dead Historical Figures Museum for ten years. Each day, he swept, dusted, and polished the exhibits while the hordes of visitors studied the lives of long-forgotten historical figures. Many of the items on display were of the curiosities, revealing strange occurrences and phenomena linked to those once-living people.

While performing his janitorial duties one morning, Vincent came across an unusual object. It was a peculiarly designed box with an ancient eerie aura. The curator had forgotten to mention the box’s existence. Intrigued, Vincent pocketed the mysterious box and decided to investigate further that night when the museum was closed.

As the janitor cleaned the last exhibit, he secretly opened the box in the janitor’s closet. And suddenly, he found himself transported to another world - the 17th century France. He had become Baronne de Morges, an influential lady with a reputation as a mystic, intelligent, and cunning. The world around him pulsed with life and opulence he hadn’t experienced in his humble life.

With no warning or explanation, Vincent, as Baronne de Morges, found himself surrounded by people who had once been exhibit pieces in the museum. Each of them was at a similar crossroads, swept away from their mundane lives to live in the shoes of historical figures they once only read about. They shared the same bewilderment and confusion - why were they here?

As the days went by, Vincent discovered the world from the Baronne’s perspective. But he was smarter than that, and he began to look for clues to unravel the mystery of his situation. He learned of her connections and influence, how her insights had been sought by the most significant figure of that era, Cardinal Mazarin. With the knowledge and charm of the Baronne, Vincent navigated through the intrigues and power plays of the French court.

Getting comfortable with his new life, Vincent, as Baronne de Morges, helped a debtor escape imprisonment. As reward, the debtor thanked him by providing a room in the Palace of Versailles. He and the other souls now had a roof under the luxurious mansion.

Vincent’s new life was smooth as silk, accumulating wealth and influence. Still, deep inside, an unquenchable thirst for knowledge made him seek answers. With Vincent’s intelligence and the Baronne’s nous, he realized that the only way to piece together the puzzle was to meet the source. He visited the powerful Cardinal Mazarin.

The Cardinal had always valued the secret knowledge of the Baronne. Their meeting was arranged, and Vincent, still in disguise as the Baronne de Morges, talked with the Cardinal, revealing as many inside knowledge about him he had gleaned. The Cardinal shared an uncanny story of the box; it was known as the Resurrection Box, passed down through the ages by the Morses line. It was a tool that allowed one to experience the life of another person of their choosing.

The silence of the room then told its own tale. He had been trapped. The Box had become the Bone of Contention between the two most influential people in France - the King and the Cardinal. It was a power play that neither would back down on.

The gears shifted in Vincent’s mind. His thirst for knowledge had placed him in a significant game of chess. He had to play well to survive, but a different real-life pawn was on the board.

In the middle of the night, the palace was swallowed up by chaos. Fights broke out, and soldiers rushed about, in search of the Box that was said to be stolen. Vincent found himself cornered, and in the scuffle, he accidentally pushed a soldier, driving him towards the Box. As the soldier lunged, he slipped and fell, but the crash off the floor revealed something different. Perhaps it was the shock influenced by the extravagant lifestyle, perhaps it was his newfound cunning self.

They all realized that the Box was just an ancient decoy. But in between the clatter, Vincent had subtly passed a simple but genuine Resurrection Gem from his era, hidden in his hand, to the cardinal’s. The gem was the true power behind the resurrection box.

In the aftermath of the chaos, Vincent found himself back in the janitor’s closet within the museum. All that remained was the knowledge and mystery of the box. As the janitor, he pocketed the decoy Resurrection Box and resumed his duties.

With his calm demeanor and secret history of Baronne de Morges, Vincent resumed his humble life. Still, as the story makes its rounds amongst exhibits and historical halls, the question of the Bone’s identity remains.
