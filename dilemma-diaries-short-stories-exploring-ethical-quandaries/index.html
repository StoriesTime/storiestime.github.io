Dilemma Diaries: Short Stories Exploring Ethical Quandaries


The day the mailmen delivered the package had been an ordinary one. It was a sunny afternoon, with a cool breeze that carried the smell of nearby flowers. Susan, a moderately successful lawyer in her town, had just finished a complicated case and had been off work for a few days to relax at her country cottage. She had just settled into her favorite armchair with a cup of warm tea and an engrossing novel, when there was a knock on her door.

Unusually, Susan decided to check who it was instead of ignoring the disturbance. Perhaps it was a neighbor she had forgotten to invite to the movie night she had abruptly canceled due to her work-related stress.

She opened the red-painted door and was surprised to find a tall man in a hat she couldn’t quite make out the color of. He was holding a rather large and ornate package.

“Good evening, Susan. I apologize for this unusual visit,” said the man, removing his hat to reveal salt-and-pepper hair, an unusual touch for such an overwhelmingly well-dressed individual.

“Funny, I didn’t think anyone would be coming by today.” Susan replied, looking at the package suspiciously. “Who are you and what’s this?”

“I’ve traveled far to deliver this, my dear.” He paused for a moment, as though searching for the right words. “I was instructed to deliver this unto you as your rightful inheritance. It was locked in the attic of your childhood home where you grew up before moving to this beautiful countryside. I’m a friend of your father’s, and he wanted you to have it.”

Susan blinked in confusion, her eyes widening as she looked from the man to the mysterious box. Curiosity usually won over her cautious nature, but she hesitated.

“Listen, I understand your skepticism. Your father gave me a key, and I assure you I am who I say I am.” He dug around in his pocket, and to her confusion, Susan found herself accepting his word.

“Why would my father send you all this way? And why now?”

“It’s a matter of time, Susan. Time he couldn’t explain in his letter. Too many things on his conscience,” the man explained. “Now, would you like to open your father’s gift?”

She nodded hesitantly, completely entranced by the man’s words and the mysterious package. He stepped aside, and as Susan gingerly took the package from him, she felt as though she were holding something incredible and ethereal yet undeniably solid.

As she placed the package on a table, she reached for a pair of scissors, and in one swift incision, the wrapping vanished, revealing a delicate wooden box. The metallic clasp shone in the afternoon sunlight and Susan opened it gently, revealing an oddly-shaped object that seemed more like an heirloom than anything else.

“Hmm, it’s a locket.” Susan said with a thoughtful tone. She unclasped the chain and examined the inside of the locket, which contained an old photograph of a young girl. It seemed to sparkle mysteriously in the last bits of sunlight.

The man looked at her eagerly and said, “Your father wanted you to have it. Said it was your true calling, the key to unlocking your potential and understanding life.”

Susan frowned, intrigued, but not entirely convinced.

“What do you mean by ‘unlocking my potential?’ I’m already a successful lawyer. What else could there possibly be?”

The man smiled, looking just a bit melancholic. “To see the corners of the world that you could never fathom, my dear,” he said softly. “He always believed in you.”

Later that night, Susan tossed and turned in her bedroom, plagued by the enigma of her father’s delivery. Her pride in her successful career and in her past accomplishments had been shaken by the mysterious locket.

The next morning, she lay in bed contemplating the events, weighing the new sense of dissatisfaction she felt and the pull the locket seemed to have on her. Finally, she rose and silently took it with her, as she ventured out into the quaint town she lived in, to seek answers.

Susan’s first stop was the local antique shop. She was met with a warm greeting from the shop owner, Jane, a friend of Susan’s since her childhood.

“Hello, Susan. Haven’t seen you in a while,” said Jane, her face lighting up with genuine cheer. “I heard about the case you won recently. It must be paying off.”

“Thank you, Jane. Yes, it has been quite a whirlwind. Do you know anything about this locket I’ve inherited from my father?” Susan asked, her curiosity overflowing into her words.

Without hesitation, Jane’s eyes flicked to the locket and she seemed to think for a moment before answering. “If my memory serves correctly, your father was an old friend of mine, and that locket seems familiar. It appears to be an antique, but I’ve never seen an exact replica.”

A Friendship Rekindled

Over the course of the morning, Jane and Susan found themselves at a small cafe, trading stories about their lives and their memories of each other’s pasts. The locket lay dormant on the table before them, as though waiting for its rightful secrets to be divulged.

Susan was mid-sentence when she paused.

“Jane, why did my father give you this package to deliver? It doesn’t make sense.”

Jane looked up from her cup of tea, her face solemn. “After your mother’s passing, your father began to travel. Sometimes he would go months without a letter, until one day, he returned, much older. It was then he realized he needed to pass something on to you - he believed this locket would.”

She paused, gathering the right words. “He hoped the experiences that he had whilst traveling would also be yours.” Susan couldn’t help but see the glimmer in her eyes. “He told me that this locket was the key to a new life, a different path.”

Susan stared at the locket, unable to deny the allure it had. She could feel the weight of her father’s dreams resting on her shoulders and the world seeming to beckon her with open arms.

The next day found Susan standing in her front yard, the locket securely tucked around her neck. As she gazed upon it, she was struck once more by the power it encompassed.

This time, however, the power was not so mysterious. It was the feeling of liberation, of stepping into the unknown and seizing control of her future.

In that moment, Susan felt the wind from the pages of her old novel flutter gently in her face, the sun beginning to set just as the young girl’s journey had begun. She knew that she now had a choice - and so began the true adventure of her life.
