Voices of the Forsaken: A Supernatural Tale of Lost Souls


In the ghostly expanse of the forgotten Mannheim Forest, whispers of the forsaken echoed in the chilling air. Tales of lost souls who perished in the shadows of history drifted through the desolate woods, haunting the living who ventured near the once-thriving village—now a relic buried deep in the souls’ relentless calls.

These voices, in spiraling murals of confusion, serpented their way through the tree trunks, birch and chestnut, their tendrils splintering into the night sky. Among messengers of unrest, the slumbering spirits scampered, their hushed lyrics shrouded in a cloak of thick spectral air.

The year was 1357. Württemberg, a worldly domain devoid of light, bled into twilight’s bowels each night, staining the horizon with the festering wounds of lost time. Here the forsaken found solace, or so its inhabitants believed. Neighboring townspeople whispered of the unnatural grove—its eerie tapestry told by the calcified eyes of an old crone living nearby, her twisted limbs bearing whispered warnings within their gnarled wood.

Frayed clasps of the vile plague claimed the settlements’ despairing townsfolk, leaving only a wistful widow named Helene behind. Her heart, enshrouded in a veil of solitude, sought comfort in these forsaken whispers. Unseen forces guided her through the shadows, the nocturnal veil a sanctuary for stranger creatures than the diurnal world could dream.

In this land beneath the moon, Helene found communion with others like her. A dying tradesman, a perfidious traitor, and a vengeful tyrant whispering counsel from beyond the Veil—a chorus of destitute souls united by an undying thirst for the resonance of human companionship.

Their stories echoed across the ancient lands, singing sorrows, evoking injustice, and entwining them in a gossamer web. The voices lured Helene, beckoning her to join their ranks, granting her a spectral pursuit that would ultimately carry her into their hallowed halls.

Guided by the cryptic illusions and ethereal trickery spun by the spectral chorus, Helene embraced her own destiny, woven like silk amongst the afflicted souls. To her surprise, the forgotten beings welcomed her presence, urging her to spin vivid tales of her own.

It was a tale of a Raven haired sorceress, forbidden love, and a curse entwining destiny with tragedy. The veil between the lands grew thin, and inspiration flowed, converting Helene’s darkened whispers into powerful poetry with every beat of her heart.

In her newfound communion with the forsaken, Helene discovered the bond that would tether her presence to the cursed forest, forever. Her legacy intertwined with that of the lost souls, a cascade of lamentations seething through history’s pages, a testament to the cryptic borderland that barely witnesses the light of the day.

Yet there existed one entity—a spectral harbinger of ominous forces—eluding even the swarming voices of the forsaken. This harrowed spirit bore terrible wisdom and ancient secrets tethered to the grove, its presence cloaked by barren night. They called this enigmatic presence Drachenwolf: a harbinger of unseen calamities that bore terror with every enigmatic shadow cast by the ethereal entity.

Knowing that a far graver fate might befall Helene should she brush against the Drachenwolf, she learned to navigate the crooked paths surrounding the spectral refuge. Helene’s tale was one of anguish, ecstasy, and, finally, a haunting existence at the heart of the Labyrinth—a place where the forsaken whispered final goodbyes to the world they once knew only too well.

In tribute to her tales weaved amongst the shadows, the spirits toiled, their voices entwining with fears and hopes chiming in orchestral whispers as if to remind the forsaken of their transient nature.

Helene’s existence emboldened the spectral tapestry of the forsaken, and the starless expanse of the Mannheim Forest echoed with truth, love, and torment of unparalleled magnitude. In the forgotten woods, the voices of the forsaken reveled with the widow, delighting in the playful shadows cast by moonlight—a nightly ballet of eternal salvation and despair.
