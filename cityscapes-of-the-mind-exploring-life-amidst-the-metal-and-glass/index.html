Cityscapes of the Mind: Exploring Life Amidst the Metal and Glass


In the labyrinthine metropolis of Mettlerae, nestled between towering labyrinthine mettarscraper complexes, a life of contrasting rhythms pulsed. Gleaming edifices cast shards of light that danced through the waning day; the fully-illuminated night was stacked upon the fading day. Hence each step forward came to look much like retreat.

Everywhere among the city’s veins echoed siren melodies of metal and glass, crescendoing and then fading. Echoes often lingered until twilight’s very end. The pulsating rhythm of the city grew more and more entwined with that of the individuals within it.

At edge of the city’s farewell party, a peculiar outcropping known as “The Glass Furrow,” nestled an avant-garde saxophonist named Ding Anx. His lips were nimble, their strikes were gentle, vibrations reverberated so subtly that they described an amalgamation of notes, which then wrapped around each structure in the panorama. His music seemed to pass right through them, bringing rooftops alive with notes of sentimental lament.

Ding Anx, driven by both his profession and a profound passion for mettarscraper exploration, persisted in his mission to reach the apex of Mettlerae’s tallest structure, the eponymous “Mettlerae Milestone.” Its height equalled that of Mettlerae’s dreams; a quadrillion stories above the city’s ever-shifting landscape, most claimed they could see God. For Ding Anx, the undefeatable Mettlerae Milestone was not only the highest point of the city, but also the sanctuary of liberation.

He often referred to the.GLASSN, a group of uncompromised visionaries whose discourses melded with the saxophone. The.GLASSN ignited the prairie fire of Ding Anx’s imagination, unravelling the obscure depths of visual and audial constellations. Together they desired to unravel the mysteries of the city’s outward and inward pathways – how to play upon the song of its pulse to reach the heights beyond, through the divine mettarscraper that was Mettlerae.

One day, while the mettarscrapers hummed with roiling anticipation of dusk’s arrival, Ding Anx found himself on a rooftop reachable only through a neighboring mettarscraper, one of seemingly infinite dimensions. Among an ensemble of scurrying voices he almost drowned, yet his saxophone echoed to the heavens, i.e. the Mettlerae Milestone: “Hey, sunlight! Dice-toss between dawn and twilight, find who wins but don’t forget to shake hands with the unusual horizon!”

From his aerial observation, Ding Anx admired the crystal expanse of roofing tiles, each carrying chandeliers that shimmered encouragingly. Entranced, he recognized in his heart the origin of the unexplored audio landscape opportunity he’d been seeking. He began to navigate the labyrinth of glass and steel epitomized by the colossal mettarscraper beside him. The interplay of light and darkness transformed the city into a grand symphony perfumed by various mythical narratives.

Ding Anx’s exploration of Mettlerae’s cityscape continued. Successively, he found himself within the heart of the city – the place where sound and light waved with equal resonance. He had fallen into the rhythm of the mettarscraper world, but remained ever alert for moment to ascend the Mettlerae Milestone.

Overwhelmed by intricate sights and sounds, he found solace on a deserted floating terrace of a mettarscraper, nestled between others, as if it was lost in mid-air while traversing from one mettarscraper to another. There, he glimpsed the “Garden of Mettalumen,” a garden of minimal socio-spatial complexity with plenty of breathing space for the measurements of mettalumens who’d found their place beneath the towering mettarscrapers.

Through the mettalumens’ unique expressions of dissolving sound and light, Ding Anx experienced a surreal bliss, unknowable and unending. Yet for all their peculiarity, they were a voiceless mirror of the society above, both parties ensconced within the cityscape.

In time, while absorbing and embodying the music of the city, Ding Anx began to develop an understanding of the rhythmic cityscapes beneath the mettarscrapers. He noted that the cityscape of Mettlerae, like his own, was a cacophony of both desire and resignation. It was this push and pull that influenced Mettlerae’s harmonic structures. The ever-intricate labyrinth of metal and glass between the mettalumens and those dwelling above escalated in complexity, seeking new melodies, and ever-new harmonies.

One day, a solitary figure ascended through the tangle of mettarscrapers and arrived at the floating terrace. Clad in black robes matching those worn by the mettalumens and bearing a violin with him, he introduced himself as an old friend of Ding Anx’s – Luo Zhe. Luo Zhe was a purveyor of violins; each one was reputed to have been crafted from the broken fragments of Mettlerae’s discarded dreams.

Luo Zhe unveiled to Ding Anx the secret of the Garden of Mettalumen: the mettalumens were, in fact, remnants of Mettlerae’s fallen dreams, crystallized in time, frozen in everlasting beauty. Luo Zhe’s violin, he explained, was a living testament to this city – each note, each string, gleaned from the shards of Mettlerae’s past, resonating with its soul. He beckoned Ding Anx to join him, promising to help reach the Mettlerae Milestone.

The mesmerizing enigma of Luo Zhe, the misunderstood musician, and his violin interweaved with Ding Anx’s saxophone accompaniment to cast a spell. They began nightly duets from atop the floating terrace, exalting the Garden of Mettalumen’s luminous melodies, enshrouding Mettlerae in harmonious sound and light.

With each passing day, Mettlerae’s skyline was transformed as if a magical inpouring of time had tipped a kaleidoscope above the city’s ancient rhythm. The two musicians, Ding Anx and Luo Zhe, summoned an unprecedented aural spectacle upon the floating terrace, which resonated through Mettlerae’s cityscape.

Yet, beyond their reach still towered the unscalable Mettlerae Milestone. Ding Anx released a bracing note, as if challenging the very heavens. This inextricable mettarscraper seemed impervious even to an entwining of music and dream, still manipulating Mettlerae’s rhythms in ways that reached only so far.

A new awareness of the city dawned upon them one fateful night: the Mettlerae Milestone was not a physical edifice; its true height reached beyond the mettalumens between the mettarscrapers. The cityscape pulse, ever-lasting, was an immaterial fabric of the dreamers and dreamt, palpable and flowing with all the might and dynamism of life itself.

Through a last forlorn whisper on his saxophone and an ultimate draw on Luo Zhe’s violin, the restrained sound swelled within the Garden of Mettalumen, its dissonance embraced a deeper level of primal feelings. As for the cityscapes of Mettlerae, though containing the pulse of the mettalumens and the mettarscrapers, they stretched beyond their physical presence.

Unbeknownst to the entire city, the immaterial cityscape stretched up into the heavens untouched, unto the divine sanctuary.

And so, in the grand Mettlerae symphony, an intricate tapestry woven in the zigzagging silhouette of a labyrinthine metropolis, the magical Wraith Ensemble – composed of Luo Zhe and Ding Anx – left the present world but found their way to the heights of that invisible city, the realm of unseen dreams.
