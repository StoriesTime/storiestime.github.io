The Betrayer's Curse: Revenge Served Cold


The wind howled through the barren landscape, the dark sky above reflecting the emptiness below. A single figure trudged across the frozen ground, their breath coming in short, sharp gasps as they made their way toward the distant mountains. The journey was long, but the weight of vengeance pushed them forward, relentless and unforgiving.

Korin had been a soldier once, a leader among his people. His name had been known across the lands for his courage and his unwavering loyalty. But now, that name meant nothing. It had been tarnished, stripped of its dignity by the very ones he had trusted most.

He had been betrayed.

The memories were still fresh in his mind. He could almost feel the sting of the steel as it cut through his flesh, the betrayal seeping into his bones like poison. His closest friend, a man he had fought alongside in countless battles, had turned on him, selling him out to the enemy for a promise of power and wealth. Korin had been left to die, abandoned by the one person who had sworn to fight beside him until the end.

But death had not come that day. Instead, Korin had awoken in a dark cell, broken but not defeated. The scars on his body told the story of his torment, but they were nothing compared to the scars on his soul. He had sworn to himself, then and there, that he would not rest until he had his revenge.

And so, here he was, in the frozen wastelands, making his way to the only place where he might find the answers he sought—the old temple, buried deep within the mountains. Legends spoke of a curse, an ancient power that could bring about the most brutal of retributions, but it came with a price. The curse could only be wielded by the one who had been wronged, and once invoked, there was no turning back.

Korin had nothing left to lose.

As he approached the temple, the air grew colder, the wind more biting. The stone structure loomed before him, ancient and weathered by time. It was said that only those with a pure heart and an unyielding desire for justice could find their way to the temple. Korin had no illusions about purity, but his thirst for revenge was as pure as the ice that surrounded him.

Inside, the air was thick with the weight of centuries. The walls were lined with faded murals, depicting ancient rituals and forgotten gods. At the center of the room stood a stone altar, surrounded by flickering candles that seemed to burn with an eerie, otherworldly glow.

Korin approached the altar, his heart pounding in his chest. He had heard the stories, but he had never believed them to be true. Now, standing before the altar, he realized just how wrong he had been.

A voice echoed in his mind, low and guttural, yet strangely familiar. “You seek vengeance, Korin. But vengeance is a poison that will consume you, body and soul. Are you prepared to pay the price?”

Korin clenched his fists, the cold stone beneath his fingers grounding him. “I am prepared. I will pay any price. I seek justice for the wrongs done to me.”

The voice paused, as if considering his words. “Justice? Or is it something else you seek? A punishment for those who betrayed you? A punishment that will burn them as deeply as the wound they inflicted upon you?”

Korin’s voice was steady, though the rage simmered beneath the surface. “I seek both. I seek their destruction.”

The candles flickered and then flared brighter, casting long shadows that twisted and danced along the walls. The air thickened with an oppressive force, and the stone beneath Korin’s feet seemed to shift, as if the temple itself were coming alive. The voice returned, now louder and more commanding.

“Very well, Korin. The curse shall be yours to wield. But remember this: vengeance is a fire that can never be quenched. It will burn until there is nothing left of you but ash. Are you still willing to proceed?”

Korin hesitated for only a moment before answering. “Yes. I am ready.”

A blinding light enveloped him, and for a moment, he was lost in the sensation of power coursing through his veins. The pain of his past, the betrayal, the suffering—all of it faded into nothingness. All that remained was the singular, overwhelming desire for retribution.

The light faded, and Korin stood alone in the temple once more, his body thrumming with a new, dark energy. He could feel the curse within him, a force as cold and unrelenting as the winds that howled outside. The path ahead was clear, and Korin knew what he must do.

He turned and left the temple, his steps deliberate and measured. There was no turning back now. He would find his betrayer, and he would make them pay.

Months passed, and Korin’s name became a whisper in the shadows, a warning to those who crossed him. He had become a ghost, hunting his former comrades one by one. Each encounter was more brutal than the last, his vengeance unfolding with a cold precision that left no room for mercy.

His former friend, the one who had sold him out, had risen to power in a distant city, his name now synonymous with wealth and influence. But his success had come at a cost. Korin had followed the trail of blood, each step leading him closer to the man who had once been his brother.

When they finally met, it was in a grand hall, the air thick with the smell of opulence. The betrayer, now a man of great stature, stood before him, his eyes widening in recognition. “Korin,” he spat, his voice laced with both fear and disbelief. “You should have died. You were supposed to die.”

Korin’s eyes were cold, the curse within him thrumming with anticipation. “I did die,” he said softly. “But not in the way you expected.”

The betrayer took a step back, his hand reaching for the dagger at his side. “You don’t understand. I did what I had to do. You were weak. You always were.”

Korin’s laugh was dark and hollow. “Weak? No. It was you who were weak. You sold your soul for power, and now it is time to pay the price.”

With a swift movement, Korin reached out, his fingers curling around the betrayer’s throat. The man struggled, gasping for breath, but it was useless. The curse had already begun its work, and the vengeance served cold was already taking its toll.

As the betrayer’s body convulsed, Korin’s thoughts drifted to the past, to the bond they had shared. It felt like a lifetime ago, but the betrayal still burned as brightly as ever. The curse had been his choice, but the man before him had chosen his own path, one that had led to this moment.

The betrayer’s body went limp in Korin’s grasp, and the curse that had fueled him slowly began to ebb away. The vengeance had been served, but it had not brought the satisfaction he had expected. Instead, it left a hollow emptiness within him, a realization that no amount of revenge could heal the wounds of betrayal.

Korin dropped the man’s lifeless body to the floor and turned away. The curse had been fulfilled, but the price had been steep. His soul was now as cold and barren as the land he had crossed to get here.

As he walked away from the lifeless form of his betrayer, Korin realized that revenge, no matter how sweet, could never undo the past. It could only consume the future.

And in the end, he was left with nothing but the chilling wind, the coldness of the curse, and the weight of his choices.

The End.
