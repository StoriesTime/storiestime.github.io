Drowned Kingdom: The Intriguing Tale of the Celtic City of Ys


In the heart of Brittany, where the Atlantic waves crash against the rugged coastline, lies a legend shrouded in mystery and sorrow. It is the tale of the Celtic city of Ys, a magnificent kingdom lost to the depths of the sea. The story of Ys begins with a powerful king named Gradlon, whose daughter Dahut was as beautiful as she was wilful.

Dahut, known for her enchanting voice and captivating charm, held the heart of many suitors but desired power above all else. She would often sneak out of the palace at night, her laughter mingling with the ocean breeze as she roamed the shores under the light of the moon. It was during one of these nocturnal escapades that Dahut encountered the enigmatic wizard, Malgven.

Malgven, with his silver hair and piercing gaze, was rumored to possess ancient knowledge and magic that surpassed any mortal’s understanding. Intrigued by Dahut’s thirst for power, he offered her a magical artefact: a golden key that unlocked the secrets of the deep seas. Mesmerized by the promise of unrivalled power, Dahut accepted the key without hesitation.

As days turned into nights, Dahut’s fascination with the key grew, and she soon convinced her father, King Gradlon, to allow her to use it to protect the city from invaders. Unbeknownst to Gradlon, the key held a dark secret – it controlled the floodgates that held back the relentless ocean from engulfing the city of Ys.

Driven by her desire for dominion, Dahut used the key to manipulate the tides, allowing ships laden with treasures to enter the city’s harbor while sealing the fate of those who dared to oppose her. The people of Ys whispered in fear of Dahut’s newfound powers, but none dared to confront the king’s beloved daughter.

As the city prospered under Dahut’s rule, whispers began to spread of a looming catastrophe – a punishment from the sea itself for meddling with forces beyond mortal comprehension. One fateful night, as a storm of unprecedented fury descended upon Ys, the ocean surged forth with a vengeance, breaching the floodgates that Dahut had so recklessly controlled.

The mighty waves crashed upon the city, swallowing buildings, streets, and lives in their unforgiving embrace. King Gradlon, in a desperate attempt to save his people, mounted his horse with Dahut clinging to him and rode towards higher ground. In the chaos that ensued, Malgven’s sinister laughter echoed through the tempest, his true intentions laid bare.

As the waters rose around them, Gradlon realized the extent of Dahut’s folly and the price they would pay for her thirst for power. With a heavy heart, he made a choice that would seal Ys’s fate forever. In a moment of sacrifice and sorrow, he flung Dahut into the raging sea, where she vanished beneath the waves, never to be seen again.

The city of Ys, once a jewel of the Celtic lands, sank beneath the waters, its towers and spires swallowed by the ocean’s depths. Legend has it that on stormy nights, the bells of Ys can still be heard tolling mournfully from beneath the waves, a haunting reminder of the kingdom lost to pride and greed.

And so, the tale of the Celtic city of Ys endures as a cautionary saga of hubris and consequence, a reminder that power sought at any cost inevitably leads to destruction. May its memory serve as a solemn warning to those who would dare to defy the natural order of the world, for the sea is unforgiving, and its depths hold secrets best left undisturbed.
