Meadow Musings: A Year in the Life of a Small Farm


The sun was just starting to peek over the horizon as I stepped out onto the porch, cradling a steaming cup of coffee in my hands. It was the first day of spring, and the farm was stirring to life around me. The dewy grass sparkled in the growing light, and the air was filled with the sweet scent of blooming apple trees. It was a moment of pure joy, and I felt grateful to be living my dream on this small farm in rural Vermont. The Early Days of Spring As I took my first sip of coffee, I gazed out at the landscape before me. The snow was melting, and the fields were beginning to transform from barren winter to vibrant green. It was a time of renewal, and I felt a sense of excitement and possibility. This was the moment I had been waiting for – the chance to plant seeds, tend crops, and nurture the land back to health. One of the first tasks on my agenda was to divide and replant the garlic. Last fall, I had harvested a bounty of scapes and now it was time to prepare the bulbs for their annual growing cycle. I carefully dug up the cloves, spreading them out on a wire rack to dry and cure. The pungent aroma filled the air, and I couldn't help but feel a sense of pride knowing that this field would soon be teeming with life. As I worked, I heard the usual spring welcomes from the resident creatures. A chorus of birdsong echoed across the meadow, punctuated by the distant moo of the cows in the nearby pasture. Even the chickens were clucking good mornings to one another, as if acknowledging the new season and all its potential. April and the Awakening of the Pastures With the garlic underway, I turned my attention to the fields that needed to be turned – literally and figuratively. The perennial flowers I had planted last fall had seen nothing of the attended care they required and the native grasses needed to be finger-metalled on down.
