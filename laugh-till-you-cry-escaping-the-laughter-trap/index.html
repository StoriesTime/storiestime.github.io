Laugh till you Cry: Escaping the Laughter Trap


Once upon a time, in the small, quaint town of McLaughlin, nestled in the hillsides of Pineapple Hollow, was a peculiar old amusement park. It began its life as Pineapple Hollow Amusement Park during the early 1900s, instilling joy and laughter into the hearts of countless visitors from all corners of the world. However, as years went by, the park fell into a state of disrepair, slowly becoming a symbol of decay rather than merriment.

It was here, among the abandoned roller coasters and forgotten games, that a young woman named Lucinda found herself, bound by an inexplicable force to a mysterious laughter that echoed throughout the park. Terrified and confused, she had already spent weeks trying to escape, but it seemed that no matter how hard she tried, she couldn’t break free from the laughter’s spell, finding herself captured in a never-ending cycle.

Loosely based upon the mysterious laughter that understands the true nature of Lucinda’s predicament, a group of five eccentric adventurers embarked on a journey to Master Pineapple’s restaurant, perched atop the highest hill behind the park. Obsessed with the enigmatic park and its passioneating Laughter music, they had devoted their lives to unravelling its secrets, running on adrenaline, whimsy, and a seemingly unparalleled supply of caffeinated tea. Strangely enough, they were convinced that this spectral eaterie held the answers that none but the laughter did.

Despite its appearance - an authentic mix of vintage jukebox melodies, cherry red vinyl banquette seating, and leopard print wallpaper - Master Pineapple’s offered a peculiar twist in its approach to laughter and fun. Though visitors may walk around and indulge in an unending feast of fried pineapple tacos, parrot burgers, and ice cream sundaes topped with umbrella sticks or soda syphons, minimal sound emanated from the patrons themselves. On the rare occasions where noise did permeate the air, it was more of a timid giggle or soft chuckle rather than absolute hysteria.

Seated at the door, they looked on, nodding in a conspiratorial manner to each other. Upon exchanging knowing glances, they agreed that something was undeniably off about this peculiar little gem. Waiting for an opportune moment to enter unseen, the group devised a plan. Each member was to employ their unique talents and quirks in a bid to uncover the truth behind the laughter’s mysterious hold over Lucinda.

Soon, the young and daring Mara, whose love for acrobatics could outshine the legendary Buster Keaton, took her place on the restaurant’s elevated dance floor. Spectators observed in awe as she moved, seeming to defy the gravity itself in a frenetic display of human agility.

Next in line was the ever-sarcastic Gareth, whose prowess for standing back and turning the most complicated of tasks into a mundane operation was the stuff of legends. He took on the challenge of persuading the staff to allow him a brief tour of the restaurant’s haunted kitchen.

Their sister, Edna, a brilliant poet, decided to envelop herself in a soft blue cloak, wrapping herself in a world of rhyme and rhythm. She perched herself atop the bar, improvising spirited verses for the various diners.

And finally, in came the fiery-tempered Isabelle, whose one-and-only purpose here tonight was to unleash the chaos of her violin. Though seemingly uncoordinated on foot, she did possess an inexplicable talent lying dormant within her; the ability to create haunting melodies that the silverware would, on a good day, reverberate to.

As night unfolded and inspiration heightened, laughter continued to entrap young, forlorn Lucinda, who had been pacing around the once-great Ferris wheel in search of a way to entertain the distant spirits of laughter. All the while, the adventurers continued with their foolhardy mission determined to rescue her.

It wasn’t until Edna had finished herthird poem about magic and sweet pastries that she noticed the laughter was beginning to quieten down. Slowly but surely, the distinct hum was fading away, as if a vacuum were being sucked within the park itself. Realising the power of their combined talents, they were suddenly presented with a shocking truth: it had worked.

Sensing that the danger was now behind them, the group emerged from the shadows only to find Lucinda, the once-laughing-hearted young woman, staring at them mutely. Surprised and perplexed by the group’s sudden arrival, Lucinda asked about the faint giggles that were still floating around, suggesting the laughter hadn’t entirely vanished. The adventurers tried to explain how their efforts had weakened the laughter’s hold over Lucinda, but their words were lost on a girl who had been locked away in a world of enchanting whimsy and noise.

Sharing moments filled with heart-stopping adventures or, in certain cases, awkward stumblings, they tried to bring Lucinda back to the real world. Unfortunately, their pleas and warnings fell on deaf ears as laughter seemed to beckon her back into its clutches. However, she refused to conform this time, determined to savour their company and finally remembering how she’d managed to escape the park all those weeks ago.

With the help of the gathered crowd, Master Pineapple’s patrons were able to guide the skeptical young woman to the restaurant, allowing her to mingle with them and offer her apologies for her unfathomable behaviour. As they sat together around the vintage cherry red banquette seating, ordering fetuccine al dente pineapple pasta and flame-grilled pineapple burgers à la mode, they exchanged stories, laughter - and tears - of their harrowing, yet unforgettable experiences in the once joyless, forgotten Pineapple Hollow Amusement Park.

Despite the ordeal, the five peculiar friends parted ways on that fateful night, promising to carry with them the unbreakable bond forged on the dance floor of laughter and tears, as well as the ever-alluring memories of Pineapple Hollow’s mysterious and captivating Master Pineapple’s restaurant. The park, once a pit of gloom, now twinkling with each ray of sun as it struck the rusting steel and peeling paint, had changed, its heart no longer echoing with the haunting, forsaken notes of laughter, but with the joyous little laughter of the people who had ventured beyond the depths of night to find the long-lost melodies of their dreams.
