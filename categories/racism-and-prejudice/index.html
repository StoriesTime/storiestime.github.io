Prejudice no longer defined Eldermoor, but the forging of new relationships had lit a spark for generations to come. Maya had grown up with a strong sense of her Latino heritage, yet she often felt the dual weight of cultural pride and the harsh realities of prejudice. At college, she had faced microaggressions, dismissive comments that chipped away at her self-esteem. “Where are you really from?” was a question she had grown tired of answering. Maya felt a shift within her as these voices harmonized into a song of shared pain but also a resilient hope.
