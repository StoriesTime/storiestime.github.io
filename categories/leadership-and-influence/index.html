In the midst of this turmoil, a woman named Elena whispered promises of guidance. She was a therapist by profession, well-acquainted with the fragility of human emotions. Throughout her career, she’d witnessed resilience flourish in unexpected forms, but as she stepped outside her apartment after the quake, the unimaginable scenes before her challenged all she’d learned. Instead of artistic murals that once adorned the city, grotesque images of ruin now occupied the streets. Smoke billowed from buildings like the cries of the lost. Elena’s instincts urged her forward. People looked to her not just for organizational prowess but also for solace during those dark moments. When they shared their fears of deep-seated loss, she didn’t shy away. The days took a toll on Elena. Conflicts arose as people’s emotions ran high, and fear often morphed into anger.
