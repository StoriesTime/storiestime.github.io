Frost Giants and Fire: A Norse Saga of Eternal Conflict


In the realm of Niflheim, where the air was as thick as the mist that shrouded the land, the Frost Giants stood as titans, their beards as stiff as the ice that encased their mountains. For centuries, they had watched the world beyond their frozen wasteland with a mix of curiosity and contempt. The mortals were fragile, easily swayed by the whims of the gods, and the giants had grown accustomed to their isolation. However, their tranquility was disrupted when the sparks of Muspelheim, the realm of fire, began to flicker across the border. It was a signal, a call to arms from the fiery clan, who for generations had worshipped at the altar of Surtur, the lord of flames. The giants, whose names were known only to the wind – Kóri, Jarki, and Finddr – recognized the threat. They bristled at the thought of fiery enclaves spreading like canker on the protected skin of Niflheim.
