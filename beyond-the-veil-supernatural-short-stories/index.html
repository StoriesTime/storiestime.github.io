Beyond the Veil: Supernatural Short Stories


The old mansion at the edge of Briarwood had been abandoned for decades. Weathered wood creaked in the wind, windows stared out like empty eye sockets, and the garden had long surrendered to wild tangles of ivy and thorn. Locals whispered tales of shadowy figures glimpsed through the cracked panes, eerie lights flickering at midnight, and a sorrow that seemed to seep from the very walls themselves.

Marla had heard the stories all her life, but they never touched her curiosity—until the night she found the pendant.

The Pendant and the Whispered Call

It was in her grandmother’s attic, buried beneath moth-eaten quilts and stacks of forgotten letters, that Marla discovered the pendant. A delicate silver charm shaped like a crescent moon, it held a faint glow that pulsed like a heartbeat. As she clasped it between her fingers, a whisper tickled her ear so softly she almost thought she imagined it.

“Beyond the veil,” it breathed.

Intrigued, Marla pocketed the pendant and, driven by a force she couldn’t explain, found herself drawn to the mansion before the night’s end.

Crossing the Threshold

Stepping into the mansion’s doorway was like passing into another realm. The air shimmered, thick with the scent of old paper and cold stone, and a faint chill traced down her spine. The pendant grew warmer against her chest.

She began to explore, light from her flashing phone revealing dust particles suspended in beams that sliced through the gloom. The walls were lined with faded portraits—faces solemn, some with eyes unnervingly lifelike, seeming to watch her every move.

In the parlor, the whispering grew louder, shifting from indecipherable murmurs to something more coherent.

“Seek… the heart… beneath the floor…”

Marla’s breath hitched. Beneath the floor? The wide oak planks creaked beneath her feet, but nothing seemed unusual. Then, in the corner, a tapestry fluttered despite the absence of breeze, revealing a trapdoor cleverly camouflaged within the floor.

Her fingers trembled as she lifted it, revealing a steep staircase that spiraled down into darkness.

The Chamber Below

Descending cautiously, Marla’s phone light flickered but remained steady enough to guide her steps. The air grew cooler, denser, as the staircase emptied into a circular chamber lined with ancient runes etched into stone.

At the center lay a stone pedestal, upon which rested a book bound in worn leather.

The pendant pulsed once more, illuminating shimmering words etched into the pedestal’s edge:

“To those who cross beyond the veil, the forgotten may speak, and the past may awaken. But heed this: not all truths bring peace.”

Opening the book carefully, Marla found pages filled with a mixture of faded ink and symbols she recognized from the runes. It was a journal—a chronicle of the mansion’s original owner, Elysia Thorne, a woman rumored to be a medium and keeper of secrets beyond mortal sight.

Elysia’s Legacy

The entries spoke of Elysia’s attempts to communicate with another realm, a parallel existence she called the Veil. She wrote of spirits trapped in the space between life and death, desperate to be heard. But her final entry chilled Marla to the core:

“The veil thins tonight. The borders between seen and unseen blur. What lies beyond is not empty, but filled with echoes—echoes of sorrow, regret, and rage. If the veil is pierced, one must be ready to face what dreams cannot contain.”

Suddenly, the room flickered; shadows danced wildly along the walls. A cold wind swept through the chamber, carrying a chorus of voices—some weeping, others murmuring words of warning. Marla clutched the pendant, which now shone brighter than ever.

Voices Beyond the Veil

“Help us,” a voice whispered, soft but urgent.

Another sobbed, “Remember… our stories…”

Marla realized the spirits were trapped in this in-between realm, forgotten by time, aching to be freed or understood. Their echoes had long been muffled by disbelief and silence.

With growing resolve, Marla read aloud phrases from the journal designed to open a passage—a ritual Elysia had documented but never completed.

The chamber began to shimmer, the stone walls dissolving like mist, revealing a twilight expanse filled with shifting figures—ghostly forms wandering in a liminal landscape of lost memories and forgotten dreams.

Embracing the Forgotten

Marla stepped into the grey expanse, heart pounding with a mix of fear and compassion. The spirits reached out, not with menace but with longing for connection.

She listened, each whisper a thread weaving together a tapestry of lives cut short, of stories untold. There was sorrow for lost loves, regret for deeds unmade, joy for moments that shone through the darkness.

By recognizing them, Marla felt a shift—a release.

The veil began to close gently behind her as the spirits faded, but not into oblivion; instead, they left a warmth, a peace that touched the mansion and the world beyond.

The Mansion’s New Beginning

Marla emerged from the chamber changed. The pendant, once pulsing with unrest, now rested cool and still against her skin.

In the days that followed, the mansion seemed less a place of fear and more a sanctuary—a bridge between worlds, a space where stories were honored and echoes could finally rest.

Word spread of strange, beautiful lights and soothing music drifting from the house at twilight. The villagers, once wary, began to leave flowers by the gate, a quiet acknowledgment of the veil lifted by one who dared to listen.

Marla kept the journal, vowing to continue Elysia’s work—bridging the gap between the living and those beyond, honoring the echoes of the forgotten.

Beyond the veil, she had discovered that the thin line between worlds held infinite tales waiting to be heard—and sometimes, a little courage is all it takes to unravel the mysteries that bind us all.
