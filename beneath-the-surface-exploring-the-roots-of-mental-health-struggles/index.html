Beneath the Surface: Exploring the Roots of Mental Health Struggles


It had been a quiet evening when Marissa found herself sitting on the edge of her bed, staring into the dim glow of her phone screen. The messages had stopped coming in—at least for the moment—and the familiar tension in her chest seemed to loosen, but it was fleeting. Her mind was still racing. She had made it through another day, but in the silence, the weight of her own thoughts pressed down on her like an invisible force.

Her heart pounded as memories from her childhood flickered across her mind. There had always been something hidden beneath the surface of her life, something she had never fully understood. She was tired of pretending everything was fine, tired of fighting her own mind, tired of feeling like she was constantly battling something she couldn’t name.

But here, alone in her room, she finally began to ask herself the questions that had always lingered just beneath the surface: Why do I feel this way? What’s really going on with me?

Marissa had always been the picture of strength, at least on the outside. She excelled in school, maintained a close-knit group of friends, and worked hard to meet her family’s expectations. But despite the outward appearance of success, something inside her had always felt… off. It was as if her life was a carefully constructed façade, hiding an underlying struggle she couldn’t escape.

The Unseen Struggles

For years, Marissa had suppressed the thoughts that bubbled beneath her consciousness. Mental health struggles, she thought, were for people who couldn’t “handle life.” She had always believed that if she worked harder, stayed busier, and kept pushing through, the feelings of sadness, frustration, and self-doubt would eventually go away. But they never did. They only seemed to grow stronger, more persistent, lurking in the background, waiting for her to slow down so they could pounce.

When she first began feeling overwhelmed, it was easy to dismiss it. “Everyone feels like this sometimes,” she would tell herself. But as time passed, the feelings grew more intense, until they seeped into every aspect of her life. The low moods would stretch into weeks, robbing her of the energy to do even the simplest tasks. Her sleep patterns became erratic, and she found it harder and harder to get out of bed in the morning. Her social interactions began to feel like a performance—smiling, nodding, pretending to care about small talk, all the while feeling like she was drowning inside.

There were moments when the struggle seemed to lighten, and she thought she might be okay. But then it would come back, always when she least expected it. The anxiety would take over in waves, gripping her chest and making it hard to breathe. Sometimes, it was the weight of overwhelming sadness, and other times, it was the constant hum of worry that refused to turn off. The deeper she dug into her own mind, the more confused she became. It was like trying to solve a puzzle that didn’t have all the pieces.

The Roots of Mental Health Struggles

One afternoon, during a therapy session that Marissa had reluctantly started attending, her therapist introduced a concept that resonated deeply with her—the roots of mental health struggles. Her therapist explained how many mental health conditions, like depression, anxiety, and even more complex issues like PTSD, often had origins in childhood experiences. The mind and body were interconnected, and unresolved trauma, neglect, or stress from an early age could manifest in adulthood as psychological and emotional struggles.

Marissa listened intently, the words echoing in her mind. She had grown up in a household where emotions were often overlooked or dismissed. Her parents were loving but busy, caught up in their own struggles to make ends meet. There were times when she felt invisible, her needs for connection, understanding, and care overlooked in the hustle and bustle of daily life. It wasn’t that her parents were neglectful; it was that they simply didn’t know how to support her emotional needs. They did what they thought was best, but Marissa had learned to bury her emotions deep inside because there was no room for them in her world.

As she reflected on this, she realized that many of her current struggles—her feelings of inadequacy, her anxiety, her sense of disconnection—were rooted in those early experiences. She had learned to suppress her emotions because that was the only way to survive in her family’s environment. But now, in her adult life, those suppressed feelings were coming back to haunt her, disrupting her relationships, her career, and her peace of mind.

The Impact of Unresolved Trauma

Trauma, whether it’s from childhood neglect, emotional abuse, or even witnessing a traumatic event, can leave deep scars on a person’s psyche. But trauma doesn’t always look the way people expect. It’s not always physical. In fact, emotional trauma can be just as damaging, if not more so, than physical injuries. And unlike physical wounds, emotional wounds aren’t always visible. They don’t leave scars on the skin, but they can leave deep impressions on the mind and soul.

For Marissa, the realization that her mental health struggles were tied to her early experiences was both liberating and painful. She had spent so much time blaming herself for not being “strong enough” to handle life’s challenges. But now, she began to understand that her struggles were not a reflection of her weaknesses—they were the result of unhealed wounds from her past. It was like a heavy weight had been lifted from her shoulders, and she could finally see the path toward healing, though it was still unclear and daunting.

The process of healing from trauma is complex and requires time, patience, and a willingness to confront difficult emotions. For Marissa, this meant opening up to her therapist about her past, acknowledging the pain that had been buried for so long, and allowing herself to feel those emotions without judgment. It meant learning to reframe her thoughts and beliefs about herself and the world around her, challenging the narratives that had held her back for years.

The Importance of Seeking Help

One of the most important lessons Marissa learned was the value of seeking help. For years, she had tried to navigate her mental health struggles on her own, believing that asking for help was a sign of weakness. But as she began to explore the roots of her struggles, she realized that reaching out for support was actually an act of strength. There was no shame in needing help to heal, and in fact, seeking help was a crucial step toward regaining control over her life.

Whether it’s through therapy, support groups, or confiding in a trusted friend, seeking help is essential in addressing mental health struggles. Marissa had spent so many years trapped in her own mind, trying to solve problems she couldn’t understand. But now, she was learning that healing was not something that had to be done alone. There were people who could guide her through the process, and she didn’t have to face her pain in isolation.

Finding Hope in the Struggle

As the weeks passed, Marissa began to see small changes in herself. She started taking better care of her physical health, focusing on nutrition, exercise, and sleep. She allowed herself to rest when she needed it, without feeling guilty. She practiced mindfulness, learning to live in the present moment instead of getting caught up in past regrets or future anxieties.

Most importantly, she began to forgive herself. For so long, she had blamed herself for her mental health struggles, believing that she was somehow failing at life. But now, she realized that her struggles were not a reflection of her worth. They were simply part of her journey, and they didn’t define who she was.

The road to healing wasn’t easy, and there were still days when the darkness crept in. But Marissa had learned that healing wasn’t a linear process. There would be setbacks, yes, but there would also be moments of triumph. She was no longer afraid to face the parts of herself that had been buried for so long. She was learning to embrace her story, with all its twists and turns, and to understand that she was worthy of love, support, and compassion—even from herself.

Beneath the surface, there was still much work to be done, but for the first time in a long time, Marissa felt a glimmer of hope. She was no longer just surviving—she was beginning to heal. And that, in itself, was the most powerful step she could take.
