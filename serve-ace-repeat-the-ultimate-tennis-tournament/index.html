Serve, Ace, Repeat: The Ultimate Tennis Tournament


Once upon a time, in the thriving, sun-soaked city of Sunnyvale, the most incredible tennis tournament in the history of the world descended upon the quaint spectators who gathered with bated breath. Aerial banners with stylized eagles and whimsical font unfurled overhead, their proclamations celebrating “Serve, Ace, Repeat: The Ultimate Tennis Tournament”. Beneath one such drapery, a frenzied father attempted to peel his rowdy, roller-skating toddler from a gourmet hot dog stand, crying, “Come on, Junior! There are going to be tennis stars!”

Sunnyvale’s tennis courts transformed into a shimmering mecca, bustling with excitement, creating a marvelous mirage of high-strung sports aficionados and innocent onlookers. Five grand, improbably talented and remarkably handsome athletes arrived, garnering respect and admiration from the public. These undeniable champions of sport included Evelynn “Ace” Arlinson, an enviable athletic goddess who once denied a romantic suitor by politely explaining, “I’d rather date my tennis racket”; Carson “Serve” Sturbridge, an Oceanic wave-rider whose relaxed, yet stoic air whispered of crystal-clear waters and bracing sea salts; Jasper “Repeat” Renwick, a stuttering, studious type who appeared to have more affinity for numbers and periodical tables than courtside woes; Ralph “Ball” Ratcliff, a jovial, gut-busting raconteur who delighted audiences with recent exploits involving monkeys, World War II submarines, and mysterious strangers in Swiss hotels; and last, but not least, Dr. Vivian “Spinner” Sexton, a wizened sage-clad, physician who just happened to conduct secret, clandestine investigations into what these athletes faced.

As the fanfare neared, in their respective lockers, the five competitors prepped for the unprecedented battle. Evelynn tightened every cord in her glossy dance leotard, her agile fingers fluidly preparing her perfectly-proportioned, disciplined body for battle. Carson’s fingers snuck through shells in abandonment, shivering as they fell, an uncanny relational dance to his rhythmic breaths. Jasper focused, biting his lip as his thoughts swirled like an entangled spin spiral, his knuckles whitening with every minute push, he assuaged his fears solely through self-calculated equations. Ralph cracked up at his own jokes, unraveling a ball of yarn of unpredictability, laughing, and inadvertently, arousing his competitive spirit.

Dr. Vivian, largely unflappable, nevertheless displayed a keen interest as she noted the peculiarities in each of her charges. Though discreet, the doctor had over the years gathered countless micro-details about each athlete, stealthily gathering incriminating evidence and filling her clandestine journals with idiosyncrasies. These revealing tabs of each champion became gossip fodder for the twisted souls she shared with, lovingly referring to them as “The Ace Files.”

With the sun sinking and its shimmering light finally embracing the modest spectacle of tennis royalty, the athletes brought on their determination and preparation. With the rapt attention no less than the whole world, Serve, Ace, and Repeat, perspiration irradiating, conquered each match with their courageous spirit, overwhelming force, and haughty prowess. The yell of victory echoed through the valley of the Gambled Club of Tennis Courts, held up high for all to satisfy infinite vicarious cavorting beams in the captain of succor seen.

That night, Ralph summoned the energy to recount his victories with fabled voice freewheelers, closely following the Weeping Winers who retired bashfully into their everlasting silhouettes. Regardless of the chaotic and mercurial nature of the world where unbelievable stories often remain untold, the lustrous court of the grand tournament, the rise of Sunnyvale’s legendary triumvirate, would forever echo the brilliance of the five unmatched competitors – along with their loyal, undaunted conscientious observer, Dr. Vivian. Thus continue endless legends to inspire for the ages.
