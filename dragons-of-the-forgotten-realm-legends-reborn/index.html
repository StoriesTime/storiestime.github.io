Dragons of the Forgotten Realm: Legends Reborn


The winds howled across the mountains, carrying with them the whispers of ancient secrets. Far beyond the reaches of time, hidden in the forgotten corners of the world, lay the realm of Velaris. A realm where dragons once roamed free, their scales shimmering like precious gemstones in the sunlight. But those days had long since passed. The dragons had vanished, their presence nothing more than myth and legend.

Yet, the legends of the dragons of Velaris were far from forgotten.

Chapter 1: The Awakening

Elara sat at the edge of the great forest, her fingers brushing the pages of an ancient tome she had discovered in the ruins of her village. The firelight flickered and danced around her, casting long shadows on the weathered stone walls. The book was bound in dragonhide, its pages yellowed with age, but the words within still pulsed with a strange energy.

“For centuries, the dragons slumbered,” Elara whispered, reading aloud from the book. “Their hearts bound to the land, waiting for the time when the Forgotten Realm would call them forth once more.”

Her village, hidden deep within the western reaches of the continent, had long been isolated from the rest of the world. But Elara had always felt a pull toward something greater, something beyond the quiet existence she had known. The book had come to her in a dream, its presence urging her to seek out the truth of the dragons’ disappearance.

As she turned the pages, a strange sensation gripped her. The ground beneath her feet trembled ever so slightly, as if something ancient was awakening from a long slumber. She shook off the feeling, dismissing it as the result of too many nights spent in the flickering glow of the fire.

But deep within her, Elara knew that the legends were more than just stories. The dragons of the Forgotten Realm were real. And they were returning.

Chapter 2: The Prophecy

The village elder, a woman named Maelis, was the only one who seemed to understand the weight of Elara’s discovery. The elder had lived for decades, her life intertwined with the myths and prophecies that had shaped Velaris for generations.

“Your dreams have led you to the truth, child,” Maelis said, her voice like the rustle of leaves in the wind. “The dragons are not gone. They are merely waiting, dormant, until the time is right.”

Elara sat in the elder’s small cottage, her mind racing. “But why? Why did they leave us? And why now?”

Maelis closed her eyes and sighed, the weight of history pressing heavily upon her. “It is said that when the Forgotten Realm is in peril, the dragons will rise to protect it. But they will not return without a cause. Their hearts are tied to the land itself, and if it is in danger, they will awaken to restore balance.”

“Peril?” Elara asked, her brow furrowing. “What kind of danger could threaten the entire realm?”

Maelis did not answer immediately. Instead, she rose from her seat and moved to a shelf lined with scrolls and artifacts. She pulled down a small, intricately carved box, its surface covered in ancient symbols. With a soft click, she opened it, revealing a crystal that glowed with an inner light.

“This,” Maelis said, holding the crystal up to the firelight, “is the Heart of Velaris. It is said to hold the very essence of the realm itself. If it is shattered, the land will fall into chaos, and with it, the dragons will be lost forever.”

Elara’s eyes widened. “But… why haven’t we heard of this? Why isn’t anyone else preparing for the threat?”

“Because the Heart has not yet shown signs of breaking,” Maelis replied. “But the signs are there, child. The dragons feel it. And you, Elara, are the one they have chosen to awaken them.”

Chapter 3: The Dragon’s Call

The journey ahead was fraught with danger. Elara knew that the path she was about to take would lead her into the heart of the Forgotten Realm, a land where even the bravest warriors dared not tread. But she had no choice. The dragons were awakening, and she had a destiny to fulfill.

With nothing more than a sword at her side and the ancient tome clutched tightly in her hands, Elara set off toward the mountains that bordered the realm. The road was long and treacherous, filled with the remnants of forgotten civilizations and the ever-present sense that something was watching from the shadows.

As she made her way deeper into the wilderness, the air grew colder, and the once-verdant forests gave way to barren plains of jagged rock. The further she traveled, the more the weight of the land seemed to press down upon her. She could feel it—the pulse of the Heart of Velaris, faint but undeniable, calling to her from deep within the mountains.

One evening, as she camped beneath the stars, a strange noise echoed through the night air. It was distant at first, but then it grew louder, a low rumble that vibrated through the ground. Elara’s heart raced as the sound grew nearer, and before she could react, a massive shadow passed overhead.

Looking up, Elara gasped in awe. A great dragon, its scales gleaming like molten gold, soared through the night sky. Its wings beat the air with a power that seemed to shake the very earth beneath her feet.

The dragon landed before her with a graceful, earth-shaking thud, its eyes glowing like twin embers. Elara stood frozen, her breath caught in her throat.

“You seek the dragons, child,” the dragon spoke, its voice a deep, resonant echo in her mind. “The time has come to fulfill your destiny.”

Elara took a step forward, her voice barely a whisper. “I… I don’t understand. Why me?”

The dragon tilted its head, regarding her with ancient, knowing eyes. “Because you carry the blood of the last dragonriders. You are the last of your kind, the one chosen to bridge the gap between humans and dragons once more.”

Chapter 4: The Last Dragonriders

The history of the dragonriders was a long and storied one. For centuries, the bond between dragons and humans had been a powerful force for good, with riders and dragons standing side by side to protect Velaris from the forces of darkness that sought to destroy it. But over time, the bond had weakened. The dragonriders were hunted down, their existence erased from history, until the dragons themselves vanished into the forgotten corners of the world.

Elara’s bloodline, however, had never been fully lost. The last dragonriders had hidden their lineage in secret, passing down their knowledge through whispers and dreams. And now, it seemed, the dragons had chosen Elara to revive that ancient bond.

“Your destiny is not merely to awaken us,” the dragon said, its voice growing softer. “You must unite the dragons and the people of Velaris once more, before the Heart of Velaris is shattered and all is lost.”

Elara nodded, understanding the weight of her task. But she knew she could not do it alone.

“You are not alone,” the dragon said, as if reading her thoughts. “The others will come. The other dragons, and the riders who are destined to join you. Together, you will face the coming darkness.”

Chapter 5: The Gathering of the Riders

As Elara continued her journey, the dragons began to reveal themselves one by one. Each dragon was unique, with its own personality and abilities, but they shared one common purpose—to protect Velaris from the forces that sought to tear it apart.

The first to join her was Kaelen, a dragon with shimmering silver scales and eyes that glowed with a quiet intelligence. Kaelen had once been the leader of the dragonriders, and though his strength had faded with time, his wisdom remained as sharp as ever.

Next came Nyx, a dark dragon whose scales seemed to absorb the light around her. Nyx was a master of shadows, able to slip through the smallest cracks and strike without warning. She was elusive and mysterious, but her loyalty to Elara was unquestionable.

Then there was Thalor, a massive dragon whose scales were like polished emeralds, and whose roar could shake the heavens themselves. Thalor was a warrior at heart, proud and unyielding, but beneath his tough exterior lay a deep respect for Elara and her mission.

Together, they formed the last of the dragonriders, a bond that would not only save Velaris but restore the ancient magic that had once been lost to time.

Chapter 6: The Final Battle

The forces of darkness, led by the ancient sorcerer Vornak, were closing in on the Heart of Velaris. His dark magic had corrupted the land, causing the very earth to tremble and crack. The dragons and their riders would have to face him in a final, desperate battle to prevent the realm from falling into eternal darkness.

Elara, now fully bonded with her dragon, stood at the forefront of the battle. The sky above was filled with the roars of dragons and the clash of steel, as the riders fought to protect the Heart from Vornak’s army.

As the battle raged on, Elara knew that the true power of the dragons lay not in their strength, but in their unity. Together, they were unstoppable.

With a final, mighty roar, the dragons unleashed their full power, driving Vornak and his forces back into the shadows from whence they came. The Heart of Velaris pulsed with renewed energy, its light banishing the darkness that had threatened to consume the realm.

Epilogue: A New Age

With Vornak defeated and peace restored, the dragons once again took their place as protectors of Velaris. The bond between dragons and humans was stronger than ever, and Elara, now known as the Dragon Queen, led her people into a new age of prosperity.

The legends of the dragons of the Forgotten Realm were no longer just stories—they were the truth, reborn.

And as long as the Heart of Velaris continued to beat, the dragons would watch over the realm, their legacy forever intertwined with the fate of the world.

Thus, the legend of the Dragons of the Forgotten Realm was reborn, a story of unity, courage, and the eternal bond between dragons and humans.
