Hasta La Victoria, Senor! A Historical Fiction of the Alhambra


The sun began to set over the sprawling and haunting beauty of the Alhambra, casting a golden glow over the red-stone towers and mosaic courtyards of the legendary citadel. High above the city of Granada, nestled in Spain’s very own Sierra Nevada mountains, this was more than just another picturesque evening. Here, beneath the stony grandeur, there awaited a tale as rich and evocative as the colors of the Alhambra itself.

An intriguing figure darted through these parts; a man of intellect and audacity, who was known as Alaric, a trusted advisor to the great Boabdil, the last Nasrid Emperor of Granada. Alaric was the only person whom Boabdil would trust with planning and orchestrating his most secret and clandestine operations. The room twinkled with intrigue as Alaric informed the Emperor of whispers that had arrived from the far reaches of the famished kingdom, declaring their imminent approach.

“The enemy’s fleet is humming with power; bankers from Venice and flooding with men and tools of war, ’tis said they come like a tempest,” Alaric narrated. Boabdil, the bereft but visionary leader, turned pale but resolute. In his dramatic determination, the tattered remnants of the once-flourishing and elegant kingdom found hope.

As Alaric voyaged on, his eyes caught the glint of the evening sky upon the ornate bauble, a symbol of hope and fortune for the Alhambra: a golden key, its shadow caressing the complicated arabesques of the parapets above the mesmerizing courtyard. Hesitating briefly, Alaric reached out a cautious and knowing hand. A hush settled upon the interior of the palace.

The golden key chimed and resonated, its sound echoing through the grand chambers, ominously answering the call of the doomed kingdom. Alaric stood frozen, the golden glow from outside reflecting in his wide, fearful eyes. Suddenly, his unwavering resolve became apparent. This was not just any key; it was the Key of Granada, a relic passed through generations to a worthy leader, a talisman imbued with an ancient power.

For months, Alaric and Boabdil used the key in their clandestine operations, bouncing from shadowed stairway to hidden chamber. Alaric brought forth an unusual sequence of welterweights, chandeliers, and sundry, each carrying their own whispered secrets and absorbing the Alhambra’s storied history. These items were subtle harbingers of the battle for the kingdom; symbols imbued with ancient purpose, sometimes bizarre, sometimes dangerous.

But in the end, all that remains is the tale of the last bravo paradise; Granada’s ultimate surrender to the gun and guile of the invading Christians. The waning light illuminated the somber Alaric, who approached the golden Key of Granada one last time. His intent gaze then wandered to the grand ballroom, where the ghosts of grandeur once danced and reveled in their lavish paradise.

A whispering suspicion raced through his mind as he stood before the room. His eyes darted to the delicate coffer that had lain dormant all these months, waiting for its moment to be unveiled. The key opened the coffer; inside gleamed the Alhambra with one more secret chamber. A silver horn lay nestled within, its presence sparking questions.

“Sound this horn,” Alaric urged, “and all of Granada shall rise again!” Boabdil paused, his gaze flickering between his loyal advisor and the mysterious horn. Hope weighed heavily against the somber mantle of loss and defeat.

As dusk settled over the Alhambra one last time for many generations, Boabdil’s wavering decision filled the chamber. And in that moment, as he weighed the ancient Silver Horn, a shimmering glint appeared in the soft twilight - the reflection of the last golden key as it fell from his fingers and plummeted into the abyss.

With a roar from Boabdil, the grand courtyard echoed as the Key plummeted into the encircling shadows. It fell through the earth, far below the visage of the Alhambra. Moments later, a shattering sound broke the silence, and golden light flooded from deep within the stony edifice. Another golden key had fallen, but the olivine one remained concealed in darkness, its purpose fulfilled.

In that hour, while the Nasrid towered collapsed one by one, removing the vestiges of the Alhambra from the sight of men for generations, the lords of the lost world stole away to oblivion, as the kings, queens, sultans, and proud soldiers of Granada disappeared into the machinations of earth, stone, and fate.

And when, in long, ageless future times the sun rose above the silent Alhambra, the usurpers fell upon the empty lands. The remaining keys to Granada welled with the ancient mysteries of lost memory, a weak light amid the dark of the unsaid and the shadows of time passed.

The Key of Granada lay still, silent and echo-free, in the cold decline of a world that was. Now devoid of any heartbeat or secret, that burdened relic still retains the knowledge of a kingdom that was once known throughout the desert lands, bound to its people and legends, until the end of all endings.

The End
