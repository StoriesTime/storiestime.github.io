Harmonious Pursuits: From Grammy Winners to Budding Geniuses, Inspiring Stories about Music


In the magic-lit alleyways of Montreux, where time has a peculiar glow over the cobblestone streets and forgotten memories, Poppet used to roam. A scrappy, folksy ingénue with a jazzy_notifier heart, she was on an ambitious search of sorts - restoring the glory days of her recently deceased grandfather’s lost band, The Windbreakers. Immersed in a quest like no other, she meticulously poked and plundered dusty archives, archaeological proof that once placed in the right lap –was magic.

Poppet, with her magnetic curls and fearless indigo eyes, succeeded somehow in courting the elusive spirits of the past, turning up hidden treasures in unassuming boxes. An untouched saxophone here, a moth-eaten drum there, and a hidden cache of dog-eared sheet music glowed like gold in her determined hands.

Harmonious Pursuits

One rainy afternoon, as Poppet touched the weather-beaten cover of a tattered library book, she stumbled upon the rare 1968 vinyl titled “Harmonious Pursuits,” by a one-time only collaboration of young and ambitious Grammy winners. Intrigued, she hurried home, the echoes of their music filling the room and her heart. After playing it repeatedly, the tracks began to blur one into the other like a melancholic symphony echoing in her memory book of forgotten melodies.

The story of the Harmonious Pursuits – the impromptu symphony of Earth, Wind & Fire, The Rolling Stones, and Eric Clapton – was not universally acclaimed. Many argued that the motives from one’s soul they lifted was shallow and fleeting, exploiting youth itself as a commercial tool. Poppet wanted to prove them wrong.

Together with her newly discovered cousin, Fetch, a quick-witted comedian who occasionally fooled around at clubs on the same block The Windbreakers once roamed, she spun together a plan. Hidden in her grandfather’s tunes, they composed a story waiting to be told – his tale of longing, lost and later found. Poppet pressed play on the vinyl and sang with her soul into the mic, while Fetch ran barefoot in the rain, strumming his guitar, dancing, laughing and begging life to hear the genuine harmonious pursue, unchanged.

Primal Beats of Inspiration

One fateful night at the Montreux Jazz Festival, that same place where her grandfather fell in love with jazz in his youth, Poppet and Fetch were given the rare opportunity to delcare their musical roots on the same stage. Their song told the story of The Windbreakers, once more a part of the eclectic mix of dreamers, poets, and musicians on this night. Their presence stirred a lush bloom of vibrating colors of the rainbow and haunting beats that won over the most critical of spirits in the crowd.

That night brought a sea of change – for Poppet and Fetch. They had found a shared voice that inspired others to see their own torches burning in the dark. Gehard, the famous Teddy Bear band’s accordion player who also held the keys to the Montreux’s art corridors, gifted Poppet their lost shadow of inspiration – an old accordion that smelled of rust, sweat, and time. A relic that incantations of emotions tucked into its ancient, moth-eaten notes.

On this EP, she would stitch together the thin veil of sound that once led The Windbreakers into music history. The keepsakes of the past now echoed in full harmony again in the memory of her grandfather – his footprints meticulously noted in the moss between the cobblestones of Montreux. Through this journey of Harmonious Pursuits, Poppet and Fetch embarked on a personal revolution, weaving music like a neat line between an illustrious past and a vibrant future.
