The Forgotten Gods: Short Stories of Mythical Realms


In the age when the cosmos brimmed with life and mystery, the realms of gods sprawled across the vast expanse of existence. Among these celestial beings, some were celebrated and revered, while others gradually faded into oblivion. These forgotten gods, abandoned by time and memory, lingered at the edges of myth, their tales whispered only by winds through ancient trees or carried upon the tides of dream.

I. The Weeping Grove

Long before men took to carving stories into stone, the Weeping Grove was a sanctuary where the god Salethar dwelled. A deity of sorrow and healing, Salethar’s touch could ease the most grievous of wounds—not just physical, but those of the heart and soul. The grove was alive with silver-leaved trees that shimmered in perpetual twilight, their branches weeping dew that could mend broken spirits.

Villagers nearby often left offerings—a small bowl of honeyed water or a sprig of lavender—hoping to gain favor and solace. Yet as the years passed and new gods of war and fortune captured the people’s hearts, Salethar’s name grew silent. The grove, once a vibrant echo of compassion, became a shadowed memory.

One dusk, as twilight kissed the leaves, a young woman named Elara stumbled into the grove, fleeing grief that gnawed at her mind. With tears streaking her dirt-smudged face, she collapsed beneath a weeping tree. To her astonishment, the dew dripped softly onto her skin, warmth spreading through her as the sorrow lifted. Elara awoke to a quiet trembling in the air—it was Salethar, awakening from slumber, stirred by her need.

“Why have you forgotten me?” he asked, voice like wind through leaves.

Elara, startled yet comforted, whispered, “The world has no place for grief now.”

Salethar’s eyes glimmered sad and infinite. “Grief is the seed of healing. Without sorrow, joy is hollow.”

That night, a pact was made between goddess and mortal: Elara would sing Salethar’s forgotten songs, and in return, the god would grant peace to those who sought him. Word spread subtly, and slowly the Weeping Grove flourished anew, a hidden place where healing was whispered back into the world.

II. The Clockmaker’s Idol

Beyond the realm of glaciers and frost, in a city where time was sacred and counted, there existed a forgotten god called Ardarel. Once the divine master of time’s passage, Ardarel crafted the Eon Clock—a colossal, intricate mechanism that marked the rhythm of days and seasons. His fingers, nimble as mercurial winds, wove destinies like threads into a tapestry.

But as mortals warped time for their ambitions—through machines, calendars, and commerce—Ardarel’s influence waned. The idol of the god, a delicate clockwork figure spun from bronze and crystal, gathered dust in the market square, overlooked and neglected.

A curious child named Tovin discovered the idol during a festival. With trembling hands, he wound the tiny key on its back, and the clock burst into life, opening shimmering portals into the past and future. Through these passages, Tovin glimpsed the memories of the forgotten gods, their stories of creation, love, and loss.

In his heart, he understood the significance of Ardarel’s gift: time was not merely a measure but a living, breathing force that connected all beings. His joy rekindled the spirit of Ardarel, who whispered from the shifting shadows, “Remember, child—it is not time that enslaves us, but how we choose to live within it.”

Tovin became the guardian of the clock, a keeper of sacred hours and forgotten tales. Through him, Ardarel’s presence was restored—not as a ruler of time’s chains, but as a guide to its wonder.

III. The Dance of the Starborn Twins

In the deepest night sky, where constellations weave their silvery patterns, lurked gods of ancient light—Myrion and Lyra, the Starborn Twins. Their celestial dance created the shifting constellations, influencing fate and granting glimpses of hope to lost travelers.

But when mortals ceased to read the stars, turning instead to the cold logic of machines, the twins’ glow faded, and their dance grew faint. They fell to the mortal realm, their once radiant forms dimmed, now wandering the earth in human guise.

Separated by fate and yearning, they wandered cities and forests, seeking each other across the vastness. One night, under a rain-soaked sky, fate intervened. A young astronomer named Kira, tirelessly charting the dying stars, noticed a strange pattern forming—a celestial dance rekindled by unseen hands.

With a breathless heart, she traced her telescope toward the horizon and beheld the twins, locked in a mortal embrace. Their union reignited the light of the heavens, rousing other forgotten deities and reminding the world of the sacred mysteries writ in starlight.

Myrion and Lyra’s story resurfaced in myth and song, rekindling wonder in a world otherwise blinded by the mundane.

IV. The Silent Forge

Deep within the molten core of a dormant volcano, the god Volkar once forged weapons that could shape destinies. His very breath was fire, his hammer thunder in the forge. Mortals praised him when crafting swords and tools, invoking his name in oaths.

Yet as the age of miracles faded, and iron gave way to brittle machines, Volkar’s forge grew cold and silent. The volcano, once a beacon of creation, became a barren husk.

A blacksmith named Jens, disillusioned with mechanized factories, sought the ancient heat to rekindle his soul’s spark. He ventured into the volcano’s heart, where ashes whispered legends long forgotten. There, Jens found the dormant forge and, with reverence, struck his hammer.

A spark blossomed into flame. Volkar’s spirit stirred, manifesting as molten shadows and glowing eyes. “You bring life to the silent forge,” the god said. “Through your will, I return.”

Jens forged a blade imbued with both mortal toil and divine fire, a symbol of lost craftsmanship and enduring spirit. Through the blade’s legacy, Volkar’s flame danced once more, reminding all that creation requires both passion and preservation.

V. The Serpent’s Promise

Not all forgotten gods dwelled among light and glory. In the depths of tangled jungles, the serpent god Zha’kel slithered through mist and myth. A deity of secrets and hidden knowledge, Zha’kel whispered truths to those brave enough to listen.

Centuries ago, Zha’kel’s voice shaped the fates of kingdoms and scholars. Yet fear of the unknown and darkness pushed his worship into shadows. Only a few dared seek him beneath the sprawling canopy.

Isla, a wandering scholar with a thirst for forbidden wisdom, ventured into the jungle’s heart, drawn by tales of Zha’kel’s promise. She found a shimmering serpent coiled atop ancient glyphs, eyes gleaming with silent power.

“Speak, and I shall teach,” the god hissed.

Isla listened as Zha’kel revealed knowledge that could unbind minds and challenge reality. But knowledge came at a price—a fragment of her soul to be forever entwined with the serpent’s ancient essence.

Accepting the bargain, Isla became a vessel for both truth and enigma, carrying forward the god’s legacy in whispered words and forgotten languages, ensuring the serpent’s promise endured beyond memory.

Epilogue: Echoes in the New Dawn

The Forgotten Gods are not lost but wait patiently in the folds of time and space, in the hidden crevices of human belief and longing. Their stories, once vibrant and loud, now murmur quietly in dreams, in the flicker of candle flames, in the stillness before dawn.

Each forgotten god reflects the facets of mortal experience—grief and healing, time and destiny, love and light, craft and creation, knowledge and mystery. Through the hands of seekers like Elara, Tovin, Kira, Jens, and Isla, these divine echoes return, shaping the world anew.

The forgotten are merely waiting—for when the world needs them, their myths shall awaken, and the realms of gods will shimmer once again.

The Forgotten Gods: Short Stories of Mythical Realms invites you to listen for those soft whispers in the wind, to look beyond the veil of time, and to remember that no god is truly lost; they live on in the stories we tell and the mysteries we cherish.
