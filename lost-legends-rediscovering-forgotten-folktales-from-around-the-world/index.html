Lost Legends: Rediscovering Forgotten Folktales from Around the World


In the rugged terrain of Himachal Pradesh, India, lies a region shrouded in mystery and steeped in folklore. The whispering wilds of the Western Himalayas have long been home to the Kinnar, a long-lost tribe believed to possess extraordinary powers and wisdom. According to legend, the Kinnar were a peaceful and enigmatic people who dwelled in these forgotten mountains, living in harmony with nature and governed by a complex etiquette of respect and reciprocity. Their villages were hidden behind a veil of secular traditions, jealously guarding their ancient secrets and treasured stories. The tales of Kinnar spread gradually from village to village, recounting stories of flames that danced in unkept flames and feathers worn like a golden mask. To outsiders, it seemed a cacophony of meaning and image, a clumsy telling of history. However, for the listeners of old tales, each line possessed an emphatic echo: of dancers haunting green and dark fir forests. Krishan Kumar, a primary school teacher from the Bhuntar region, strolled alone in a corresponding passage, speaking every day to travelers, residents and anyone who listened to share an idea. "No one wants to talk about anything", he said quietly, eyes concave, breathing heavy in transactions. Every day, the woods grew shadowy before he involved mysteries instead.
