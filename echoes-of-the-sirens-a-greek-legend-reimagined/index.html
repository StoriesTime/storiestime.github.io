It was a place of ancient myths and forgotten lore, where the gods of old still whispered secrets to the winds, and the echoes of their stories resonated through every shore. In this mystical world, there lived three Nereids - daughters of the sea god Nereus - who possessed the enchanting voices of the Sirens. Their names were Kalliope, Lysandra, and Chrysantha, and each one sang with a distinct voice that could lull the most rugged of sailors into a deep slumber. The story of the Nereids began thousands of years ago, in the era of heroes, when the Odyssey of Odysseus was still a feat to be told and retold around campfires. According to legend, the three sisters were torn between their love of music and their duty as guardians of the maritime trade. They spent their days singing with words of warning, cautioning mariners to avoid the treacherous waters that clung to the coast of Elyria. But, like the call of the sirens, their voices grew sweeter, and their melodies irresistible. Sailors began to sail their ships closer and closer to the island, drawn by the hypnotic beauty of the Nereids' songs. The sisters' concern grew for the fate of those who succumbed to their allure, for they knew the islands were cursed, with reefs beneath the waves waiting to shatter the hulls of unsuspecting vessels.
