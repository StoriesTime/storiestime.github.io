The Unbearable Lightness of Being: Heidegger and the Existentialists Wrestle with Life's Meaning


In the hallowed halls of academia, nestled among the ivy-covered brick and mortar, semesters unfurling like green leaves upon the earth, there was an ongoing debate. A gathering of young minds, fueled by ambition, vodka, and naivety, came together to challenge the teachings of preeminent philosophers. Central to the discourse was an existentialist theme that persisted in the hearts and minds of the participants - a theme, The Unbearable Lightness of Being.

One such philosopher, Martin Heidegger, had long battled against weightlessness of the soul. This relentless quest led the existentialists to question their very existence - a fragile dance of birth and death.

Among the young scholars debating this momentous matter of life and filosofía was an adolescent prodigy. This young rebel with multiple bands of anachronistic thoughts disrupted the serene wisdom of those established philosophers.

Aristotle, relieved at engaging with the youth, a smile tugging at his long-dandy beard, welcomed the callow freshman to the roundtable discussion.

“Young padawan,” he began in an ancient booming voice, “The key to understanding life’s enduring purpose lies in the fleeting moments unseen by the mass. Only the elixir of wisdom, the Logos if you will, shall cure thy pathogenic philosophies.”

But our rake-thin and nimble Odysseus disciple had another vessel in mind, a cup overflowing with existentialist truths - Heidegger’s ideas on the burden of existence.

“Ah,” our neophyte fired back through the haze of his eau-de-vie alcohol fuelled rhetoric, “I shall wake ye from your philosophical slumber. That Heideggerian heavy mantle ye speak of? A contrived creation no more useful than the clothes upon your corporeal frames.

“Life is light, not cumbersome, oppressive, or laden with superficiality. Our brief existence is a kaleidoscope of ambient experiences, fleeting moments both joyous and tragic, that weave together our existence’s tapestry. Their sum is more than any single thread’s weight can bear.”

This brave rattling of the cage stirred an old philosopher’s curiosity.

Plato, stifling a yawn and folding his arms over his tattered toga, asked, “Pray tell, how does the idea of ’lightness’ capture the range of human experiences? Doesn’t it trivialize the suffering, ecstasy, and devastation that we all endure?”

“Our existential burdens?” inquired the student with sly, twinkling eyes, “They are but a veil distracting humanity from finding solace in the zeitgeist. These aches, cares, and intellectual heavyweights are interwoven into the essence of our existence.”

Zeno, the stoic, furrowed his brow. Observing the heated argument, he stared at the earnest young man, then gestured to Plato. “Isn’t existentialism an unanswerable riddle? It’s a Sisyphean task attempting to comprehend or define what it means to be.”

The young man smiled, confident and assured in his theories. “No, let me soothe your perplexed minds. Our sojourn on this mortal plane infuses our lives with weightlessness, a liberation from the mundane earthly quotidian, a freedom of thought and spirit - unbound and permanent in its essence.”

The hallowed men sat, speechless before this assertive youth who challenged their very raison d’être. While the young scholar was ultimately expelled from the academy for his heresies, the argument lingered in the hearts and minds of the philosophers, nestled amongst ivy and academic pursuit.

With every seed, through time and cultivation, ideas begin to sprout, to grow, and with luck, they transcend the boundaries of philosophical debate. The Unbearable Lightness of Being persists, a perpetual dance of joy and sorrow, permanence and impermanence, carrying on within the heartbeats of a ever-evolving humanity.

This subtle contest of ideas ignited upon campus has rippled throughout history and into our modern lives - lectures sparking from the chandeliers of prestigious universities to think-tanks provocatively questioning our knowing.

Where are we going when we ask about our purpose?

Do we add weight or do we soar lighter than air in the cosmos of understanding?

In the end, our answers unfold like a dance of the celestial ballet - both heavy in significance and wonderfully light. That which is obvious may also simply be right.

Two thousand years hence, our existentialist quarrel continues to bear witness to the ever-present struggle: How do we begin to grasp the true essence of life and our place in it?

The Unbearable Lightness of Being stands as a testament to the enduring vigor of these spirited debates, a plea and a call to expand our understanding of the infinite possibilities embedded within our fragile existence. May your own journey be enlightening and even delightful, as you grapple with the intricate mysteries inherent to the human experience.

The End
