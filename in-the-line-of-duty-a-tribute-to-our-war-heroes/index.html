In the Line of Duty: A Tribute to Our War Heroes


The month of May is a time of great significance for many countries around the world, as it marks the day they honor the memories of their war heroes. For the United States, Memorial Day is a day to pay tribute to those who have made the ultimate sacrifice in the line of duty. It is a day for the nation to come together and reflect on the courage and bravery of those who have served in the armed forces. A Brief History of Memorial Day The history of Memorial Day dates back to the end of the Civil War when a bouquet of flowers was placed on the tomb of an unknown Union soldier in Arlington National Cemetery. The tradition was started by Major General Oliver O. Howard in 1868, who wanted to honor the memory of the fallen soldiers. Over the years, the day has evolved but its purpose remains the same – to honor the memories of those who have died while defending their country. The Last Post Every year, on Memorial Day, at precisely 3:00 pm, a lone bugler stands tall in Arlington National Cemetery, playing the haunting tune of "Taps." The last post is a poignant and emotional moment, as it serves as a reminder of the sacrifices made by those who have given their lives for their country. The sound of the bugle is a call to respect, gratitude, and remembrance. A Day to Remember As we pause to reflect on the sacrifices made by our war heroes, we are reminded of the importance of their service. Their bravery, selflessness, and commitment to defending our nation are qualities that our society greatly admires. We take pride in their achievements and stand in awe of the anonymous heroism they displayed in the name of duty. Honoring the Fallen From Arlington National Cemetery to local Veteran's Memorials across the nation, Americans gather to honor the memories of the fallen. Wreaths are laid, flags are raised, and tears are shed. The ceremony is a moving tribute to the sacrifices made by the brave men and women who have defended our nation. The Story of One Hero The story of Sergeant Major John L. Rigby III exemplifies the very essence of what it means to be a war hero. On September 12, 2004, under heavy enemy fire, Sergeant Rigby rushed to the rescue of his wounded comrades, shielding them with his own body. He absorbed the blast of at least 40 rounds of ammunition, shielding his friends and family from harm. His actions that day would eventually claim his life, but his selfless act would inspire countless others. His Last Words As Sergeant Rigby lay injured and bleeding on the ground, his last words to a medic were, "Make sure the messenger gets the message." Those words would become a symbol of the sacrifice he made for his friends, his family, and his country. His unwavering commitment to duty, even in the face of overwhelming death, inspires us all. Honoring the Legacy In the United States, there are over 6,000 graves in Arlington National Cemetery alone that honor the memories of our war heroes. Each and every one of them tells a story of bravery, selflessness, and an unwavering commitment to duty. Their legacy continues to be a source of inspiration for us all. The Price of Freedom The price of freedom is not free, but it is a price that many have paid in the line of duty. Their sacrifice, selflessness, and bravery are qualities that we should continue to honor and remember. It is the stories of these heroes and their families that motivate us, honor their legacy, and remind us of the value of their sacrifice. Until We Meet Again As we wrap up the somber yet powerful tribute to our war heroes, we are left with a glimpse of a simple headstone with a poignant inscription: "On guard."
