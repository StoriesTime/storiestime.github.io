Hiking through the Sagadas: A Rainforest Adventure in the Philippines


The sun slowly emerged from behind the mountains, casting golden rays upon the lush green expanse that stretched before us. My friend Alex and I were embarking on an adventure that would take us deep into the heart of Sagada, a mystical and captivating region nestled within the Philippines’ Cordillera mountain range.

As we began our hike, the crisp mountain air filled our lungs, invigorating us with each breath. The trail, flanked by towering trees that seemed to protect us from the rest of the world, snaked its way up the verdant mountain slopes, leading us ever deeper into the realm of Sagada’s enigmatic rainforests.

We were on the lookout for the endangered Philippine eagle, often referred to as the “Haribon,” a bird that thrives within the lush canopies above us. According to local legend, encountering the majestic creature heralded good fortune and prosperity for the one fortunate enough to witness its beauty.

As we hiked further, the terrain grew more rugged and treacherous, with moss-covered boulders dotting the path and the occasional landslide. Nevertheless, the challenge invigorated us, and our determination grew stronger with each step we took.

During breaks in our journey, we marveled at the vibrant ecosystem that surrounded us. The verdant foliage teemed with life, from the tiny, iridescent insects flitting about to the colorful orchids that bloomed along the trail. The forest seemed to be alive with countless hidden secrets, beckoning us to delve deeper into its mysteries.

The air was thick with the sound of birdsong as we continued our ascent. The lush undergrowth disappeared, replaced by stony paths that wound between ancient, gnarled trees. Finally, we reached the summit, where we were greeted by the vast expanse of the Cordillera mountain range. The view was breathtaking, and as we stood there, our hearts swelled with humility and awe at the raw beauty that surrounded us.

But our journey was far from over. Further into the rainforest, we would encounter the ethereal Shadows of Sagada, ancient waterfalls that cascaded down the mountain faces. We knew that they were guarded by the spirits of the Balanganew, the local animist believers. As we ventured closer to their secret abode, we could feel the ethereal presence of these unseen beings.

The first of the Shadow Falls, Bokong Falls, revealed itself to us, its misty veil enveloping us like a cool, refreshing embrace. The waters, believed to possess healing properties, flowed with a force that seemed almost sacred, as if the spirit of the mountain itself was flowing through them.

A short distance away, we discovered another cascade, the busal Falls. Its powerful torrents crashed against the rocks below, producing an enchanting symphony of water and wind that echoed throughout the rainforest. According to local folklore, the balo river – the force behind the falls – was home to a mystical serpent. We could almost feel its ancient presence; even now, we can still hear the ripple of its mystical song.

Finally, we arrived at the pièce de résistance: the Sugong Falls. As if guided by an unseen hand, the sun chose that perfect, serene moment to break through the clouds. The light flickered across the water, illuminating cascades of silver droplets that danced to the rhythm of the wind and water.

Feeling a sense of reverence for the magical world that surrounded us, we paused to offer a silent prayer of gratitude to the spirits of Sagada. We knew that we had been blessed to witness the incredible majesty of the Philippines’ rainforests.

As we descended the mountain, ready to return to the real world, we couldn’t help but feel a profound sense of appreciation for the enchanting experience that had changed us forever. And now, we could only dream of the day when we would once again be invited to return to Sagada and embark on another intimate journey through its captivating, mysterious heart.
