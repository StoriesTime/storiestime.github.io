From Manager to Leader: Harnessing Influence for Growth


Elena had always been a diligent manager, known for her sharp decision-making and knack for ensuring the operations ran smoothly. When she first stepped into the role of a team leader, she took pride in how well she executed tasks, kept deadlines, and made sure her team stayed on track. In her mind, success was simply about doing the job well—no more, no less. But as the months passed, something began to gnaw at her. Despite meeting all the set goals, her team wasn’t growing. She wasn’t growing.

One afternoon, after another team meeting where things seemed just fine on the surface, Elena found herself staring at a piece of paper on her desk—scratches of a to-do list, strategic plans, and goals she had meticulously crafted. They were all good ideas, but something was missing. She wasn’t sure what, but the feeling of stagnation was undeniable. It wasn’t until a conversation with her colleague Tom that the lightbulb went off.

“Elena,” Tom said with a hint of concern, “you’re doing everything right, but it feels like your team isn’t really engaged. They’re doing the work, but they don’t seem to own the work. You’re managing, but are you leading them?”

Tom’s question lingered in Elena’s mind. Wasn’t managing enough? What was this thing about leading? She thought she had been leading by example, but now that Tom mentioned it, she began to see the gap.

She knew the answer to her question would affect her team’s future. If she didn’t start leading them, they might never truly excel. The idea of leadership wasn’t just about being in charge, it was about creating an environment that encouraged growth—growth in herself and her team.

Chapter 2: The Foundation of Influence

Elena began her journey by researching the qualities that distinguished great leaders from good managers. What she found surprised her. While managers focused on maintaining order, leaders inspired and nurtured. They influenced those around them—not through authority or rules, but by fostering trust, motivation, and collaboration.

One aspect that stood out to Elena was the concept of emotional intelligence. Great leaders understood the emotions and motivations of their teams, helping them unlock potential in ways that a simple managerial approach could not.

She realized that her team needed more than just clear directions; they needed a sense of ownership and personal connection to the work they were doing. Elena began to focus on building deeper relationships with her team members. Instead of just assigning tasks, she started asking questions that encouraged reflection. “How do you feel about this project?” or “What do you think we can improve here?” By showing genuine interest in their thoughts, she encouraged her team to think more creatively and take more initiative.

But it wasn’t easy. At first, her team was confused. They were used to the structured, directive approach that had worked so well under her management. Now, she was asking them to become more involved in decision-making processes, to take responsibility for not just the work, but for its impact.

Yet, Elena persisted, slowly starting to see changes. Team members began taking more ownership of their tasks, suggesting new ideas, and feeling more accountable for the outcomes. But it was clear: the shift from managing to leading wasn’t just about changing the approach. It was about developing new skills, both in herself and in her team.

Chapter 3: The Power of Empowerment

A few months into her transformation, Elena recognized that leadership wasn’t about being perfect—it was about being present and empowering others to rise to challenges. She began to delegate not only tasks, but responsibilities. Elena understood that real growth happens when people are trusted to step up. So, she stopped being the bottleneck for every decision. She gave her team members the freedom to lead certain projects, make key decisions, and even fail.

She watched as her team members became more confident. Sarah, who had always been a reliable but quiet contributor, took the lead on a major client presentation. Carlos, an engineer who was initially hesitant to speak up, began suggesting process improvements that saved the company time and money. Even Michael, who was known for being somewhat disengaged, became deeply involved in mentoring new employees.

But the road to becoming an empowering leader wasn’t always smooth. There were bumps along the way—setbacks, moments of doubt, and even some resistance from team members who preferred the old way of doing things. Elena had to constantly remind herself that leadership was about guiding through those difficult moments and showing resilience, not just in her decisions, but in her actions.

One pivotal moment came when Elena had to make a tough decision regarding a key team member, Jason. He had been underperforming, and his behavior was affecting the morale of the group. Elena could have easily handled the situation with a firm managerial approach—reprimanding him and moving on. But this time, she decided to lead differently. She sat down with Jason and had an open conversation. She asked him what was going on, what challenges he was facing, and where he felt he needed support.

The conversation revealed that Jason had been struggling with balancing personal issues and work, and the team’s workload was overwhelming him. Instead of pushing him aside, Elena listened and worked with him to come up with a plan for improvement. Over time, Jason became one of the most reliable members of the team, a testament to the power of compassionate leadership.

Chapter 4: The Ripple Effect

As Elena’s leadership style evolved, so did the culture within her team. There was a noticeable shift in how they collaborated, communicated, and supported one another. Her influence began to spread beyond just the immediate team. Colleagues from other departments sought her advice, and the executives took notice of the positive changes in team dynamics.

Elena realized that the essence of leadership was not in the title, but in the ability to inspire others to be their best selves. The moment she understood that leadership was about empowering people to grow, that was when she began to see her team—and herself—grow.

The biggest change was in her own approach to challenges. Instead of looking for solutions herself, she started asking questions that prompted her team to come up with answers. “What would you do if you were in charge of this project?” or “How can we improve this process?” Elena no longer felt the weight of the world on her shoulders because she shared it with her team.

As the months passed, Elena’s leadership transformed from a process of managing tasks to a journey of inspiring and empowering those around her. Her team flourished in ways she never expected. The most significant growth was not just in their work outcomes but in their personal development and confidence.

Chapter 5: The Legacy of Leadership

Years later, Elena reflected on her journey. She had learned that great leaders are not defined by the number of projects they complete or the size of their teams, but by the impact they have on others. She had built a team that was capable of not only achieving its goals but of surpassing them. Her success was no longer measured in deliverables, but in the growth and autonomy of the individuals she led.

Her leadership had created a culture of continuous learning, mutual respect, and empowerment. Her team was no longer just a group of individuals working together—they were a unit, driven by shared values and a common vision.

Elena had moved beyond the realm of management into something more profound: leadership. And with that came the true power to create change—not just for her team, but for herself, and for the organization as a whole. Her story became an inspiration to others, a testament to the fact that the journey from manager to leader is a journey of transformation, both inside and out.

And in the end, Elena realized that true leadership wasn’t about leading others—it was about leading them to lead themselves.

The Takeaway: Leadership as Influence for Growth

Elena’s story is one of growth, resilience, and empowerment. It highlights the crucial shift from managing to leading and emphasizes the importance of influence over authority. By nurturing emotional intelligence, empowering her team, and focusing on long-term growth, Elena was able to transform both herself and her team into strong, independent leaders. True leadership is not about dictating; it’s about inspiring others to rise to their potential and guiding them to achieve their best. And when a leader does this, the ripple effect can foster growth on every level.
