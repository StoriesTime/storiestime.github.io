A Timeless Love: Tales from the Taj Mahal


Once upon a timeworn, love-worn land, and nestled within its purest jewel was a tale of ageless beauty. A tale so profound that its invaders stumbled upon it with each calendar’s turn; A story of The Red City’s radiant heart – the iconic Taj Mahal.

In the verdant gardens around the palace of life, there bloomed a union of unparalleled love between a king and a queen, the breeze speaking of their epic love tale. Ahilip, the Agra city’s ruler, and Mahalini, his beloved queen, tasted the bitter-sweet dance of life for thousands of selfless moments.

As the winter of their love story nears its final twilight, Ahilip, the warrior king, departs his physical world leaving behind an intriguing memory. Hamoon, a young apprentice of paper artistry, finds himself consumed by curiosity about the mysterious tale of his land’s greatest wonder: the Taj Mahal.

Driven by the boundless curiosity of youth, Hamoon tunes ears to the stories shared by the dusty lips of his ancestors in the village’s teahouses and libraries. Each shared both legends and historical truths, melding time with themes of the timeless love between the king and queen. “Such is this architectural wonder!” He muses, entranced by the beauty of the Taj.

On a warm moonlit night, blowing the wind’s highest and warmest desire, hope springs anew in Hamoon’s heart. With modest brush and ink in hand, he prepares a parchment—a tribute inspired by his beloved Taj Mahal.

In his dreams, he imagines awaking in the palace, where the night is pervaded by the whispers of the king and queen’s eternal love. Yet alas, even with time’s ticking softly upon his parchment, the palace remains stubbornly silent.

In a desperate attempt, Hamoon casts his art into the palace’s heart, the Taj Mahal’s life-cradling centerpiece—the Black Khanjar. In his wildest dream, he imagines a union of divine proportion: ink and blade creating one eternal ode to pure love etched into time.

But as this flickering wish of a hopeful artist ends, the palace’s silence remains heavy. The Taj Mahal stands enveloped in supporting whispers of age-old love but does not awaken to Hamoon’s call. The empire stands unfazed as the artist’s Hands, broken-hearted and reality-blunted, cease their dance within the teahouse.

In language of old, the moonlit whispers are heard: “Taj perhaps doesn’t need your tale of love, for it already stands eminently immortal.” The Taj Mahal and the love story of Ahilip and Mahalini shall stay AGOLDEN memory, undisturbed and happy in their quiet, timeless splendor.

And thus, as Hamoon learns the value of his craft and the sacredness of history, the empire lives on, with love in its heart and pure memory of the sun setting on their existence. In this timeless love story, the whispers meet in the palace, while the story goes on. Both the Taj Mahal and Hamoon’s art exist side by side in their memory, a beautiful painting, where colors of the nation blend.

Dawn now peels open the shroud of night, revealing the delicate lines of reality. Nation still stands, with Taj all its pride. Remembering, reliving and teetering at the edge of Ahilip and Mahalini’s love. Spring blooms and colors awake, leaving behind the story of their eternal love in the garden of the Taj.

For history and legacy are our wisest teachers, a brush and a sword etching love, and, as such, the tale of The Taj shall live.onalphabetically.
