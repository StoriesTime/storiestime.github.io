My Haunted House: Comedy in the Afterlife


It was a typical Tuesday afternoon when Emily received the news that her deceased grandmother's will had been finalized. To Emily's surprise, she had inherited a sprawling mansion in the sleepy town of Willow Creek. Little did she know, the property came with a rather unusual stipulation: the house was rumored to be haunted. As she stepped inside, a chill ran down her spine. Cobwebs clung to the chandeliers, and a thick layer of dust coated every surface. The once-grand foyer seemed to sag under the weight of its own history. A macabre painting of a vampire hung on the wall, its subject's eyes seeming to follow her as she moved. Emily shrugged off the unease and began to explore the mansion. Room after room was filled with an assortment of strange and eclectic items, as if her grandmother had been a collector of oddities. Among them was a shelf filled with taxidermied animals, their glassy eyes seeming to stare judgmentally at her. A leather-bound book creaked open on its own, and a single sheet slipped out, fluttering to the floor. The title, "A Treatise on Ghostly Coexistence," seemed to mock her. She decided to tackle the kitchen first, in hopes of finding some modern amenities to appeal to her vegan lifestyle. Alarms sounded, and the lights flickered as she fumbled with the ancient appliances. Frozen chickens rotting in the deep freeze only added to the ghastly atmosphere.
