Spectral Seances: Communicating with the Dead


In 1892, the flamboyant Colonel Elkins had just embarked on what the local newspapers called his “quest for the unknown.” He would bewitch the provincial high society with consultations, seances, and psychic dramas.

Despite their initial skepticism, Catherine, a young woman living in the quaint town, had grown increasingly fascinated by the Colonel. His demeanour and intelligence left her captivated as he hosted nightly séances in his palatial estate. Almost every evening, guests from the town’s aristocracy would fine the dimly lit, candle-lit drawing room turning effortlessly into an abode for the supernatural.

One fateful night, Catherine mustered her courage to attend the séance alone. Oblivious to the aristocratic frills and protocols, she chose to accompany, a sort of spiritual commune that felt tangible and transcendent.

A sea of whispers

As she entered the room, she observed the guests seated in pairs on mahogany chairs, their faces illuminated by the dim glow of candles. Catherine’s gaze wandered the room, stopping momentarily at the Colonel. He looked at her, giving her a warm smile in affirmation of her arrival.

“Welcome, dear Catherine, we’re just about to begin,” he whispered.

In front of the fireplace stood a round mahogany table with four chairs. The Colonel gestured for her to take a seat on the empty chair. The four mediums, barely audible voices working as one, began to chant in an eerie monotone that pervaded the atmosphere like sea waves.

In no time, the whispers had swelled into fervent cries. ‘Can you hear us?’ they breathed into the darkness. ‘Oh, sweet spirits, come forth. Let our reunion be not in vain.’

A cold breeze swept through the room. Candles flickered violently. Catherine’s heart raced in rhythmic pace with the whirling wind, and something deep within her was yearning, craving, it had to be there.

The hands may come

As the séance proceeded, Catherine felt inexplicable energy surge through her veins. Her entire body trembled as she clenched the edges of her chair.

The Colonel, sensing her unease, leaned toward her whispering, “Catherine, the spirits sometimes choose to communicate through us. Allow yourself to be one with the energy.”

As she followed his instructions, she felt a force, something dark and yet enigmatic, pulling her hand away from the chair’s edge. It gently lifted into the air, just a few inches. Pouring all her control over it, she watched in bewilderment as her other hand also rose.

Sweat poured down her forehead as the room grew colder. The remaining attendees chanted in an ethereal symphony, and Catherine felt her connection to the realm of the known fading as her hands rose higher into the air.

A reunion of souls

It is said that spectral seances can evoke more than the dead. With her spirit open, Catherine was flooded with memories of her past, her deepest thoughts, her every fear and desire.

Like a conversation with herself, one that transcended time, space, and even existence, Catherine realized that life and death were merely two ends of the same bizarre ribbon.

As the journey intensified, Catherine was stripped of her inhibitions, her delusions, and fear. The séance, for what it promised to reveal, embraced her with a force that she could barely handle.

Her heart ached, yet, she had never felt so close to a feeling of belonging and imperishability as in that chilling, mystical moment.

In the hazy afterglow of the séance, the room dimmed. Unseen hands guided Catherine’s back into her seat, and with the return of her heartbeat, familiarity entered her life again. She blinked, only to realize the séance had hypnotized her into an unbearable acceptance of the impermanent world.

Steeling herself and grabbing her shawl requisite for a night like this, she left the room. A thousand flames flickered within her heart; curiosity, awe, and the realization of the intrinsic need embedded deep within; a communion of sorts.

Catherine knew, she could never escape this newfound attraction to the seance as the Colonel’s guest, for in the heart of the night, she would longed to find the veil of mortality lifting, unveiling glimpses of the ethereal world, where life breathed beyond the boundaries of death.
