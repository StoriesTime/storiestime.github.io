The Melody of Inclusion: A Symphony of Diverse Friendship


Once upon a time, in a small town known for its vibrant arts scene, lived a young girl named Ella. She was a DACA student, an aspiring musician, and a lover of diverse friendships. Ever since she was little, Ella would pick up any instrument she could get her hands on – from the ukulele in kindergarten to the violin in her high school years. Her passion for music only grew stronger with time, and soon she found herself enrolled at the local music conservatory.

Ella was an incredibly talented musician, but, like all artists, she had insecurities. Her classmates included pets of wealthy families who, unlike her, could afford numerous lessons and instruments. Despite this, Ella had a unique talent – her ability to create harmony out of any group of sounds.

One day, Ella’s teacher gathered the students to discuss the upcoming music festival. Excitement buzzed in the room, but the teacher announced that there would be an open audition, and only one student would be chosen to perform at the festival. This sent a wave of anxiety through the class, but Ella didn’t feel scared; she felt determined.

Ella knew it would be difficult to stand out when her classmates had access to the best resources, so she came up with an elaborate plan. Instead of focusing on sheer talent, Ella would create a beautiful composition that captured the spirit of unity and friendship. She would incorporate elements from each of her classmates’ cultures and incorporate them into a symphony, proving that music brings people – and friends – together.

As Ella gathered the courage to approach her classmates, she realized she had some unexpected doubters. Her friend Janelle, an incredibly gifted violinist from a similar background, strongly disagreed with Ella’s concept. Instead, Janelle believed that only remarkable skill would separate the contestants and determine the festival’s winner.

However, Ella knew their friendship was too valuable and too deep to be damaged by their differing views. So she approached Janelle with an open heart. As they talked, Janelle reluctantly opened up about her troubled past and how she felt unworthy of success. Ella realized that her talent, hard work, and passion were all tainted by doubt and fear of being a disappointment. Ella shared her own fears and insecurities, and they found a momentary refuge in discussing their vulnerabilities.

Together, Ella and Janelle began working on the composition, incorporating shameless flavors from their classmates’ cultures. They scribbled down ideas, nominated musical styles, and emailed their school teacher with excitement. The composition came to life under the guidance of their teacher, who worked with the girls to create a true symphony of diverse friendship.

The day of the audition finally arrived. The conservatory buzzed with nervous energy, but not Ella. She had a melody of inclusion in her heart. As the moment arrived for her performance, the auditors and the audience gathered, including her doubting classmates. Ella took the stage and played her composition.

As the music flowed, faces in the audience lit up one by one, reflecting a spectrum of emotions. With pride pulsating through her veins, Ella proudly looked at her friends, their eyes locked together in a concert of their own. With every note, friendships were solidified, and unease dissolved into excitement.

Later that day, as the school teacher announced the winner of the audition, Ella and Janelle exchanged nervous glances. The teacher announced that the composition had impressed the auditioners so much that the judges would not select just one winner – instead, they would collectively admit the entire group of friends as winners. Every student who contributed to the beautiful, harmonious composition would get the opportunity to perform at the annual music festival. Morning had turned to night, and yet, their victory continued to echo through the halls of the music conservatory long after the announcement.

From that day forward, Ella knew her music had never only been a melody – it was a song of unity and inclusion. A celebration of the vast tapestry of friendships that she and her classmates lovingly weaved. The Melody of Inclusion had become more than just a composition; It was a testament to the power of diverse friendships and teamwork. Their symphony of different backgrounds, talents, and beliefs became a symbol of the beauty of inclusion. Together, they proved that the best music could transcend divisions and unite the pure passion of friendship.

Ella and her classmates had succeeded in creating a masterpiece; a living story that transpired and enraptured those who heard the symphony. And in the end, their music shook the foundation of the music festival, echoing deeply into the hearts of the attendees and the students, inspiring countless diverse friendships to bloom in the years to come. The Melody of Inclusion would forever be remembered, not among stars on a screen, but within the beautiful symphony of the hearts.
