The Influence Factor: Unlocking Leadership Potential in Everyday Moments


Every morning, when the city was still wrapped in the soft haze of dawn, Mara would step onto the first rung of the subway stairs. The rush of commuters, muffled announcements, and the clatter of trains filled the air, but she noticed something others didn’t—the subtle power of influence in the smallest interactions. No grand speeches, no chief executive titles, just simple moments that quietly shaped outcomes.

Mara was neither a CEO nor a renowned public speaker. A project manager by profession, she often found herself reflecting on leadership—not the kind extolled in glossy magazines but the tangible, everyday leadership that influenced decisions, shaped moods, and altered paths. This was her fascination: unlocking leadership potential in the ordinary, everyday moments.

A Chance Encounter and a Lesson in Listening

One particularly hectic Tuesday evening, Mara was standing on the subway platform, preparing to board the train home, when she noticed a small commotion. An elderly man, confused and slightly frail, was trying to read the complex digital signboard that shifted every few seconds. People hurried past, eyes glued to their phones, oblivious.

Mara approached with a gentle smile. “Can I help you with the schedule?”

The man looked up, relief washing over his face. “Thank you. I’m trying to catch the 6:23 to Greenfield but I keep losing track in all the changes.”

With just a few moments, Mara explained the schedule changes and even waited with him until the correct train arrived. What seemed like a minor act of kindness rippled unexpectedly. The elderly man, Mr. Thompson, happened to be a retired school principal who, after conversation, shared insights about nurturing young leaders through empathy and communication.

That brief encounter became a catalyst for Mara’s deepening understanding of leadership. Leaders aren’t just those who hold power—they are connectors, those who tune into the needs of others even amid chaos.

The Power of Questions

Back at the office, Mara began to experiment with a new method inspired by that lesson: instead of issuing directives, she started asking more questions during meetings.

At first, her team was puzzled. “Why are you asking us so many questions now?” one member asked during a project briefing.

“I want to understand your perspectives better,” Mara replied. “Let’s explore the possibilities together.”

What unfolded was surprising. Team members offered ideas they usually kept to themselves. Morale lifted. The project delivered results more innovative than ever, with a stronger sense of ownership by everyone involved.

This simple change—shifting from telling to asking—revealed how leadership often lies in nurturing curiosity and encouraging participation.

Influence in the Smallest Gestures

One afternoon, Mara attended a conference on digital transformation. During a networking break, she observed a junior attendee shyly standing apart from the crowd. Instead of moving on, Mara approached her.

Introductions led to shared experiences and career advice. The young woman, Saira, later confided how nerve-wracking large conferences could be and how that conversation gave her renewed confidence.

This interaction reminded Mara that influence doesn’t always require a platform or authority; it can be present in encouragement offered quietly, the small gestures that validate someone’s presence.

Harnessing Emotional Intelligence Everyday

Mara’s growing understanding of leadership emphasized emotional intelligence—recognizing and managing emotions in oneself and others. When her team faced a sudden project setback, she resisted the urge to react with frustration.

Instead, she acknowledged everyone’s disappointment, shared her own feelings, then guided the team to focus on solutions. The climate shifted from blame to collaboration. Recognizing emotions transformed a potential crisis into an opportunity for collective resilience.

Leadership Without a Title

Months passed. Mara’s approach drew attention. Her company noticed improved team performance, better communication, and innovation fueled by diverse ideas. But what Mara cherished most was the quiet internal shift—the realization that leadership is not confined to titles and positions.

Leadership is an influence factor, present in everyday moments: the choice to listen, to ask, to encourage, to empathize. It is accessible when one decides to show up authentically and intentionally.

The Ripple Effect

One Friday, Mara received an email from Saira, the junior conference attendee. She had just been promoted to a leadership role in her team, inspiring others as Mara had once inspired her.

“Your belief made a difference,” Saira wrote. “It showed me that leadership starts with simple choices we make every day.”

The words echoed Mara’s own journey, crystallizing what she had come to understand so deeply: unlocking leadership potential doesn’t require grand gestures or lofty ambitions. It unfolds silently, moment by moment, when people connect, support, and influence one another genuinely.

Embracing the Everyday Leader Within

Mara’s story is a testament to the profound impact latent in everyday leadership—the influence factor that empowers ordinary people to make extraordinary differences. It’s about focusing less on authority and more on authentic connection, recognizing everyone’s role as a potential leader.

From assisting a confused commuter to fostering inclusive dialogue in meetings, these unsung leadership moments create a mosaic of influence that shapes organizations and communities alike.

Reflective Takeaways: The Influence Factor in Practice

  1. Listening Actively
    Leadership begins by truly hearing others. It requires presence and attention, enabling empathetic responses that build trust.

  2. Asking Thoughtful Questions
    Encouraging curiosity leads to collective problem-solving and innovation, shifting power dynamics toward collaboration.

  3. Recognizing Emotional Currents
    Understanding emotions within teams aids in navigating challenges and maintaining morale during setbacks.

  4. Valuing Small Gestures
    A kindness, encouragement, or timely advice can empower individuals to step into leadership roles.

  5. Modeling Humility and Authenticity
    Leaders who reveal vulnerability inspire openness and create environments conducive to growth.

Leadership is not the privilege of a few; it is the potential of many. The influence factor thrives in everyday moments, waiting to be unlocked by those willing to see beyond titles and spotlight to the human connections that truly move us forward.

Mara’s journey illuminates a path accessible to all: leading with presence, purpose, and heart, transforming ordinary encounters into extraordinary leadership opportunities.

In your life, what small moment today could be the seed of leadership growth? Sometimes, the quietest moments speak the loudest.
