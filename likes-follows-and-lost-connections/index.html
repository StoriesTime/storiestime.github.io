Likes, Follows, and Lost Connections


It was late in the evening when Mia unlocked her phone, almost unconsciously scrolling through the feed that had become her nightly ritual. The screen glowed softly, casting faint shadows across her bedroom wall. Notifications piled up—likes trickling in from strangers and friends alike, the occasional new follower adding themselves to her numbers. “A whirlwind of validation,” she thought in amusement, though the humor was bittersweet.

Her account had grown steadily over the past two years—a curated collection of perfectly balanced photos, witty captions, and pieces of her life she allowed the world to consume. She’d started simply, sharing travel snapshots and personal anecdotes. But as the likes accumulated, so did the pressure to deliver content that would keep people engaged. It wasn’t just a hobby anymore. It was like she had become a performer on a stage, waiting for applause.

Still, she couldn’t shake the hollow feeling each night as she stared at her newest uploads. They were doing well, as usual—hundreds, sometimes thousands, of little hearts blossoming onscreen. Yet, for all the approval her virtual world showered upon her, the silence in her physical life screamed louder.

Mia rubbed her temples, scrolling absently, when her thumb stumbled on something unexpected. A familiar face stared back: Ian. She hadn’t thought of him in months, maybe years—a face pulled from the archives of her past, now effortlessly resurfaced by the algorithm. A pang of something more sorrowful than nostalgia hit her as she stared at his profile picture, a candid shot of him in the middle of a hearty laugh. She remembered that laugh. She remembered when hearing it had been one of her favorite parts of her day.

Without thinking, Mia tapped on his name. There was the stream of his life—pictures of distant countries, goofy snapshots of him holding animals, and several posts tagged with people she didn’t know. New friends. He hadn’t followed her back. Not yet, at least.

The truth hit harder than she expected. Ian had slipped away in the kind of way that wasn’t dramatic but was definite—a slow fade, quiet and natural as the turning of seasons. Once inseparable, their lives now moved in orbit far away from each other, his path spiraling toward unknown constellations she could only glimpse from the telescope of social media. They hadn’t fought; there wasn’t a tragic ending to their friendship. It just… dissipated, overshadowed by schedules, priorities, and, ultimately, distance.

Mia closed his profile picture and leaned back against her pillow. What had gone wrong? She could still remember their late-night talks when he’d share absurd stories he’d heard at work, his sharp takes on movies, or his eccentric theories about the future. She remembered laughing so hard she’d cry, completely forgetting the world outside for just a few hours. Friendships like that weren’t supposed to end so quietly, were they?

The next morning, she lingered over coffee longer than usual. Ian’s profile was on her mind, and with it came a flood of memories. Carefree moments she hadn’t thought about in ages—the road trips, inside jokes, heartfelt talks. She stared into the distance, her laptop open but ignored. Out of habit, she checked her own account again. The likes had climbed, but it didn’t come close to compensating for the ache growing in her chest.

She wanted to reconnect, but how? Dropping someone’s life for years and suddenly showing up again felt intrusive. Mia set her mug down and opened Ian’s profile again. Maybe she could leave a simple comment—something casual?

Her fingers hovered over the keyboard. What could she even say? Everything seemed too distant or contrived. Even if the algorithm had dragged his face back into her orbit, did reconnecting even work that way?

That evening, she couldn’t shake the idea. She pulled up her messages and stared at their last exchange—a short text about grabbing dinner sometime. They never had. Now years later, she wondered if reaching out would be strange… or if maybe it would be worth it.

Finally, with a deep breath, she typed out a message:
“Hey Ian, I was scrolling earlier and stumbled on your pictures. Looks like you’ve been having some amazing adventures—I’m happy to see life’s treating you well. I miss catching up. Any chance you’d want to reconnect sometime?”

She stared at the screen for an eternity before hitting send. The glossy surface of her phone reflected her hesitation, capturing her uncertainty, excitement, and anxiety all in one stolen glance.

The message sat unread for hours. Mia tried to distract herself—putting on a show, cleaning the house, cooking dinner—but nothing stopped her from opening her phone every few minutes to check. Still nothing.

Days passed before she saw the notification—the little bubble that marked Ian’s name on her home screen. Her heart raced as she opened the message.

“Mia! Wow, it’s been forever. I wasn’t expecting this, but it’s amazing to hear from you. Honestly, I’ve missed you too. I’d love to reconnect sometime—maybe coffee next week?”

Relief and joy rushed through her. It felt like breaking open a door she didn’t realize had been shut for so long. They made plans, and true to his word, he showed up—just the same Ian she had known years before. They laughed over coffee like they hadn’t skipped a beat, sharing the gaps in their lives and catching each other up on everything unspoken between them.

Not every lost connection could be revived, Mia knew. But in Ian’s case, their first meeting didn’t feel like restarting from scratch. Instead, it felt like reclaiming something that had simply been misplaced.

And when she logged online that night, scrolling past fleeting likes and clicks, she didn’t feel the same hollow ache as before. Some connections, she realized, were worth far more than the numbers on a screen.
