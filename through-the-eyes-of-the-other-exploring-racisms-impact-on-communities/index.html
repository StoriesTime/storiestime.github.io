Through the Eyes of the Other: Exploring Racism's Impact on Communities


In the quiet town of Willow Creek, nestled between rolling hills and biodiverse meadows, the rhythm of life seemed to be in harmony with nature. Yet, beneath the surface of this picturesque paradise, lay a hidden reality, a subtle yet insidious strain of racism that tainted the lives of its residents.

Ella, a bright young journalist in her mid-twenties, was the first to spot the cracks in the seemingly perfect façade of her small town. As she delved deeper, she began to reveal how the sun-kissed faces of the town’s African American residents were a stark contrast to the carefree smiles of their Caucasian counterparts.

Ella wanted to create a story that would both educate and inspire. Her vision was to unravel the tangled threads of Willow Creek’s hidden racism and expose the damage it had caused in the community. However, she wouldn’t embark on this journey alone. With the help of a diverse group of friends and allies, she bravely stepped into the realm of the unknown, determined to reveal the hidden colors of her small town.

The first to join Ella was Katherine, an architect by profession and a onesie-wearing activist at heart. She had a unique perspective on the town’s layout and could see how the geography affected the opportunity distribution for Willow Creek’s residents.

James, a local teacher, came next. He had a wealth of personal stories about the town’s history and the individuals he had taught over the years. With his guidance, Ella hoped to understand the roots of the town’s racism and the paths it had taken.

Finally, the group was joined by Mary, a hardworking single mother of two with a fierce determination to fight for her children’s future. Her experience as an essential worker within the local government showcased the day-to-day impact of racism on Willow Creek’s most vulnerable residents.

Together, they embarked on a journey that would challenge their beliefs, and sometimes, even their friendships. They met with elders who shared stories of a time when Willow Creek was a more open-minded town. They spoke with residents who confronted the injustices they faced daily, and they analyzed data on the town’s demographics, housing, employment, and education systems.

Through their collective efforts, the group uncovered a web of intertwined systems working together to unfairly disadvantage Willow Creek’s minorities. The town’s housing policies were built on redlining, concealing the hills and prime properties from the Black residents. The employment map was marked by vast gaps between Caucasian and African American representation in the higher-paying sectors. And the education system seemed to be favoring the white students, who held a greater number of opportunities for success.

Documenting these findings, Ella published her story in the town’s newspaper. A story designed to shatter the illusions of a perfect, harmonious town tucked away in the rolling hills. Uncovering the unseen layers of the town’s past, she showed how a generations-old truth had been kept hidden, casting Willow Creek into a present fraught with inequality.

The townspeople reacted differently to the revelation. Some opened their eyes to the truth and vowed to change the system, while others clung stubbornly to their beliefs, choosing instead to obscured their view. Others, still, remained silent, unwilling to admit the reality in their beautiful town.

Nonetheless, Ella’s journey had opened many eyes, both within and outside the town’s borders. Willow Creek’s hidden colors had been revealed, and the journey to heal the scars of segregation had begun.

Through their tireless efforts, the group sowed the seeds of change, hoping that a new generation of Willow Creek residents might experience a town where everyone’s eyes could see the colors in the sky without the shades of the past clouding their view.
