Footprints of Adventure: Stories of Travel and Culture


Rain fell incessantly, transforming the bustling streets of Guayaquil, Ecuador, into a slick, lustrous mess. It was the perfect time to travel, according to Alejandra. Filled with anticipation and anxiety, she had devised an ambitious plan-a plan that took courage and the adventurous spirit of her grandparents. Alejandra was going to uncover a lost city, a city lost in time, swallowed by the dense Ecuadorian jungle.

The locals whispered about an ancient civilization that once existed in the forest near Tungurahua, a volcano known as ’the throat of the mountain.’ They shared tales of an urban heart hidden amongst the verdant foliage. Stories of gold and precious stones, sacred temples, and a labyrinth-like city plan. Alejandra knew that this civilization was linked to her heritage, and she could no longer ignore the call of the unknown.

With nothing but the weight of her determination and an all-consuming curiosity, Alejandra ventured into the rainforest. Carrying limited supplies, and wearing the essentials, such as boots for trekking, comfortable clothing, insect repellent, and a trusty compass, she navigated through the labyrinthine, mysterious terrain.

The forest was its own world, an enigma unto itself. It was honest, but deceptive. Lush greenery could turn into a treacherous swamp, and vegetation could pretend to be a pathway or the entrance to an unknown world. Alejandra was not daunted-she carried a collection of machete, flashlight, and her unwavering spirit. Every now and then, she would stub her toes on a peculiar stone, feeling the weight of a long-lost city beneath her feet.

As time passed, the untamed rainforest began to reveal glimpses of the civilization she sought. Unearthing pottery shards, intricate stone carvings, and semi-preserved murals, Alejandra felt like she had waltzed into a world long gone. However, her happiness was circuitously short-lived. She began to uncover remnants of destruction-scorched stones and rubble that led to her realization.

In that moment, it all fit into place-evidence of a volcanic eruption that buried the once-thriving civilization under metric tons of ash, mud, and time. Alejandra felt the loss of such a culture, but found solace in the thought of a city that once bloomed beneath the abundant canopies of the jungle.

Alejandra’s journey back to Guayaquil was bittersweet. With her heart full of stories and sights that she’d witnessed, she knew she had left her footprints on the soil of a historical enigma but found serenity in the salutes of a civilization and a piece of her family’s history.

As she thought of her grandparents, who had ignited the adventurer in her, Alejandra realized that they, too, had their footprints on this land-long, profound footprints embedded deep into the heart of the lost city’s lore. Alejandra would share her story far and wide, etching her footprints on the sands of time, ensuring the memory of this lost civilization remained alive.
