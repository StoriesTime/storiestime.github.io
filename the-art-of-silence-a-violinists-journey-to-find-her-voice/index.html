The Art of Silence: A Violinist's Journey to Find Her Voice


As she stepped onto the worn wooden stage, Elara felt a familiar sense of unease. With smooth, unblemished fingers, she wrapped the bow of her violin across the strings, the notes spilling from her instrument like a gentle stream. Her music was known as haunting, evocative, and mesmerizing. Yet, a secret weighed heavily upon her mind: despite her echoing melodies, Elara had never spoken her truth. Her music had become her voice, the physical manifestation of emotions she struggled to express. With delicate gestures, her fingers navigated the intricate patterns and symmetries of the classical repertoire. Still, in quiet moments, she heard the subtle whispers of an inner self waiting to be captured on paper, yearning to be expressed. These unshared thoughts bruised her heart, yet remained hidden. Elara began her music education at the tender age of five. Entranced by the rhythmic palpitations of the violin, she felt an untapped vessel in herself, crave-ingly aware of the melodies emanating from the instrument. Gradually, she applied herself to develop the technical prowess of that intrinsic sound. Once an accomplished prodigy, she embarked on different stages of her life. Critically acclaimed performances across the nation solidified her passion for music, injecting intricate divergences in tapestries that bathed in rhythmic endeavour. As she reached her late twenties, a remarkable shift took place in Elara's artistic journey. With songs etched more graphically within her imagination, she ventured further to paint vivid memories as living memorials associated with her past loves, childhood joys, and merciless conflicts she faced.
