Innocence Lost: Children of the Battlefield


I still remember the day the world changed forever. It was the day war descended upon our small village, shrouding it in a veil of fear and destruction. I was only eight years old, but the memories of that day have seared themselves into my mind like a branding iron. My name is Angela, and I was one of the lucky ones. I lived with my family in a small village surrounded by lush green fields and towering trees. We lived in a simple world where children played without a care, and laughter filled the air. But on that fateful day, everything changed. The War Arrives As the sun began to set, a chorus of screams pierced the air. Bombers, both friendly and enemy, screamed through the sky, dropping devastation upon our once-peaceful village. The ground shook beneath our feet as explosions rocked the earth, sending shivers down my spine. My mother, Estelle, quickly grabbed my hand and that of my sister, Madame Élisabeth, and ushered us into the safety of our cellar. We listened in horror as the chaos raged on above, our village disappearing beneath the roar of cannons and gunfire. Among the bewildered soldiers and terrified civilians, a soft voice whispered words of comfort to a lost and scared young boy. The memory would become one of the defining moments of the war. Peter Crane, a young artist and officer, found solace in those children, much like those he encountered on many unfolding battlegrounds. A Brother in Arms Becomes Family As the war ebbed and flowed like a flood, countless thousands of people continued to be displaced and victimized. Just as rapidly, ancient friendships surfaced, as common belief reconnected citizen-children and veteran-soldiers fighting against national foes. Amid this chaos, Estelle decided it was time for us to leave, to find a safer place where our children could be protected and nurtured.
