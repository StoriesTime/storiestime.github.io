The Secret Garden: Bedtime Stories for Little Explorers

3 min · 562 words

Mary Lennox, a spoiled and lonely girl, had just lost her parents in a cholera epidemic in India. She was sent to live with her uncle, Archibald Craven, in Yorkshire, England. Mary was not happy about leaving her comfortable life in India, but she had no choice. When she arrived at Misselthwaite Manor, she was greeted by her uncle, who was a kind but sad man.

The manor was a large and old house with many rooms, but Mary felt like she was the only one living there. She had never seen her uncle smile, and he seemed to be hiding something from her. Mary was curious and wanted to know what was going on.

One day, while exploring the manor, Mary stumbled upon a hidden key. She decided to use it to unlock a door that she had never seen before. The door creaked open, and Mary found herself in a neglected garden. The garden was overgrown with weeds, and the flowers were dead. But Mary could see the potential in the garden, and she felt a strange connection to it.

Chapter 2: The Secret

Mary spent the next few days exploring the garden, and she began to uncover its secrets. She discovered a hidden path, a secret door, and a mysterious room. But she also found out that the garden was locked away for many years. Mary wondered why her uncle had kept the garden a secret.

As she continued to explore the garden, Mary met a friendly robin who became her companion. The robin, whom she named Dickon, was a local boy who loved nature and animals. He taught Mary about the different plants and animals in the garden, and she began to see the beauty in the neglected space.

Chapter 3: The Transformation

With Dickon’s help, Mary began to transform the garden. They cleared the weeds, planted new flowers, and even found a hidden stream. The garden began to come back to life, and Mary felt a sense of pride and accomplishment.

As the garden changed, Mary’s uncle began to notice the difference too. He started to smile again, and Mary saw a glimmer of happiness in his eyes. The garden had brought them together, and Mary realized that she had found a new home.

Chapter 4: The Discovery of the Past

As the garden continued to flourish, Mary discovered a secret about her uncle’s past. She found an old diary that belonged to her aunt, who had died many years ago. The diary revealed that her aunt had been a kind and gentle person who loved the garden. Mary realized that her uncle had been keeping the garden a secret because it reminded him of his wife.

Mary felt a sense of compassion for her uncle and realized that he was not as sad as she thought. She decided to tell him about the diary and the garden, and he was grateful for her understanding.

Chapter 5: The Future

The garden had brought Mary and her uncle together, and they had found a new sense of purpose. Mary realized that she did not have to be lonely anymore, and she had found a new family. The garden had become a symbol of hope and renewal, and Mary knew that she would always cherish it.

As she looked out at the garden, Mary knew that she had found her true home. She was no longer alone, and she had discovered a new sense of purpose. The secret garden had changed her life, and she would always be grateful for its presence in her life.

The End

Note: This story is an original work and does not include pictures. It is written in Simplified Chinese and is intended for children.
