Through the Eyes of a Child: Discovering the Healing Power of Home


Once upon a time, in a small and quiet town, there lived a young and curious child named Lily. The town was known for its harmony and the deep-rooted love for the community that each resident shared. This bond made the town a haven for generations that called it home. The people of the town always strived to maintain this culture by passing down the traditional values, wisdom, and secrets from one generation to another. This sense of continuity allowed the town to grow stronger with time, while still preserving its identity. One of these secrets was the healing power of home.

Lily was fortunate to grow up in this nurturing environment. She learned early on the importance of community and the strength that comes from being part of something bigger than oneself. Her favorite place in the town was her home, which always seemed to be a gathering spot for family, friends, and neighbors. Here, she experienced warmth, love, and understanding. Over time, Lily started to cherish and value the special bond she had with her home, as it felt like an extended family member that never left her side.

One day, an unfortunate event forced Lily’s family to relocate to another city for her father’s work. Although this move presented new intriguing opportunities, it also came with challenges. As Lily looked out the window of their new home, all she could see were unfamiliar faces and complex city noises. It didn’t take long for her to realize that, without the familiar comfort of her beloved town and home, life seemed much harder and more disconnected.

Days quickly turned into weeks, and Lily started to miss her old life. The warm camaraderie of her former neighbors and the endless opportunities to play outdoors seemed like a lifetime ago. Even though she made new friends at her school, she couldn’t shake the feeling that something was missing. Her home no longer felt like the safe haven that it once was, as she now found herself visiting it merely as a place to sleep and store her belongings.

One day, during a Self-Inquiry session led by her art teacher, Lily confided in her about her feelings of loneliness and homesickness. The art teacher saw the struggle in Lily’s eyes and gave her a small task that would ultimately change her life: to close her eyes and remember the scent and feel of home. As soon as Lily did that, she was transported back to the warmth and safety of her beloved town. The sweet smell of her mother’s homemade bread and the touch of soft, cozy carpets under her feet brought back a sense of belonging that had been missing for so long.

The next step was to use this memory to create something that would remind her of home and start cultivating a new bond with their new home in the city. Lily decided to build a terrarium. Together with her mother, they collected a small portion of soil, a handful of seeds, and borrowed a few precious plants from her old home. Within this mini-garden, she planted the seeds of her childhood memories and the essence of her past home.

Day by day, Lily’s terrarium grew. The once barren container slowly filled with life as the plants and flowers reached out for the sun and stretched their roots towards the future. For the little girl, it was a miracle that brought hope and healing. When she gazed upon her creation — a small world filled with life, just like her old home — she realized the magic that was unfolding before her.

As she meticulously tended to her terrarium, Lily began to find solace and understanding in the new environment. Her once barren and eerie new home was gradually becoming a place filled with life, love, and memories. Embracing her new surroundings the same way she cherished her past, Lily found the strength to create a bond and a sense of belonging in her new town. The terrarium became the bridge that connected her past and future, teaching her that the essence of a home lies not only in its walls but in the memories, warmth, and love one carries in their heart.

Throughout her journey, Lily had discovered the healing power of home. Two homes, in fact: one nestled in the tranquil countryside, the other rooted in the heart of a bustling city. Both homes became sources of strength, warmth, and comfort, as the lessons she learned from them shaped her character and guided her into adulthood.

Years later, as Lily founding her own family, she carried the wisdom of her journey with her. While her once-empty new home was now brimming with life, her terrarium remained a cherished symbol of her growth and transformation. Passed down through generations, her story serves as a gentle reminder of the enduring power of love, resilience, and the eternal bond between a child and the home that nurtured and shaped them.
