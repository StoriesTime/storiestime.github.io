Life on the Edge: A Frontier Woman's Journey


It was a crisp spring morning when Emily Mayfield stepped off the stagecoach and onto the dusty main street of Willow Creek. The year was 1885, and she was a schoolteacher from the East Coast, with a dream to establish a new school in the untamed west. Emily's eyes scanned the horizon, drinking in the breathtaking vista of rolling hills and vast expanses of untamed grasslands. As the stagecoach driver, Jebediah, handed her a worn leather satchel, Emily's thoughts turned to the boarding house, where she would be staying till she got settled. She paid the driver and joined the steps of the stagecoach with a thoroughfare of Deuces Gulch being her view on the backside of Willow Creek.
