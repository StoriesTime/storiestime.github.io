The Last Colony: Stories of Space Survival


The hum of the engines reverberated through the walls of the colony ship, Andromeda’s Dawn, as it cut through the void. The crew aboard had long since grown accustomed to the strange, almost soothing sound, a constant reminder of their journey through the stars. Outside the reinforced glass windows, distant stars shimmered, and the cold expanse of space stretched beyond comprehension.

Among the crew members was Commander Elias Roan, a seasoned leader who had seen many missions in his time, but none like this. His brow furrowed as he peered over the control panel, his hands moving in practiced precision as he adjusted the course. The mission was supposed to be a simple one—explore a newly discovered habitable planet, survey its resources, and establish a foothold for future colonization. But what had started as a dream of hope had quickly descended into a battle for survival.

The planet, which they had named Seraphis, was not what they expected. Instead of lush greenery and breathable air, they found a desolate, barren landscape with a toxic atmosphere. Their ship had been damaged during their descent, and the crew had been forced to take refuge in the last habitable section of the vessel—a small, fortified section of the ship known as the Sanctuary. With no way to return to Earth and limited resources, the crew was left with one grim task: survive.

Roan sat back in his chair, glancing around the Sanctuary. The space was cramped, its metallic walls cold and sterile. Crew members moved around him, their faces tired and drawn. There was no time for rest—every hour was a fight for resources, for food, for air. The life support systems had been severely damaged, and repairs were slow and uncertain. The once optimistic crew had now become a tight-knit group of survivors, bound together by necessity rather than hope.

“Commander, we need to talk,” said Dr. Amelia Hargrove, the ship’s lead scientist. She was standing by the entrance, her face ashen but determined. Her voice carried a weight of urgency.

Roan nodded, gesturing for her to enter. “What’s the situation, Doctor?”

“We’ve detected something… unusual on the planet’s surface,” Hargrove began, pulling up a holographic display on the wall. The image showed the desolate landscape of Seraphis, but there was something else hidden beneath the planet’s crust—an anomaly, a massive underground structure.

“I’m listening,” Roan said, his attention piqued.

“We don’t know what it is, but the readings suggest it’s some kind of ancient technology—advanced, far beyond anything we’ve encountered. It could be a source of energy, or it could help us understand why Seraphis is so… hostile.” Hargrove’s eyes narrowed. “Whatever it is, it might hold the key to our survival.”

Roan leaned forward, his gaze fixed on the hologram. The idea of finding something on Seraphis that could change their fate was tantalizing, but it also came with risks. No one knew what lay beneath the planet’s surface, and after their ship was damaged during the descent, they couldn’t afford any more mistakes.

“I need a team ready in two hours. We’ll send a small scouting party to investigate. But remember, our primary goal is survival. If anything goes wrong, we pull back immediately.”

“Understood,” Hargrove said, her tone resolute.

The crew had been on Seraphis for weeks, and the planet’s hostile environment showed no sign of easing. The atmosphere was toxic, and the ground was riddled with jagged rock formations that made travel treacherous. The team Roan had assembled—Hargrove, Lieutenant Marco Sanchez, and Engineer Selene Ward—prepared for the expedition with careful precision. They suited up in their exo-suits, which provided life support and protection against the harsh elements.

Roan stood at the airlock, his mind racing with thoughts of what awaited them on the planet’s surface. He knew the stakes. Every moment spent on Seraphis was a gamble. Yet, the hope that they might find something—anything—that could help them survive made it all worthwhile.

“Commander, we’re ready,” Sanchez called out from the airlock.

Roan nodded. “Let’s get this over with.”

The airlock doors slid open, revealing the bleak expanse of Seraphis. The sky was a sickly green, the clouds swirling in unnatural patterns. The ground beneath their feet was cracked and barren, with no sign of life in sight.

“Stay close, and keep your comms open,” Roan ordered. The team moved out, their boots crunching against the rocky surface.

As they made their way toward the coordinates provided by the sensors, the eerie silence of the planet enveloped them. The only sound was the hiss of their life support systems, a constant reminder of how fragile their existence had become.

Hours passed before they reached the site. A massive chasm stretched before them, the edges jagged and uneven. At the bottom of the chasm, hidden beneath layers of rock, lay the entrance to the structure. It was unlike anything Roan had ever seen—a smooth, metallic surface that seemed to hum with energy.

“It’s… alive,” Hargrove whispered, her eyes wide with awe.

Roan stepped forward, examining the entrance closely. The metal was warm to the touch, as if it had been recently activated. He glanced at his team. “Prepare for a controlled breach. Sanchez, Ward—cover the perimeter.”

The two officers moved into position, their weapons at the ready. Hargrove began working with the tech, trying to interface with the structure.

Minutes passed before the entrance slid open with a soft, mechanical hiss. The dark interior beckoned, its depths mysterious and foreboding. Roan led the way, his pulse quickening as they descended into the unknown.

Inside, the air was cool and dry, and the walls were lined with strange symbols and intricate patterns that seemed to shift as they moved past them. It was clear that whatever this place was, it had been built by an intelligence far beyond their own.

“This is incredible,” Hargrove said, her voice filled with awe. “The technology here is… far beyond anything we’ve ever seen.”

Roan motioned for her to be quiet. “We don’t know what we’re dealing with yet. Stay alert.”

As they moved deeper into the structure, they came across a vast chamber. At its center stood a massive, glowing orb—its surface smooth and iridescent. It pulsed with a rhythmic energy that filled the room with an almost hypnotic glow.

“This is it,” Hargrove said, her voice trembling with excitement. “This is what we’ve been searching for.”

Roan stepped forward cautiously, his hand hovering near the orb. There was something almost otherworldly about it, something that felt both dangerous and magnificent at the same time. He wasn’t sure what it was—whether it was a source of power, a relic, or something far more sinister—but he knew that it was their only hope.

Before anyone could say another word, the ground beneath them began to tremble. The walls of the chamber shifted, and the sound of machinery whirring to life echoed throughout the structure. The orb began to glow brighter, its energy pulsing in waves.

“Get back!” Roan shouted, but it was too late.

A blinding light erupted from the orb, engulfing the entire chamber. Roan felt himself being pulled into the light, his body weightless as though he were floating in the void. His mind raced, and then everything went black.

When Roan awoke, he was no longer in the chamber. The world around him was dark, and he could feel the coolness of metal beneath him. Slowly, he pushed himself up, his head pounding. He looked around, disoriented.

The rest of his team was scattered around him, groaning as they came to their senses. Hargrove was the first to speak.

“Where… where are we?” she asked, her voice weak.

Roan surveyed their surroundings. They were no longer on the surface of Seraphis. Instead, they appeared to be inside some sort of vast, abandoned ship or station—its walls lined with strange, unfamiliar technology.

“I don’t know,” Roan admitted. “But whatever we triggered, it’s brought us here. And I have a feeling it’s not over yet.”

As the days passed, the crew explored the strange station they had found themselves in. It was clear that it had been abandoned long ago, but the technology still functioned—albeit erratically. It wasn’t long before they discovered that the station was a remnant of an ancient civilization, one that had vanished without a trace.

The crew began to realize that the orb had transported them to this forgotten place for a reason. They weren’t just surviving—they were part of something much larger. The station held secrets, and those secrets might hold the key to not only their survival but to the survival of humanity itself.

But as they delved deeper into the mysteries of the station, Roan couldn’t shake the feeling that they were not alone. Something was watching them, waiting. The orb had opened a doorway, but it had also set something in motion—something that might be their undoing.

For the survivors of Andromeda’s Dawn, the fight for survival was far from over. The last colony had found a new world, but it was a world filled with shadows, dangers, and questions that no one could answer.

And the truth was, they might not survive long enough to find the answers.

End of Chapter One.
