He had broken George Herman's record for the most home runs in a single season in 1921, zoning in on 59 dingers. Adding to his impressive tally, his door-slamming power leading the New York Yankees to four championships cemented his legacy. But Ruth's life wasn't always the storybook tale of triumph. Born in Baltimore on February 6, 1895, to Katherine and George Ruth Jr., he faced an uncertain future growing up in a low-income household. His comeback story became a fine framework for generations of baseball hopefuls searching for salvation.
