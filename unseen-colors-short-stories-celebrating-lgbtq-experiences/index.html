Unseen Colors: Short Stories Celebrating LGBTQ+ Experiences


Max had always believed that colors held secrets, layers of meaning that most people never saw. To them, colors were something tangible, something to decorate their world, a simple way to define mood or beauty. But Max knew better. To Max, colors were more than just pigments; they were emotions, desires, and experiences layered upon one another, each hue carrying its own weight, its own power.

He had long ago realized that his understanding of colors extended beyond what his eyes could see. When he was younger, Max thought he was strange for seeing the world in ways others didn’t. But as he grew older, he found solace in the way his world unfolded—like a painting that no one else could quite interpret. This, he knew, was his gift, the ability to feel the unseen colors.

One of those colors, the one he had yet to name, existed within the most important part of himself: his identity. It had taken years of trial and error, fear and acceptance, to begin to define what that color was. Max had always felt like he didn’t fit the usual molds. His friends would talk about relationships, and he’d listen, unsure if they were describing something he could ever understand. The words “man” and “woman” seemed incomplete, and Max found himself questioning his place in a world that seemed to box people into categories they could never fully escape.

Max’s friends, Lucy and Jack, were always there for him. They didn’t quite get it, but they were patient, offering him space and understanding. They never forced him into a mold, and over time, Max began to realize that the world didn’t need to understand him for him to feel whole. And so, for a long while, Max lived in a quiet, solitary peace, surrounded by unseen colors, unsure of how to bring them to life.

But as time went on, he began to feel a stirring deep within, a sense that something was ready to emerge. It wasn’t just the abstract yearning of his youth anymore; it was a deep, undeniable pull that demanded expression.

Max’s life in the city felt mundane, like a series of unconnected scenes from a movie that never quite made sense. Yet, on a Friday evening in late spring, everything changed. He wandered into a small, dimly lit bookstore—a place he had passed a thousand times but never noticed before. Inside, the bookshelves were stacked high, stretching toward the ceiling, filled with stories of adventure, love, and discovery. But it was one small section that caught his eye, a sign that read “Unseen Colors.”

Curious, Max reached for a book on the top shelf, its leather cover warm beneath his fingers. He flipped it open, only to find a letter penned in elegant handwriting:

“Dear Reader,
If you’ve found this book, it’s because you are ready to see the world differently. You are ready to see the colors that others overlook, the shades that exist beyond the spectrum of what you have been told is ‘real.’ Here, you’ll learn to see the love that cannot be defined, the lives that cannot be categorized, and the truths that have always been hidden. You are about to embark on a journey that will reveal your own spectrum.”

Max felt a shiver run down his spine. It was as if the words were written for him. As he continued to read, the book revealed stories of people like him—those whose identities could not be easily named or confined by labels. They lived in the in-between spaces, exploring the fluidity of their existence and embracing the many shades of love, desire, and self-expression.

The stories spoke of love in all its forms—romantic, platonic, self-love, and even love for the world around them. Each person in these stories discovered their truth, their unseen color, and learned to live it out loud. For Max, these stories felt like a revelation, like a key unlocking a door to a new world.

Max left the bookstore with the book clutched in his hands. He had found what he didn’t even know he was searching for. As he walked through the city streets, he began to notice the small things—the way the sunlight bounced off the glass of the skyscrapers, the way the people around him moved with purpose, and the subtle rhythm of the world that had always been there, waiting to be seen.

Part Two: Colors in Bloom

Max’s journey of self-discovery began in earnest that summer. Every morning, he sat by his window with the book, reading passages that resonated deeply within him. He experimented with new ways of thinking about himself and the world around him. The book spoke of fluidity, of rejecting the binary concepts of gender and identity, of embracing the ever-changing nature of self-expression. It was an invitation to shed old beliefs and explore a world where everyone could be free to live their truth.

And yet, with this newfound freedom came fear. Max had spent so long hiding pieces of himself, keeping them tucked away for fear of rejection, that the idea of sharing his truth with the world felt impossible. He wasn’t sure how people would react if he embraced this freedom fully, if he let the world see the colors that had long been hidden.

But there was something beautiful about this fear, something that felt like a challenge, a call to rise above the uncertainty. One evening, as Max sat at a local coffee shop, he watched people laugh and talk, some with friends, others alone, but all of them existing in their own little bubbles of experience. He wondered if anyone else felt like he did—if they too had unseen colors they were too afraid to share.

Then he saw her. Across the room, a woman sat with a sketchbook, her dark hair spilling over her shoulders as she bent over her work, focused and serene. Max felt a strange pull, an unspoken connection. There was something about her, something that stirred his heart in a way he had never felt before.

For the first time, Max didn’t feel the overwhelming urge to look away or hide. Instead, he felt the need to reach out. But how? He was still so uncertain of himself, still so unsure of what he wanted, or even who he was.

As if reading his mind, the woman looked up, her gaze meeting his. She smiled, the warmth of her expression inviting him in. She gestured toward the empty seat across from her. Without thinking, Max stood and walked over, his heart pounding.

“Hi,” she said, her voice soft but confident. “I saw you looking. I’m Iris.”

Max felt a surge of warmth flood through him. Her name, Iris, like the flower, like the rainbow. It was a perfect fit.

“I’m Max,” he replied, his voice betraying the nervousness he felt inside.

They spent the next few hours talking, sharing stories, and laughing. Iris was an artist, someone who lived her life through colors, both literal and metaphorical. She spoke of her journey with openness, embracing her own spectrum of identity with grace and strength. It was clear to Max that she was someone who had long since accepted her truth, someone who didn’t apologize for her colors.

Max found himself mesmerized by her words, by the way she spoke about life and love, about being true to oneself no matter the cost. And when she asked him about his own experiences, Max felt the stirrings of something he had never allowed himself to feel—acceptance.

For the first time in his life, Max realized that the color he had been searching for was not something external, not something that could be defined by anyone else. It was his own truth, his own fluidity, his own capacity to love and be loved.

Part Three: Embracing the Spectrum

Over the following weeks, Max and Iris grew closer. They spent their days exploring the city, talking about their dreams and fears, their pasts and presents. Max learned that Iris had struggled with her own sense of identity, having spent years trying to fit herself into a box that didn’t quite suit her. But now, she was free. She had embraced all the colors of her being, each shade representing a part of her story.

As their relationship blossomed, Max began to understand the beauty of being open, of sharing the unseen colors within. He started to realize that there was no singular version of himself—he was a spectrum, constantly shifting, evolving, and growing. And that was okay.

In time, Max found the courage to share his truth with the people in his life. He spoke to Lucy and Jack, telling them about the journey he had been on, about the colors he had discovered within himself. To his surprise, they embraced him with love and understanding, no questions asked. It was as if they had known all along that Max was more than what he had shown them.

Max no longer felt the need to hide. He was no longer a prisoner of his fear or uncertainty. He had discovered that the world was full of people who, like him, were embracing their own colors—people who were learning to live authentically, despite the world’s expectations.

As summer turned to fall, Max and Iris continued their journey together, exploring the depths of love, identity, and self-expression. They knew that their story was just beginning, that there would be challenges ahead. But they also knew that they were no longer afraid to be seen, to share their truth, and to live in full color.

And in that moment, Max understood what he had always known but had never fully embraced: the unseen colors were not hidden at all. They were waiting to be revealed, waiting to be celebrated. And he was finally ready to let them shine.

The End
