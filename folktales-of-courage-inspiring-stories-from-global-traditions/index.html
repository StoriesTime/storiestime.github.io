Folktales of Courage: Inspiring Stories from Global Traditions


In a small village nestled in the mountains of rural Japan, there lived a young girl named Kiyomi. She was known throughout the village for her exceptional bravery and kindness. Every year, the villagers would gather at the local shrine to celebrate the festival of Setsubun, a celebration of good fortune and the coming of spring. This year, Kiyomi decided to do something different. She sought out the village elder, a wise and aged man named Hikaru, to learn about the legendary warriors who had once protected their village from invading armies. Hikaru smiled, his eyes twinkling with a hint of storytelling magic. "Come, Kiyomi, let me tell you the tale of the brave warriors who forged our village's path to prosperity." The Legend of Ōkami-no-Mitsuharu

"Hundreds of years ago, a great king from a neighboring land invaded our village, seeking to assert his dominance. His armies were numerous and strong, but our warriors were few. Yet, they possessed something that no steel or stone could match – their unwavering courage." Kiyomi leaned in closer, her eyes wide with excitement. "Who were they, elder?" "Ah," Hikaru began, "Ōkami-no-Mitsuharu, the Great Wolf Prince. His father, the great emperor, had entrusted him with the sacred duty of protecting their homelands. Ōkami-no-Mitsuharu rode into battle, his fierce wolves and honor at his command. They carved a path of resilience through enemy lines, inspiring their comrades to join the fight."
