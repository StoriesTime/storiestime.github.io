Griffins and Guardians


After the Fall of the Great Cities, humanity scraped and clawed its way towards the shattered pieces of a new world. In this era of darkness and chaos, when danger lurked around every corner, civilization needed protectors.

The Griffins, creatures half-eagle, half-lion, with the wisdom of both the skies and the earth, emerged at the dawn of the new world as Guardians. These noble beings roamed the ashes of countries, lending their strength, valor, and superior senses to safeguard their charges. Across the ruined land, they upheld the fragile threads of civilization and stood like beacons in the darkest of nights.

The first Griffin to bear the mantle of ‘Protector’ was Rimthar, a golden creature with piercing blue eyes that shone like sapphires in the fading light. Though many centuries had passed since his time, stories of Rimthar’s deeds continue to echo in the northern lands. From his prodigious feats, the legacy of the Griffins as the Guardians was born.

Rimthar patrolled the ruins, searching for those in need, especially the young and the vulnerable. For he saw in their eyes, sparkling like the white snow and as clear as the blue sky, a flicker of hope, a resilient light, even when their despair seemed to swallow all around them. This is his story.

When the Great Cities fell, the Aughmag, a reclusive tribe in the frozen north, found themselves at the precipice of their own collapse. Struggling to survive under the ice and snow and amidst the destruction, the tribe had lost faith in their own ability to endure. In stark contrast, their younglings maintained a vibrant hope, so infectiously radiant, it gnawed at their resilience.

The youngest of the Aughmag’s younglings was Nollaig, a four-year-old girl with hair as black as the night and as soft as it was cold. She filled her days with tales of Griffins and their great wisdom, which she had heard from her grandfather during nights by the fire.

One day, during a deep snowfall, while the adults were hunting for food, Nollaig sought warmth in the only place she could: the old clan elder’s dwelling. Entering, she found the crumbling walls illuminated by the weak and flickering light of a solitary candle. There before her, half-cloaked in shadow, stood Rimthar.

The powerful Griffin gazed upon the young girl, the light from the candle reflecting in their brilliant blue eyes. In a voice like the fall of leaves in autumn, spoke Rimthar, “Aughmag, I have long sought you. I am Rimthar, Griffin, and I come to share the wisdom I possess, as guardian of your people.”

In awe, Nollaig listened as Rimthar imparted to her the knowledge of the Griffins, of ancient wisdom, and the duty to protect and preserve their world. The girl listened wide-eyed, absorbing every word like the parched ground drank rain. And as she soaked in the teachings of the Griffin, Rimthar noted that perhaps Nollaig already held within herself, the strength to decipher the riddles of their new world.

When the adults returned, weary and burdened with game, they were shocked to find Rimthar standing in the dim light amongst their young. Yet, it was not their shock that rivaled Verity, but the keen eyes of Nollaig, now as sharp and clear as the winter’s sky. Many would say that Nollaig’s eyes held within them the wisdom of ages, the resilient spirit of her people, and a glimpse of the better future that now lay ahead.

Though spoken in hushed whispers, the tales of Rimthar and Nollaig spread, and soon every ear could hear of the Griffin and the young Aughmag girl who learned in the elder’s dwelling. In the years that followed, the age of Griffins saw great growth, and the tattered threads of civilization were woven into a tapestry stronger than ever.

Rimthar continued to stand as a Guardian, their presence a lasting light in the darkest hours, and Nollaig grew into a valiant leader amongst the Aughmag. For she, like the Griffins, had discovered the wisdom stored within her and learned to harness it.

Together, Rimthar and Nollaig forged an unbreakable bond, uniting the Griffins, the Aughmag, and every other surviving nation under the banner of wisdom and protection. The Solemn Oath, as it came to be known, bound all their hands and hearts and stirred an ever-growing desire to preserve the world for future generations.

And so, Rimthar, Griffin, shone like a guiding star in the constellation and Nollaig, the young Aughmag girl, became a beacon of wisdom, illuminated by the knowledge the Griffin shared with her.

Seasons and centuries passed, and as new stories emerged of the Age of Griffins and their Guardians, a new thread of hope was woven, illuminating a world once shrouded in despair. For mankind had found their light; a golden Griffin and a girl with eyes more ancient than their years.

The future may hold many challenegers to come, but as long as the Griffins and their Guardians remain to watch over and defend, the spirit of the world may yet endure.
