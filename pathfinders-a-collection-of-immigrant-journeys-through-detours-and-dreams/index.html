Our story begins with two such souls: Maya and Rajiv, a young couple bound for the promised land of America, where they hoped to forge a new life and escape the crushing poverty that haunted their village. Despite their love, they were tethered to the earth by a thousand burdens, each step a painful pull as they departed their homeland.

Under the wilted limbs of the Kamini trees, their village square was abuzz with well-wishers, bittersweet parting words, and the rattle of brass pots in honor of their passage. Maya’s mother, Narni, clutched her daughter’s sleeve as the belly of the emerald-green ship hove into view, its presence as much a promise as the cutlass that guarded it against the sea’s whims.
