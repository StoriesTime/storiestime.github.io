At its center was the town's mayor, Alice Wright, a woman whose charm and diplomacy had won the hearts of the townspeople. Unassuming and unassuming, Alice Wright had risen through the ranks of Oakdale's politics, striking friendships with influential business leaders and strengthening the town's economic growth. Her reputation as a public servant was cemented, so much so that it seemed impossible that beneath her polished persona lay a hidden agenda. Behind closed doors, Alice orchestrated a complex web of favors, using her power to line the pockets of select individuals and silence anyone standing in her way. The building contractor, Jack Harris, was one such benefactor. Jack had received numerous lucrative contracts, granting him control over key public projects, while Alice received handsome kickbacks. Rumors and Allegations Word of Alice's shrewd portfolio began to spread among the townsfolk. At first, they dismissed the whispers as mere gossip, but as evidence mounted, it became increasingly difficult to ignore. Oakdale's police chief, Captain James Wilson, noticed anomalies in the town's financial records, where unnecessary expenditures were inserted, and key financial decisions attributed to Alice. Determined to get to the truth, Captain Wilson began gathering his plans to interview key witnesses and construct a case against Alice Wright. As he dug deeper, the politician's seemingly saintly reputation began to crumble. Office assistants, scorned and coerced, would tell a different story altogether: one of stifling control and creating an environment of fear.
