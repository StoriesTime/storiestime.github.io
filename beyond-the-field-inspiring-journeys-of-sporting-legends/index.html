Meet Rachel McAdams, a 30-year-old Paralympic wheelchair basketball player from Australia. Growing up, Rachel was always fascinated by sports, but a spinal cord injury at the age of 17 changed her life forever. She had to relearn how to walk and eventually transition to a wheelchair. As she struggled to come to terms with her new reality, Rachel discovered her passion for basketball. She began attending wheelchair basketball games, watching tutorials, and practicing obsessively. Born with prosthetic legs, Axel's family encouraged his athletic pursuits from a young age. He found solace in running, pushing his limits and exploring the outdoors. Axel faced numerous setbacks, including injuries and technological failures. Nevertheless, he continued to refine his stride and fine-tune his prosthetics.
