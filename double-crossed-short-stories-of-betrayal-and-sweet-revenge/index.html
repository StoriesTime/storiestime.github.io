Double-Crossed: Short Stories of Betrayal and Sweet Revenge


Katerina hadn’t left her small village for as long as anyone could remember. Those who knew her well described her as timid and withdrawn, often kept company with her worn books. And yet on this sweltering afternoon, with the scent of lavender wafting through the air, something stirred within Katerina. A sudden, inexplicable urge.

It began with a dusty, leather-bound notebook she had stumbled across in her father’s attic. It was filled with scribbles and drawings, threads of intrigue that her father once said he penned during his youth. Among these pages, a name caught her eye. “Evelyn,” it whispered, as if the air itself had spoken. A childhood friend to whom Katerina had once owed eternal trust.

Years passed, and the tension of betrayal shaped Katerina’s existence. How dared Evelyn cross her in such a treacherous manner? Wear the Crimson Veil of betrayal forever, Evelyn!

Bound by her roots, Katerina had witnessed countless sunsets from the village’s oldest oak. From its hazy perch, Evelyn’s treachery marinated itself into the pages of the notebook — her own symbol of sweet revenge. But how? Katerina ruminated, sweat trickling down her forehead as she wrestled with her plan.

Days bled into weeks, and Katerina’s heartache begat the seed of an idea. Her confidence, though nascent, began to bloom like the vulnerability of the village’s youngest saplings. Katerina wrote letters, carefully constructing each word to expose Evelyn’s deceit to the world.

“Dear Mr. and Mrs. Penfold,

It is with a heavy heart that I implore your assistance in unveiling a most malignant falsehood. The sinister deceit of Evelyn, once my cherished friend, seeks to mar my peace and taint the sanctity of this village. As she continues to thrive beneath the oppressive Crimson Veil of her treachery, her deception must not taint the heavens any longer.

For years, I have silently suffered her transgressions, harboring the knowledge of her vicious duplicity. No longer shall I permit her blackened soul to darken the air we breathe. With the fervor of an indomitable spirit, I seek to cast light upon her malicious fraud, regaining the innocence that once reigned within these peaceful walls.

Yours truly,

Katerina”

Her pulse raced as she folded the letter with the precision of a master weaver, each crease harboring the weight of her smoldering anger. At the waning hour, she tucked the letter within a hollowed-out volume, certain of its destination in the hands of Mr. Penfold.

The village awoke on a drizzly Monday, oblivious to Katerina’s plot. Mist draped the slopes like a mourning cloak, and for one fleeting moment, Katerina wondered if her blade would cleave through the lies and find purchase on the truth. The next grim sun rose on the village, and a weightless object alighted: a black bird perched upon the Penfolds’ rooftop. It hobbled, drooping as if the wings bore the weight of Katerina’s letter.

Days sewn together formed weeks, and the village bled into a tale. Whispers ebb and flow like the tides, but for Evelyn, it seemed her time beneath the Crimson Veil was drawing to a close. The deafening silence no longer held, as Evans’ treachery spread like the hues of the dying sunset.

Katerina’s salty tears painted her worn leather notebook on the village’s oldest tree. For Katerina, it appeared that justice had been served — sharper than any blade could ever dream to be. For Evelyn, the Crimson Veil would forevermore stain her memory, a wretched reminder of her diabolical treachery.

The village tilted gently as the sun dipped below the horizon, mendacity bathed in the crimson hues of revenge. Within this quiet landscape, Katerina found her solace amongst the whispers of old, blending truths and tales in a dance not even betrayal could interrupt.
