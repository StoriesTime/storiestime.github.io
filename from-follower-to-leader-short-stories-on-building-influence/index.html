From Follower to Leader: Short Stories on Building Influence


It was a cool autumn morning when Eliza first stepped into the bustling city of Harrowsfield. The streets were filled with a mix of hurried workers, locals catching up with their neighbors, and tourists snapping pictures of the old brick buildings lining the avenues. To the untrained eye, it was just another city, but to Eliza, it was her new beginning.

She had been hired as an entry-level employee at a renowned marketing firm, known for its innovative campaigns and dynamic work culture. She was young, eager, and full of potential but without much experience. Despite her qualifications, she knew she had much to learn if she wanted to rise in the ranks. Harrowsfield was the place where she could prove herself, but Eliza had a long way to go. She wasn’t yet a leader; she was just another follower, another cog in the machine.

Chapter 1: The First Step

Eliza’s first few weeks at the firm were nothing short of overwhelming. She was surrounded by experts in their fields, all of whom seemed to have their paths already paved out. From her first interaction with Nathan, the head of creative strategy, to meeting Sarah, the team leader who managed day-to-day operations, Eliza was both in awe and intimidated.

She took careful notes during meetings, asked questions when needed, and observed her colleagues closely. As she sat quietly at her desk, she noticed something unusual. Nathan was often the one leading discussions, guiding the team through brainstorming sessions, and providing feedback. Sarah, on the other hand, was always there to ensure the work was getting done efficiently, stepping in when necessary to offer support. Yet, the one thing that stood out was their ability to command attention without being domineering. They had influence, the kind that made people listen, respect, and follow their lead.

Eliza wanted that. She knew it wasn’t something you could simply obtain through job titles or experience. It had to be earned, built over time. She wasn’t yet sure how to begin, but she was determined to figure it out.

Chapter 2: Understanding Influence

It didn’t take long before Eliza realized that leadership wasn’t about being the loudest or the most assertive. Influence, she discovered, came from the ability to make others feel valued. She began to notice how Sarah and Nathan interacted with the team. They didn’t just give orders; they engaged with their colleagues, listened intently, and encouraged input from everyone. Their confidence came not from dominating conversations, but from empowering others to contribute their ideas.

One afternoon, Sarah called Eliza into her office for a one-on-one meeting. Sarah, with her sharp yet approachable demeanor, leaned back in her chair and said, “Eliza, I’ve noticed how quickly you’ve been picking things up here. You’re always eager to learn, and that’s a great trait. But I want to challenge you. You’ve been a fantastic follower, but I think it’s time you start thinking like a leader. The next step is yours to take.”

Eliza was taken aback. She hadn’t expected such direct feedback. “What do you mean?” she asked, a little unsure.

Sarah smiled. “Leaders are not just the ones who tell people what to do. They’re the ones who inspire others to act, who encourage collaboration and innovation. It’s time to stop waiting for permission to contribute your ideas. Start stepping up in meetings, share your thoughts. And most importantly, help others rise with you.”

That was the turning point for Eliza. She understood that leadership wasn’t about waiting for the right moment—it was about creating those moments. It was about taking initiative, even when it was uncomfortable, and showing others the way forward.

Chapter 3: The Power of Initiative

The following weeks were a whirlwind of personal growth for Eliza. She started taking initiative, offering her thoughts during meetings, and volunteering for projects that others were hesitant to tackle. At first, it felt unnatural. She was used to following the flow, sticking to the tasks assigned to her. But slowly, she began to see the impact of her actions.

One day, during a team brainstorming session, Nathan turned to her with a raised eyebrow. “Eliza, what do you think about this idea?” he asked.

She froze for a moment. This was the moment she had been waiting for, the moment where she could prove herself. “Well,” she began, nervously at first, “what if we took a different approach? Instead of targeting the typical demographic, we could focus on a younger, more digitally-savvy audience. They’re more likely to engage with our brand on social media platforms.”

Nathan nodded thoughtfully. “I like it. What do you think, team?”

To Eliza’s surprise, her suggestion was met with genuine interest. The team started discussing her idea, adding their own twists to it. By the end of the meeting, they had a plan in place to test the new approach. Eliza felt a sense of accomplishment, not because of the idea itself, but because her voice had been heard, and it had sparked a new direction for the project.

From that moment on, Eliza’s confidence grew. She continued to step up, take on more responsibility, and offer her insights. She began to see the value in taking risks, speaking up, and engaging with her colleagues. The more she contributed, the more others started to turn to her for advice and feedback. She was no longer just following; she was leading.

Chapter 4: Building Trust and Relationships

As Eliza’s influence grew, she realized that leadership was about more than just ideas or actions. It was about building trust and relationships. She started making an effort to connect with her colleagues on a personal level, taking the time to understand their strengths, weaknesses, and aspirations. She wasn’t just interested in their work; she was interested in them as people.

One day, after a long week of meetings and deadlines, Eliza noticed that one of her colleagues, Mark, seemed particularly stressed. He was usually the one to crack jokes and keep the mood light, but today he was quiet, absorbed in his work.

“Hey, Mark,” Eliza said, walking over to his desk, “you okay?”

Mark looked up, surprised by the concern in her voice. “Yeah, just a lot on my plate right now. Trying to keep up with everything, you know?”

Eliza nodded. “I get it. If you need help with anything, I’m here. You’ve been carrying a lot lately.”

Mark smiled, clearly relieved. “Thanks, Eliza. I might take you up on that.”

Over the next few days, Eliza and Mark worked closely on a project together. She offered him support when he needed it and encouraged him when he doubted his abilities. By the end of the project, Mark had not only completed his tasks on time but had also gained a renewed sense of confidence in his work. Eliza’s willingness to help him without expecting anything in return created a bond of trust between them.

This experience reinforced a crucial lesson for Eliza: leadership wasn’t about getting credit for everything—it was about lifting others up. True influence came from the relationships you built and the trust you earned.

Chapter 5: Leading the Team

Months passed, and Eliza’s reputation within the firm continued to grow. She had transitioned from a follower to a leader in her own right. Her ideas were regularly sought after in meetings, and her colleagues often came to her for advice or collaboration. She had built a network of allies and supporters, not by being the loudest voice in the room, but by being the most consistent, the most reliable, and the most willing to help.

One day, Sarah called her into her office again. This time, there was a different energy in the room.

“Eliza, I’ve been watching your progress, and I’m impressed. I think it’s time for you to take on a leadership role. How would you feel about managing a new team for our upcoming project?”

Eliza’s heart raced. It was a big step, one she hadn’t anticipated so soon. But she knew she was ready. “I’m ready,” she replied with confidence.

That day marked the beginning of a new chapter in Eliza’s journey. She wasn’t just following anymore; she was leading others, guiding them with the same principles that had helped her grow: initiative, trust, and empowerment.

Chapter 6: A Leader in the Making

As the months turned into years, Eliza continued to refine her leadership skills. She took on more challenging projects, mentored junior staff, and became a trusted voice in her company. But through it all, she never forgot the lessons she had learned in her early days as a follower. Leadership was not about titles or accolades—it was about inspiring others, empowering them to achieve their potential, and helping them grow alongside you.

Eliza had started her journey as a follower, unsure of her place in the world of business. But with time, persistence, and the right mindset, she had built the influence necessary to become a leader. She was no longer the one waiting for opportunities to come her way; she was the one creating opportunities for herself and for others.

And as she stood before her team, ready to take them on their next big project, she realized that leadership wasn’t just about guiding people; it was about sharing the journey together.
