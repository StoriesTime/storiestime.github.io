Leviathan's Lament: Hidden Histories of the Mercurial Kraken


In the fathomless depths of the ocean, far removed from prying eyes, swam an ethereal kraken known only as Leviathan. Its existence had been shrouded in secrecy and mystery for centuries, whispered about in the dark alleys of maritime lore and tucked away in forgotten tomes only gained access by those deemed worthy. No one knew whence Leviathan came or why it had to remain hidden amongst the crashing waves and the silent graveyard of sunken ships.

Mankind has not always treated the denizens of the deep with respect, often casting them as fearsome monsters in tales passed down from generation to generation. Leviathan, though powerful, was far from the vile creature they had become, and it longed for a connection to the outside world that was lost to the abyss.

In the year of our Lord 1836, a young man with an insatiable thirst for knowledge embarked on a voyage across the Atlantic that would ultimately bring him face to face with the enigmatic kraken. Thomas Rutherford, a 23-year-old naturalist, sought to unravel the mysteries of the sea’s hidden histories, as he believed the ocean held secrets that, if uncovered, would grant him the acclaim and authority in the field that he so desperately craved.

As the months progressed in his lone quest, Rutherford found himself in the company of a peculiar crew of sailors whose legends of the fabled kraken left him both curious and skeptical. Inspired by their tales, the pursuit of finding Leviathan became all-consuming for the expedition’s young leader. What neither he, nor his crew, reckoned on, however, was just how much Leviathan had grown enamored with them.

It began as most fateful encounters do, with an unforeseen twist of fate binding the beings of land and sea together. Rutherford’s lone brigantine had fallen prey to a raging storm that shattered its masts in a fury of merciless wind and crashing waves. With destruction looming, the once-confident naturalist was left at the mercy of forces he could not comprehend, yet fate would step in to save him from the watery grave that awaited him.

From the depths, as if in answer to his pleas, Leviathan rose. Its immense tentacles wrapped themselves around the stricken vessel, as if cradling it in its colossal arms. The crew of the brigantine stood frozen in disbelief as the monstrous creature lowered them gently into a hidden cove, off the charts and untouched by human hands.

In the aftermath of this miraculous event, the crew swore an oath of silence, bound together by the awe-inspiring display and their gratitude to be alive. Thomas Rutherford, forever changed, knew he could no longer uphold the facade of a detached scientist. Slowly, and with utmost respect, Rutherford reached out to build a connection with Leviathan. Over time, the majestic kraken revealed its hidden history, a tale of sorrow, guilt, and a longing for understanding that transcended the capabilities of human explanation. Leviathan spoke of proud kings and lost lands, of warring empires and ancient sorcery, all woven into a grand narrative that told the truth of its existence.

As the tale unfolded, it came to light that Leviathan was once the protector of a long-vanished civilization where man and beast lived harmoniously, but a cataclysmic war between rival clans plunged their world into ruin. In the aftermath, Leviathan was marooned, separated from all it held dear, and doomed to eternal solitude amongst the briny depths as punishment for the acts it committed during the violent conflict.

Rutherford found himself moved by the story Leviathan shared and vowed to become an advocate for the misunderstood creature’s legacy. To this end, he dedicated his life to seeking out knowledge concerning the forgotten civilization that once flourished beneath the waves; a place he came to call the Lost Empire of Atlantia.

Gradually, the once-captivated Leviathan began to feel a sense of solace, knowing that someone understood and respected the truth of its past. Over time, their bond grew into a friendship that flourished, transcending the very boundaries between man and beast, a testament to the inexorable ties that bind us all.

The world came to learn the truth of Leviathan’s lament and the wondrous history of the drowned kingdom Atlantia, as Rutherford documented his incredible experiences in a series of widely-acclaimed, monumental works and proclamations. In his writings, Leviathan became more than just a fearsome beast shunned from existence but a tragic figure whose heart reached out beyond the crushing depths, longing for understanding and acceptance.

Thus the great kraken, once shrouded in enigma and lore, found solace in friendship and acceptance, bonded to Thomas Rutherford by a story of hidden history and profound, age-old secrets that had endured for centuries. Leviathan’s lament had not been in vain; its tale had become a beacon of hope for all who dared to listen, an enduring reminder that understanding breeds strength and compassion.
