Bridging Cultures: Architectural Styles from Around the World


The moment you step into a city, town, or village, you notice something unique about its structure and design – the architectural style. From ancient temples of Japan to slender Gothic spires of Europe, architecture gives a glimpse into the land's past and character. It's a reflection of a community's history, cultural practices, social status, and philosophical leanings. As you travel across the world, you stumble upon a breathtaking array of architectural styles, each as vibrant as the emerald hills of New Zealand and as intricate as the finely glazed palaces of Syria. Buddhist Sensibilities: A Japanese Tale Let's start in Japan where serenity cascades through wood and stone, epitomizing Zen architecture. Situated deep in the Miyajima Island lies the Itsukushima Shrine nestled between the ocean tides and is skillfully nurtured by devotees of Shintoism. Considered as one the Three Great Shrines of Japan, the understated elegance of Itsukushima harmoniously blends simplicity with Zen vigilance.
