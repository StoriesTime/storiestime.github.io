Through the Eyes of a Child: Adventures in Pandemic Times


Little Alex lived in a small town nestled in the heart of the country. Life had always been simple, full of laughter and boundless energy. However, when the pandemic struck, the once-routine moments became a mystery, and the simplicity vanished into a blurred, uncertain future.

In his usual fashion, Alex’s curiosity led him to seek answers and make sense of the new world order. Being only eight years old, he had a unique perspective on the situation. He looked at the world through the innocent eyes of a child, sometimes missing the harsh realities and other times sensing the palpable fear that loomed over everyone.

The First Bubble

Alex discovered the concept of a “bubble” within his first week of classes transitioning to remote learning. Days grew shorter, and school felt distant. Before the pandemic, interaction with classmates was a source of joy, a place to share laughter and ideas. But now, it had shrunk to a small blue icon on a screen. Friends, like the bubbles floating in their fishbowl, lived in a different realm that Alex couldn’t reach.

Yet, he found comfort in the creation of his very own bubble with a plastic wand his mother bought him. Each bubble, a sphere of glorious transparency, would burst and scatter into the air upon contact. It was the perfect representation of their lives, a fleeting moment that quickly dissolved. Even though his mother affectionately called them his ’therapy bubbles,’ Alex knew there was more to life than just these bubbles.

The Lost Playground

As the pandemic progressed, one of Alex’s favorite places, the public park, closed its gates. His playground had been his sanctuary, a place of boundless energy spent in tireless climbing, swings reaching new heights, and bonds formed with friends. But that lifeline was snipped, leaving a void in his life.

Desperate to keep the playful essence alive, Alex started to create his make-shift playground in the confines of his home: an old, wooden plank laid out as a slackline, a precarious rope swing hung from a tree in the backyard, and cardboard boxes crafted into fortresses. However, there was a gnawing dissatisfaction, and he found himself yearning for human smile-lines, palpable joy, and the laughter he used to share in the park.

Pandemic Birthday Blues

Turning eight was a very big deal for little Alex. His friend’s zoom birthday party did not feel the same as the grand celebration he’d imagined, the one filled with balloons, giggles, and cake smeared across his face. The reality of living in a pandemic cast a shadow on the birthday fun. However, Alex didn’t let the anomaly dampen his spirit. With determination, he transformed his small living room into a festive arena, as the mask-adorned zoom guests looked on, their smiles somehow reflecting his own.

The Art of Connection

While the outside world lurched into a realm of uncertainty, Alex found solace in the art of connecting with his loved ones. Delight started seeping back into his life as he began writing letters to friends, boasting colorful drawings, infectious laughter, and doses of sunshine. Furthermore, his connection with the elderly neighbor blossomed as they exchanged hand-made masks and stories through their windows.

The Silver Lining

Though fear and uncertainty continually swirled around him, Alex managed to find a silver lining. The pandemonium shaped his innocence, making him realize the value of freedom. For him, the pandemic had amplified his imagination, developing his resilience, and teaching the profound art of connection.

In the darkness of the pandemic, little Alex had found an escape: his own unique way of living and creating within the confines of the world confined by the virus. The story of his adaptation became a beacon of hope, to one day, break free and soar - like his bubbles - towards the vibrant colors of normalcy.
