Frida Kahlo: The Art of Resilience and Passion


Frida Kahlo, renowned for her vibrant paintings that reflected both the beauty and the pain of life, sat surrounded by her art supplies in her home studio in Coyoacán, Mexico. Born on July 6, 1907, to a family of modest means, Frida had always known a life of hardship. But it was her accident on a bus at the age of 18 that changed her life forever. As she picked up paintbrush after paintbrush, the jar of turpentine beside her in the flickering candlelight, Frida poured her emotion into the canvas. Her eyes, shaded with an unending legacy of pain from her constant surgeries and her life-saving spine implants, connected with the colors, using a surplus of emotion— while tattoos gleamed across every limb like emotional landmarks.
