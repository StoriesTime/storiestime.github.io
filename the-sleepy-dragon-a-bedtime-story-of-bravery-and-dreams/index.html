The Sleepy Dragon: A Bedtime Story of Bravery and Dreams

3 min · 562 words

Once upon a time, in a land far, far away, there lived a sleepy dragon named Morpheus. Morpheus was not like other dragons; he didn’t enjoy causing chaos or scaring villagers. Instead, Morpheus loved to sleep, and he was very good at it. He had a cozy cave deep in the heart of the Misty Mountains, where he could slumber undisturbed for weeks on end.

One day, a young girl named Elara stumbled upon Morpheus’ cave while she was exploring the mountains. She was amazed by the sight of the snoring dragon, with wisps of smoke escaping from his nostrils as he dreamt. Elara had heard tales of the fearsome dragons that terrorized the land, but Morpheus seemed different. He looked peaceful, almost serene, in his slumber.

As Elara watched the dragon, she noticed a small satchel lying by his side. Curiosity piqued, she approached the satchel and found a note inside. It read, “To whomever finds this, wake the dragon and share with him your bravest dream.”

Elara was hesitant at first. Dragons were supposed to be dangerous, and waking one up could be perilous. But the note intrigued her, and she felt a strange connection to the sleepy dragon. Taking a deep breath, she summoned her courage and approached Morpheus.

“Morpheus,” she called softly, “I have a dream to share with you.”

The dragon stirred, his emerald eyes slowly opening to gaze at the young girl before him. “What is your dream, little one?” he rumbled, his voice deep and soothing.

“I dream of a world where everyone is brave enough to follow their hearts and pursue their passions,” Elara said, her voice unwavering.

Morpheus listened intently, his eyes sparkling with interest. As Elara spoke, the dragon’s scales began to shimmer with a soft, warm light, and the cave filled with a gentle, dreamlike aura.

When she finished, Morpheus smiled, a rare sight for a dragon. “Thank you for sharing your dream, Elara. It takes true bravery to open your heart to others.”

From that day on, Elara visited Morpheus often, sharing her dreams and listening to the dragon’s wisdom. She learned that Morpheus had once been a guardian of dreams, but as people stopped believing in the power of dreams, he had fallen into a deep slumber, his own dreams fading away.

Determined to help her new friend, Elara embarked on a quest to reignite the people’s belief in dreams. She traveled far and wide, sharing stories of bravery and inspiring others to pursue their dreams. Slowly but surely, the people began to believe once more.

As belief in dreams spread, Morpheus grew stronger, and his cave became a sanctuary for dreamers from all walks of life. Together, Elara and Morpheus nurtured the dreams of the world, filling the land with hope and courage.

And so, the sleepy dragon became known not for his fearsome roars, but for the warmth and inspiration he brought to all who dared to dream. Elara and Morpheus showed the world that bravery comes in many forms, and that sometimes, the most powerful act of courage is to dream.

As the years passed, the legend of Morpheus and Elara spread far and wide, inspiring generations to embrace their dreams and face the world with courage and hope. And in the heart of the Misty Mountains, the sleepy dragon slumbered peacefully, surrounded by the dreams of those who believed in the power of bravery and dreams.

The end.
