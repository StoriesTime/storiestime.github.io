Chords of Destiny: Stories of Immortal Music Legends


The tavern had seen centuries come and go, nestled at the edge of a forgotten forest where shadows whispered secrets to the wind. Known as the Harmony Haven, it was a haven for travelers, dreamers, and seekers of truth. On this particular evening, its aged wooden beams bore witness to the start of an extraordinary story.

The faint hum of a guitar resonated through the air as the door creaked open. A stranger stepped into the dimly lit room, cloaked in a midnight-blue coat. His appearance was peculiar, but his attitude seemed ordinary enough. The patrons barely glanced at him as they indulged in the music spilling from the bar’s stage—a local group playing improvised jazz. But there was something about the stranger that the tavern itself felt compelled to notice. His footsteps carried a rhythm not born of human intent but something far older, something ancient.

Dominic, the barkeeper, was wiping a glass, but his movements faltered as he caught sight of the stranger walking directly toward him. The man’s eyes were captivating—like the depths of a limitless ocean. His presence seemed to resonate with the sounds around him, bending the notes subtly in harmony with his every motion. Dominic had a lifetime in music venues, yet he could only whisper one thought to himself: “He’s here for something more than the drinks.”

“I’m looking for someone,” the stranger said, his voice smooth and melodic, sliding into the room like a forgotten tune.

“What’s the name?” Dominic asked, his curiosity rousing with tangible heft.

“I don’t know their name,” the stranger replied, his expression soft. “But I know their sound.” He opened his coat, pulling out what seemed like fragments of sheet music. The scraps emitted a faint glow that pulsed in time with the distant beats of the jazz set.

Dominic glanced down at the floating fragments, then back at the stranger. “For someone searching by sound, you seem to have your own melody.”

The stranger smiled faintly, nostalgia flickering in his features. “Everyone does, in their own way. But I follow the immortal chords.”

Laughter erupted from a nearby table, drawing Dominic’s attention as a forceful strum interrupted the mood. A guitarist with wild eyes, equally enthralled by drink and brilliance, proclaimed, “Immortal! Everyone’s chasing immortality around here, friend. Whether it’s written in ink, sung to the heavens, or etched in strings.”

“Immortality is not a chase,” the stranger replied evenly. “It finds those who live their music.”

Dominic leaned in, intrigued despite himself. His work in the tavern hadn’t prepared him for abstract conversations that turned mundane nights into prophetic epics. “Immortal music legends, huh? Like the ones we read about? I take it you’re here collecting stories?”

“No,” the stranger replied, “I am the thread connecting them.”

Long before the conception of Harmony Haven, the world had unknowingly birthed extraordinary musicians. Some were composers, pioneers of sounds molded to awaken humanity’s truest emotions. They weren’t simply human creators—but sparks ignited from something eternal, channeled into corporeal flesh. Each legend left behind marks of their music woven into reality: certain chords that vibrated higher than earthly dimensions or whispered secrets embedded deeply in orchestral masterpieces.

Mozart, the playful genius, had found one such chord in childlike wonder. He described it as hearing “angels conducting” through him. Centuries before Mozart, there was Yendara of the Eastern Valleys—a flute player whose melodies stretched across battlefields, ceasing wars in mid-conflict and leaving soldiers weeping beneath skies that seemed to pulse with love. Legends like Janis Joplin and Jimi Hendrix joined unknowingly, like fragments completing an unfinished symphony. They moved the world like flames bursting from cold stone, uniting countries, lovers, and strangers.

The stranger explained quietly, his words hanging in curiosity. “Over time, the chords—woven like golden filaments—dim in power. I find those still connected.”

Dominic realized what the stranger brought wasn’t a metaphorical weight but a lingering mission. “So, people’s music echoes destiny closer than others?”

“It’s not talent alone—or fame,” the stranger said. “It’s the resonance. Some harmonize with their soul so completely their results return ripple cascades—but many falter unwittingly missing resonance.”

Some say traveling across dimensions steals something costly.
