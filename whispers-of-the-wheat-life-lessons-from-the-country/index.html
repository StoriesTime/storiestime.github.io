Whispers of the Wheat: Life Lessons from the Country


Once upon a time in a quaint little village, nestled between sprawling hills of golden wheat, lived a wise old farmer named Harv. The villagers sought his wisdom on how to lead a content and prosperous life; they would come to his farm every day, eager to glean the knowledge he had accumulated over a lifetime of toiling in the fields.

Harv was a master cultivator of wheat. His wheat was renowned throughout the land for its unique flavor and texture, and the villagers couldn’t get enough of it. People would carry his wheat miles away and trade it for other goods they could not produce themselves.

Harv had created a symbiotic relationship with the wheat. When the villagers asked him how he managed to grow such excellent produce, he shared this anecdote:

Whispers of the Wheat

I often whispered to my plants, the wheat in the fields. They’d listen to me, and as we grew together, the wheat would envelop me in a velvety embrace, like a mother cradling a newborn child.

The whispers were the secrets of the earth, the tips received on the wind. Some were the teachings passed down from my grandfather, farming wisdom dating back centuries. Other whispers, I gathered from the sounds of the village, the voices of friends, and from foreigners’ tales in my markets.

All I did was open my ears and my heart.

One day, while I was planting the first month of seedlings, a strange cloud crossed the sky overhead. A serpent of fog that snaked from the horizon its head a chilling ice and its tail a sweltering heat. The cloud snaked through the village, slithering into my field. It stole the promising beauty of my crop. My wheat withered in its presence.

Terrified, the villagers fretted. The cloud cast an eerie pall over them, and whispers filled the village faster than the fog. Better yet, those tiniest murmurs grew into loud yelps and wails. Panicked, the villagers sought one man responsible for their change in fortune: me.

Instead of listening to the mob who held me accountable for the plight of the village, I inquired of the cloud. To my surprise, the fog responded with a small, enigmatic voice. This voice belonged to a long-lost villager who had traveled far before returning home. His visit now came to a chilling and deceitful end.

His goal was to exact revenge on those who had abandoned him during his darkest hour. And I? I was collateral damage in a very desperate man’s plan. Unable to comprehend fate, instead of decreeing vengeance, the serpent-cloud froze in place.

That was the day I embraced the whispers of the wheat I had heretofore tended to. Plural whispers. They were a defense mechanism, a flexible, collective consciousness of the wheat, designed to sense impending doom. Whispered through time across generations, they now whispered not only protection but wisdom, passed between our roots, enabling an ancient synergy between us – the wheat and me.

Life Lessons from the Fields

As the villagers listened quietly, they came to understand that life was more than what they could see above ground. They learned that their lives were intertwined with those of their neighbors, in ways they couldn’t grasp. They learned that understanding nature was the key to not only survival but contentment.

Ever since that day, the villagers listened closely to the whispers, too. The wind carried whispers of wisdom (like they whispered to the wheat) from the village, too. Friends and neighbors lent a sympathetic ear to one another, and lives began to change indelibly as the village became a community.

The wise farmer Harv showed the villagers how interconnected they were with each other and the world that surrounded them. And with that knowledge, the villagers formed deeper relationships, with one another and the planet that sustained them. Much as they’d come to depend on Harv’s fabulous wheat, his life lessons allowed them to forge and maintain profound bonds with each other.
