Palette of Palates: A Taste Expedition


In the bustling heart of Paris, nestled cozily in a quaint corner of Montmartre, there stood a modest door with an inconspicuous brass plaque - “Palette of Palates: A Taste Expedition”. Those who found it, spoke little of discovery or luck. Their lips merely fluttered in quiet whispers in reference to something akin to serendipity.

One sultry afternoon, Etienne Dupont, a rising chef who had just returned from an acclaimed culinary expedition in the Amazon, unlatched the door with an air of excitement etched on every line of his dashing features. He was welcomed by the heavenly scent of rosemary and basil mingling with what seemed like every spice from every corner of the world.

Maison Palette, as the enthusiastic owner personalised the quaint establishment, had rested untroubled by time, lapsing in a slow dance with shadows in the dimly lit corner where it had chosen to reside. The marvellous mix of rustic wooden touches and pungent perfumes coupling with the warmth of the sun that padded through the high windows, played to the tune of an old painter - crafting dreams, emotions and the subtle fear of fulfilment.

While Etienne propped upon the worn-out leather swivel chair, his fingers absently twirling the salt and pepper shakers that bore scratches and doodles of an unmistakably creative aura, Madame Bovary, a food historian with 34 years of expertised under her belt and an insatiable appetite for spices and flavours, decided that these were fortuitous grounds for her next stupendous endeavour. She felt it. The tickle under her ribs grew by the second, urging her to turn the handle; the temptation of an uncharted territory had finally seeped into her pores.

The door creaked, inducing Madame Bovary to suppress a shiver that momentarily tremored down her spine. Etienne glanced at her with surprise, never would he have predicted the assembly of a culinary connoisseur, especially clad in fluttering oversized glasses, a crumpled shirt that screamed of a thousand secret ingredients, and unkempt hair kissed by flavours from around the world.

And as their eyes met, they found themselves silhouetted against the backdrop of life’s kaleidoscope. They instantly forgot the gentle melody playing in the background, unaware that old Nick, the venerable custodian of Maison Palette, would improvise a music score only to lull them into a shared dream.

The journey began expectantly, with stories scratched on paper and parchment, stirred spices in various stages of aromatic uncertainty, forming a banter in the air that threatened to overcook their anticipation. Etienne recounted what he had seen amongst the diverse spices of Brazil; Madame Bovary could not help but flaunt her records of guilds lost and savoured memories of pre-industrial-era ingredients in Italy. Brewed coffees and whiskeys materialising through Nick’s ghostly fingertips blended splendidly with the landscape of talk that resided beyond words. As Etienne narrated repetitive phrases “I’ve seen a bird, I’ve tasted the Amazon,” he could not suppress the sensation of taste that lingered on his tongue, the texture of every spice untouched by civilisation.

Madame Bovary, she of the expert palate, must’ve conjured up flavours over the centuries; those aromatic tapestries woven by centuries of gastronomy. She frequently interjected with her recipes - her exotic omelette, and her earthy risotto that had unwittingly retained the scent of Venice. An invisible pot bubbled in their midst, stewing together flavours mangled through years of talk, compelling Nick to drift back into his realm of titans, lost in a haze of pieced-together clairvoyance.

A scarlet sun dipped itself into the horizon, painting the sky in multiple layers of melting marzipan. As twilight filled the room, the eager-beaver moonbeams cast a dance on the window panes, each flickering illumination giving life to the mineral-rich wall-mounted collections that reflected a symphony of colours borrowed from salt-rising spices.

In no time, the trio of strangers, drenched in the warm embrace of an unambiguous union, had passed into the realms of the exotic. The flavours woven, burnt, and lovingly tasted inside that esoteric door, carried a hint of the impossible fused with human fantasies. At the Palette of Palates, the sunsets whispered more than the moon ever gleamed.

Etienne Dupont laid down a ruler on a deeply marked recipe - his homemade chocolate truffles embellished with a dash of smoked paprika. Madame Bovary dispensed a fruit compote, generous in sugar but paradoxically unsweet - its granules like treasure embarked on a confluence with cardamom and cinnamon.

Propped against the chair, Etienne bumped his knee against the secret compartment intended for storing ancient sauces that Hausfrau Bovary procured on her expeditions. A potion came spilling - bright crimson, potent, the taste of time danced on the tongue; a sensation as brainwashed as the lingering kiss of a long-lost affair. As he tasted it, Etienne noted “Unfathomable”, traced a finger on the taste memory. It was recorded, and thus given life. Random footsteps echoing upstairs told them the door had escaped the polite whisperings, and unwittingly paused at the entrance.

A young girl, with a tapestry of freckles and sparkling hair, nicknamed her a following - “Isabella,” had stumbled on the moments’ pause, the sudden silence wedged into the metallic loops of a distinguishing moment. She could hardly imitate her mother’s apron tying and grape-stomping embrace of ingredients - her passion, rather impromptu, created the restroom-rush-hour aroma, distinguishing itself as both bewitching and bewilderment. Maison Palette hummed in sync with her whispering giggles.

The panoramic view of the walls where spices were depicted in the murmuring tones of time – reminding Etienne of his non-stop sojourn through the tangled world of wild spices – decorated with brave success, an epitome of human discovery and exploration.

The painting called “Absinthe Group” of Vincent van Gogh captured their shared revelation of dedicating their lives to a form of artistry they found deep within their beings. It was what marked them: discordant yet harmonious dancers - Madame Bovary curled her hand over the dusty chalice; Etienne lifted a vibrant glass in his grasp with a tilt of the soul.

Cheers rose amongst the fruity fermented scents swirling in the air of Palette of Palates; clinking glasses prevailed, a memento of moments unfold as night spun into dawn. The thick air brimming with treasure, Maison Palette lay existent in spite of the sun that cast a deep sleep upon its environment.

Isabella’s laughter chimed, bouncing at the seams of pungent aromas, and the rosé swirled and sang on their tongues. The taste of history oozed on as the cheap tapestry overhead sighed its farewell to lingering dreams. Dreams that slept, aground in the endless sea of flavours; within the walls of Palette of Palates: A Taste Expedition.
