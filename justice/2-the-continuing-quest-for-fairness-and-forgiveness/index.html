Justice/2: The Continuing Quest for Fairness and Forgiveness


Once upon a time, in a land far away, there lived a wise judge named Mantis. She was renowned for her fairness and ability to bring justice to even the most complicated cases. Judge Mantis believed that everyone deserved a second chance and was always willing to show compassion and forgiveness.

One day, in the bustling town market, an old woman named Raven approached Judge Mantis with a peculiar case. “Your Honor,” she said, lifting her voice above the excited chatter of merchants and townsfolk, “I want to press charges against my own son, who has caused much trouble in our family.”

Judge Mantis sighed and looked at the old woman with kindness. “As your judge, I am bound to hear your case. However, it’s essential to understand why you want to bring charges against your own son.”

Raven hesitated. Her eyes filled with tears. “The past few months have been challenging as my son, now the age of eighteen, squandered away his inheritance. He no longer helps with household expenses nor respects my wishes. I am now forced to mortgage my ancestral home to repay his debts.”

Judge Mantis listened carefully and said, “Naming one’s child as the antagonist is never an easy step. But in the end, justice must follow the evidence and reason. Let us begin.”

Over the following days, Judge Mantis meticulously examined the case. She interviewed the old woman, her son Ermine, and various relatives and neighbors. At the conclusion of the investigation, Judge Mantis called for a meeting between all parties involved.

“Having listened to your stories,” she began, “I have come to a conclusion. I find Ermine, your son, guilty of squandering his inheritance and causing distress within the family. However, it is crucial to remember that this son is still flesh of your flesh and blood of your blood. He is not beyond redemption.”

Raven gasped. “But, Your Honor, how can you possibly consider him not guilty?”

“I have decided to grant Ermine a 22-day probationary period,” Judge Mantis said calmly. “During these 22 days, Ermine must live in his mother’s house and contribute to the family expenses as he once did. You must judge his efforts at the end of the probation, and the decision to let him stay or ask him to leave must be mutual. This way, you can see if he can change for the better.”

Raven, although initially hesitant as her son sheepishly stood beside her, agreed to the terms.

Ermine’s probation began, and he started attending to tasks around the house with sincerity and commitment, showing his earnest desire to change his ways. The old woman watched her son struggle to find his footing again and noticed slight improvements daily. At the end of the 22 days, she decided to forgive him as his efforts were genuine and considerable.

Judge Mantis’ compassionate approach left an indelible mark on the town; she taught them how to innovate in their quest for justice, showing fairness and forgiveness regularly. From that day, they began to understand that second chances had the power to save relationships, reform character, and bring about a better future. And sometimes, justice was as much about kindness as it was about ruling fairly.
