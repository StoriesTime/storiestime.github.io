Orbiting Worlds: Short Fiction in Deep Space


The stars stretched across the void in perfect silence, a million pinpricks of light scattered across the immense expanse of space. The ship, Lethara, a sleek, silver-black vessel, drifted past the rings of the gas giant Korgath, its engines dimming to near silence as it approached the small, seemingly inconspicuous moon orbiting the planet. The crew aboard the Lethara was on a mission unlike any other: to explore the uncharted worlds, the small, forgotten satellites scattered like dust in the darkness.

Captain Eira, the seasoned leader of this expedition, stood in the command deck of the ship, her eyes fixed on the viewscreen. Her dark hair was pulled back into a tight bun, and her sharp eyes reflected the soft glow of the control panels. Around her, the crew moved with practiced efficiency, preparing for the task ahead.

“Captain,” a voice broke through the hum of the ship’s systems. It was Lieutenant Aiden, her second-in-command, his voice a low murmur. “We’re nearing the moon’s orbit. The surface is largely uncharted, no readings for lifeforms, no unusual activity. It’s… almost too quiet.”

Eira glanced at the data streaming across her console. “Prepare the landing party,” she said, her tone steady but thoughtful. “We’ll begin our scans on the surface immediately.”

“Understood, Captain,” Aiden replied, moving swiftly to issue orders.

Eira stood alone for a moment longer, her gaze drifting to the distant stars. There was something about this mission that felt different—more profound. They had explored many moons, countless planets, but this one, this small, dark world, called to her. She couldn’t explain it, but she sensed it, the pull of the unknown. As if something ancient and forgotten waited for them on this remote orb.

The surface of the moon was as desolate as they had expected, a barren wasteland of rocky outcrops and rolling dust storms. The sky was a swirling grey, with heavy clouds hanging low over jagged cliffs. Yet, even in the stark emptiness, Eira could feel the weight of something heavy in the air, something unseen but undeniably present.

She, Aiden, and the rest of the landing team disembarked from the shuttle, their suits adapting to the thin atmosphere and extreme cold. The team moved with purpose, scanning the terrain with their instruments, searching for signs of life, or perhaps more importantly, signs of something more ancient.

“There’s a structure,” Lieutenant Ilya called out, pointing to the far horizon. Through the mist and dust, something jagged and metallic caught the light. A faint reflection, almost like a shimmer. “Looks like an old ruin.”

Eira’s heart skipped. This was it. The first major discovery of their expedition. “Let’s move,” she ordered, her voice resolute.

As they trekked toward the structure, the weight in the air grew heavier. The ruins appeared to be built of materials far beyond anything they had encountered in their travels. Dark metals, layered with patterns that shimmered, not like any natural phenomenon, but as though they had been crafted by an intelligence long forgotten.

When they reached the entrance, a large archway carved into the stone, they found no visible signs of energy or technology. The air inside was still, the silence deafening. The walls were adorned with intricate carvings, alien symbols whose meanings were completely foreign to them.

“These carvings,” Ilya murmured, running a gloved hand along the surface. “They’re not just decorative. They seem to be a language. A history.”

Eira nodded slowly. “A history we may never fully understand.”

The team split into smaller groups to explore the interior of the structure. The air grew colder, and the walls seemed to pulse with an ancient energy. The deeper they ventured, the more they felt it—the sensation of something watching them, something ancient and powerful that had once lived here, something that had left its mark on this world.

After hours of exploration, they came upon a central chamber. In the center was an enormous, glowing orb, suspended in mid-air. It hummed with a soft, rhythmic pulse, casting an eerie light across the chamber. The energy radiating from it was palpable, like an invisible force that tugged at their very being.

“What is it?” Aiden whispered, his voice barely audible.

Eira stepped forward, her heart racing. “I don’t know, but we’re about to find out.”

She approached the orb carefully, feeling an odd sensation in her chest as she neared it. The closer she got, the more she felt her surroundings fade. The walls, the ground beneath her boots, everything seemed to blur. Only the orb remained, its glow brighter now, its pulse quickening.

Suddenly, the orb flared to life, sending a surge of energy through the room. The crew staggered back, the air crackling with static. Eira felt a sharp pull—something deep within the core of the orb was calling to her, an invitation, a message. Before she could react, the world around her spun, and the orb’s light engulfed her completely.

When Eira awoke, she was no longer in the chamber. She was floating in space, or what seemed to be space. There were no stars here, no familiar constellations. Instead, she was surrounded by an endless expanse of swirling colors—purples, blues, and golds that seemed to shimmer and pulse in a slow, deliberate rhythm.

Her mind raced. She was still conscious, still aware, but her body was weightless, untethered from everything she knew. The sensation was both liberating and terrifying. She felt as though she had crossed some boundary, some veil between realities.

A voice echoed in her mind, not spoken aloud but felt, like a whisper brushing against the edges of her thoughts.

You have entered the space between worlds.

The words hung in her mind like a riddle. The space between worlds? What did that mean? She turned, but there was nothing to see—only the swirling colors, the vast emptiness stretching forever in all directions.

Then, before her, a figure appeared, an ethereal being composed of light and shadow. Its form was fluid, constantly shifting, but there was something undeniably familiar in its presence.

You seek knowledge, Captain Eira. You seek the answers to questions that have no end.

Eira’s voice trembled as she responded, though she had no idea how she could speak here. “What is this place? Why am I here?”

The figure’s shape shifted again, its presence growing more defined. You are here because you have touched the forgotten. This place exists between all things, where time is but a concept and existence is a fleeting thought.

Eira’s heart pounded. “The orb… it brought me here?”

The orb is a conduit. It is the key to unlocking the path between the realms. What you seek lies beyond the veil, but be warned: not all who enter this space return.

Her mind raced with questions, but the figure raised a hand—its form glowing with an intense light. To find the answers you seek, you must choose. Will you accept the journey that awaits you, or return to the world you know, never to know what lies beyond?

Eira hesitated. The pull of the unknown was strong, almost overwhelming. But something inside her—a deep, unwavering curiosity—pushed her forward. She had always known that to truly understand the universe, she had to venture into the dark places, to face the unknown without hesitation.

“I choose to stay,” she said, her voice steady now. “Show me what lies beyond.”

The figure nodded, and with a gesture, the swirling colors intensified, spinning faster and faster around her until they enveloped her completely. The force of it was overwhelming, and she felt her consciousness being stretched, pulled apart, and then remade.

When the sensation finally ceased, Eira found herself standing once more in the chamber, the orb before her, its glow dimming as if it had finished its task.

Her crew was there, their faces filled with confusion and fear. “Captain?” Aiden’s voice broke through the fog in her mind. “What happened? Where did you go?”

Eira took a deep breath, feeling the weight of the moment settle upon her. She had crossed a threshold, one that few had ever dared to approach. The answers she had sought were beyond their reach—at least, for now. But she knew this was only the beginning. There were other worlds, other orbs, and the space between them was calling.

“I saw the universe as it truly is,” she said softly, her eyes reflecting the strange light of the orb. “And it is far more complex and beautiful than we could ever imagine.”

As the crew prepared to return to the ship, Eira stood for a moment longer, gazing at the orb. The journey ahead would not be easy, but it was the only path that mattered now. The exploration of the unknown was a task that would consume her, body and mind, but it was a path she was destined to walk.

And so, with the Lethara turning back toward the stars, the crew embarked on a new journey, one that would take them not just to the farthest reaches of space but into the very heart of what it meant to be a part of the vast, ever-expanding cosmos.
