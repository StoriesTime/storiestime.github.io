Leaving Everything Behind: A Journey of Immigration


It was a rainy afternoon when Yasmin sat by her window, staring out at the city she had known all her life. The streets below were a blur of water and movement, much like the thoughts swirling in her mind. She had lived in Cairo for over twenty-eight years, surrounded by the warm embrace of her family, friends, and the comforting chaos of a city that never slept. But now, everything seemed to be changing.

The decision to leave was not an easy one. It had been years of contemplation, of sleepless nights and endless discussions with her parents. But now, it was real. The offer had come through — a scholarship to study in Canada, a land far away, with a world so different from the familiar bustling streets of Egypt. A chance to build a future she had always dreamed of, to escape the limitations she felt in the city that had shaped her.

Her hands trembled slightly as she picked up the letter once again. It was official. She had been accepted into one of the top universities in Toronto. The dream was alive. But at what cost?

The weight of leaving behind her family, her friends, and her entire world felt almost unbearable. Would she ever find a place in this new, foreign land? Would she be able to start over, to carve out a life for herself?

Her phone buzzed with a message from her mother, a simple question: “Have you made up your mind?”

Yasmin sighed. She had made up her mind, but the reality of it all was just beginning to settle in. There was no turning back now. In the coming weeks, her life would be packed into suitcases, and she would board a plane that would take her to a new country, a new beginning.

Chapter 2: The Farewell

The days leading up to her departure were a whirlwind of emotions. Packing her belongings felt like a cruel joke. How could she fit a lifetime into two suitcases? She rummaged through her childhood bedroom, picking up old books, photographs, and trinkets that told the story of who she was. Each item felt like a piece of her, and the thought of leaving them behind was harder than she anticipated.

Her father, who had been quiet throughout the process, finally spoke up one evening as they sat around the dinner table.

“Yasmin,” he said, his voice thick with emotion, “I know this is hard for you. But remember that you are not leaving us behind. We will always be with you, even across the miles. This is your future, and we support you.”

Yasmin nodded, fighting back the tears. Her father’s words were comforting, but they didn’t ease the ache in her chest. She had always been close to her family, especially her younger brother, Ahmed. The thought of leaving him behind made her heart heavy. They had grown up together, sharing everything from childhood secrets to dreams for the future. Would he be okay without her?

On her last night in Cairo, Yasmin took a walk along the Nile, the water reflecting the lights of the city. She had walked this path countless times before, but tonight it felt different. The weight of the decision she had made was now a physical presence, pressing down on her. The night was calm, the cool breeze a bittersweet reminder of the life she was about to leave behind.

As she stood by the river, Yasmin felt a mixture of fear and excitement. She didn’t know what lay ahead, but she knew that this was a step she had to take. For herself, for her future.

Chapter 3: The Arrival

The flight was long, the kind of journey that felt like it lasted an eternity. Yasmin sat in her seat, looking out the window as the plane soared above the clouds. She had always dreamed of this moment, but now that it was happening, she wasn’t sure if she was ready. Canada was vast, its landscapes so different from the crowded, vibrant streets of Cairo. She had read about it, heard stories from others who had made the journey, but nothing could have prepared her for the reality of stepping into this new world.

When the plane finally landed in Toronto, Yasmin’s heart raced. She had arrived. The cold air hit her face as she stepped out of the airport, a sharp contrast to the warm, humid climate of Egypt. The city was everything she had imagined – clean, organized, with wide streets and tall buildings. But it also felt alien, like a puzzle she didn’t yet understand.

Her first few days were a blur. The university was bustling with students from all over the world, each with their own story, their own reason for being there. Yasmin had expected this, but it still felt overwhelming. She missed the familiar faces of her family and friends. The language barrier was the most immediate challenge. Although she spoke English, it was not the same as being immersed in it every day. The accents, the speed of conversations, the slang—it was all so different.

Her roommate, Maria, a friendly girl from Brazil, welcomed her with open arms. Maria had been in Canada for a year already and was eager to show Yasmin the ropes. She introduced Yasmin to the campus, showed her where the best coffee spots were, and helped her navigate the subway system. But despite Maria’s kindness, Yasmin couldn’t shake the feeling of isolation that had settled in her chest.

One evening, after a long day of lectures, Yasmin sat in her room, scrolling through her phone. She saw pictures of her family’s gatherings, of her friends laughing together. It was then that the loneliness hit her. She had made the right choice, she knew that, but it was hard. It was hard not being there for her family, for Ahmed, who had just started high school. She had missed his birthday, and the thought of him growing up without her felt like a loss she couldn’t quite describe.

But it wasn’t all despair. Slowly, she started to find her rhythm. The professors were supportive, the students were curious about her background, and she even started making new friends. She joined a cultural club that celebrated diversity, where she could share her Egyptian heritage and learn about others’ experiences.

The cold Canadian winters were tough at first, but Yasmin found solace in the snow, in the quiet that came with it. She began to appreciate the beauty of the changing seasons, the stillness that came with winter, and the promise of spring.

Chapter 4: The Struggle

Despite the progress she had made, the challenges of immigration were not easy to ignore. The cultural shock was more profound than she had anticipated. In Egypt, the pace of life was fast, filled with noise and constant movement. In Canada, everything seemed to slow down. People took their time, enjoyed their coffee, and lived at a different pace. Yasmin had to learn to adjust, to embrace the stillness and the quiet. But there were days when the silence felt overwhelming, when she longed for the noise of Cairo’s streets.

The academic pressure was also a new experience. In Egypt, Yasmin had been a top student, but in Canada, she was one of many. The expectations were high, and the competition fierce. There were days when she questioned whether she was good enough, whether she had made a mistake in coming here. But she pushed through, telling herself that this was a part of the journey.

One afternoon, as she sat in a café on campus, sipping her coffee, a student approached her. It was an older woman, who introduced herself as Sarah, a graduate student from the United States. Sarah had noticed Yasmin sitting alone often and invited her to join a study group. Yasmin hesitated at first, but the warmth in Sarah’s smile convinced her to take a chance. That evening, as they worked through assignments together, Yasmin realized something important. She was not alone in her struggles. Everyone had their own journey, their own challenges, and it was okay to ask for help.

Chapter 5: Finding Home

Months passed, and Yasmin’s journey of immigration began to take on a new meaning. The loneliness was still there at times, but it was no longer as overwhelming. She had found a community, both in her academic life and in the diverse group of friends she had made. She still missed her family terribly, but technology made it easier to stay connected. Video calls, social media, and instant messaging had bridged the gap, allowing her to share her life with them even from thousands of miles away.

One day, as Yasmin stood on the balcony of her apartment, looking out at the city skyline, she realized that she had found a balance between her past and her present. She had left behind everything she had known, but in doing so, she had gained a new perspective. She was no longer just a girl from Cairo; she was a student, a global citizen, someone who had the courage to step out of her comfort zone and build a new life.

The journey of immigration had not been easy, but it had been worth it. Yasmin had learned that leaving everything behind did not mean losing it. It meant growing, adapting, and finding a new version of home in a place that was once unfamiliar. As the years passed, she continued to forge her path, knowing that no matter where life took her, the essence of who she was—her roots, her family, her heritage—would always be with her.

And in that moment, she finally understood that immigration wasn’t just about leaving a place behind. It was about taking everything that mattered with her, wherever she went.
