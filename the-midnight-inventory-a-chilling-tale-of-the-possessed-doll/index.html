The Midnight Inventory: A chilling tale of the possessed doll


When night falls in the quaint town of Hallowell, an eerie stillness consumes its streets and alleyways. The old mansion at the end of Maple Lane is no exception. Number 13 Maple Lane whispers tales of gothic mysteries and the supernatural.

Behind its walls, an ancient wooden doll stood on a dusty mantelpiece, its piercing blue glass eyes seemed to follow every visitor who dared to enter the mansion. From time immemorial, it had been passed down through generations of the last living family—the troubled Thornwoods.

Melissa Thornwood, the last living heir to the mansion, had neither the heart nor the stomach to part with the mysterious doll that had brought her family both prosperity and misery—a fabled heirloom interlaced with prosperous good luck and sinister misfortunes. Mysterious occurrences had indeed shaped the history of the Thornwood family, and the doll, christened “Velvet,” appeared possessively watchful over its masters.

One midsummer evening, the mansion once again became host to its darkest secret. Melissa sat in her study, engrossed in 17th-century literature, as the daylight softened into dusk. Velvet’s glass eyes reflecting the last embers of sunlight—a strange uncanny feeling took over the room.

As the moon peeked through the veil of twilight, Melissa thought of the midnight inventory—a centuries-old tradition accompanying the progeny of the Thornwood lineage. A purported obeisance to continue their family’s fortune by tending to the doll’s mysterious and sinister needs.

“I must honor the tradition,” whispered Melissa, knowing the consequences—yet strangely drawn to her fate.

Dressed in emerald velvet, punctuated with silver embroidery, she ascended the spiraling staircase to her childhood chamber adorned with ornate portraits of her ancestors. The door creaked under her soft touch, revealing the faded, tattered curtains hanging limply. Soft groans hissed around her, as the floorboards complained from disuse.

As the clock ticked midnight, Melissa pulled out a wooden chest from beneath an old rocking chair. The darkened room cast ominous shadows, driving her further into its secrets. She knew these untold stories harbored the answers she sought to Velvet’s malcontented mutterings.

“Be it known that I surrender to thy sinister demands,” she whispered to the cold winds that traveled through the room.

The clock’s hands followed the second’s tremble—melancholy ticktocks announced the commencement of her witchy deed. Amidst crimson velvet drapes and flickering candlelight, artifacts from the darkest corners of Hallowell emerged.

Melissa laid the items on her mother’s antique rocking horse, assembling an altar for the doll. A hairbrush carved from ebony, a porcelain rattle, and a miniature silver casket—overshadowed by Velvet’s piercing gaze. A trinity of unhallowed treasures—for one whose heart delighted in their curses and spells.

Entwining a circle of dried lavender and rosemary, Melissa invoked the spirits dwelling within the hallowed objects. By these, she opened a gate to the otherworld.

Casting a spell of protection, she sang,

Help me, Mother of Mercy, To appease our family’s torment, Give me the power to reclaim that which is mine, Bless our hearth with wanton peace.

The air cooled and grew heavy, electrified with energy. A sudden crackle of thunder signaled a proper balance, and with whispered threats of old, Velvet came alive amidst the tempest.

The doll’s laughter melded with the creak of floorboards, playing tricks that toyed with Melissa’s senses. As it clambered about the room, playthings writhed and twisted, casting sinister silhouettes upon the walls. Velvet’s persistent and gloating giggles seemed to delegate authority—a puppeteer to a marionette.

As she resisted this inexplicable power and embraced her ancestral tradition, Melissa felt Velvet’s sinister tether strangling her spirit. In the assurance that any resistance brought the full wrath of her imposed instincts, she allowed herself to be drawn in by the malicious anchor, submitting to its imperious laughter.

She swayed, arms extended to the rhythm of its malevolent dance. The room felt lighter, the air thinner, and the shadows within started to dissipate, unraveling the stranglehold passing through the generations.

With a final snap, Velvet’s laughter faded, and it became still. With wavering strength, Melissa forced her agitation into a closing chant.

_May thy laughter cease into the dark solitary expanse it knows, And may this rite absolve us from thy vicious retribution, Return to thy sinister slumber, Velvet.

The remaining candle flame flickered—fishing out the last bit of netherworld energy and speared it through the air. The room grew colder, shrouded by darkness, and the sinister veil lifted. The midnight inventory came to an end; its effects solely experienced by her and Velvet.

The ancient doll stood motionless, its piercing blue eyes still following as Melissa left the room—the tales of the Thornwoods and their possessed doll forever branching into the annals of Hallowell’s lore.
