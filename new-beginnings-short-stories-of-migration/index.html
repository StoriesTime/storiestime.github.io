New Beginnings: Short Stories of Migration


The first time I heard the whispers, I couldn’t comprehend what was happening. Now, the wind and I share a secret language that only we can understand. As the tender leaves of spring give way to the blazing hot days of summer, there’s an urgency in the wind’s whispers. I’ve learned that with each season, there’s a story to be told.

This summer, my life changed dramatically. I was a simple laborer in a small rural village in India, eking out a living, passing time. But then the whispers started.

“What if…” they asked me, “you left everything you’ve known, and embarked on a journey to a land filled with fortune and opportunities? What if you started anew, with nothing more than the wind’s whispers as your guide?”

One night, as the whispers were at their peak, I made my decision. I packed all my belongings, hardly anything, but that which mattered most, into a small bag, and stepped out into the night. The sound of my heart pounding drowned out the whispers as they grew quiet. I took a deep breath, and walked away from the village, my family, my friends, my home.

The journey was hard. I walked and walked, the wind blowing whatever way it wanted, leading me through unknown terrains, across streams, up mountains, and back down again. I slept under the stars, in the empty husks of abandoned barns, and other abandoned places. I was a bird without a cage, and freedom was my song.

As I journeyed, I came to understand the language of the wind. It murmured to me the names of the places and people I would encounter. I filtered through these whispers and realized that I could learn the local languages. So, on empty streets and at marketplaces, I opened my ears and heart to the languages of the lands.

Through sheer will and refusing to surrender my dreams, I reached a city bustling with life. The whispers told me that an old man lived there who would help me. His name was Abdul. I found him in a dimly lit corner of the city, in a quaint little café, sipping his evening tea. His eyes were the kindest I’ve ever seen, filled with a wisdom that only years could buy, and his hands shook slightly. I was him, someday. I approached him and told him of the wind and its whispers.

Abdul could only smile. “The wind has brought you to me, haven’t it?” he whispered.

Abdul showed me kindness like I’ve never known. He put me up in his tiny apartment, cooked meals for me, and taught me the ways of the city. Life in the city was a strange dance, but Abdul helped me avoid the wrong steps.

One day, a man came to Abdul’s café. He had a thick accent, his words heavy with the wisdom of the East. He listened to my tale, and he looked at me with eyes that held a glint of curiosity.

“I hear you can speak many languages, is that true?” he asked.

I nodded, wondering where the wind was leading me now.

The man reached into his waistcoat pocket and pulled out a small, rough leather notebook. He handed it to me.

“I need your help,” he said.

I glanced at the photograph on the first page. It was of an old man, kind eyes filled with the same wisdom as Abdul.

“Help me find my uncle,” the man whispered. “He’s been lost, and I must find him before it’s too late.”

A thousand questions circled in my mind, but in the end, the wind led me to just one. I took the notebook, and we began our journey, following the whispers of the wind.

And so, my life started anew, far away from the village where I was born, in the city filled with life and heartache, in the moments where I stood on the edge, ready to jump with a single push, into the arms of the wind.
