Ghost Elections: A Surprising Victory


In the small, quaint town of Millfield, the annual Ghost Elections had become more than just a spooky tradition for the frightened residents. It was a moment of uncertainty and suspense that hovered in the gloomy autumn air. The ghosts of Millfield, after all, were not ordinary specters. They had a history, a past, and an undeniable longing for a sense of belonging. And every year, at the stroke of midnight on October 31st, they emerged from the shadows to cast their votes, choosing a living candidate to serve as their advocate and representative in the world of the living.

Millfield’s human inhabitants had long ago grown weary of the subtle manipulation that came with the annual event, but it was impossible to ignore. The Autumn Festival, which coincided with the Ghost Elections, was the most vivid reminder of their town’s dark history. The people there had to tolerate the spectral citizens, even if it meant sticking their heads out into the chilling, unyielding darkness that surrounded Millfield.

Mariana, a brave, mischievous eighteen-year-old, frantically ran her fingers through her tousled, shoulder-length hair as she struggled to zip up her dilapidated old blue raincoat. The eerie wind howled and whispered through the trees, while the neighborhood’s Jack-o’-lanterns stared at her from their haunted pedestals. It was time.

The holiday festivities would begin in a few moments, but Mariana had a more pressing matter to attend to. As the chosen spirit medium for the night, it was her responsibility to communicate with the honorable ghostly representatives for the town of Millfield. Although thoroughly unnerved, she knew drawing the ire of the town’s ethereal populace was risky.

She silently made her way through the deserted streets, broken pavement crunching beneath her unwavering footsteps, until she reached a fork where an arch marked the entrance to the cemetery. A dim dusky light seemed to radiate from the tombstones’ spectral whiteness, eerily floating above the scorched earth. As the veil between worlds grew thinner, she felt a sudden connection, an electrifying energy she could barely contain. Up until now, she had never felt so fully awakened.

As she entered the cemetery, she couldn’t shake the sense that every dead eye from the crypts was watching her, dissecting her, preparing to judge her future actions. Wandering deeper into the labyrinth of the dead, a sudden infrared glow pulsated from one of the crypts up ahead.

Mariana approached, kneeling before the entrance. In a hushed voice, she addressed the waiting spirits, “I am Mariana Lopez, chosen human representative, here to communicate on your behalf. Please, speak your wishes. I promise, I’ll carry out your will.”

As the ghostly figures emerged, she braced herself for the inevitable terror that would ensue. Their skeletal forms seemed to reach out from their graves with a palpable sense of gravity. Before Mariana’s quivering frame, a manifestation of the ghostly council gathered, and the senior most spirit spoke with an ancient gravelly voice.

“Child, we are ready to initiate the proceedings of this Ghost Election. We–”

A sudden silence befell the cemetery. The air grew frigid, and dark, inky shadows seemed to gather around their presence. The crowd erupted with whispers and murmurs, spreading an unsettling tension throughout the assembly. Mariana clenched her fists to suppress her anxiety and frowned, unsatisfied with the abrupt interruption.

Just as the spell of anxiety approached its maximum intensity, a blinding light engulfed the tombstones and their surroundings, followed by a spectral voice resonating above the scene: “For this election cycle, our chosen candidate shall be revealed to both living and spectral worlds alike. Within the spectral realm, we propose the resurrection of the esteemed, and decisive Elder Woodruff Larkin.”

United, the assembly whispered amongst themselves in heavy anticipation – both thrilled and frightened by the prospect of resurrecting Elder Woodruff Larkin. Mariana would soon discover that his bewitching influence over her town put her in an extraordinary predicament, the likes of which she had never faced before.

Months passed in the blink of an eye. Larkin’s influence started to ripple through Millfield, altering the town’s ghastly power structure and redefining its limits. Mariana, playing the role of both politician and diplomat, was tasked with addressing the furor among the living communities growing increasingly uneasy with the opaque balance of power.

She knew inside that her actions lately held more onus than she could have ever comprehended. Often found pacing her modest room, her guilt consumed her, as she mulled over the weight of her responsibilities. Her relationship with the living and spirit populations of Millfield had grown complex, but her faith in their interconnected destinies bonded her more closely with her haunted hometown than she had ever imagined possible.

As the curse of providence tightened its vice upon her, she knew her resolve could not waver. The time had arrived for her to decisively tip the delicate scales toward harmony. And in that pivotal moment, Mariana steeled herself for one final stand.

The small town gathered before her, those who lived and those who had once lived—invisibly, the unusual latticework of a febrile atmosphere cocooned Millfield in a defiance of the ordinary. Only peace could unite them all.

Mariana ascended to the podium and took a deep breath. She fixated her eyes on the gathering crowd, the hopeful faces of the dearly departed—including her own grandparents—for motivation, and with earnest conviction soared through the hallowed air, penning a new chapter in Millfield history.

“What once seemed impossible has become inevitable—Millfield must look not to what divides us but to what unites us. The living among you know the power and influence of Elder Woodruff Larkin, a force equal on both our sides.”

The murmurs subsided as she continued. “As our chosen medium and advocate, I too have learned the folly and fear that weighs heavily on this town. You too bear this burden, the knowledge that we share this world with those who have passed on. We must accept this, and not flee from it. We have stood together during Ghost Elections and embraced the spirit of unity.”

Mariana paused, and the air grew still. “Millfield, today we stand on the edge of reconciliation, one that transcends the boundaries that separate us.” Her words quivered, hopeful yet fragmented, like a shaken glass half-filled with clouded water.

She pressed on, courage oozing from every syllable, and reveled until her impassioned voice rose once more. “Together, we can save our town, those who wake in the dark, those who sleep in the light. We declare a union of unity atop the crumbling bridge that cleaves the realms of the dead from the living. We need only adhere to its decrees, verifiably honoring our word and acceding to the will of the dead, as we expect the same in return.

“From this night forth, let the era of Ghost Elections fade. But let’s remember the lessons learned during these tempestuous times—the righteousness of sacrifice, the strength of a unified community, and the power of hope that dares to dream of a better tomorrow for both us and our ethereal kin.”

The crowd erupted with thunderous applause, tears streaming down the faces of the town. United, they broke the chains of their collective fears, the curse of the Ghost Elections lifted. With powerful voices raised in a resounding oath, the bonds of their newfound unity tightened around their memories, drawing them towards a future unseen, yet undeniably hopeful.

And so, in the ghostly town of Millfield, as life altered and the shadows bade farewell to their last spectral echo, Mariana, the girl they chose, stood assured in her newfound strength. The battle she had waged was not her own but one orchestrated by an ancient world, buried beneath time’s ravaging hands. And it was her destiny, as much as their own, to renew the bonds and make peace for all of them.

As Millfield reveled in the ripe chill of that starlit night, welcoming the wonder of sweet whispers in their ears, for the very first time, they’d invited themselves into the grand ballet of the living and the dead—and were forever enchanted.
