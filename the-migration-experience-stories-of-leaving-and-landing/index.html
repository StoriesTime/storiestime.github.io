The Migration Experience: Stories of Leaving and Landing


The old suitcase had seen better days. Its leather edges were worn, the metal clasps oxidized into a dull green, and the once-bright canvas had faded to a muted tan. Mariam gripped its handle tightly as the crowded bus pulled away from the village, the skyline of her hometown slowly shrinking in the rearview mirror. She was leaving behind not just a place, but a lifetime etched in the faces of familiar people, the scent of the jasmine trees, and the gentle hum of the river that had nourished her family for generations.

Mariam’s migration journey was more than a physical shift; it was a transformational passage loaded with stories of departure and arrival—a common but deeply personal experience shared by millions worldwide. In her heart was a mix of hope, fear, grief, and anticipation, emotions that intertwined as tightly as the threads in her grandmother’s old shawl.

The Roots of Departure

For Mariam, the decision to migrate was seeded by economic hardship and the repeated whispers of instability that plagued her region. Crop failures, droughts, and dwindling opportunities shaped everyday conversations. Her brothers had already left—one to a faraway city, the other overseas. Each had sent word of their new lives, stories filled with hardship but also the glimmering promise of a better future.

Her own departure was threaded with silent goodbyes: the mother who helped stitch the hem of her dress, the father who gave her a sturdy walking stick carved with ancient symbols, the neighbors who promised to keep the hearth fires burning. Leaving was leaving behind, but not erasing.

Migration is woven with such departures: leaving the familiar soils, the landmarks of childhood, the cadence of native tongues. These journeys are punctuated with a mixture of loss and liberation—loss of place, but liberation from circumstance.

The Odyssey of Transit

The journey was not just one of miles but of challenges and unexpected kindnesses. The bus journey itself was a microcosm of migration—people packed together with stories, trades, and silent prayers. Mariam met Amina, a woman bound for a distant city. From Amina, Mariam learned about navigating bureaucracies and the unspoken rules of city life.

At the border, the scene was an anxious tableau of people, some exhausted and resigned, others alert and eager. Migrants clutched papers, blinked through long queues, and exchanged nervous laughter. The customs officers were gatekeepers but also the first test of belonging in this new life.

Transitions like these—the border crossing, the airport waiting rooms, the cramped buses—have a rhythm, a heartbeat, a collective human experience that is often invisible to the world but vital to the traveler.

Arrival: The New World

Landing in the city was a sensory explosion. Mariam’s eyes drank in towering buildings piercing the clouds, the incessant honking of vehicles, and the kaleidoscope of signs in languages she was still learning. The air was heavy with unfamiliar smells—the spicy aroma of street food, the acrid scent from factories, the sweet fragrance of blooming orchards in city parks.

Despite the dizzying pace, Mariam found the early mornings soothing as the city woke. She took solace in the park’s quiet corners and the bustling marketplaces where the hum of commerce was a different song but resonant with possibilities.

The new life was not easy. Finding work meant confronting the barriers of language, recognition of credentials, and sometimes, the harsh politics of being an outsider. But with each challenge, Mariam found resilience growing—a courage that arises from necessity, a strength born from the experience of leaving and landing.

The Stories Migrants Share

Every migrant carries stories, each a thread in the vast tapestry of human movement. Some speak of perilous crossings over treacherous seas, others of quiet nights spent in dormitories housing dozens. Mariam’s stories included cooking meals with neighbors who spoke a different dialect, learning the city’s rhythms, and slowly finding her own space.

In her new community, she discovered others like herself—people who carried the stories of exodus and arrival. There were moments of shared struggle and celebration, conversations that crossed cultural divides and wove new understandings. These narratives of migration are vital because they capture not just displacement but also adaptation, resilience, and renewal.

The Inner Migration

Mariam realized that migration is not solely an external journey; it is an inner voyage as well. It meant reevaluating identity—who she was as a daughter, a worker, a community member. It involved reconciling the longing for home with the realities of the present.

Every letter home, every call with family, was a lifeline to the past. But the present demanded forward thinking—to build bridges between old customs and new practices. In that balance, Mariam found her strength.

The Tapestry of Migration

In the unfolding of her life, Mariam became a weaver of stories, a keeper of memories both lost and gained. Her migration experience echoed a universal human condition—the desire to transcend limitations, to seek security, dignity, and hope.

Migration is about movement, yes, but it is also about connection. It knits continents together through human ambition and longing. It challenges assumptions, fosters empathy, and enriches cultures.

Mariam’s suitcase may have been worn, but it was full—full of stories, lessons, heartbreak, and hope, carried forward into every new dawn she greeted in her adopted home.

The migration experience, with its complex layers of leaving and landing, resonates across time and place. Each story, like Mariam’s, adds depth to our understanding of what it means to move forward, to belong, and to keep alive the ties that define us.

Through these stories, we learn that home is not just a location on a map. It is a journey — sometimes long and difficult — toward belonging, purpose, and identity. And in sharing these stories, the world itself becomes a little smaller, a little closer, a place where all journeys converge.
