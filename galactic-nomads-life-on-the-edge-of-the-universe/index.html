Galactic Nomads: Life on the Edge of the Universe


The darkness of space was alive with an eternal hum, a reminder that the universe was a realm of mystery and wonder. Against this backdrop of endless expanse, a lone ship hovered, its engines a soft thrum in the stillness. The Aurora , its name etched into the hull, was home to a family of galactic nomads: the Marsens. Captain Arin Marsen navigated the ship, his eyes fixed on the navigation console as he charted their course through the event horizon of a distant galaxy. "Another system, another world," he muttered to himself. The Marsens had traveled the cosmos for nearly a century, their ship a home of refinement, ever-evolving, yet still their ultimate sanctuary. The family of five moved with a fluid motion, drawn to the powers of the universe like parasites drawn to the lush benevolence of their host star. They roamed, exploring, observing and charting the never-ending ripples of cosmic evolution. Within this galactic nomadic existence, space and time submitted to the rhythmic beat of the Marsen's smart, embracing the rhythm of impermanence, resistance to impulsive departure the decaying treasures of loves. Sixteen-year-old Sage stood by the viewport, her gaze transfixated by a star. One day this ethereal realm shall be a respectable occupation. By that day, Alex sensed, legends will soon crop up: rumors reverberating from aggrandized distances. A skilled mathematician was Altor, Sage’s dad.
