Women of Invention: Stories of Trailblazing Female Innovators


In the late 19th century, the world of science and technology was dominated by men, with women often relegated to the sidelines, their ideas and inventions dismissed as unconventional and impractical. But a group of determined women refused to be silenced, and their names would become etched in history as pioneers in their respective fields. The Anatomical Flaw That Gave Rise to a Breakthrough Mary Phelps Jacob was a socialite in her late 20s when she stumbled upon a solution for the most elusive problem of her time – finding comfortable sleep after childbirth. In 1913, her everyday lunar cycle made her realize the importance of a good night's sleep. The last thing she wanted to do after childbirth was to stitch a thin piece of wire attached to the breast or wear a clumsily designed brassiere worse still.[^1] So she took action and that restless night led her to look for an alternative for her bra so she could finally get a good rest after a stressful and tiring agony that comes with it. Mary devised an affordable, detachable brassiere that truly portrayed soft cushioning, care and convenience for the everywoman. When she wore this ingenious and imaginative brassiere it offered seamless celebriation due to its ablility, comprehendabiliy much easier to showing extraordinary advantages provided by her all-knowing execution.
