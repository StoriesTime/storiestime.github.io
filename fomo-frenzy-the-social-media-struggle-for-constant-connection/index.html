FOMO Frenzy: The Social Media Struggle for Constant Connection


Amelia stared at her phone, hunched over on the park bench, her Slice of Life coffee untouched beside her. The screen glowed with vivid colors, offering a stark contrast to the dying daylight. FOMO Frenzy, her mother would’ve scoffed, had she known Amelia was sitting in the same spot she did every evening, scrolling endlessly through her social media feeds. Posts from everyone else’s seemingly perfect lives filled her screen—their Instagram stories, TikTok videos, and Snapchat streaks all feeding into her growing sense of insignificance. Lost in thought, Amelia barely noticed the elderly couple walking a yappy little dog who’d just snatched her Pumpkin Spice Latte napkin.

Chapter 2: #Hashtag Happy

Scrolling down, she came upon a new post from a long-lost schoolmate who’d just booked a fortnight at an exclusive resort in Bali with their “squad.” They were currently living their best lives, hiking through tropical rainforests, diving into crystal-clear waters, and treating themselves to drinks with little umbrellas. She scrolled faster, frustrated that she couldn’t be there too, wishing she could slap that elusive screen back to reality. A swarm of likes and comments rose up on the posts, filling Amelia with a growing panic—How could they all be having fun without her?

Chapter 3: The Glare of Comparison

Her eyes welled up with tears, embarrassed by her intense emotions. These feelings choked her like this tree’s sprawling vine, thick and unwavering, strangling her ability to breathe. Convinced she was a wretched loser, she couldn’t help but venture into the darkest corners of her memories—every rejection she collected like dusty trinkets lining the walls of her mind, every instance of her unworthiness, circling like vultures above a dying carcass.

Chapter 4: The Echo of Silence

But the harder she scrolled, the more the world seemed to belong to these so-called influencers and their constant stream of updates. Everywhere she turned, she found faces like hers—never smiling, always comparing—always trying to chase the elusive shadow of perfectly curated lives. Therapy optional, her sister had written on their group chat, but Amelia couldn’t bring herself to type ‘YES’ in the empty box. She couldn’t bring herself to expose her raw, pulsing vulnerability to anyone—not even a stranger behind a computer screen.

Chapter 5: The Unfriending of Amelia

The realness of her situation hit her like a gust of wind. Here she was, hoping to find meaning within the digital chaos, longing to immerse herself in the thralls of social media. But instead of turning towards the virtual light, she found herself on the precipice of an abyss, her fear of falling in continuously gnawing at her fragmented self.

Her thumb hovered over the unfriend button, the idea seemingly innocent, but a small, instigating voice refused to let it rest. “Do it, Amelia,” it said. “Delete them, block them, vanish from their lives.” Purposefully, she scrolled through her carefully curated list of acquaintances, each picture accompanied by a different cloud tag—#greekweek, #springbreak, #sdgisbest—none that belonged to her.

Chapter 6: The Rumbling of the Storm

A notification from her sister, Luna, finally caught her attention: “[Instagram]areyoureallyok? Send a dog pic if you’re really okay.” With a shaky breath and a reluctant touch, Amelia tapped out a response. “I’m pushing the like button right now of that rainforest picture. No dog pic,” she sent back.

“LOL, gotcha,” Luna replied, knowing all too well that Amelia would probably still be browsing hashtags when it was time to vote for President. Her sister knew she never dipped her toe into the raw dirt of reality often—too afraid of what might wriggle out from beneath the surface.

Chapter 7: The Whisper of Growth

It was an impulsive decision that led her to leave the bench and walk away from the constant swarm—the flashing screens, the endless pings, the mindless chatter. The cool air seeped its way into her skin, sending thrills down her spine, liberating her from the shackles of her own creation. She decided to walk, seeking out the familiar oak tree at the end of the street. It was there, she found, the perfect spot for leaning against its inviting strength and breathing the late-night air that smelled of damp leaves and the faintest scent of rain.

Chapter 8: The Unraveling of Threads

Her eyes slowly adjusted to the dark, revealing the urban sky stitched with a million stars. She thought of those stars tonight, fixed in their precise orbits around a quiet world, and wondered if they ever felt dizzy from the spinning. And in that moment, she made a new choice—one that sounded poorly against the gentle rustling of the leaves above—she would unweave the tightly knit fibers of FOMO Frenzy.

She would greet life as it came, one crumbling leaf at a time, one breath at a time, one star-studded night. And the strangest thing happened as she cradled herself within the tree’s protective embrace. It started with just the faintest whisper of a thought, and as it rippled through her, she felt it for the first time in a lifetime of misguided synchronicities: She didn’t need constant connection to define her value in an increasingly fractured world.

Chapter 9: The Dawn of Freedom

Slowly, she withdrew her phone from her back pocket. With a soft tap, she opened the email from a mysterious old friend, who agreed to meet her the next day at the same bench Amelia sat on each evening. Your soulmate is waiting, the email promised, and in that moment, she realized that maybe, just maybe, this was her new pursuit of happiness.

As she stood up and dusted herself off beneath the colossal branches of the oak, she glanced back at her phone. She noticed, for the first time, her smile in her most recent selfie. Content with the reflection before her, she slipped her phone back in her pocket and breathed the night in full. Her journey of self-discovery would begin tomorrow, she decided, but for tonight, she would sleep knowing that she wasn’t the puppet anymore, but the one drawing the strings.

And as she walked home that night, she promised herself that she would no longer be a slave to FOMO Frenzy, nor tire herself with the peculiarities of social media, but embrace the simple fact that she too, was a living star, undimming and bearing witness to a wildly unpredictable journey that nevertheless belonged to her—a journey with a thousand stories yet to be told.
