Mind Control: The Supernatural Conspiracy


In the scorching midday heat of a small, unassuming town, Jonathan stirred awake, shades of a relentless sun casting a searing glow on his still slumbering face. He stretched languidly, unraveling his tangle of limbs from an abandoned armchair in the shabby living-room. Dust motes swam in the lazy beam of light like tiny sardines, prancing their way out of a nomadic existence into the murky haze of unventilated air. Disheveled tendrils of unruly hair clung to the beads of sweat accumulating on his brow, the most glistening testament to the oppressive force that was the unrelenting sun outside.

In the dimly lit regions of Jonathan’s mind, a jumble of memories rushed through the corridors of his consciousness like stray cats seeking shelter from an oncoming storm, leaving behind a maelstrom of chaos that threatened nothing short of his sanity. Jonathan, once an icon of academia, the toast of intellectual circles, was banished from his life’s calling and left to wander the desolate, desiccated outskirts of life under mysterious circumstances. He had, after years of isolation, nearly forgotten the pleasures of a simple life. In this small town, his memories were allowed to fade gently into the background, becoming shades of a bygone era.

Reluctantly, Jonathan pulled himself from the refuge of that armchair, his muscles complaining after a night of rest in the unforgiving embrace of cast-off furniture hugging the earth in quiet reverence. He stumbled toward the window, curtains flung open to reveal a broad, aerial panorama of the small town and its quaint inhabitants.

The autopilot of his daily routine managed to extract him from the trance of his limited reverie, as he padding silently toward the kitchen to measure out his life with tea, as he would often joke to himself. His movements were erratic, following an indiscernible path that appeared both improvisational and forgotten. Yet, simplicity had its charms, drunk with the sweet, bitter tang of green tea and the company of silence.

As Jonathan sipped the tainted green broth, the sound of a knock shattered the symphony of his solitude. Though the solitude was less symphony and more cacophony, but no matter.

Jonathan was not expecting company. In fact, his life was so far removed from the lives of others, the concept seemed almost laughable. Having removed himself from society, even the simplest of human interactions made him feel lightheaded, and therefore he hesitated, staring blankly at the door.

Summoning courage from a long-forgotten reserve, Jonathan moved toward the entrance of his dark, dusty abode. He reached the door, fingers fumbling with the lock, metal catching on metal like the twisting limbs of long-vanished lovers. When at last it opened, modesty abandoned, Jonathan found himself confronted by two figures, strange and unsettling, clad in suits of deep navy blue, eyes as black as sable ink.

“Jonathan Kingsley?” One lilted, the question hanging in the air like a vulture perched atop a carcass.

At the sound of his full name, Jonathan felt the chill of a forgotten winter sweep through the recesses of his being. “Yes,” he replied, voice steady, not wanting to expose a trembling weakness.

“Thank you for seeing us,” the same unequivocal voice continued. “We’re here regarding—”

There Jonathan paused, recognizing in their presence the same darkness that had haunted him, the same mysterious conspiracy that had claimed his career, his dignity, his life. In the hushed company of some nameless enemy, that shadowed, unspoken force, the truth arrived like a wraith in the night.

“Can you tell me…what exactly is this?” Jonathan asked. “Why have you found me?”

The pair exchanged a glance, a silent language of the naming of conspirators, before the first copurred, “You will understand that we tread lightly in these matters.”

As he spoke, his companion lifted their hand and made a gesture that caused the air to shimmer, the room to twist and bend. Time seemed to fold upon itself, an origami of existence.
