Twilight Tales: Folkloric Stories for the Modern Reader


The village of Anavern lay nestled at the base of the great Serpentine Mountains. Renowned for its lush vineyards and serene vistas, it was a favored destination for innumerable visitors. Yet, long-standing whispers bestow its reputation with a sinister aura, warn its inhabitants of enchanted spirits which wander the nearby woods. A panoply of enchanted spirits, that thrive by consuming human endeavor, pause to contort the actions of men. For those who are audacious enough to heed them, a life of beguiling and power might take root; woe to those who stray from their guidance, for they are led down a path of insipid obscurity.

One day, a rashly ambitious young man named Lenot ventured into the heart of the woods in search of such a spirit. Born in Anavern, Lenot had plagued his mind pondering the lives of those who dared defy the village’s expectations. As was customary, at the tender age of twenty, his father had pressed upon him the burden of the family vineyard. Nevertheless, Lenot scoffed at the mundane life his father had thought fit for him. In due time, he left to explore the wilds, graciously supplied by his father with provisions.

In the heart of the forest, Lenot came upon a towering hollow tree, the entrance of which he found veiled by many foot-long thorns – an unmistakable herald of the presence of a spirit. Determined to prove himself amidst the village’s bewilderment, Lenot bravely faced the throngs of general forest adorning the vicinity of the tree. He embraced the test of wit and fortitude to make his maiden foray into the spiritual world.

In his solitude, he waited for hours until night had descended upon the forest. Indeed, darkness was the only fitting dress for the arcane. Suddenly, the sound of ethereal whispers crackled through the air. Confused, Lenot stumbled to find the source of this disembodied presence. It was as if the wind spoke with the voice of his ancestors, cajoling and tempting him through the haze of the dying day. Only then did he understand: the darkness birthed the spirit’s essence, their hidden eyesight unmasked the air of deceptions, and their malicious whispers revealed the frailty of rational thought, translated by Lenot’s mind into a new language, intelligible to his surprisingly listening ears.

Guided by this alien symphony, Lenot approached the hollow tree. The path adorned with its barbs, imbued with a supernatural component, reeled Lenot in. He disdained the probability that the thorns’ purpose was to inflict harm. Instead, Lenot had grown ardently confused about the true character of the spirit inside. Was it righteous or malicious? Eager to unravel the coda to his quest, he swallowed his pride and succumbed to his instincts; he dared to push aside a sharp branch of thorns, inserting his body into the darkness.

The air seemed to take form just as he entered the hollow. The unfinished marble silhouette he withstood the gaze of many celestial adults. Each stood with their arms outstretched subtly, hairs on their arms bristling even in the cold delight of nightfall. Their melodious whispers and unstoppable urges sounded like a fountain’s churn. He begged them to bestow upon him the arcane domain of bewitching the village - a token to ratify success in their eyes and a net to capture dreams beyond their reach.

His captors toyed with his desire - a flicker here, a ping there. In unhinged alien eloquence, one spirit whispered omens and another Reality’s spilled pears. Another maliciously sniggered as Lenot rubbed his sweaty palm, while still more just stared, filling the hollow oblong room with their animated transparency.

Simultaneously struck by awe and panic, Lenot’s mind grew frantic, unable to follow the twisted thoughts of these spectral beings. But unbeknownst to him, the raw power emanating from them was far more physical than he imagined. Answering the quivering uncertainty gripping his throat, an elder air spirit spoke, their voice more palpable than the collectively stifling whispers of others.

“Human,” the elder whispered audibly, “I render to you a choice – one that cannot be altered once chosen. To lead a life of beguiling power or fade into obscurity with no memory of sharp dreams or joys. Speak your answer promptly and know that there shall be no second chances.”

Awash in fear yet Determined, Lenot weighed the desolation of obscurity against the heartbleeding woe of influencing Anavern’s lives. As the whispers of the hollow closed in to permit his decision, he gnashed his teeth at the pain he would inflict on those dear to him. “I choose bewitchment!” Lenot proclaimed, gibbering in trepidation.

The air seemed to rejoice in his assent, igniting the cave with a cacophony Lenot had never heard before. Flickers of glee and malice manifested before him in a staining whirlwind, twisting every aspect of tangible thought in Lenot’s cradle of a mind. With a final pulse of energy, the air spirits each issued his soul a different vital gift; bewitching power, authority over dreamscapes, and a binding curse.

Lenot emerged from the hollow with a newly formed air of mystique. Wails of woe whispered through the night as the spirits dismissed their creation. With a subtle show of cunning, he secured power over the village and united a cult of misguided wanderers. In due time, Anavern’s vineyards took on the shade of its new leader’s bewitching control – and through it all, the counterfeit of life flickered in Lenot’s once innocent eyes.
