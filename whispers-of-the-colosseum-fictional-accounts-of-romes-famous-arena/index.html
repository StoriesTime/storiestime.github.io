Whispers of the Colosseum: Fictional Accounts of Rome's Famous Arena


The ancient city of Rome was a land of emperors and senators, gladiators and charioteers. Amidst its bustling life stood the colossal and magnificent Colosseum, an unforgettable symbol of Rome’s rich history and Rome’s fascination with blood and entertainment.

Amid these vibrant echoes, whispers of the Colosseum’s past are alive. Few people know the full story of its construction, the legendary battles fought within its walls, and the lives of its inhabitants. Here, we will share tales of those who mattered the most—unseen by the eyes of history.

It was the year 80 AD, when Emperor Titus ordered the construction of the grand amphitheater, today’s Colosseum. Thousands of slaves toiled day in and day out, carving out the monumental structure from the heart of Rome’s living city. Each night, they would retreat to their overcrowded shelter without food or proper rest.

One such slave was Alexius. He lived with a wistful nostalgia for his home and family, lost long before, as his hands bled from the harsh labor. One day, in secret, as the temple to the gods was being constructed within the amphitheater, a small statue caught his eye. It was a bust of Mercury, the messenger of the gods, and Alexius couldn’t resist reaching out. He whispered, “If you’ll give me strength and skill for one decisive fight, I’ll give you the wealth that I’m destined to gain.”

The next day, a powerful voice echoed through the quiet crowd, announcing that a gladiator had killed himself in his prison cell, leaving a prized fortune behind. The prize was a sword made from the finest metal, its hilt encrusted with precious stones and more gold than one man could spend in a lifetime. The spear, the shield, and the armor of this anonymous warrior were valued as much as the sword.

The words of the proclamation fell upon Alexius’ ears, and he understood that his prayers had been answered. He grabbed the statue of Mercury, pressed it to his chest, and pledged his destiny to the god. He stepped forth from his underground prison to the light of day as Alexius the Gladiator.

Day by day, his deeds echoed loudly within the walls of the Colosseum. He would fight fiercely, slashing his way through countless enemies until one day, an unexpected opponent stood before him. This challenger was a gladiator rumored to be unbeatable, Trucidus by name.

Every eye was on Trucidus as he stepped into the arena, clad in a suit of black armor, seemingly invincible. Alexius had felt the same misfortune before, and he knew that today was his time. He felt the energy, the strength of Mercury pulsing through his limbs, his resolve taken form.

The whistle blew, and the face-off began. As the two gladiators locked eyes and raised their swords, Alexius’ blood-curdling war cry shook the arena and made the hearts of thousands tremble. In but a flicker of an eye, the two figures clashed.

The slash of a sword, an agonizing crunch of bones - and Trucidus went down. As Alexius stood victorious over his fallen adversary, he realized his destiny had changed forever. But his reward would prove damning.

On the outside of the Colosseum, merchants sold the prized goods of their victory. Alexius, flush with wealth from his victory, was seen as the conqueror of Rome’s champion. Yet, as the days passed following his triumph, whispers within the ancient walls of Rome’s amphitheater whispered of nefarious deeds and powerful curses.

It was said that Alexius had offered great tribute—some would say a sacrifice—to Mercury for his victory. That as the crowd watched on and the amphitheater roared with delight, he had slaughtered a pure white stallion in the honor of Mercury.

And it was believed that these strange acts filled the Colosseum with a dark, unworldly spirit. That from that day forth, few gladiators would ever challenge Alexius. And with the blood of Trucidus and of the stallion, Alexius would forever bear the weight of that dark bargain.

With great strife, Alexius fled to Rome’s outskirts. The whispers followed him, as did his riches and the weight of the curse. He built a lavish villa, hoping to domestic his demons. Yet, the whispers would not let him rest.

For ages, Rome would remember him as the cursed gladiator. He would never hold another title or conquer another triumph. His legacy would be rooted in mystery, boldly displayed upon the ancient Colosseum’s thick and sturdy walls.

Where once powerful emperors and senators ruled Rome, now stories of Alexius and Trucidus, the whispers of the past, were whispered again. And the echoes of that day and the ancient amphitheater danced upon the wind of time.

Just as the sun was beginning to set over the ruins of the city of Rome, a soft, rustling voice floated through the air. The newcomer said that he had heard the tales of the gladiator Alexius and the whispered secrets of the Colosseum.

From that day forth on the winds through Rome, tales of Alexius, his rivalry with Trucidus, and his pact with Mercury would never die—or perhaps never be known—just whispered in the annals of Rome’s ancient past. “Whispers of the Colosseum”, Rome continued to call them to the present day: an intricate web of mystery that forms just one part of Rome’s rich history and timeless tales.
