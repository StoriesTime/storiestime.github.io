The Spotlight Snatcher: Shining a Light on Performing Arts for Empathy


Emilia had never been one to shy away from the spotlight. As a child, she danced with abandon; and the tiny stage in her living room boasted countless impassioned performances from the little dynamo. Growing up, Emilia continued to perform, immersing herself in theater, dance, and music. As she grew into a young woman, she cultivated her skills, expanding her presence to local stages, and eventually captivated audiences around the world.

Yet, her love for the performing arts extended beyond her own dreams of stardom. Emilia yearned to share her passion with others. For Emilia, the stage wasn’t just a platform for her, but an opportunity to give back. With this thought in mind, she founded “Light It Up!” - a program that brought the enchanting world of performing arts to underrepresented communities.

The program centered on a few key principles. Foremost was providing empathy. Together, the team donated their time, talents, and equipment, to light up venues for those who might not otherwise have such opportunities. Simultaneously, Emilia hoped to strengthen communities, enriching them with the boundless power of performing arts to foster connections, create camaraderie, and, simply, put smiles on faces.

Light It Up! quickly gained traction as partnerships formed between local schools, non-profits, and community centers. As stories spread of elderly care home residents breaking out in song, children with special needs blossoming on stage, and families from all walks of life sharing in the magic of theater, the organization’s reputation grew.

Soon, Light It Up! found itself with ample occasion to celebrate. Festivals and fairs abounded, and the team traveled the world bringing joy to all. Yet, among such immense global outreach, Emilia never lost sight of what was most important: providing opportunities for people underrepresented in the performing arts.

One late summer evening from the midst of their busiest season, Emilia found herself standing backstage amid the buzz of an eagerly anticipated community outreach performance. Festoon lights twinkling against the dusk, a venerable old theater pulsed with excitement. This was the headquarters of Light It Up!’s latest project, an ambitious partnership with a local non-profit that catered to people with development disabilities.

As the audience began to chant, Emilia reveled in the vibrant hustle and bustle before the show. Production team, actors, and dancers alike buzzed about, nail-bitingly conscious of the importance of tonight’s performance. Emilia was prepared to submerge herself in the ambiance; pots of makeup for those on stage, flutters of laughter as performers prepped their hearts for renditions, and the sheer magic of music sending chills down the spine.

The curtain rose, and the stage blossomed to life. The auditorium pulsed with energy as artists succeeded one another, hearts pounding, knees weak, sweat and tears alike ripe with the intensity of performances. Emilia exchanged a furiously proud glance with her team as the audience responded with cheering and delighted applause. This was but one of countless examples she had witnessed of the electrifying empathy that ran through the veins of performing arts. “This is why I founded Light It Up!” Emilia had whispered to her friends. Tonight, she thought, we’re truly shining a light.

And so, through the whirlwind of her success, Emilia always remembered her roots. She danced and performed, fearlessly immersing herself in every capacity she could manage, but her real joy stemmed from her desire to share her passion with the world. Emilia shone a light on performing arts for empathy, capturing eyes, hearts, and minds alike.

Emilia took us on her journey to launch “Light It Up!” and its incredible impact. Let us remember her message as we face our personal performances. Just as Emilia encourages her troupes on the biggest of stages and in the harshest of conditions, let us be brave enough to extend opportunities, illumination, and empathy to the world around us. For like her, we can become “spotlight snatchers,” illuminating and embracing life’s moments one connection at a time.
