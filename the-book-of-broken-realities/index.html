The Book of Broken Realities


Lena had always been an avid reader, the kind of person who would lose herself in books, even on a crowded subway or in the dim light of a cozy café. But there was something different about the book she found on that rainy afternoon.

It was tucked away in the back corner of a second-hand bookstore, one she had passed by countless times without ever stepping inside. The old store had an eerie, almost forgotten feel to it. The scent of dust and aged paper filled the air, and the dim light only made the shelves seem more cramped and ancient. There were no flashy signs, no friendly storefront displays—just the promise of something hidden beneath layers of time.

She hadn’t even meant to go inside that day. But the rain had started to fall in torrents, and she had sought refuge in the nearest shelter. The bell above the door rang faintly as she entered, its sound drowned by the storm outside. For a while, Lena wandered the narrow aisles, glancing at the spines of books, some of which she recognized and others that seemed strange and foreign. Her hand brushed against a thick, leather-bound volume, its cover dark and unmarked.

She pulled it from the shelf, intrigued. There was no title, no author’s name, just a symbol—an intricate spiral, drawn with gold ink, that seemed to shift and dance in the dim light. It was as though the symbol was alive, twisting and turning in a hypnotic rhythm. The weight of the book in her hands felt strange, too heavy for its size, as if it contained more than just pages. There was a distinct sense of foreboding that clung to it, but Lena couldn’t resist the pull.

With a mixture of curiosity and unease, she opened the book to the first page.

The paper was old, the ink faded, but the words were unmistakable:

“This is the Book of Broken Realities. Once you read it, the world will never be the same.”

At first, Lena thought it was some sort of joke or a strange work of fiction. But as she flipped through the pages, she realized that each entry was more than just a tale—it was a world. Each page told the story of a reality that had been fractured, twisted, or destroyed in some way. Some were familiar, while others were bizarre and incomprehensible.

One page depicted a reality where humans had evolved with wings, soaring across vast skies above cities made of glass. Another spoke of a world where time ran backward, and people aged in reverse. The descriptions were vivid and detailed, yet there was a sense of profound loss in each one.

She turned the page.

“In this reality, the stars have all gone out. The sky is empty, and there is no hope of light. The world is cold, and its people have learned to live in the dark.”

The next page was even more unsettling.

“In this reality, every person has a mirror version of themselves, but the mirrors are not reflections—they are twisted, distorted, evil versions. The two sides are constantly at war, each trying to destroy the other.”

The more Lena read, the more she became captivated by the tragic beauty of these worlds. The book felt like a portal, and with each page, she felt herself slipping further from her own reality, sinking into the pages, as if the stories were becoming her own.

It was then that she realized the truth—this book wasn’t just a collection of stories. It was a map of broken realities, each one a fracture of something that had once existed. Some of these realities had collapsed entirely, while others still persisted, albeit in altered forms. And as she read on, Lena began to understand something even more disturbing: the lines between these realities were not as fixed as she had assumed.

Chapter 2: The Consequence

Lena spent the next several days obsessively reading the book. Every spare moment she had, she was buried in its pages, losing herself in the fragmented worlds. She began to notice strange things happening around her—subtle shifts in her own reality. It was as if the book’s stories were bleeding into her life.

At first, it was just a sense of déjà vu. She would encounter people she felt she had met before, in other places, in other lives. The world around her began to feel distant, like it was fading in and out of focus. The weather would shift unpredictably—sunshine in the middle of a storm, or a cold breeze in the dead of summer. Her friends and family started to notice changes in her, too. They commented on how she seemed distant, as if her mind were always somewhere else.

But the most unsettling event came one evening when she was walking home from the bookstore, the book still in her bag. As she passed by an alley, she caught a glimpse of something in the corner of her eye—another person, or perhaps a reflection, standing in the shadows. But when she turned to look, there was nothing there. For a brief moment, however, the world seemed to shift. The alley was different—darker, twisted, and more sinister. The air was thick, and the ground felt unstable beneath her feet.

She stumbled back, her heart racing, and when she glanced at the book again, she saw something new. The spiral symbol on the cover was glowing faintly, its movement more pronounced. It was almost as though it was watching her, waiting for her next move.

That night, as Lena lay in bed, she had a dream—or was it a vision? She was standing in a vast, empty landscape, with nothing but darkness surrounding her. She reached out her hand, and the air shimmered before her, revealing a broken mirror. The reflection in it wasn’t her own. It was someone—something—else, staring back with hollow eyes.

The book’s words echoed in her mind: Once you read it, the world will never be the same.

The next morning, Lena’s reality had begun to unravel. The small inconsistencies had grown more pronounced. People she knew seemed to act out of character, as though they were from an alternate version of themselves. She couldn’t tell if they were real or if she was losing her grip on what was real. Her life had become a series of blurred lines, and she was slipping further away from the world she once knew.

Chapter 3: The Search

Determined to understand the book and the strange occurrences that were now a part of her life, Lena set out to find the answers. The bookstore had vanished. She searched for hours, retracing her steps, but it was as though it had never existed. There was no trace of the old, dusty store or the mysterious bookseller who had sold her the book. It was as if the store had never been a part of her reality.

She turned to the book itself, desperate to find a clue, something that could help her make sense of what was happening. As she flipped through the pages, she found an entry she had missed before. It was a warning, written in bold, red ink:

“The Book of Broken Realities is not just a collection of lost worlds. It is a bridge, a doorway between them. And once you step through, there is no turning back.”

Lena froze. She had already stepped through. She had already begun to live in the broken realities. The lines had blurred, and now she was caught between them.

There was no going back.

Chapter 4: The Unraveling

Lena’s world continued to fracture. Time began to warp around her. Days bled into nights, and moments of clarity grew rarer and rarer. She would find herself in places she didn’t recognize, surrounded by faces that were familiar, yet somehow wrong. The fabric of her existence was unraveling, and she could feel herself slipping deeper into the abyss.

The book had shown her the realities, but it hadn’t prepared her for the consequences of knowing them. The more she read, the more she understood that the world she had known was just one version among countless others. And some of those versions were darker, twisted beyond recognition.

But there was still hope, buried deep within the pages. A final entry, one that seemed to glow with a faint, otherworldly light, caught her attention:

“To restore balance, to heal the fractures, one must be willing to sacrifice their place in the world. A price must be paid, and the reality must be mended.”

Lena knew what she had to do. She had to let go of her reality—the one she had once known—and allow the book to guide her through the broken worlds. Only then could the fractures heal.

But at what cost?

Chapter 5: The Sacrifice

Lena stood at the crossroads of countless realities, each one more fractured than the last. She could feel the weight of the decision pressing down on her. To repair the brokenness, she would have to surrender herself to the void, to become part of the story.

And so, with a single, resolute breath, Lena stepped forward, into the spiral of broken worlds. The book in her hands pulsed with energy, and for a moment, she felt herself fall through the fabric of reality.

The world shifted.

The Book of Broken Realities closed, its final chapter written.

And Lena was no more.

The world around her was still. The lines between realities, once broken and scattered, began to heal, mending the fractures and restoring balance. But Lena, the reader, the traveler between worlds, had become part of the book, a piece of the story that would live on, waiting for the next soul to discover its secrets.

For in the Book of Broken Realities, no one was ever truly lost. They were just waiting to be found again.
