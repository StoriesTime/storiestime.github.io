The search revealed yet another room, its metal door sealed behind an unauthorized patch job. Alexander's gut tightened as he located the makeshift safe, half-hidden beneath a pile of discarded equipment. The combination dial glimmered faintly in the dim light. With delicate fingers, Alexander set the numbers, waiting for the resounding click of the safe's release. The door opened, revealing a shocking sight: a trove of classified documents and encrypted files, containing the very secrets that had been leaked to their adversaries. Their mole was named Henry, a man well-versed in the intricacies of cryptography, who had carefully planted this digital garden of disinformation. His role, thoughtfully wrapped in an air of obscurity, gave him the necessary leverage to manipulate information without arousing suspicion. Alexander continued his investigation, finding encrypted files and documents within Henry's temporary hideout, holding evidence of the mole's activities. One hint in particular hinted at Henry's seemingly unassailable position inside MI6 – perhaps they had not been deceived after all, but led into this mole trap on the premise that suspicions would be triggered quickly. Alexander documented the evidence meticulously, committing every scrap to memory. He then carefully set fire to the proof, committing their files to the rising inferno, fueled by the compressed gases inside the submarine. He sealed the evidence within the incinerated husk of the safe, intent to ensure the double agent's cover would be forever buried. At the upper-water mark, the inferno melted, providing no outward indication that anything had ever been there. Fickle lake creatures sensed an under-visited aspect of the waters and swam swiftly away from the surface, instinctively knowing to keep a distance from signs of malice. With an enigmatic smile etched on his weathered lips, Alexander receded back into the ever-shifting world beneath the surface, an elusive silhouette disintegrating into the moonlit iniquity of twilight on the calm waters of the abandoned lake. ...
