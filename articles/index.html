Once a thriving station alive with activity, it had become a forgotten relic, a shadowy echo of the past that drifted silently through the void. Many had sought the secrets buried within its abandoned halls, yet none had returned. Captain Elara Voss had heard the tales—whispers shared in dimly lit bars and smoky lounges on distant worlds. The Ulysses, her battered but reliable ship, was her only companion on this solitary quest. She stepped into a hallway dimly illuminated by flickering emergency lights, shadows creeping along the walls reminding her that she was very much alone. As she moved deeper into the heart of the space station, she noticed the remnants of a life once lived. Personal effects cluttered the floors—old photographs, small toys, and even a rusted medical kit that, judging by the markings, hadn’t been touched in years. She picked up a photograph, its edges tattered, depicting a group of smiling faces. These were the people who had once called Thessalon home, oblivious to the fate that awaited them. Each step reverberated through the empty corridors, a rhythmic reminder that time had pressed on while the station lingered in stasis. A thunderous alarm, suddenly blaring from an unseen source, made Elara jump. She whirled around, adrenaline surging as the air thickened with tension. Focusing her flashlight on the deeper recesses of the station, she felt an undeniable pull toward the command center. Whatever secrets lay within its walls might soon reveal themselves. What had happened to them? The shells of scientific instruments and communication arrays were strewn about—a stark contrast to the orderly perfection that had been intended. She began her investigation, piecing together the shattered remnants of what had occurred on this forsaken outpost. It became increasingly clear that Thessalon was not just a vessel for research; it was a fortress holding something sinister. Her instincts tingling, Elara scanned the remaining data. Those lost souls had been studying the very fabric of life itself, interacting with unknown organisms, and breaking the boundaries of biological science. The records hinted at incredible advancements, paired with a constant fear of manageable risks leading to catastrophic outcomes. Suddenly, a series of footsteps echoed down the corridor—the unmistakable sound of someone, or something, moving through the shadows. Echoes of laughter suddenly resounded, high-pitched and disembodied, reverberating off the metallic walls. Stomach churning, she took a step back, pressing her back against the cold, unyielding surface of the wall. It felt like a dream, like the boredom of isolation had birthed hallucinations. Yet, what if these sounds were indeed real? With every ounce of courage, she began to approach the shadows at the end of the corridor, where flickers of movement danced against the metallic backdrop. Just as she reached the archway, the illusion shattered. A figure emerged—a small humanoid silhouette, dirty and torn, coated in what appeared to be remnants of the station’s shattered walls. It was only then that she realized it wasn’t a ghost of Thessalon's past but a living creature, gaunt and frightened. The child-like being looked up at her with wide eyes that spoke of fear and despair. "Help me," the entity said, barely more than a whisper while its voice trembled. Elara lowered her weapon, curiosity overshadowing her instinct for survival. "Who are you?" she asked gently. "What happened here?" The creature nodded furiously, pointing behind it. “Monsters. They want to hurt me.” Elara’s mind raced as she processed this new information. "What monsters?" “Not like me,” it whispered, echoing the remnants of childlike innocence. "They were the crew. They became… different." Panic surged through her. The station had become a breeding ground for something unnatural, something that had twisted the very fabric of the crew into monsters.
