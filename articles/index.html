Years of neglect and abandonment had taken their toll, leaving behind a labyrinth of dusty corridors, cobweb-covered chandeliers, and hidden secrets waiting to be unearthed. It was only when I had agreed to oversee the painstaking restoration project that I discovered the whispers. At first, I dismissed it as the creaking of the old wooden beams or the wind rustling through the trees outside. But as the days passed, I began to notice a persistent, ethereal whispering emanating from the walls themselves. It was soft, almost imperceptible, yet unmistakable. I'd dismissed it as mere superstition, but now I wondered if there was truth to the tales. The whispers grew louder and more insistent as I explored the labyrinthine halls. I began to notice patterns and regularities in the words and phrases, as if the whispers were addressing me directly. At first, I thought it was my imagination running wild, but the more I listened, the more convinced I became that the whispers were a genuine phenomenon. The article, scrawled in flowing script, spoke of a time when Lord Edwin had refused to listen to his sister's pleas. The whispers, the vengeful and sorrowful whispers of Willowdale Manor, had driven Lady Victoria to madness.
