In the quiet moments, after the rain had washed away the noise of the world, I would listen to the trees, the creatures, and the wind. They spoke in whispers of a shared dream, where the land, the trees, and the creatures were one, interconnected, and interdependent. But on that day, the rain's voice was loudest of all. With her final whisper, the rain's droplets began to slow, ceasing their gentle rhythm. The world outside returned to its ordinary routines, but I knew that something within me had shifted. I had heard the whispers of the universe, and in those whispers, I had discovered a new truth: I was not alone.
