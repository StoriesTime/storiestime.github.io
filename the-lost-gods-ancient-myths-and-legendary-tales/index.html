The Lost Gods: Ancient Myths and Legendary Tales


In the heart of the world, where the boundary between the realms of men and the divine was thin, there existed a pantheon of gods, whose power was so vast and so ancient that even the oldest legends could hardly describe their glory. These were the Lost Gods, whose names faded into myth, whose temples were swallowed by the earth, and whose stories were whispered on the winds that passed through forgotten lands.

Long before the rise of civilizations that would shape the world, the Lost Gods roamed freely. They were neither bound by time nor space, their dominions spanning across the earth, sky, and sea. Yet, as centuries passed and the human race grew in numbers and strength, these gods faded from sight, their once great power reduced to the distant echoes of ancient tales.

The God of Endless Waters: Thalmaris

Thalmaris, the God of Endless Waters, was one of the most revered of the Lost Gods. His realm stretched over the oceans, lakes, rivers, and every droplet of water on the planet. His domain was infinite, for he was the keeper of all water—fresh and salt, clear and murky. He was said to be the first to emerge from the Void, the primordial nothingness before creation.

Thalmaris was neither cruel nor merciful. His essence was pure, untainted by the whims of mortals. However, his moods were as unpredictable as the waters he controlled. Calm rivers could suddenly swell into raging torrents, peaceful seas could become violent storms. Yet, he was a protector as much as he was a destroyer. Thalmaris ensured that all life was sustained by water, but when angered, he could drown entire civilizations in his fury.

The earliest tales of Thalmaris spoke of a time when he helped the first humans survive their desperate journey across the seas. A great famine had struck the lands, and the humans, starving and on the verge of extinction, set sail in search of new lands. But their voyage was fraught with peril. Thalmaris, moved by their suffering, guided their ships with the currents, leading them to an island rich in fertile soil and freshwater streams.

However, this act of kindness did not go unchallenged. As humans grew prosperous on this island, they began to take Thalmaris’ gift for granted. The rivers that flowed freely, the lakes that glittered in the sunlight, and the seas that offered fish in abundance—they began to see these as mere resources to exploit. They dug canals and diverted streams, polluting the waters and taking more than they needed.

Thalmaris, who had once been their benefactor, withdrew into the depths of the oceans, his anger turning the seas into turbulent, unpredictable forces. Ships that sailed on the waters without reverence sank without warning, and entire coastal villages were swallowed by the ocean. It was said that the people who had once been saved by his mercy were now haunted by the god’s wrath, their descendants cursed to forever live in fear of the water.

The Serpent of the Skies: Ilhaereth

Far above the earth, where the air was thin and the stars twinkled with ancient knowledge, there lived Ilhaereth, the Serpent of the Skies. Ilhaereth was a god of wisdom, who coiled around the heavens, watching over all things. His massive wings, vast as entire mountain ranges, spread across the skies, and his body was made of shimmering scales that reflected the stars themselves. It was said that Ilhaereth had seen the birth of the first constellations, and that every star in the sky was a memory of something he had witnessed in the eons since his birth.

Unlike Thalmaris, Ilhaereth was not a god of physical force. He did not rule with storms or floods, but with knowledge. His eyes could pierce the veil of time, seeing past, present, and future in an eternal spiral. He was both revered and feared by those who sought to uncover the secrets of the universe. It was said that Ilhaereth could grant wisdom beyond mortal understanding, but only to those pure of heart and free of greed.

The legends told of a mortal, a scholar named Eryndor, who journeyed to the highest mountain in search of Ilhaereth. Driven by a thirst for knowledge, Eryndor believed that if he could commune with the Serpent of the Skies, he would gain an understanding of all things—life, death, the stars, the earth, and even the gods themselves.

For years, Eryndor climbed the treacherous slopes, enduring frostbite, starvation, and exhaustion, until he finally reached the summit. There, he found Ilhaereth, coiled around the peaks like a living constellation. The serpent’s eyes were like twin moons, glowing with an otherworldly light.

“I seek your wisdom,” Eryndor said, falling to his knees. “Grant me the knowledge of the universe.”

Ilhaereth’s voice echoed like the sound of wind through the vast emptiness of the heavens. “Knowledge is a gift, but it is not without its cost,” the serpent spoke. “Are you willing to pay the price?”

Eryndor, consumed by his desire for enlightenment, nodded eagerly.

“Then listen well,” Ilhaereth said. “The truth is as vast as the skies, and as dangerous as the stars themselves. Knowledge can destroy the mind as easily as it can enlighten the soul. You will understand all things, but you will never be able to forget them.”

And so, Eryndor’s mind was opened to the vast, incomprehensible truths of the universe. The secrets of creation, the lives of the gods, and the fate of all beings danced in his mind like stars in the night sky. But in his quest for wisdom, he lost his sanity. Overwhelmed by the sheer magnitude of what he now knew, Eryndor descended the mountain, never speaking again, his eyes wide with the terrible knowledge that had consumed him.

The people of the nearby villages would sometimes hear his whispers in the wind, but none dared approach him, for it was said that Ilhaereth’s gift had turned Eryndor into something not quite mortal.

The Weaver of Shadows: Shaltra

Shaltra, the Weaver of Shadows, was the third of the Lost Gods. She ruled over the darkness between the stars, the spaces between moments, and the hidden corners of the world. Her realm was neither evil nor benign; it simply existed, as necessary as the light. She wove the threads of fate, guiding lives in the quiet moments where no eyes could see. Her touch was soft, her voice gentle, but her power was immense.

Shaltra was the one who whispered secrets to the dreamers, who guided the lost souls in the realm between life and death. Mortals feared her because they feared the unknown, the silent spaces where the gods could not be seen. Yet, those who were brave enough to seek Shaltra’s wisdom would find her to be a compassionate and understanding deity. She did not meddle with the affairs of mortals, but her influence could be felt in the quiet moments of reflection, in the darkness where the mind could wander freely.

The myths tell of a young woman, Kira, who had lost everything—her family, her home, and her hope. She wandered through the world in search of meaning, until she reached a dark forest where the trees whispered secrets of the gods. There, in the heart of the forest, she found Shaltra, weaving her web of shadows.

“I have lost everything,” Kira said, her voice trembling. “Can you help me?”

Shaltra looked at Kira with eyes that held all the mysteries of the universe. “Everything you have lost is part of the fabric of your fate. It is not for me to undo. But I can show you a path, if you are willing to see it.”

And so, Shaltra revealed the threads of Kira’s life, showing her the hidden patterns woven into her destiny. Kira saw that her losses were not random but part of a greater design, one that would lead her to something greater than she could imagine.

But the price of this knowledge was steep. Kira could never return to her former life. She could never live without the awareness of the invisible threads that bound her to everything around her. She left the forest with a heart heavy with understanding, but also a sense of purpose that would guide her for the rest of her days.

The Forgotten Gods

As time wore on, the Lost Gods faded from the memory of humanity. The temples that once stood in their honor crumbled into ruin, the rituals and sacrifices forgotten. But their influence remained. The seas still roared with the power of Thalmaris, the stars still shone with the wisdom of Ilhaereth, and the shadows still whispered with the guidance of Shaltra.

And perhaps, in the quiet moments when no one is watching, they still walk among us, hidden in the corners of the world, waiting to be remembered once again. Their names may have been forgotten, but their stories are timeless, echoing through the ages like the sound of waves crashing against the shore or the rustling of leaves in the wind.

In the end, the Lost Gods are not lost at all. They are simply waiting for the day when the world is ready to remember them. And when that day comes, they will rise again, their power as great and as ancient as the myths that shaped them.
