Operation Midnight: The Heist of the Century


It was a chilly winter evening in Paris when the call came in. Auguste Dupont, a retired safecracker, stood up straight in his chair as his phone rang shrill. He let it ring a few times, knowing it was a call he couldn't ignore.

The voice on the other end was cold, characteristic of a man who knew what he wanted and wasn't afraid to take risks. Dupont listened intently as the stranger on the phone laid out the stakes. "Auguste, I need someone with your… unique skills," the man said in a smooth tone. "Meet me at the Old Red Pyramid in an hour. We'll discuss the details then." Dupont lit a cigarette, took a long drag, and nodded even though the man couldn't see him. He knew the Old Red Pyramid, a dingy bar on the outskirts of the city. What he didn't know was the man behind the voice or the mission that would change the course of history.

When Dupont arrived, the Old Red Pyramid was dim and deserted except for one customer - a tall, lean figure sitting in the corner sipping a whiskey. "That's the one?" Dupont asked, lowering his voice as he took a seat next to the stranger. "The one and only," the stranger responded with a hint of a smile. "Meet Professor Laurent. A man with a PhD in art and rare treasures. He's why you're here, Auguste." Dupont's eyes scanned the room before turning back to the stranger. "What makes him think I'm the man for the job?" "Because you're the only one I trust," Professor Laurent said, looking up from his drink before turning back to his book. "You, Auguste, are a legend in your own time. They call you 'The Ghost'. Understandable since you've been in and out of bars, ain't mastered an invisibility cloak yet?" Dupont shifted uncomfortably in his seat. "Save it, Doc. Tell me what this is about."

The Professor snapped his book shut. "The Musée d'Orsay's mythical blue diamond, the Azur Royale, is one of the rarest gems on the planet. Worth an estimated 70 million euros. Tonight's mission, however brief, is to obtain it." Dupont raised an eyebrow, intrigued. "Why?" "For reasons I'm about to share with you, the diamond contains irrefutable proof of disgruntled relationships between heyday banking and Napoleon III's heterodox protectorates," The Professor justified.
