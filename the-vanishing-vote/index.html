The Vanishing Vote


‘‘Sir, there’s a woman at the front desk wishing to see you urg—’’ a haggard intern interrupted my slumber; I needed coffee, stat. I rolled my eyes, brushing a wayward lock of salt and pepper hair from my forehead. Anna, clearly frazzled, sighed deeply, ‘‘She claims to represent The Vanishing Vote.’’

‘‘The what now?’’ I raised an eyebrow, confusion creasing my brow. ‘‘Did you get her name?’’

Anna hesitated, doubt clouding her eyes. In response, she presented a slip of paper, the elegant, flowing script difficult to discern in the dim light. ‘‘Esmeralda Picard.’’ I stroked my chin, deep in thought. ‘‘Have her wait in the conference room. I’ll be along shortly.’’

With a nod, she scurried off, her shoes tapping against the tiled floor. True to her word, Anna had indeed followed protocol to the letter, ensuring that Esmeralda, whoever she was, awaited my presence in one of our private chambers. As I made my way to the conference room, I couldn’t help but wonder about her origins; after all, The Vanishing Vote was an idea, if not a reality swallowed by the tumultuous waves caused by the shadow of democracy itself.

As the door to the conference room creaked open, Esmeralda rose from her seat, an elegant, tall figure draped in a deep emerald cloak that seemed to shimmer with an ethereal glow. She met my gaze with piercing, haughty green eyes, and extended a slender hand adorned with mysterious, ancient-looking rings.

‘‘Senator,’’ she said, her voice honeyed and captivating, ‘‘you sought an audience with The Vanishing Vote, and here I stand before you.’’ My grasp on her hand was firm but brief, as a shudder ran down my spine. There was an otherworldly quality to her presence, an unsettling aura that seemed to seep from the very fabric of her being.

‘‘I suppose I did,’’ I began, my voice laced with uncertainty. ‘‘Then tell me, Esmeralda of The Vanishing Vote, why should our electoral system change to accommodate your ‘‘amendments?’’ Her lips curled into a smirk, revealing a set of pearly whites that seemed to hypnotize. With a flourish of her hand, she invited me to take a seat, retaking her place at the head of the table.

‘‘Senator, our world has long been governed by a flawed system of votes.’’ She crossed her legs, intertwining her fingers thoughtfully. ‘‘The electoral system, as it stands, is outdated and won’t survive the transformation our society is poised to experience.’’ She paused, gauging my response. ‘‘Gone are the days of paper ballots; the future belongs to a newly developed form of digital votes afloat, embedding encryption we naturally cast in our dreams.’’

I considered her words, my brow furrowed. ‘‘And what, pray tell, is the purpose of The Vanishing Vote?’’

‘‘To bring democracy kicking and screaming into the 21st century,’’ she smirked, ‘‘and allow for a more transparent form of governance. Instead of waiting in long lines, trudging from one polling station to another, our citizens could now cast their vote from the comfort of their homes, DMs, dreams, and hearts.’’

Since then, Esmeralda and I have spent countless hours dissecting every intricate detail of her Vanishing Vote proposal. We’ve grappled with the pitfalls of such a system – the potential vulnerability that lies in sleep, the nightmares that could be spun like webs of political influence, and the unchecked biases within our subconscious molding the vote. We poured over hordes of research, seeking a way to forge security measures that would protect this utopian vision.

Months skipped by recklessly; Esmeralda and I, fueled by coffee and cryptic dreams, worked day in and day out in our private chambers. Our reliance on one another grew uncomfortably close, an intimacy fostered by sleep as we shared the depths of our desires, fears, and ideals, seeking solace in the shadows of the subconscious.

But soon, our dreams became tainted with doubts, perversions of our sleep woven into Esmeralda’s narrative, crafted by the hands of political titans seeking power at any cost. As the world beyond our sacrosanct chamber crumbled into civil war, economic turmoil, and environmental devastation, Esmeralda herself slowly vanished, a mere whisper, a wisp of what the Vanishing Vote was envisioned to be.

My heart shattered against the floor like glass, echoes of the words left unspoken a tangible sting upon my senses. ‘‘The Vanishing Vote I offered you, did it not promise a democratic utopia whose time had come?’’ Her voice, once high and melodic, was now but a rasp, frail and haunted. Before I could answer, her body, barely visible, dissolved into the air, leaving behind nothing but the glimmer of her rings. Her fervor extinguished, smothered by the chaos of a crumbling society, the whispered breaths of defiance echoing through history.

I lay there on the cold floor, lifelessly staring, feeling the fissures of hope crack wider, tears bleeding into my tanned cheeks. The Vanishing Vote was now nothing more than a rare tale of fated encounters, an age-long dream with alarmingly surreal consequences. I slowly rose to my feet, my once fervent ambition vanished, humbled by the formidable weight of the world’s deceit.

And so, as I left the conference room that evening, darkness cloaked my soul. With heavy steps, I descended the steps of our political empire, clanging through the shadowed halls, finding solace in the embrace of the night, mourning the loss of Esmeralda and the fractured promise of The Vanishing Vote.

As the election approached, I knew with certainty that the time for a new journey beckoned. For the people needed a voice, and if not Esmeralda’s vision of The Vanishing Vote, at the very least, with quiet determination, I could lead them to shatter the shards of a bygone era and build a world that could sustain the fragile beauty of democracy.

And in that solitude, I vowed to uphold the truth, the essence of democracy, vanquishing the distance between reality and hope by securing the future of our voting system. For now, as dawn approached, the world awaited a minuscule glimmer of light, of possibility and change, fueled by the memory of Esmeralda and the ethereal promise of the Vanishing Vote.
