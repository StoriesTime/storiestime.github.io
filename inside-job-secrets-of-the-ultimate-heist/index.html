It was the summer of 2008, and the world was on the brink of a financial meltdown. The global economy was reeling from the collapse of the housing market, and countless investors were left holding worthless mortgage-backed securities. Amidst the chaos, a group of high-stakes thieves had been secretly plotting their most daring heist yet. Led by a charismatic mastermind, they had been observing the intricate inner workings of Wall Street's biggest banks and investment firms. They were a team of brilliant and seasoned professionals, each with their own unique skill set, and a unified goal: to infiltrate the heart of the financial system and steal a small fortune. Their target was none other than the esteemed Angelo R. Mozilo, the former CEO of Countrywide Financial, who was accused of inflating the value of mortgage-backed securities and prioritizing short-term profits over long-term stability. Mozilo had amassed a vast fortune by exploiting the vulnerability of others, and the team was determined to bring him down, one penny at a time. The group was diverse, to say the least. There was Rachel, a former securities analyst turned rogue, with a steely gaze and a penchant for digging up dirt. Next, there was Baddy, a brilliant young hacker with unparalleled access to some of the world's most secure systems. They also had Sally, an expert in forensic accounting, capable of pinpointing discrepancies in even the most complex financial trails. And, of course, there was Jack, the self-proclaimed " inside man" – a trusted executive with unparalleled access to the very heart of the financial system. The secret meeting took place in a dingy basement bar off the lower east side of Manhattan, away from prying eyes. Over cheap whiskey and half-remembered laughs, the team went over their mission plan. As they spoke, their voices dropped to hushed tones, barely audible over the thump of hip-hop music.
