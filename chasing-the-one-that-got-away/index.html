Chasing the One That Got Away


The rain had begun to fall heavily, the kind of storm that made the streets glisten under the glow of streetlights, reflecting the steady rhythm of tires on wet pavement. Michael stood at the edge of the sidewalk, watching the world blur by. He wasn’t in a rush; his footsteps were slow, deliberate, as if he were pausing at the edge of a precipice—unsure whether to take the leap or walk away. In his mind, he was chasing something, but it was no longer clear whether it was a person or a memory. Perhaps it was both.

Michael hadn’t always been the type to chase after things. He was a planner, meticulous and precise in his choices. But this, this was different. It was the pull of something intangible, something he could almost touch, but never quite grasp.

Her name was Ava. They’d met three years ago at a coffee shop on a crisp autumn morning, both reaching for the last blueberry muffin at the same time. Their hands had brushed, and a spark had ignited, something neither of them had anticipated. It was the kind of encounter that felt like destiny, a moment that felt preordained. It was all too perfect, too easy. They spent the morning talking about everything and nothing—books, films, travel, music—but most of all, they talked about dreams.

Ava had a way of speaking about her future, her ambitions, as though they were already in the process of being realized. Michael was captivated by her optimism, her belief that anything was possible. She was different from anyone he had ever met. She made him feel alive in a way that was foreign, exciting.

But life, as it always does, had a way of complicating things. Weeks turned into months, and as the relationship deepened, so did the subtle cracks. Ava had dreams—big ones. And Michael? He had a life that needed maintaining, a job that demanded his attention, friends who expected him to be present. It wasn’t that he didn’t love her; he did. But he had a way of retreating into the comfort of routine, of stability. Ava, on the other hand, was chasing something else—a life that had no boundaries, no expectations. And eventually, their paths diverged.

The break-up had come without warning. One evening, she sat him down on the couch in their small apartment, her eyes bright and determined.

“I think we’re holding each other back, Michael,” she had said, her voice barely above a whisper.

He didn’t understand. He still doesn’t, not completely.

That was the first time he had felt that feeling—the one that tugged at the edges of his heart whenever he thought about her. He had tried to let go, tried to convince himself that it was for the best. But the truth was, he had never really stopped thinking about her.

Over the next few months, Michael tried to move on. He dated other women, kept busy with work, and spent time with friends, but nothing compared to the way Ava had made him feel. There was a piece of him that remained frozen in time, caught between the past and the future, never able to move forward.

And then, just when he thought he was starting to find peace, she appeared again.

It was a random encounter, or so it seemed. He was standing in line at the bookstore, thumbing through a novel he hadn’t read in years, when he heard a familiar voice behind him.

“Michael?”

He turned, his heart skipping a beat. There she was, standing a few feet away, just as beautiful as ever, though there was something different in her eyes—something distant, like a part of her had been lost to time.

“Ava,” he said, his voice thick with emotion. “I didn’t expect to see you.”

Her lips curled into a soft smile, and for a moment, it was like nothing had changed. But the silence that followed felt heavier than it should have.

“I’ve been meaning to call,” she said, her voice tentative. “I’ve been thinking a lot about… about us.”

The words hung in the air, suspended between them, and Michael couldn’t help but wonder if this was his chance. Was this the moment he had been waiting for? Was she finally going to tell him she had made a mistake, that they were meant to be together after all?

But instead, Ava continued.

“I’ve changed, Michael,” she said, her voice almost apologetic. “I’m not the same person I was when we were together. I’ve been traveling, moving around, trying to figure out who I am without anyone else’s expectations.”

Michael nodded, a lump forming in his throat. He knew exactly what she meant. He had tried to figure out who he was too, but it had never been as simple as shedding old layers. For him, the world wasn’t something to be chased; it was something to be conquered, one step at a time.

“I understand,” he said, his words soft, but with a finality he hadn’t intended. “I hope you find what you’re looking for.”

Ava smiled again, though it didn’t reach her eyes. “I think I already have.”

And with that, she was gone—vanishing into the crowd, just like she had the first time, leaving Michael standing there, holding onto the memory of something that had never truly belonged to him.

For days after, Michael couldn’t stop thinking about their conversation. What did she mean? Was this really it? Was there still hope for them, or was she finally free of whatever ties had bound her to him?

He found himself asking questions that had no answers, replaying every moment of their relationship over and over, trying to pinpoint where things had gone wrong. But the more he thought about it, the more he realized something.

It wasn’t just Ava he was chasing—it was the version of himself that had existed with her. The carefree, hopeful man who believed anything was possible. The one who didn’t worry about the future or cling to the safety of routine. He had lost that person somewhere along the way, and now, in the wake of her absence, he was trying to find his way back to him.

The days turned into weeks, and Michael found himself taking a different approach to life. He quit his job, moved to a new city, and began pursuing things he had once only dreamed of. He took up painting, something he’d always wanted to try but never had the time for. He started traveling, exploring new places, meeting new people. It wasn’t easy, and there were moments of doubt—moments when the loneliness crept back in and he missed Ava more than he cared to admit.

But slowly, something began to change. The more he embraced the uncertainty of life, the more he began to feel the stirrings of something new, something exciting. And though Ava was still in his thoughts, he found himself less obsessed with her memory. He had learned something important through the pain of their separation—that life doesn’t wait for you to be ready. It doesn’t pause to let you catch up. You have to chase it, or it slips away.

And so, Michael kept chasing. But this time, it was no longer the elusive figure of Ava that drove him—it was the future, the endless possibilities that lay ahead. He had learned that sometimes, the one that got away isn’t a person at all. It’s a version of yourself that you’ve lost along the way, and the only way to get it back is to keep moving forward, one step at a time.
