Stellar Solitude: Tales from the Last Space Station


523 days had passed since the crew of Aurora's Hope had abandoned Earth in a desperate bid to preserve humanity's legacy in the face of catastrophic climate change and societal collapse. Now, the last space station in the Sol system floated alone, a beacon of hope amidst the vast expanse of space. The crew of six had set a new course, leaving behind the ruins of a war-torn Earth. Their mission was to establish a generation ship, and from there, expand humanity's presence into the stars. Commander Sarah Patel's Log Entry - Day 650 The routine never ends. Hull inspections, life support checks, farming, and research. We're treading water, but it's essential to see if there's a chance of reclaiming the planet. Still no signals from Earth. It's like we're the last ones left. [^1] Commander Patel stood on the station's central hub, surveying the empty decks. Their crew quarters and communal areas were spartan but efficient. After a decade of this work, she couldn't shake the feeling of isolation. With the annual schedule piling up, Patel's gaze drifted toward the Georgia-shaped hangar where the crew's personal spacecraft, scavenged from old military ships, and the four cramped cryopods for future expansion awaited transformation into fully-fledged habitats. "Patel to engineering," Patel hailed the intercom.
