As she soared above the rolling hills and vast skies, Lady Kaida's majestic dragon, Khara, responded effortlessly to her every command. However, beneath the radiant surface of Kaida's world, a cauldron of tensions simmered. Kemmir, a young and ambitious rebel leader, seethed with resentment towards the established order. He and his cohorts harbored a vow to shatter the intricate web of alliances built upon dragon riding, deeming it an outdated relic shackling humans to the mercy of their regal companions. The seeds of discord had been sown, and the once-strong bond between humans and dragons began to fray. It was then that Kaida realized the true nature of the threat she faced. She had always known that Kemmir's rebellion was a ticking time bomb, but she had never imagined that it would reach the very heart of her people. The betrayal cut deep, and for the first time in her life, Kaida felt a pang of doubt. As the dust settled, and the chaos subsided, Kaida stood before her people, her voice trembling with emotion. She spoke of loyalty, of duty, and of the sacred bond that existed between humans and dragons. But it was too late. The damage had been done, and the thread of trust had been severed. The world of dragon riding was forever changed, and Kaida's people began to forge a new path, one that would lead them down a road of uncertainty and strife. The last dragon rider, once revered and respected, now stood alone, a symbol of a bygone era, a reminder of the loyalty and betrayal that had forever altered the course of their world. In the end, it was not the dragons that Kaida had fought to protect, but the very people she had sworn to serve. It was a bitter truth, one that she would carry with her for the rest of her days.
