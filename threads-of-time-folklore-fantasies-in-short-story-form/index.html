Threads of Time: Folklore Fantasies in Short Story Form


In the quaint village of Kalkin, nestled between the mountains and the deep sea, people lived in harmony with nature. They relied on the threads of time to keep their traditions alive, narrating folklores that had been passed down through generations.

Ela, the village weaver, embarked on the most significant task of her life. Your average weaver wouldn’t dare venture into such a splendid quest. The village’s well-being, dependent on their looms, relied on her senses and expertise. Tonight was different - Ela believed that her love for weaving would be impervious to anything, even time itself.

Ela worked tirelessly, weaving a tapestry with a daunting request written on it. ‘The Threads of Time.’ Her grandmother always said stitch by stitch, one dreams are woven into existence. For Ela, she wanted to bring the village’s fragmented tales of the past into a beautiful whole. The folklore’s magic seemed distant, destined for only a skilled weaver-craftswoman.

Ela’s fingers danced across the strings of time, and her hands breathed life into the wonderful narratives of Kalkin. The tapestry captured the village’s folklore well - how Radea, the weaving maiden, unraveled the time threads to save the village from the clammy grasp of a lurking demon.

Her heart heavy, Ela wove Radea into the tapestry; she bore the same name. The resemblance stirred unease within her that Kalkin needed saving from sinister forces. Still, the timeline of their fates was different, as Ela lived during the present era. A chilly reminder wound around her heart that perhaps the cycle will always exist; a weaving maiden must unravel fate to maintain order in Kalkin’s existence.

She segregated herself into a realm of guilt-ridden motifs and creaky looms. The dull neighbors with their lame depictions paused in spectral whispers. Threads woven into the past, present, and future consumed her spirit. There will be one miraculous instance that breaks the cycle.

She touched the broomstick of a dusty loom that had seen a thousand sunsets; she suddenly sighed, her gaze wandering toward the silky threads waiting to be stitched on the canvas – dark threads.

Ela watched, awestruck, as the very fabric of time seemed to twist and turn around her. Each dark thread represented a negative force trying to strangle the village’s pure energy.

Realizing her duties, she decided to introduce her ancestor into the tapestry. Corrupted by dark threads only existed in legend, but her heart knew otherwise. Ela wove with cautious hands, fierceness in her loom-stained fingers, for she painted the portrait of an ancestor protecting the girl Radea. Threads harmoniously exchanged their hue and consistency.

As the tapestry neared completion, Ela’s inheritance surged through her face, her arms, and finally into the threads she steered between her hands. Radea and her ancestor’s eyes locked together, becoming intrinsically bound.

Ela completed the tapestry, hoping to avert the predicted calamity. Eyes brimming with pride, she then let the final thread slip through her trembling fingers. The woven stories silently etched on tapestry frames stitched subtle and potent magic in its embrace.

Inexorable time surged forth, accosting the village, absorbed within Ela’s tapestry. An unpredictable song of weft and warp embedded within the threads sang a melody ancient and timeless.

As desired, the threads of time were smoothed out, the tapestry started unraveling, and anomalies undulated within Kalkin’s sphere. The demon of time melted into nada: the void. Their village distanced itself from unpredictability, reshaping its future, returning to the celestial and mortals’ balance.

Ela raised the loom toward the light, where last ribbons of dark hues disappeared, leaving behind a new dawn. Time had been trouble-free forever from the first delicate seed of creation until the present.
