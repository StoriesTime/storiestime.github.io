When Heroes Rise: Stories of Unyielding Altruism


In the midst of a global economic downturn, a small town in the American heartland struggled to stay afloat. Pennieville was once a thriving community, united by the values of hard work and self-reliance. However, when the town's largest employer, the steel mill, closed its doors, the ripple effect was felt throughout the local economy. As families struggled to make ends meet, 35-year-old Alex Jonas found himself facing a crisis of his own. A former steelworker, Alex had lost his job alongside hundreds of others and was left with no insurance to cover his wife's expensive treatments for her MS. As the town's morale began to plummet, a soup kitchen started as an informal arrangement among friends and family soon turned into a volunteer-driven endeavor. Alex's sister, Mayor Sarah, called upon her old friend Rachel Eve, a nursing professional, to join forces to create a tight-knit group of volunteers for the soup kitchen. Long-time Penneville residents Dustin Switzer and Alice Davidson joined the team, carrying the passion to service from their school souls, afterward further galvanizing the joined team.
