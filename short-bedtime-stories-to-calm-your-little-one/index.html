As they swam, Celeste explained, “The ocean and the sky are always connected. When the stars twinkle, they send their light down to us. And we, in turn, send our glow back up to them.”

Luna watched in wonder as schools of glowing fish swam around them, their bodies shimmering like stars. “It’s like we’re part of the stars,” Luna said, her voice filled with awe.

Celeste nodded. “Yes, Luna. The stars and the sea are always in harmony, each reflecting the beauty of the other. Just as you are part of this world, the world is part of you.”

As the two swam through the glowing waters, Luna felt a deep sense of peace. She realized that even though she couldn’t reach the stars, their light was always with her, guiding her through the dark ocean waters.

When it was time to return to her rock, Luna felt different. She no longer felt lonely or afraid of the dark. The ocean, with its sparkling creatures and endless beauty, was a place full of magic—just like the stars above.

“Thank you, Celeste,” Luna said, her heart full of gratitude.

Celeste’s light pulsed brightly, like a gentle hug. “Remember, Luna, you are never alone. The stars are always watching over you.”

And with that, Luna drifted into a peaceful sleep, knowing that the light of the stars would always be with her, guiding her through every night.

As the ocean waves whispered softly, “Goodnight, Luna. Sleep tight.”

Both Ollie and Luna found comfort in the wonders of the night. No matter where they were, whether it was the forest or the ocean, they discovered that the dark held its own kind of magic. It was not something to fear but to embrace, knowing that there was always something beautiful just waiting to be discovered. And with that, both Ollie and Luna drifted into peaceful dreams, filled with the light of the stars.
