It was a typical Monday morning at the European Space Agency's (ESA) mission control center in Darmstadt, Germany. Astronauts, engineers, and scientists were busily preparing for the upcoming "Aurora" space mission to Mars. Little did they know, their work would soon be hijacked by a strange and intriguing phenomenon - cosmic echoes. The story began in the year 1997, when a spacecraft carrying two astronauts, Jack and Sarah, disappeared during a routine mission to Mars. All contact was lost, and the spacecraft was presumed lost forever in the vast expanse of space. Or so they thought. During a routine astronomy observation of the Mars orbit, a strange radio signal was picked up by the ESA's monitoring team. The signal seemed to be a faint, human-like voice, calling out for assistance in Mathematics and Physics. The team was baffled, thinking it might be a malfunction or even a joke from a prankster. However, as the signal continued to transmit, the team realized it was something more extraordinary.
