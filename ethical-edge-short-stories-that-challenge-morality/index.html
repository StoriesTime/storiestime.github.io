Ethical Edge: Short Stories that Challenge Morality


Once upon a time, in the quaint, idyllic town of Millfield, a peculiar event rocked the serene community. Wreath-making, an annual charity event spearheaded by the local elderly society, had led to an unexpected dilemma. Over thirty unique and exquisite wreaths were submitted, each tied with a tag bearing the wreath-maker’s name.

As the townspeople voted on their favorite wreath, a curious and subtle design caught the eye of a local school teacher named Alice. Alice, known for her strict adherence to both the letter and spirit of the law, found that the winning wreath seemed wholesomely, charmingly crafted by Mrs. Larkins, an elderly woman residing in the community’s nursing home. However, upon examining the contestants’ list, Alice discovered one name missing—the destination for the awarded money.

Alice, who had always put emphasis on fairness and transparency, couldn’t shake the feeling that something was amiss. As she tried to rest her troubled conscience, she received a mysterious call. From the voice of someone she could not place, she was urged to keep digging. The mysterious caller mentioned someone named “Mr. Sinclaire,” leaving Alice thoroughly perplexed.

With mounting curiosity, Alice embarked on a delivery run to Mrs. Larkins’s nursing home. Before presenting her concerns, she decided to discreetly chat with one of the nursing-home employees. Much to her surprise, the employee revealed that another resident, Mrs. Merit, had made a wreath identical to Mrs. Larkins’s. This revelation led Alice to uncover the secret partnership between the two women.

Alice was conflicted; on one hand, she saw two residents leveraging their skills to create something beautiful. On the other hand, the fairness of the contest seemed compromised. The next day, Alice found herself at the Millfield town hall, face-to-face with the elderly society. With her suspicions heard, a heated debate ensued as the voting results were voided and the case forwarded to the town’s council.

Over the town council’s deliberation, Alice found herself reflecting on her original belief in fair play. Were the actions of Mrs. Larkins and Mrs. Merit deserving of praise or was a contest’s integrity more significant? Meanwhile, the council members debated. An elderly member, old enough to remember the early days of Millfield, intervened and subtly reminded them of the essence of community in Millfield.

Ultimately, they decided to organize a special viewing, allowing the entire town to “vote” on their favorite wreath. As the supporters of both Mrs. Larkins and Mrs. Merit passionately lobbied on the two women’s behalf, the entire town gathered to deliberate.

On the day of the event, the square swarmed with anticipation. It was one of those rare moments when Millfield’s air seemed pregnant with emotion. Families debated, friends discussed and, children chimed in their favorite. By the evening, as the final ballots were cast, a clear victor emerged: Millfield’s community spirit.

Where Alice once saw a moral quandary, she now understood that Millfield’s true essence was its unity. The town prospered together, watched over by the muted contestants and shared joys and hardships as friends and neighbors. In the end, waging a battle for a wreath contest seemed insignificant. The underlying consideration – to recognize and promote the spirit of community – suddenly eclipsed everything else.

And with that, Ethical Edge shifted, once more, as the watchful town then looked forward to the next adventure in Millfield’s ever-engaging moral journey.
