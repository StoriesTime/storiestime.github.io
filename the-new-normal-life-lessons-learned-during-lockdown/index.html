The New Normal: Life Lessons Learned During Lockdown


As I sit here in my cozy home office, surrounded by the familiar walls that have become my sanctuary during this unprecedented time of global crisis, I reflect on the past year of my life. The COVID-19 pandemic brought the world to a standstill, forcing us to reexamine our priorities, lifestyles, and relationships. For me, the experience has been transformative – an opportunity to rediscover myself and the people around me in ways I never thought possible. The first few weeks of lockdown were overwhelming. The eerie silence, the deserted streets, and the constant stream of breaking news updates created a sense of unease that was difficult to shake off. As governments issued stay-at-home orders, I felt like I was trapped in a never-ending nightmare. My job allowed me to work from home, but that meant I had to navigate the challenges of balancing work and family responsibilities while also trying to stay healthy and entertained. Learning to Prioritize As the days blurred together, I began to realize that my pre-pandemic life had become a hit-or-miss mix of constant activity, exhaustion, and distraction. I was always "on the go" – juggling work, family obligations, and social commitments – leaving little room for self-reflection and genuine connection. Lockdown proved to be a harsh teacher, forcing me to slow down and recalibrate my priorities. Paying Attention to Mental Health I made a conscious effort to focus on my mental well-being during this time. Long walks, yoga, and meditation became my daily rituals, allowing me to clear my mind, prioritize my thoughts, and cultivate a sense of calm. I learned to be kinder to myself, acknowledging that it was okay to feel anxious, scared, or overwhelmed, and that those emotions were valid and normal. Finding Joy in the Mundane One of the most unexpected outcomes of lockdown was discovering the joy in everyday activities. Simple pleasures like baking, gardening, and cooking became a source of comfort and delight. I substituted reading books and cooking shows for my team's in-person interactions, and, to my surprise, I found joy in the mundane. My evenings were filled with creative endeavors like journaling, painting, and knitting – activities that brought me peace and allowed me to connect with my inner self. Re Evaluating Relationships Lockdown gave me the opportunity to reconnect with friends and family in meaningful ways. We started virtual game nights, book clubs, and movie marathons, creating space for deeper conversations and a sense of community. I also began to appreciate the people who typically take care of me, particularly my partner and our young children. Their resilience, adaptability, and determination inspired me to become more present, patient, and grateful in our daily lives together. Becoming a Homegrown Entrepreneur One of the unexpected outcomes of this experience was learning a new skill – photography. With street photography no longer an option, I turned to capturing life from the comfort of my own home. Using my smartphone as a camera, I started taking portraits of my loved ones, documenting daily life, and observing the beauty in the mundane. This creative avenue has allowed me to tap into my creative side and appreciate the beauty in the everyday. Accepting the New Normal Over the past year, I have come to understand that the pandemic has become a new normal – a reality that we have to learn to adapt to. It has forced me to confront the impermanence of our lives, teaching me the importance of flexibility, gratitude, and resilience. I have learned to appreciate the simple joys in life – spending quality time with loved ones, savoring the beauty of nature, and creating art that brings me joy. As a result of the pandemic, I have discovered different aspects of myself: a more patient and selfless person, a creative and resourceful one, and a person more connected to others. I've rediscovered the love for adventure and the joy of learning new things. In spite of the difficulties, I feel hopeful about the future. This new normal has taught me to appreciate the simple pleasures in life and to live in the moment.
