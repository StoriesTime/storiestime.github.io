In Her Shoes: Powerful Narratives of Gender and Sexuality


Here lies the story of Isla, a young woman confidently walking in the path she had chosen for herself. The winding road of her existence showcased the strength, courage, and resilience that resides within us all.

Growing up as a tomboy in a rather traditional household, Isla often struggled to showcase her true self. Her interests and hobbies leaned more towards those typically associated with her male counterparts, and her athletic abilities would often have her joining in on their games. Her parents, well-intentioned but misguided, always sought to mold her into the perfect woman. Naturally, this led to many conflicts between the two parties. It was when Isla discovered her extraordinary spatial awareness and uncanny ability to solve complex puzzles that she began to truly understand her unique gift.

Isla had always had a knack for design, which stemmed from her childhood memories of rearranging furniture to maximize space and efficiency within her room. As she grew older, she began applying these skills to a newfound passion: architecture. A newfound love for sustainable design would soon become the central focus of her career.

The winding road of Isla’s existence led her to a prestigious architecture firm where she worked alongside some of the brightest minds in the industry. Her confidence grew with each expressed idea and design, but her true passion and fulfillment came from the challenge of reconciling ecological concerns with the human need for progress and development.

The firm’s latest project, a mixed-use urban development, was at the cutting edge of sustainable design. Isla iteratively designed a pedestrian-centered urban space – a village within the city. This design would encourage people to connect with one another, nature, and ultimately, the heart of the community. She meticulously crafted this space, paying much attention to the small details that many others might overlook. Each element was infused with a unique charm and an intuitive understanding of human nature, seamlessly blending together to form a design that would captivate and inspire its users.

However, this urban village required more funds to come to fruition, and for months Isla struggled to acquire these resources. Convinced of her vision, she refused to compromise on the quality or integrity of the project. Her determination wavered as the financial uncertainty weighed heavily on her shoulders. At this crucial juncture, an organization that championed innovative and sustainable design offered relief in the form of financial backing.

Isla’s urban village transformed into an incubator for development – an innovative, eco-friendly, and sustainable blueprint for the world. With each milestone, she felt her identity become further intertwined with the beauty of her designs. Not only did the spaces she shaped allow for human interaction and connection, but they also embodied her unique presence and the fluidity of her identity.

Through her work, Isla illuminated the importance of recognizing and supporting the talents of people whose identities do not conform to societal norms. Her story serves as a testament to the complexities and contradictions inherent in each of us, fostering the belief that each of us is capable of extraordinary accomplishments.
