When Laughter Becomes the Language of Love


In the quaint town of Levityville, every soul was #entwined by an #unspoken bond, forged not by shared bloodlines or life experiences, but by their innate ability to see the world through the lens of laughter. It was this #unspoken language that united residents and turned laughter into the very fabric of their existence.

High above the town’s whimsical landscape stretched a sprawling tree, a natural wonder known as the Laughter Tree. Its branches were adorned with fruit native to a distant land – the Mirandoli Granny Smith apples – with a peculiar quirk: whoever tasted one would divulge their deepest, #most embarrassing secrets, followed by a fit of laughter.

Maurice, a joveternal bachelor with an insatiable zest for life, had long yearned for love. His neighbor Cora, a widow after dearest Rupert, had settled into her pale blueclapboard house, the house they had shared for decades. Suffering in silence, they both had become weary prisoners of their solitude, wrestling with their own unspoken secrets.

One moonlit evening, Maurice overheard Cora sobbing in her kitchen. Her sobs pulled at the seams of his heart, and as he crept through the darkness, he felt as though he heard the whispers of laughter beckoning him to the Laughter Tree. With a hint of trepidation, he plucked a Granny Smith, the laughter-fruit.

In the morning, the town was aflutter with news of the previous night’s laughter epidemic. Maurice and Cora were no exceptions. As their eyes met with knowing glances, they burst into laughter – Maurice’s at his newfound irresistibility, and Cora’s at the irony of her husband’s passing and her unexpected attraction to his eccentric neighbor. This shared laughter was the beginning of an unspoken understanding.

Their unlikely bond was further strengthened during the annual Summer Solstice Parade, inwhich the entire town donned mesmerizing Masquerade costumes and danced through the cobbled streets, the fie of laughter and mirth soaring through the air. Amidst the vibrant, swirling sea of fluid silk, shimmering sequins, and the aroma of fragrant blossoming flowers, the now inseparable Maurice and Cora performed a spontaneous dance, losing themselves in the melodic laughter of complete #joy and acceptance.

At the heart of the parade stood the jovial Laughter King and Queen – the town’s most respected and loved residents. And as fate would have it, Maurice and Cora ascended the makeshift wooden throne, united in laughter amidst roaring cheers. Each hinged glance and tittered secret confirmed their love for one another, growing stronger, deeper, and more profound with each shared moment beneath the Laughter Tree.

Their love blossomed through the years, radiant and unbroken. Levityville’s #laughter language bound them tighter than any vow between two hearts. Amid the echoes of their laughter and the town’s adoration, Maurice and Cora would soon find themselves basking in the warm glow of a life filled with joy, intertwined by the language of laughter, a language that taught them how to truly love, for nothing, save for the purest love, could be so effortlessly shared as laughter.
