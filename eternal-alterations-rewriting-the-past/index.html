Eternal Alterations: Rewriting the Past


In the quiet village of Marnswick, nestled between luscious green hills and towering elmwoods, a great secret lay veiled, much like the mysterious wisteria creeping up the sides of the stone cottages that dotted the hamlet.

It was on a fine, warm day in the summer of 1854 that a woman named Nadia came into the village. Her appearance was peculiar; a vibrant crimson cloak draped elegantly over her well-kempt figure, and a hat adorned with the most peculiar of flowers, the deep blue cornflower, which scented the air with its sweet fragrance.

Nadia moved with the grace of a dancer, her azure eyes drawing the attention wherever she stepped in the village square. She was undeniably alluring and commanded respect, not out of awe, but by virtue of her sheer presence.

Nadia’s objective was inconspicuous, however, only a few were aware of her true purpose - to become their scribe and forever change the course of Marnswick’s history.

In the afternoon sun, Nadia visited the village elder, Marcus, who welcomed her with a weary smile. Rumor had it, the man had witnessed 37 winters, and his eyes, once a vibrant blue, had clouded like the ocean on a stormy day.

“Tell me, Master Marcus,” Nadia requested with an enchanting fervor, “How came your ancestors to Marnswick? What trials befell them? What joys did they partake in?”

Marcus pondered her questions, his brows furrowed in lingering contemplation. For years he’d told the tale, yet now it seemed all-too ordinary. “Ah, I suppose it matters not whether I speak of it once more,” he sighed, and began the narrative. As the story unfolded, Nadia scribbled away, drawing from lost memories and forgotten emotions, she documented the tale with a fervid determination.

Accordion pleats of her skirt rustled delicately as she moved to perch upon Marcus’s wooden chair, with the reddish sunlight of the waning day painting her cloak in hues of orange and gold. Her hands moved rhythmically as words spilled from her lips, coaxing restless memories to life before her quill.

“Many, many eons ago,” Marcus intoned, “Our ancestors, brave souls, ventured forth from the grand city of Eldertree-"

“A city of prosperity and culture!” Nadia jotted, pen aloft. “Tell me more”.

Marcus inhaled deeply and continued, “Yes, indeed. Eldertree. They came seeking a new life, corn to sow, rivers to fish from, and families to nourish. It was on the 12th of June, further long ago than anyone save myself can remember, that they first set foot in what we now call Marnswick.”

As Marcus came to the conclusion of the tale, Nadia paused, her eyes focused and an unreadable expression on her luminous features.

“Allow me, Master Marcus," she began, her voice tinged with a warm humor, “to rewrite the story you’ve told a thousand times."

Marcus looked at her skeptically and betrayed a hint of annoyance. “There is nothing to rewrite.”

“There might not be,” Nadia replied with an unmistakable glimmer in her eyes, “but change is ever in the winds, and with it, falls the possibility of eternal alterations.”

She took a deep breath and began anew, pen upon parchment: “In the grand city of Eldertree, where prosperity held hands with culture, resided a people profoundly unsatisfied with the life they had. Incessantly, discontent whispered within them, and in the silence of the night, their hearts yearned to be liberated.”

As Marcus listened, astonishment flooded his weathered features. Nadia’s words transformed his chronicles, but no words could explain the metamorphosis taking place. Under her influence, the village’s history was reshaped; no fact remained untouched by her omnipotent influence.

“Might I offer a revision to our collection, Master Marcus,” Nadia finished, raising her head from the parchment. “One which depicts your ancestors treasuring the journey they undertook to discover Marnswick rather than merely seeking leftovers of what they left behind?”

And as she spoke, the ancestors’ fervor was palpable, echoing in the air like the soft rustle of elm leaves under the caress of a sultry summer breeze.

Marcus only nodded, unable to do otherwise, and Nadia knew that her task was finally complete.

The crimson cloak draped on her shoulders seemed to grow heavier, weighing her down with secrets she’d become privy to. It felt strangely suffocating, intoxicating, and oddly indescribable, yet oh, so powerful. Nadia resolved to never let a single page of history fall from her grips, for she, the Scribe of Marnswick, held the power to rewrite the past.

As the sun dipped beneath the horizon, whispering the secrets of the day to her twin stars above, and as the shadows of the elmwoods danced, embraced by the hushed winds, Marnswick remained forever altered – unscathed yet changed forever within the passage of time and the will of the Scribe who dared to rewrite its history.

Eternal alterations, indeed.
