Bad Date Chronicles: Tales of Romantic Disaster and Laughter


It was one of those days where nothing was going according to plan. Sarah had swiped left on her phone for what felt like hours, half-heartedly swiping through profiles that promised ‘adventurous souls’ and ‘deep intellectual connections.’ She wasn’t even sure what she was looking for anymore. After a series of wildly unsuccessful online dating attempts, she was starting to wonder if perhaps true love was overrated. But then, just as she was about to close the app for the night, she saw him: Jake.

His profile was refreshingly different. Not a single picture with a fish or flexed muscles, just a clean photo of him with a slight smile in a coffee shop, holding a book. The bio was simple, something like: “Life’s too short for small talk, so let’s skip to the good stuff.” She smiled at the honesty, thinking this could be the one. She swiped right. He matched her within seconds. And just like that, they were chatting.

It was an easy conversation. He seemed genuinely cool. Their chats bounced from random topics to deep, philosophical debates about the meaning of life. After a couple of weeks, Sarah agreed to meet him for dinner. Nothing fancy, just a casual Italian place with good reviews. She figured that if he was as funny and charming in person as he was online, this could actually be something special.

The First Red Flag

Sarah walked into the restaurant at exactly 7:00 PM, feeling the usual nervous energy mixed with excitement. She was early, so she took a seat at the bar and ordered a glass of wine, hoping to calm her nerves. As she scanned the room, she spotted Jake at a table near the back. He was pacing. He was tall, wore a perfectly tailored blazer, and looked… confused. She waved.

He spotted her immediately and rushed over, his face flushed.

“Hey, Sarah! Sorry, I’m like, five minutes late. Traffic, you know?” He explained as he pulled out the chair across from her, knocking a glass of water off the table in the process.

“Classic,” she laughed, wiping the table off with a napkin. “It’s okay. I’ve been here for, like, five minutes anyway. How’s your day been?”

“Crazy, honestly. But mostly good. Just, uh, trying to figure out if my ex will show up at this restaurant tonight. She’s been texting me non-stop. So… yeah, we’re off to a great start,” Jake said, a bit too quickly.

Sarah froze, blinking. Did he just mention his ex?

“Wait, what?” she asked, trying to process it. “Your ex? You… invited her here too?”

“No! No, not invited. She’s just a little clingy, I guess. She has a habit of showing up wherever I am.”

“That sounds… fun,” Sarah muttered, raising an eyebrow. Was this real?

Jake seemed to sense her discomfort and tried to smooth things over, rambling about how he’d been trying to cut things off with his ex for months. He even started showing her his phone to “prove” it, holding up a series of increasingly bizarre text messages that felt more like a cry for attention than anything else.

Just as she thought she could escape, the door opened, and Sarah saw a woman in a stunning red dress step inside. She was tall, with a confident stride and eyes that were clearly scanning the room.

Sarah’s stomach dropped. This couldn’t be happening.

“Is that…?” she started.

Before she could finish her sentence, Jake was already up, waving to the woman, and calling her by name. “Lena! Over here!” he yelled, completely oblivious to the awkwardness.

The Battle for the Breadsticks

Lena approached their table, shooting Sarah a curious glance. Jake quickly stood up and introduced them. “Lena, this is Sarah. Sarah, this is my ex, Lena. We’ve been trying to stay friends. It’s, uh, working—mostly.”

Sarah smiled tightly. “Nice to meet you,” she said, forcing the words out. She had no idea what to do with this situation. She wasn’t a drama queen, but this was something else.

Lena gave her a polite nod and then, to Sarah’s complete horror, took the seat next to Jake. “Is it cool if I join you guys? I just came for the breadsticks. I mean, they’re amazing, right?”

Sarah blinked twice, trying to hold it together. “Oh, sure… breadsticks. Yeah, they’re great.”

The waiter, who had clearly seen worse in his day, walked up with menus. “Can I take your order?” he asked, looking between the three of them.

Jake leaned back in his chair. “You know what, I’ll just have the usual. You guys decide. Lena, you want to share a pizza?”

Lena, without missing a beat, replied, “Actually, I’ll just have a salad, thanks. I’m watching my carbs.”

Sarah’s head spun. Watching carbs? This was officially the worst date of her life. But the night was young, and so, apparently, was her patience.

The Unexpected Twist

The next ten minutes went by in a haze of awkward chatter and breadstick dunking. Sarah barely spoke. She could only focus on the absurdity of the situation. At one point, Lena excused herself to go to the bathroom, and Jake turned to Sarah, completely oblivious to the tension.

“So, what do you think of Lena?” he asked, as though the entire situation hadn’t been the most bizarre thing she’d ever experienced. “Isn’t she amazing? She’s a little intense, but she’s always been like that. Keeps me on my toes.”

Sarah wanted to scream, but instead, she simply forced a smile. “She’s… definitely something. You two are very charming together.”

Before Jake could respond, Lena returned, her phone in hand. “You guys are not going to believe this,” she said, sitting down and swiping through her phone. “I just found out my ex is in town, and he’s having dinner right across the street at that new sushi place. Should I go over and say hi?”

Jake laughed nervously. “I mean, you do you. I think we’re good here.” He motioned to Sarah, as though everything was perfectly fine.

Lena smiled brightly. “I think I’ll pop over there for a bit. Sarah, you cool here by yourself?”

Sarah’s mind was a whirlpool of thoughts. This couldn’t be happening. Was Lena actually going to leave them alone? Was she… seriously okay with this?

But before Sarah could process it, Lena was up, walking out the door and waving goodbye.

The Post-Dinner Therapy Session

With Lena gone, the mood between Sarah and Jake shifted awkwardly. The next thirty minutes were a struggle of half-hearted conversation and an awkward silence that seemed to stretch out far too long.

Finally, after the awkward lull, Jake leaned forward, his voice taking on a more serious tone. “So, you still feeling it? You know, between us?”

Sarah stared at him blankly. “Feeling what?” she asked, more exasperated than she intended.

He tilted his head, confused. “You know, the connection. Like, we have chemistry, right? This could really go somewhere.”

“Jake, no,” Sarah said, standing up quickly. “I don’t think so. I don’t even know who you are anymore.”

Jake blinked, a slight look of panic crossing his face. “What do you mean? I thought we had something great here.”

She grabbed her bag and stood up. “I came for a date, not a therapy session with your ex. I don’t have time for this.”

With that, she walked out of the restaurant, feeling a rush of relief. The cool night air hit her face as she made her way down the street, laughing at the absurdity of it all. In a weird way, she felt freer than she had in weeks. As she pulled out her phone to send a message to her best friend, she couldn’t help but smile.

“Date from hell. Will explain tomorrow. But on the bright side, I got free breadsticks.”

The Aftermath

Sarah eventually learned that her disastrous date wasn’t an anomaly. She’d found herself in a string of hilarious (and sometimes outright painful) romantic misadventures, each one more bizarre than the last. But with every bad date, she learned something new about herself—and about how much she didn’t want to settle for anything less than the real deal.

She didn’t give up on love. She just started being pickier. And every time she recounted these stories to her friends, they couldn’t help but laugh—because, in the end, it wasn’t about the disaster, but the humor that came with it. Because sometimes, love was about laughing at the chaos and knowing that one day, it would all come together.
