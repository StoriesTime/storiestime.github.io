Echoes of the Forgotten: Supernatural Tales of the Unseen


The winds howled through the dense forest, carrying with it a chilling sense of something unseen, something forgotten. Night had fallen quickly in the remote village of Greystone Hollow, leaving its residents with no choice but to retreat into the safety of their homes. But it wasn’t just the winds that stirred unease. The people of Greystone Hollow whispered about strange happenings in the forest, tales passed down through generations—stories of the supernatural, of entities that existed just beyond the veil of the visible world.

In the heart of the village stood an ancient church, its steeple piercing the sky, long abandoned but still standing with a quiet dignity. No one dared enter it. Not anymore. Not since the day the priest disappeared. His name was Father Alden, a man who had been the guiding light of the village for years. His sudden disappearance had left a gap, one that no one dared to fill.

But it wasn’t just the priest. It was the shadows that began to haunt the villagers’ dreams after that night. Whispers of unnatural sounds emanating from the church, flickers of figures seen at the corner of one’s eye—things that couldn’t be explained. They were echoes, echoes of something far older, far more powerful, and far more terrifying than any of them could have imagined.

Chapter 1: The Whispering Pines

Tess Harper stood at the edge of the forest, her boots crunching the frost-laden leaves beneath her feet. She had heard the stories all her life. Her grandmother, a woman of few words, often spoke of the forest’s cursed nature. “The pines whisper in the dark,” she would say, “and those who listen too closely never return.”

But Tess, a skeptic by nature, always dismissed the old woman’s tales as mere superstition. Now, however, she found herself here, standing on the precipice of the very place that had claimed the lives of so many before her. She wasn’t sure why she was drawn to the forest. There was a pull, a strange magnetism that she couldn’t explain. It had been a month since the strange occurrences began—a month since Father Alden vanished without a trace, and it seemed like the veil between the natural and supernatural had begun to thin.

“Be careful, Tess,” her brother, Marcus, had warned her before she left. “Don’t go into the woods. There’s something wrong about them, something… old.” He had seen it too, the shadows at the edge of the village, the faint flickers of movement between the trees. But Tess wasn’t afraid. Not of the forest. Not of what lingered in the dark.

As she stepped forward, the air grew heavier. It wasn’t the cold; it was something else—something oppressive. The towering pines seemed to stretch taller than usual, their twisted branches clawing at the sky like the fingers of a forgotten god. Tess could hear the faintest rustle, a low, almost inaudible murmur, coming from deep within the forest.

She pushed on, her curiosity outweighing her caution. The further she walked, the more the forest seemed to close in on her. The shadows deepened, and the whispers grew louder, though no one was there to speak. Her heart began to race. Something was wrong.

Suddenly, she stopped. A figure stood ahead of her, partially obscured by the trees, but it was clear enough to make her blood run cold. It was a man—no, not quite a man. His features were contorted, as if he were a twisted reflection of humanity. His eyes were black as coal, empty and hollow. His skin seemed pale and stretched tight, as though he hadn’t seen daylight in years.

Tess froze, her breath caught in her throat. She had heard stories of the Forest Watchers, ancient spirits said to guard the sacred ground of the forest. But this… this was no spirit. This was something else. Something worse.

The figure raised a hand, and Tess felt the pull once again, a draw deeper into the woods. A voice, soft but clear, echoed in her mind.

“Come closer, Tess. The echoes remember you.”

Before she could react, the figure vanished into the trees, as if he had never been there at all.

Her pulse hammered in her ears as she turned and fled, her heart racing with a mixture of fear and exhilaration. What had she just seen? What had the figure meant? The echoes remember you…

Chapter 2: The Church of Forgotten Souls

Tess returned to the village in a daze, the image of the figure burned into her mind. She barely spoke to anyone as she passed through the streets, heading straight for the old church. She had to know more. She had to understand what was happening, and why it seemed so personal.

The church stood on the outskirts of Greystone Hollow, its gray stone walls weathered by time and neglect. Once, it had been the heart of the village, a place of worship and solace. But now, it was nothing more than a hollow shell, its doors long since boarded up, its windows cracked and shattered.

Tess had never been inside. No one had. But tonight, the pull was too strong to ignore.

She pushed open the creaking door, which gave way with a reluctant groan. The air inside was thick with dust, and the faint smell of mildew lingered in the corners. The wooden pews, once neatly arranged, were now in disarray, and the altar stood silent, as if waiting for something—or someone—to return.

As she stepped further inside, her eyes were drawn to the back of the church, where the priest’s quarters lay. It was there, she was sure, that she would find the answers. Father Alden had spent his last days in that room, before he vanished without a trace.

The hallway leading to the priest’s room was narrow, and the walls seemed to close in on her as she walked. The whispers returned, louder now, like the faint murmur of voices just beyond comprehension. It was as if the very walls were speaking, recounting tales of long-forgotten souls trapped within the confines of the church.

She reached the door to the priest’s quarters and hesitated. The handle was cold beneath her touch, but it opened with ease. Inside, the room was empty, save for a single book resting on the desk. It was an old tome, its pages yellowed with age. The title was written in a language Tess didn’t recognize, but the symbols seemed to pulse with an unnatural energy.

As she picked it up, the room seemed to darken, the air growing thick with tension. The whispers grew louder, now a cacophony of voices, all speaking at once. They were angry, desperate, as if they were trapped within the very pages of the book.

Tess flipped through the pages, her fingers trembling, until she reached a drawing of the same figure she had seen in the forest. His eyes were black, his features twisted, and around him were symbols—arcane symbols that seemed to glow with a faint, eerie light.

The drawing was accompanied by a passage in the strange language, but there was one word Tess understood.

Echoes.

She slammed the book shut, but it was too late. The air grew cold, and the whispers grew deafening. The figure from the forest appeared once again, standing in the doorway, his hollow eyes fixed on her.

“Your blood has awakened the echoes, Tess,” the figure intoned, his voice a low, guttural growl. “You cannot escape them. You belong to the forgotten.”

Before Tess could react, the ground beneath her feet gave way, and she tumbled into darkness.

Chapter 3: The Forgotten Ones

Tess awoke in a place she did not recognize. It was an endless expanse of shadow, stretching out in all directions. The air was thick and oppressive, filled with the weight of forgotten memories. In the distance, she could hear faint echoes—voices of the long-dead, trapped in a limbo between worlds.

The figure was there, standing just out of reach. His hollow eyes fixed on her with an unsettling intensity.

“You are in the realm of the forgotten,” he said, his voice reverberating through the shadows. “This is where those who are lost to time are condemned to remain. And now, you too are one of us.”

Tess tried to move, but her legs felt like lead. The weight of the shadows pressed down on her, threatening to crush her beneath their weight. She tried to speak, but no words would come. She was trapped, a prisoner of the echoes, the forgotten.

“You have awakened the curse,” the figure continued. “The church, the forest—it was all part of the design. The echoes have waited for someone like you.”

Tess struggled against the shadows, her mind racing. She had to escape. She had to find a way back. There had to be something she could do.

Then, through the darkness, a faint light appeared. It was distant, but it was enough to give her hope. She focused on it, willing herself to move toward it. With each step, the darkness seemed to recoil, as if it were afraid of the light.

The figure watched her, his expression unreadable.

“You cannot run from your fate,” he warned. “The echoes are always watching. Always waiting.”

But Tess didn’t stop. She couldn’t. Not now.

As she moved closer to the light, the whispers began to fade, replaced by the sound of her own heartbeat. The shadows retreated, unable to hold her back any longer.

And as she reached the light, she stepped through the veil—into the world of the living.

But when Tess returned to Greystone Hollow, the village had changed. The air was heavy with a strange presence, and the whispers could still be heard, faintly echoing through the trees. It was as if the forgotten had not been left behind. They had followed her.

And now, the echoes were not just in the woods. They were in the village. They were in her mind.

The forgotten were waiting.
