Langley's wife, Sarah, took a deep breath before beginning her account. "I was at a charity event at the Hilton with my friends. We were supposed to return home around midnight. Jameson nodded, jotting down some notes. "Can anyone vouch for your alibi for the time period between 9 pm and midnight?" he asked, already knowing the answer was likely to be no. "No, we were all busy chatting and taking selfies with the other guests," Sarah replied. "I'm positive I was at the event the whole time, though. Jameson's focus shifted from Langley's wife to the business associates who had been involved in the scandal. The first person on Jameson's list was Alexander Petrov, a former business partner of Langley's. Petrov had been in deep financial trouble after Langley pulled out of their joint venture, leaving Petrov with crippling debt. Jameson decided to bring Petrov in for questioning. The detective spent hours interrogating Petrov, but the man's story checked out. Petrov had been at a conference out of town when the murder occurred. Jameson was about to let him go when Petrov mentioned something that piqued his interest. "I didn't do it, Detective," Petrov said, "but I did receive a strange call from Richard a few days ago. He was acting strange, apologizing and making amends for some wrong he thought he'd committed. I have no idea what he was talking about, but I'm sure it had something to do with his recent business dealings." The call from Petrov got Jameson thinking. Who else could Langley have been referring to in his cryptic message? He decided to take a closer look at Langley's employees, searching for anyone who might have had access to his business dealings. After scouring through payroll records, Jameson discovered that one of Langley's employees, a quiet and reserved accountant named Emily, had been missing for the past two weeks. Emily had been responsible for managing Langley's finances, and Jameson suspected she might know more than she was letting on.
