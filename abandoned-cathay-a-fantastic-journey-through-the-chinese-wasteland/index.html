Abandoned Cathay: A Fantastic Journey Through the Chinese Wasteland


In the eastern reaches of the forsaken land known as Abandoned Cathay, where once majestic cities thrived, now only whispers of crumbling walls and forsaken relics remain. The withering bamboo groves sway in the desolate winds, as the weary traveler, entire days walking beneath the infinite sky unmarred by cloud, reaches the gates of what legend tells is the fated and mysterious city of Kingdoms End. The heavy wooden doors, adorned with intricate carvings and sorrowful faces that evoke the spirit of an age lost, slowly creak as he pushes them apart.

To enter the city is to tread on the thoughts of a thousand souls – the discarded memories of a civilization banished to the brink of nonexistence. Here, tradition and dreadful silence meet; the mold-ridden pagodas and majestic gardens are poised to gently crumble beneath each step. Along cobbled streets that carry echoes of laughter, now silent, the explorer delves into the uncharted city as if traversing the days of yore.

Amidst the mirage of untold history, a charming anomaly stands – the Tea House of Silken Whisper. Its dilapidated facade camouflages the elegance that lingers within. A persistent call beckons, the same one whispering in every traveler’s ear, a silent plea to cast weariness aside and share in a rare solace. The persistent tinkle of a stray breeze chime, a relic of a forgotten melody, follows the wanderer into the depths of the mysterious tavern.

Pushing through the obsidian door hung with delicate beads, the muggy embrace of the Silken Whisper embraces the weary adventurer, whose sweaty palms mark the ancient walls that cradle him. Looming masses of dusty books and mementos lining the shelves, a fiery yellow lantern casts an eerie glow that reveals the room’s opulence. Opulent it may be, but only in the eyes of nostalgia can this dust-cloaked memory of splendor be christened as such.

Innkeeper Lu preserves the lingering threads of civility. Her weathered yet fragile countenance bears etchings of the past’s fragility. She proceeds with the timings of days long gone, emphasising the epitome of her culture. The door, swinging open with each passing guest, allows whispers of the desolate world through, only to be stifled by the relics and silence that echo within the walls of the Silken Whisper. The guest must follow her unspoken rule: honor the silence, for it is a tribute to the very essence of this forgotten place.

Situated before a fallen tiger in battle, whose head now forms the table, lounge the guests in the throes of contemplation. Steam rises sleepy and slow from the ancient cups of fragrant Oolong, while the red leaves of the Jasmine tea unravel beneath the warmth. They drink, not in the thirst of today, but in the yearning for the past.

Between sips, the patrons share twenty-four histories in the Language of Roses, each word perfuming the air with the secrets of Cathay. In this dimly-lit room rests the entire world’s weight, carried by whispers wrapped in dust. For here, where time itself seemed to pause, the stories are as compelling as the silken whispers of the moonlit silk hanging at the window.

The travelers converse with silken sashes as their wrists to share stories of the Abandoned Cathay that linger in their memories, discussing the cities of silence and the history borne in fragments as their words reveal the once Luminous Celestial. They remember the grandeur that was eastern grandeur, the zenith of Chinese symbolism, the rivers of time that coursed through the land. Here, the traveler encounters the vibrant veneration that once pervaded the land and the wisdom of a thousand generations betrayed by the sands of time.

The twilight approaches as the wanderer takes his leave, the silhouettes of the forgotten city casting long shadows in the waning daylight. On the outskirts of Kingdoms End, the boundary between memory and reality blurs. The traveler, now witness to the myriad candles of a thousand spirits – for the very soul of Abandoned Cathay dwells within these meandering lanterns – surrenders his heart, and with it, his breath.

In the embrace of the night, the silhouette of the city cloaked in memory extends to the farthest horizon. The Tears of Peony, falling quietly on the world below, unite the two realms – the realm of memory and the realm of the living. Thus, as the echoes of his heart resonate within the crumbling walls of the abandoned city, the wanderer undertakes a journey back home, carrying with him the whispering remembrances of Abandoned Cathay.
