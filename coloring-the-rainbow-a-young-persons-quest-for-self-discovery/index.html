Coloring the Rainbow: A Young Person's Quest for Self-Discovery


Rosemary couldn’t remember a time when she hadn’t loved rainbows. They were there, in her earliest memories, swirling in her imagination - ethereal spectacles of color, with a touch of magic in every spectrum. On days filled with sunshine and light showers, her tiny hands would press against the kitchen window pane, watching as the iridescent arches danced across the sky, a visual song only she could hear. In shades of azure, topaz, sapphire, amethyst, rose, violet, and gold, rainbows were as much a part of her childhood as the sandy shores of Dune Harbor and the serenade of the ridge’s whispering winds.

As the years went by, Rosemary found solace in art. Drawings, paintings, sculptures, and crafts - the more colors involved, the better. But it wasn’t until her twelfth birthday that she discovered the joy of color in a whole new way - a wondrous world unknown to her until that day. Her Grandma Eliza, who Rosemary adored, gifted her with a box of sparkling, finger-paint-loaded rainbow brushes, an inkling of a smile playing on her wrinkled lips as the wrapping paper flittered to the floor. “It’s time to embrace your inner colors,” she whispered, a twinkle in her eye as if unveiling the secrets of creation she had held all these years.

And so began Rosemary’s quest, one where palms were dyed indigo and daisies painted with crimson at the seashore. She wandered far and wide – in meadows of wildflowers, gardens of cacti, and fields of curiosity and learning – with crayons, watercolors, pencils and markers as her constant companions. She became a rainbow chaser, etching each band of the colorful arches into her soul. Every stroke she took on paper, every blend of shades, became a reflection of her thoughts, a journey of self-discovery that transformed her life.

But with every color filled canvas, there remained an unseen connection that beckoned her into the limelight of a deeper understanding. That summer break, Rosemary fumbled through Mom’s sewing box while her father tinkered with tools, her sister buried her nose in a new romance novel, and her brother tinkered with their neighbor’s prized motorcycle. Among the tangled threads, she found a sketch - her own reflection in colors as vivid and swirling as the rainbows she revered.

Taking a deep breath, she ventured to her Closet of Creativity. The shelves were laden with art materials: pots of wet-on-wet watercolors lingering on her palette, Mason jars filled with glistening paint and a rainbow of q-tips, tubes of oil paints waiting to be squeezed, each with the promise of their hidden hues. Yet none of it mattered much. She peeled open her soul with her favorite paintbrush - a rich emerald green. The first stroke started hesitantly, a timid whisper across the paper, like the murmur of waves softly lapping against the shore. But with each feather-light movement, the emerald tendrils evolved into a map of her inner landscape, its wiry veins mapping the contours of her thoughts, the subtle shifts of her moods and dreams.

As the emerald stroke thinned out, the colors began to blend, creating an ever-evolving tapestry of reds, peaches, violets, yellows, blues and greens that seemed a direct mirror to her journey of self-discovery. She discovered that like the iridescent hues of the rainbow, her color palette shifted with the change of perceptions and emotions. Sometimes – with the gust of wind from a cascade of rain – she would be a brilliant ochre and burnt umber, the hues of autumn reflecting her mood of solemnity, a time of wonderful change. And other times, in the light of a storm gone by, her paper would be awash with stormy grays, cobalt blues, and electric greens.

Her quest for self-discovery unfolded like the stacking pages of a beautifully bound journal filled with her thoughts, emotions, experiences, and reflections. And just like the rainbows that had so enamored her for so many years, the end of Rosemary’s journey was as thrilling as the very beginning. She found the zest and vibrancy of every color within her, realizing that each hue, each shade and tint represented a deeper part of her soul, her beliefs, her passions, and her connections.

Laying down her palette, breathless, yet ignited, she felt reborn, her heart beating to the rhythm of her own colors. The world seemed more vivid, more radiant and alive. She no longer saw simple colors, but the beautiful intensity of life. When she looked at the sky, she saw the shades of turquoise, reflecting her peace, her hopes and dreams. And when she sank her bare feet into the warm sand, the gentle rustling of the grains under her soles were whispers of her experiences and longings.

It dawned on her then, as the sun dipped into the ocean, its fiery yellows and oranges dyeing the clouds in a warm embrace, that she had found her true colors. Though this journey had only begun at her twelfth birthday, it felt like coming home – home to herself.

With a grateful heart, Rosemary whispered her most heartfelt “Thank you” to Grandma Eliza, knowing within that the lifetimes she had so inadvertently touched had not been in vain. She promised herself to always explore the colors within and around her, to find joy in her paintbrush and life’s little moments. Just as the sun roared its farewell to the dancing ocean, Rosemary realized, there will always be a touch of rainbow in her, a spectrum of colors she will never again take for granted, not as the girl with the paintbrush, but as the young woman forged in the very hues of her dreams.
