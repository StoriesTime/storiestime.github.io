Deceptive Reflections: A Story of Concealment and Ethical Enigma

Aldrich was quite talented, but his creative designs attracted more attention than the firm could handle. One day, he was asked to create a building that was modest and would blend seamlessly with the surrounding structures.

Aldrich was asked to take on this task of blending in as a personal challenge. He meticulously surveyed the area and noticed a pattern in the reflections of the old buildings around his proposed site. The reflecting pond near the site showed an unusual symmetry that seemed eye-catching yet deceptive. It was captivating and concealed the wretched secrets of history.

The old buildings’ reflections appeared on the pond’s surface during the day, like a mirror, but in the evening, the nearby streetlights cast a blinking, distorted, yet still deceptive image of the surroundings. It was this bizarre reflection that fascinated Aldrich, and he began to ponder on how to incorporate it into his building’s design.

The building had to be low, as per the client’s instructions, to avoid overshadowing the existing structures. He therefore decided to use this deceptive reflection as a passive cooling system. As the heat waves reflected off the surrounding buildings, they would also impact his structure. At night, the streetlights’ reflections added an element of intrigue to Aldrich’s design. So, he created an unusual pattern of lights within the interior to cast dancing, distorted shadows on the perimeter walls during the nighttime. By doing this, Aldrich wanted to mask the pattern of the streetlights and retain the illusion of the peculiar reflection that fascinated him so much.
