Legends of the Moon: The Tale of Chang'e and the Jade Rabbit


In the ancient Chinese mythology, the legend of Chang'e, the moon goddess, has been passed down for generations. Her tale is one of love, sacrifice, and devotion, with the ever-faithful jade rabbit by her side. Chang'e was a beautiful and powerful immortal, beloved by the gods and revered by mortals alike. She lived on earth during the reign of the legendary hero, Hou Yi, who was famous for his extraordinary archery skills. Their love blossomed, and they shared a deep and unshakeable bond until fate tore them apart. Hou Yi was tasked by the Jade Emperor to shoot the nine suns that threatened to destroy the earth. With his exceptional marksman skills, he successfully completed the task, and in return, he was given an elixir of immortality. However, he was married to another woman, and his love for Chang'e only intensified when he could not be with her. Chang'e, unaware of the elixir's properties, drank it, and as the elixir took effect, she ascended to the heavens, leaving behind her mortal form. Heartbroken and unwilling to part with her beloved Hou Yi, she pleaded with the gods to allow her to stay on earth. They refused, and she was banished to the moon, where she would spend eternity. The Tale of the Jade Rabbit On the moon, Chang'e found herself alone, with only her memories of Hou Yi to keep her company. She transformed a group of stones into companions but soon yearned for the warmth and companionship of a living being. Her tears fell upon the ground, imbuing the stones with the tears' magical properties, giving them life. One of the stones transformed into a small, majestic rabbit, with an amazingly large left ear and a profoundly compassionate heart. The rabbit fed Chang'e with sacred peaches, building a formidable friendship, and awards symbols of their infinite loyalty. Chang'e trusted this servant stalwartly for loyalty and affection.
