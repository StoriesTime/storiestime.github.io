Dilemma Diaries: Short Stories Exploring Ethics


Alice was a web designer who lived a simple, transparent life. Her days were filled with code, coffee, and curiosities of web design. One day, a new project popped up on her radar - a website that claimed to be the future of e-commerce. Intrigued, Alice dove headfirst into the project.

The company behind the project was a startup, something that appealed to her adventurous spirit. As she dug deeper, she found out that the founder’s name was Robert, a mysterious character who loved to keep things shrouded in secrecy. Despite his secretive nature, Alice admired Robert’s drive and ambition.

The project was like nothing she’d ever worked on before. It was a platform that aimed to revolutionize online shopping by introducing a new level of security and privacy. Buyers could remain anonymous, and sellers wouldn’t be able to access the buyers’ identities. The only identifier was a random code generated for each transaction. This was a breakthrough, especially for people who cared about their privacy.

As Alice spent more time on the project, she discovered an unusual clause in the contract. There was a small paragraph stating that every transaction on the platform would be monitored by an anonymous team of hackers. Their job was to ensure that no fraudulent activities occurred on the site. The hackers would be given full access to the users’ information, browsing history, and transactions.

Alice was taken aback by this revelation. It was a blatant violation of users’ privacy, yet it was tucked away in the fine print. She had three options: continue with the project, report the company to the authorities, or quit immediately.

Alice chose to stick with the project. She believed in the potential of the platform, and she was curious to see how they would handle the privacy breaches. Over the next few weeks, Alice immersed herself in the project, working on designs for the site. Her focus on the platform’s visuals and utility forced her to turn a blind eye to her qualms about the privacy violation.

One day, Alice found out that the anonymous team of hackers was led by a person named Maxwell. Maxwell was a reformed hacker, notorious for his past exploits. He was known for breaking into secure systems and gaining access to sensitive information. Now, he used his skills for good, or so he claimed.

Alice was conflicted. Part of her admired Maxwell’s dedication to protecting people’s information. On the other hand, she was horrified at the blatant invasion of privacy. As she struggled with her conscience, she uncovered a secret that would change everything.

Maxwell had a gambling addiction, and he was using the platform’s access to users’ data to fund his habit. He was selling the data to the highest bidder. This was a complete betrayal of the platform’s mission, and it crushed Alice’s faith in the project.

In the end, Alice had no choice but to report the platform to the authorities. She warned Robert about Maxwell’s activities and gave him a chance to fix the situation. When he failed to act, Alice resigned from the project and reported it to the Press, the police and the Data protection authorities.

The platform was shut down the following day, and the events that unfolded made headlines around the world. The tangled web of secrets and lies unraveled, and Alice was left to ponder the price of integrity and the consequences of living a transparent life in a world shrouded in secrecy.
