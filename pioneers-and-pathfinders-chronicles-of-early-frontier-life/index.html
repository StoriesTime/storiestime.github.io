Pioneers and Pathfinders: Chronicles of Early Frontier Life


The sun had set untouched by a single cloud, and the sky glanced a royal dawn to the ragtag village. By no stretch of the imagination could it be called a settlement, for the unlikeliest of communities had taken root in the face of adversity. These were the Pioneers and Pathfinders of the frontier—and they were bound by a hymn.

Life thrived in the most unlikely places, and the pockmarked land was no exception. The ground bore all that could be thrust upon it, from the swiftest currents of a river’s love to the sorest feet of a weary traveler. And though the soil may seem harsh and unforgiving, it did not lack the capacity to yield ample harvests. Perhaps it was not the land that was hard, but rather the lives that were forced to accommodate it.

Drouet was a soul built of resilience—his endurance had seen him prevail through both blizzard and drought. It was he who had led his people to this land, and his unwavering gaze was something of a beacon to his fellows. There were no words to describe the trails he had trodden, and the village that now nestled harmlessly in the bosom of the wild was a testament to his dauntless spirit.

Until other landscapes, Drouet knew only this land and its reasons to be harsh. His people knew only the face of Drouet, and the tale that followed him. Many folks whispered of his past, claiming he lived as one who encompassed the heartiness of the land. Few dared to dig any deeper, and fewer still knew that the very land thrived on the hymn of Drouet.

Each morning, as the first glimmer of dawn touched upon a makeshift altar, Drouet would find himself engrossed in an otherworldly hymn. The melody was not one that could be teased forth from any bell or pipe, but it steadied his heart, and his bond with the land deepened. Over time, the tomfoolery of his past was drowned out by what did remain in the hearts of men—a hymn.

The roiling echoes of this hymn were lousy with secrets, and whispers of untold wonders washed over the village. From its humble beginnings to its present form, the village swelled with life and possibility. Despite the difficulties faced, for each family, their meager existence was richly imbued with dreams. So, it was not mere happenstance that the land began to soften; a hymn worked its secret trickery upon the land.

One fateful day, an inky black stranger—mismatched eyes twinkling like a night sky—crossed paths with the village. It was said that this stranger radiated a strange kind of power. Their gaze cut through the air as if worn by familiar trespassers. There was no expression of warmth or cold to be found in those eyes, for they appeared empty, like the death of fire in a hearth, burning blue in the night.

Drouet was wary, but it was he who first stepped towards the stranger. They shared no words, but the mere presence of the stranger seemed to breed worry amongst the people. It was only then that the idea was given form—that maybe the stranger sought to steal the hymn from the Pioneers and Pathfinders.

It was not with morning dew or sacred oils that the altar was cleansed, but with the sweat of a thousand mothers’ hands. The very troupe of Pioneers and Pathfinders stood vigil over the altar, their eyes never straying from the stranger.

And then, with a life of its very own, the hymn seems to break free and rise above the village. It swirled into every corner of the sky, becoming one with the land. Once disgraced by whispers of supernatural power, the stranger now stood amongst the Pioneers and Pathfinders as a member of their village. From that day onward, no more whispers were heard about the stranger, as the hymn had woven them into the thread of life that ran gently through the heart of Drouet.

The village began to grow, with the spirit of the hymn forever at their side. As the Pioneers and Pathfinders toiled, this hymn continued to harden the soil beneath their feet. For, in truth, the borders of this village remained the same, and neither stranger nor villagers dared to let go of the hymn’s chimeric embrace.

In time, their songs would change, and their dreams would grow, but no part of the village—and little did they know—the stranger included, could ever escape the hymn that bound them together. For the land knew of their presence, and their boastful dreams, and it held them steadfast with the promise of a song.

In this manner, the village flourished and diversified, and the mysterious stranger retreated into the hills. Through the power of a timeless hymn, the Pioneers and Pathfinders interwove their dreams and their song into the very heart of the terra firma upon which they stood.

As the years unfolded like a storybook, the land echoed with a hymn of life. Unbeknownst to all, whispers of the stranger lingered in the shadows of the hills, as the Pioneers and Pathfinders lived on in the embrace of the land. And so, the hymn of Drouet kept vigil with them, forever, in a hymn for the frontier.
