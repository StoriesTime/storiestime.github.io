The Grand Larcenist: Crossing Borders, Breaking Laws


Once upon a time in a remote, notoriously seedy corner of the world, there lived a man who had earned the infamy and the reverential nickname ‘The Grand Larcenist’. His mastery of the art of thievery was the stuff of legend, and his exploits were whispered about in corners all over the globe. He was a master of stealth and a wizard of disguise, able to cross the most heavily-guarded borders, to rob from the richest and most powerful, and always to slip away without a trace. His name was never uttered aloud, but only in the company of bullion, precious stones, or priceless art, the gravitas of the unspoken reference was undeniable.

In a quiet period of his relentless career, The Grand Larcenist lounged idly in an anonymous pub, sipping a lukewarm pint and people-watching. A hen-pecked bureaucrat was being harangued by his screechy spouse at a nearby table, and a young, goal-oriented entrepreneur told a half-interested audience of his latest network marketing scheme. Mundanity often accompanied periods of no heists, the lunchtime crowd in this forgotten corner unremarkable in their mediocrity.

Suddenly, the swing door slammed open, letting in a gust of wind and an impeccable figure in a tailored waistcoat and fedora, accompanied by an extravagant mustache. Heads turned. Startled eyes widened. Our Grand Larcenist strutted to the bar, now the center of attention. He had chosen the most inconspicuous corner of the world to indulge a whim, the adrenaline of a thief: to steal nothing but a GUY!

A laconic exchange of glances with the bartender somehow communicated serious intent. A mystifying transaction ensued with the bartender, the three words whispered: ‘The man in the blue coat’. They appeared to be key players in a deadly game.

The Larcenist’s eyes scanned the smoky room as if searching for something more elusive than his victim. He located the unwitting mark, sitting slightly apart from the others in a peculiar solitude. The man in the blue coat, oblivious to the impending calamity, twirled a full glass of whiskey and seemed engrossed in his own solitude. The trick now began.

Constructed of equal parts collusion and illusion, the next half hour was a carefully orchestrated dance. The Larcenist crossed and re-crossed the room, weaving through a maze of tables, engaging in jovial banter, feigned acquaintances and distractions, all the while subtly guiding the victim’s gaze away from his target. A red alert siren might have sounded a more dramatic alarm.

Simultaneously, the drunken entrepreneur stumbled unsuspectingly into the Larcenist’s well-rehearsed plan. Falling to the floor, he buffeted into the victim, disarming him of his prized whiskey. Simultaneously, The Grand Larcenist’s hands deftly relieved the now-disoriented victim of his wallet, a feat of sleight of hand so subtle that one might suspect sorcery.

Within moments, the stolen whiskey replaced with an inferior substitute, and our unwitting victim none the wiser, the mark sat comfortably amongst his hustle, none the worse for wear. The masterful Grand Larcenist slipped away unnoticed, leaving behind not a scandal or a confrontation, but merely a replaced drink and the lingering echoes of a whisper in the wind.

‘The man in the blue coat’ had been outmaneuvered by the Grand Master of larceny in his very own city. The tales of their legend had swirled together, whispered into an ever-widening web of fame and infamy.

The art of the theft lay not in the stolen goods themselves, but rather the symphony of deception and distraction, which played out within the dimly-lit pub. But secrets have a way of leaking out. And ever-watching eyes have a nose for intrigue, even trapped in a corner of obscurity.
