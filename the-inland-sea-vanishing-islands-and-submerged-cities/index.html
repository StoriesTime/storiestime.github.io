The Inland Sea: Vanishing Islands and Submerged Cities


Once upon a time, in the vast expanse of Asia, lay the Inland Sea. A wonder of the world, this breathtaking sea was home to countless islands and cities. People from far and wide sought treasures hidden in its ever-shifting depths and marvelled at the beauty of those human-made structures rising from its waters.

The Inland Sea was not like other seas. It was more than just an expanse of water; it was a place full of tales, myths, and legends. Some say it was the reason the gods created humanity. Others whisper that it was once a portal to the realm of the supernatural. Whatever the truth may be, the Inland Sea possessed an enduring allure, drawing people to its mysterious shores.

In the latter days of the ancient city of Aki, a young mariner named Hiroki embarked on an expedition to explore the Inland Sea. He had heard the stories of lost cities and vanishing islands, and he desired to witness these miracles with his own eyes. His mother had warned him of the dangers that lurked beneath the sea’s tranquil surface, but he was courageous, and he had a heart full of curiosity.

As Hiroki sailed out into the sparkling blue waters, his eyes could not leave the horizon. He marveled at the sight of disappearing islands which rose from the depths only to be swallowed by the sea in the blink of an eye. Legend had it that they held the key to a magnificent secret.

The first island Hiroki encountered was a remote, ancient piece of land inhabited by the descendants of the great scholars and builders from bygone eras. The island bore the remnants of an ancient civilization, its stone structures cast in the forms of mythical creatures. Interacting with the islanders to gather knowledge and piece together their history moved Hiroki greatly.

His journey continued, exploring the sunken cities beneath the waves. The cities were filled with awe-inspiring architecture, tombs, and palaces submerged beneath the water with countless relics lay draped in wild, unfurling seaweed. Hiroki dove deep, exploring the abandoned remnants of mansions and temples with a sense of mystic wonder.

He marveled at the grand murals that remained, barely clinging to the crumbling walls, evocative of a time when colorful processions traversed these now-abandoned pathways. The cities were like time-capsules of human civilization, each holding unique stories and secrets.

Hiroki learned of a long-vanished city of the Hydrops, an advanced aquatic civilization said to have built the legendary Underwater Palace. It was rumored that when the stars aligned in a specific pattern, the palace would reemerge from the depths. Eager to unravel this mystery, Hiroki observed the skies as he sailed.

One night, as the stars shone like diamonds above him, he noticed an auspicious arrangement. Excited, he steered his ship towards the area where the palace was believed to be. As he approached the location, the vessel shook violently, as if responding to an unseen force. Suddenly, Hiroki found himself enveloped in a thick, shimmering fog. With bated breath, he waited for it to clear.

When the mist finally dissipated, there, before his eyes, lay the lost city of the Hydrops, resplendent in its underwater magnificence, gleaming and enchanting. The legend was true; the laws of nature had bent to reveal this hidden treasure to a brave and pure-hearted sailor.

Hiroki spent his time swimming between the stunning spires, admiring the intricately designed statues of mythical creatures guarding the Underwater Palace. The grandeur of these submerged structures and relics would astound even the most seasoned archaeologist.

But as much as Hiroki wanted to stay, and despite his success in rescuing relics and artifacts from the submerged city, breaking free from the mystical pull was impossible. Determined, Hiroki returned to the surface, yet it pained him to leave behind those incredible discoveries. After his thrilling adventure, he saw the Inland Sea and its mysterious wonders with newfound awe.

Now, the tales of vanishing islands and submerged cities have become one for the ages. The Inland Sea, too, suffered a tragic and sudden fate. Yet the spirits of those ancient cities remain under the waves, tales of their existence now a powerful reminder of human ingenuity and our humble connection to the ocean that binds us.

Though the Inland Sea may have vanished, the memories of its islands and cities, along with the daring exploits of brave explorers like Hiroki, continue to inspire the hearts and minds of those who listen and dream. And so, forever may the spirit of the Inland Sea remain alive in our hearts and teaching us, even in times of darkness and uncertainty, that sometimes, all it takes is a glimpse of curiosity, power, and love to unearth the legendary wonders of the world.
