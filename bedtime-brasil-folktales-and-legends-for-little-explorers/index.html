Bedtime Brasil: Folktales and Legends for Little Explorers


In the dense Amazonian rainforest, not too far from the bustling city of Brazilia, lay the sleepy town of Simples Felicidades - Simple Happiness. A quaint little village surrounded by lush green foliage as far as the eye could see, the town was home to a diverse assortment of creatures, including humans, jaguars, toucans, and even a mystical group of nocturnal beings known affectionately as “Noite Files” or Nocturnal Friends.

Every night, the Nocturnal Friends would convene in the forest clearing to engage in a delightful tradition handed down from generation to generation; a bedtime story session led by the wise old owl named Arthur. Arthur Owl had a rich, soothing voice that would lull even the most restless of creatures into peaceful slumber.

On this particular moonlit night, the clearing was unusually brimming with excitement. The Nocturnal Friends had gathered in their droves, perching on trees, settling in cozy hammocks, and curling up on fragrant patches of herbs. Among the throng was a young, inquisitive little armadillo named Andrey who had always felt left out while his friends excitedly listened to the tales of bravery, wit, and countless adventures spun by the great Arthur Owl.

“Those stories always seem so distant, so impossible,” thought Andrey, as he shifted uncomfortably in his substantial armor. ‘Perhaps if I had a story like that, I might feel more special.’ And so, Andrey made the decision to seek Arthur Owl, requesting to have a story of his own, where a humble armadillo could be the hero.

That very night, Arthur Owl welcomed Andrey to the storytelling session and acknowledged his request. To Andrey’s amazement, Arthur announced that he would indeed tell a tale of an armadillo as the hero. It was time for the town to learn a valuable lesson about courage and friendship.

Arthur began the tale, “Long ago, in these very woods, there once lived an armadillo named Aleixo, who had a tremendous gift for music playing the flute. His melodies brought so much joy to the creatures of the rainforest that he was invited to play at every gathering.”

“But what nobody knew,” continued Arthur, “was that Aleixo had a hidden fear. The deep thuds they heard in the underbrush at night, they were no more than the fastest jaguar in the forest. Orso, the fearsome predator who had never been seen by man or beast, and Aleixo was terrified.”

“And so, on the night of the great festival when all creatures of the forest were meant to join, Aleixo lay awake, too frightened to go. But the wind carried the whispers of his friends as they blamed his absence on his cowardice.”

Dejected, Aleixo accepted the chorus of murmurs and resolved to leave his forest forever, not wanting to jeopardize his friends’ happiness. Quietly and painfully, he packed his flute and embarked on a lonely journey into the unknown, shedding his tears into the earth as he left.

Andrey listened to Arthur’s tale, his heart increasingly full of admiration for Aleixo and a burgeoning sense of courage within himself. As the story unfurled, he realized that his beautifully thick shell was indeed no detriment, but a marvelous armor to be proud of. Perhaps there was room for an armadillo in the tales of valor and friendship.

“The night grew dark and Aleixo found himself before a magnificent waterfall, where he came upon the cruelest of night hags, Morbidora,” Arthur proceeded, his voice trembling just enough to make even the most stoic of audience members shiver. “Morbidora, in a mournful shriek, had cursed the forest creatures to fall ill, lying dormant during the day, rousing at night to roam without purpose.”

Arthur paused, allowing the tension to coalesce. He narrowed his eyes, enunciating every word. “And thus, Aleixo gathered his courage, transforming his once mournful flute song into a battle cry. The melody reverberated throughout the land, awakening the spirits of all creatures who responded to his bravery.”

‘Very promising,’ thought Andrey, feeling his own heart beat fiercely against his ribcage. He fancied he could feel the rhythm of Aleixo’s bravery.

With fierce determination, Aleixo confronts Morbidora, who begins to weep, undone by the tremendous power within Aleixo’s music. The sobbing serves to break her sinister curse, freeing the creatures of the forest, restoring them to their former vibrant state.

And so, under the soft moonlight and the feeling of its last rays, Morbidora granted the courageous flute-playing armadillo her blessing. From that night on, it became a sacred tradition for the creatures of Simples Felicidades to gather and celebrate festivals under the moon’s watchful gaze, honing their friendship and love for each other.

“As the festival came to an end,” concluded Arthur, “the celebration turned to quiet gratitude. And the tale of Aleixo became their eternal song. A song not of mighty warriors or fearsome beasts, but of quiet courage and never-ceasing friendship.”

With the completion of the tale, Arthur Owl looked around at his sleepy-eyed audience, sensing the invisible thread of camaraderie that had sprouted in that moonlit clearing that night. Andrey, with his cheeks puffed out in an excited grin, felt grateful for his newfound valor, looking forward to being a hero in tales to come.

As the Nocturnal Friends drifted off into peaceful slumber beneath the watchful stars, their night was filled with tales of friendships forged, stories of the great and the small, and, above all, with the reminder that courage often arrives in the guise of the most unlikely heroes.

In the heart of Simples Felicidades, nestled among the rain-drenched leaves and verdant wonders of the Amazon, the humble armadillo was reminded that sometimes, the greatest adventure is the one right beneath one’s armor.
