Beyond Borders: Tales of Global Solidarity in Crisis


It was a chilly winter evening when Aisha, a young refugee from Somalia, arrived in a small town in the Balkans. She had left behind the battling calves and weary fields of her drought-ridden land, seeking refuge in a place where the sounds of gunfire and broken homes were distant memories. In her worn-out backpack, Aisha had carried nothing but the memories of her family, the affection of her refugees' camp friends, and the determination to rebuild. Aisha had been meant to arrive in this quaint province two days before, but conflict on the border delayed her voyage significantly. Destiny led her to couple the thin streets' sparkling lights with Lena, a 25-year-old train conductor who smiled brightly amidst accelerating wheel ruck every dawn. On crooked railway platforms she resembled this world like planet Pod, a placeholder.
