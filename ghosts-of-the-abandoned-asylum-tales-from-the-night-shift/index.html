Ghosts of the Abandoned Asylum: Tales from the Night Shift


Once upon a midnight, the air heavy with the weight of secrets that the crumbling walls of the abandoned asylum held, young nurse Maria found herself drawn to the spectral whispers that echoed through the night. The asylum had been closed for decades but its eerie radiance could not be stifled. Maria neared the rotting iron gates, the moonlight casting eerie shadows across the once-familiar, now-dilapidated halls. Her curiosity, though born of fear, urged her forward.

The asylum bore signs of a storied past, stretching its weathered arms out in search of relief from its troubled memories. As Maria tread cautiously along the creaking corridors, memories of the asylum’s darker days came to life in her imagination. Tormented cries of lost souls and laughter, filled with pain and anguish, drowned out the distant shriek of the wind.

Maria raised the flashlight, its beam cutting through the suffocating darkness. She beheld the empty wards, countless rooms filled with decrepit hospital beds. The air was thick with the scent of decay, mingling with the comforting aroma of industrial cleaner that still clung to the asylum’s tiled floors. In her unfaltering resolve to bring closure to the souls trapped within, Maria encountered the tormented residents of this forsaken abyss, working as the nurse of the night shift.

For some reason, the camera crew she had brought along seemed to have vanished. Only the muddled light and the endless maze had remained. Spiraling through the long abandoned passages, Maria discovered the first ghost—a young woman with chestnut hair, clothed in the same asylum gown she had worn eighty years before. Maria approached her cautiously, only to realise the apparition appeared not aware of Maria’s presence.

The spirit floated lifelessly in one of the screeching wheelchairs, her eyes glossy and colorless, revealing her innate sadness. Maria felt a strange, inexplicable connection with the ghost, an unspoken bond, urging her to help. But what could she do? Maria had been trained to heal the living, not the souls of the dead. Yet the irresistible desire to absolve this ghost of her sorrows consumed her.

Through the darkness, Maria navigated the derelict halls, deeper into the asylum’s fray. Whispering spirits swarmed around her, seeking solace in her comforting presence. Noor, a young Pakistani boy from the nineteen-twenties, consistently disappeared from his bed whenever the nurses had turned their backs, wandering the asylum until dawn. He would materialize right before Maria’s eyes, clinging onto her skirts, begging her to lead him back to where he belonged.

As Maria journeyed deeper into the asylum, she recognized the malevolent spirit that had terrified the living a lifetime ago. He was a beast of a man with piercing blue eyes and a heart full of cruelty. A nurse—one who had passed on not too long ago—warned Maria to stay away from him. But fear had no hold over Maria. As she approached him, his mere presence unnerved her. Yet, she held her ground.

The evil spirit retreated from Maria’s determined gaze, leaving her to her cause. With a burning will to piece together the shattered lives of those who had once roamed these halls, she stumbled upon a room flanked with hidden wards designed to trap the untethered spirits. Maria carried a pocket watch given to her by an old homeless man who claimed it had the power to banish the restless dead.

In spite of the watch’s weight around her neck, Maria felt no power coursing through her. Her heart pounded, her breath barely audible in the suffocating silence. She steeled her nerves, recalling everything she had learned on the wards. Maria centered herself, summoned her last ounce of courage, and pressed down on the button of the watch. A warm wave of energy enveloped her, surging through her being like the force of a thousand galaxies.

As the spirits of the asylum felt the watch’s magic, the wailing gradually subsided. Maria witnessed the ghosts’ faces alight with gratitude as they slowly ascended the churning mist. One by one, they bid their eternal farewell. Sadness gave way to acceptance, and whispers faltered as the ghosts disappeared into the night. Maria’s heart constricted at the loss, but she knew that they were at peace.

Maria faced the malevolent beast—the one who had caused her deepest fear. With a soft touch of her fingers on the watch, she whispered an ancient, deranged spell before she pressed the button. The demon’s eyes flashed in recognition, understanding the danger he was in. He howled in rage, desperate for one final chance to unleash terror from within the asylum walls before he too dissolved into nothingness.

To her agony, the beast retained his terrorizing nature to the very end. As he vanished, the asylum shook with a terrifying crescendo. Dust and debris rained down from the decaying ceiling, the temperature plummeted with an unnatural cold that pierced Maria’s every bone. When the eerie rumble finally dissipated, an impenetrable silence saturated the night.

Now, the asylum lay barren. Alone, Maria walked through the deafening quiet, retracing her steps through the haunting maze. Panicked, she swung around at each creak and rustle, scrambling to recall where she’d left her camera crew. Their presence would have provided comfort in the eerie silence, but she knew she had to leave them in the world of the living.

Maria descended the asylum’s front steps, tears of despair welling up in her eyes as she retraced her steps towards the moonlit path. Just as the ghostly whispers began to fade and the chilling shadows embraced the night once more, Maria heard a familiar voice.

“Maria! Maria!” And there they were, her camera crew, waving torchlights into the darkness, their faces etched with relief and concern. In that moment, Maria realized that it didn’t matter, because she held the power to restore life and banish spirits with the watch in her pocket.

Once more, this forsaken asylum was free from its tortured past, and at last, Maria looked forward to normalcy, to life, and to love. But a nurse’s calling would never leave her.Maria’s journey had led her to the other side, but she was ready to cross the line back into the realm of the living. For in this life, as in the next, there was always a soul to heal.
