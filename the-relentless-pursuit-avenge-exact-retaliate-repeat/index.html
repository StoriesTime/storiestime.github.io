The Relentless Pursuit: Avenge, Exact, Retaliate, Repeat


In the shadow of the ancient mountain range lay a humble village known as Eryndor. A place simple and timeless, it was graced with the abundance of nature’s essence. Yet, under the facade of serenity, whispered the fables of an infamous tale that would forever etch itself within its walls.

Eleven moons had adorned the moonless sky on a nocturnal night when the ominous cloud draped the stars and hid the celestial dance above Eryndor. The village’s hearth crackled, and its occupants shivered, quivering in the blanket of disquiet. As the village elders gathered in the center of the town, their hearts coursed with the urgency of ancient prophecies.

“Upon the eleventh moonless night,” intoned the eldest seer, “There will emerge a shadow more powerful, one foreboding avenger from the realm of inked whispers. He who hunts down the relentless killer who had scarred Eryndor will leave behind a trail of vengeance and sorrow. And thus, fueled by fury, men will embrace the cycle of vengeance and darkness will kiss the light.”

Amid the sea of mortal souls, Adrian stood, a novice warrior wrapped in centuries of vengeance, entrusted with the holy task of avenging his lineage. A lineage forever tainted by the ink of blood—the blood of his kinsman, relentlessly spilled by the torment they christened the Shadowslasher.

A demon in earthly form, it was said, with eyes that unravelled the seer’s souls and smile dipped in the venomous dance of death. Bereft were his father’s cry from the abyss of fate’s cruel games, and the curse of blood levied unto the thirteen-year-old boy, the burden of vanquishing the darkness.

Shadows mingled with reality as the village slept under the watchful eyes of Eryndor. And amidst the silken tapestry whispered the etchings of the seers, every thread amounted to Adrian’s future.

In the predawn light, the air echoed with the consecration of blades and the roaring flames of forges, as twilight broke upon his quest. The clouded sky reflected within the locus of war, a brooch of ash fastened to Adrian’s cloak. A sharp gleam struck the flesh as the shadow defined his silhouette—a silhouette immune to the agony that coursed through his veins, birthing the pyres of vengeance.

A snare of predators, traps, and ambushes later, Adrian embarked on the periphery of the desolate. The whispers of the wind sculpted the tale of a fractured heart within the hollows of abandonment. As the mist thinned into a fragile veil of truth, the fortress of the Shadowslasher emerged.

Spire after spire, a labyrinth of ice chains blossomed, bridging chasms as if by divine hands—the creation of a world apart, one thriving in ether and the ceaseless waltz of shadows and light. A place, where even the laughter echoed, trembling, behind walls of ice.

Guided by the trembling murmur of prophecies, Adrian concluded the vigil of seers and unraveled the passage between the realm of sentient boxes, those dominions rigid with the energy of our earth. A quietude gripped his soul as the ice forged onward, and his gaze targeted the one amongst the desire line—a sealed gate of ivied redemption.

And so, like the penned frail rivers yearning to burst westwards, so did his breath swell, his purpose rooted in ancient words and bloodlines. The whispers of the world, the echoes of Eryndor’s shiver, and the twilight fire in the eyes of Adrian sparked the slumbering beast coerced from slumber.

By the hollow clamor of the chimes, he branded upon the earth myriad sounds of contrition, to beseech the guardians of Eryndor: a journey embarked upon the precipice of despair.

By the chalice of a thousand stars, garnished in the seal of Supernal—water and the earth were married, into fists of solidarity, now dipped in eternal abyss. As hands smeared with intrigue pushed the curtains of light, a request emerged. Breath and fire were the entreaty of the blades, which till the break of dawn tasted not the blood.

And then, the heart pounded wars and wails into the mighty floor below. A dolorous echo, silence probed the slain heave, and Adrian cried out with the voice of men, saints, and specters. His footprints resonated, falling upon the hollowed notes, rooted in the shadow of vengeance.

Adrian advanced through the domain of Shadowslasher, where shadows bore the impetus to dance.

A realm without a name, where eyes devoured the owing ecstasy of destruction. Where chains thrived, not just of steel but of battered souls entrapped by silence. Yet here too lay the long-awaited retribution—no wind to taunt, no leaf to confide, only the chill of the forsaken dominion.

The dance came naturally—part sagging drift, serene and rhythmic, waltzing through the cradle of tormented screams. Adrian slung his sword and roared a name scrawled in red upon the ebon pages of time—a name known only to him, whispered only in the sobs of his lullabies.

With every gasp, the world whirled. The very strings of fate entwined, tariffs upon an abyss, as shadows weaved.

Every heartbeat throbbed in the raucous, in the breathless passage of the icy world. Adrian, the boy scorned by fate, grasped the inner sanctum, where vengeance resided, and where shadows wove, searing in the heat of battle.

In this dance of despair, where shadows courted calamity under the violent skies where light wept over gaping wounds, stepped the herald of twilight.

Unmarked graves birthed Lightchaser on the edge of no-man’s land. The whispering footsteps of Eryndor, the armies of fallen—even now, life and rebirth circled upon his blades, birthed by sacrifice.

Yet, usurping path, the veiled hand—of the enigmatical Shadowslasher. The echo of Adrian’s heartbeat roused, defied the emptiness.

The hallowed silence was rent by the clash of blades devoutly signing songs—the waltz of the dying lands.

And thus, before the tales of the soulless chimes and the yearning playground of the damned, Icarus fell. That which had mercilessly hammered the world shrugged off the chains of vengeance and erupted into dust.

The domain sculpted by a heart encased in ice lay bereft—for the first time clad in silence, still. The flames of vengeance waned, the heartbeats of two divergent paths interlaced in requital and requiescat.

Adrian hastened, the creed of Eryndor flickering in his eyes. And as his shadow shunned the fiery light, the whispers of creation, redemption scraped the sky.

And so faded the herald of twilight, the pangs of requital smearing the canvas of time’s cunning. Retribution exacted, the light, undaunting, reclaimed her dominion. He descended, the unlikely child of destiny—a combatant at last wielding absolution over vengeance, a liberator of lands burdened by the fables of Eryndor.

From the spiraling shadows births redemption—a vessel of light, etched by the austerity of time, bathing in the eternal seas of the brethren, diffracting the lamplight of Eryndor.
