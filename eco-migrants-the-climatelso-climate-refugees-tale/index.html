Eco-Migrants: The Climatelso: Climate Refugees' Tale


In the quaint little village of Glaciera, nestled between snow-capped mountains and sprawling glaciers, life was once idyllic. Seasons came and went, and the people harvested icebergs that floated gently down the Crystal River. They captured them, chipped and carved them into sculptures, then sold them for frozen treats to innumerable tourists that visited the village. This, for generations, had been their way of life.

Their world was one of eternal winters, nestled in the embrace of the eternal ice. Warm breezes were only whispers, tales told to only be believed in dreams. But things changed when the world was no longer cold and silent.

The skies above Glaciera, once clear and blue, grew misplaced with storms raging, fierce winds whispering of change. The sun now melted the very core of glaciers, whereas it used to merely grace them without a touch. Summers, once hardly experienced, now boiled, leaving even the icemen drenched in sweat.

People began to leave. Not in armies, but in whispers of individuals that trickled like snowmelt into the valleys, moved by the vanishing landscape. They called themselves Eco-Migrants, displaced by the forces of Climate they once could never have imagined. The once lush greenery that lined the village streets wilted, the colors of autumn become the color of ash. Spring became riddled with tempests, clouds the shape of thunderous darkness above, unleashing floods upon the lands.

Luciana, a captain of the Icesteamers, witnessed her home transform in front of her eyes; the once frozen waters she sailed now churned with hidden fury, frothing as though they yearned to swallow the highest peaks. Rivers overflowed, and glaciers shattered, revealing shards upon which her people had thrived for centuries. The children of Glaciera built volcanoes from the frozen debris, only for them to crumble with a rumble, the dust rising silent as the screams of their remnants.

Armed with nothing but a backpack of her grandfather’s most ancient recipes, Luciana embarked on a journey seeking new lands, questing for answers. She sailed on the bitter seas of displacement, the fierce storms casting her vast through the narrative of a world changed.

Aboard her vessel, she dreamt of an ice-cream creation that would honor her people, something that would freeze time, and halt the melting. Recipes of the former life scribbled on weathered parchment papers, now held a power that Luciana hadn’t realized.

The world had begun to trade not just icebergs and mysteries, but climates as well. Global exchanges transformed lives as winter migrated, patterns of life ravaged by the relentless fervor of climate change. Natural disasters took on the form of nameless deeds, a privilege only afforded to the elements of the earth.

Luciana dreamt of a new name to be engraved in history, an honor bestowed by novel creation. “The Frozen Paradiso,” she whispered, an ice cream so extraordinary it would defy the ruthless jaws of time, turning the tide of her people’s destiny. This they would trade, a scoop of the colder days to the world that abandoned them, but roots of resilience remained within them.

She ventured into newly-formed homesteads, finding people that hummed to a new beat, trading with new coins. Each village told tales of a world melting away, but hope was not lost as her grandfather’s treasure of recipes clawed its way through time.

Together with a group of individuals, all far from their origins, Luciana found kinship in their common displacement. Mystics who moved with the sun, firestarters from the ashes of their homes, dreamwalkers whose steps created paths unknown.

Each shared their stories, finding common threads in their circumstances. From Luciana’s frozen chapters, a council of stories unfolded. A warming world required mutable minds, each with their own strengths etched into the sacred skin of Gaia.

The Frozen Paradiso was born in this council, as the first ice-cream ever tasted by the sun. Their sweet creation promised an eternal winter, just a wee bite away. Everywhere they went, they left trails of ice-cream laced with whispers of a past life, dreams of a forgotten season that once was. People tidied memories as sundaes, mourning vanished lands amidst a lifetime of possibilities.

Yet in every melting scoop, glimmers of hope endured. Stories continued to sprout, taking place not just in the lands of Glaciera, but in cities spread across time and space, situated between the chaos of continuing storms. Rainbows reflected the fusion of their dreams, cradling memories and tales.
