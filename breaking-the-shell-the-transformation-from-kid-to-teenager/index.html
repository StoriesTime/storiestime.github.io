Breaking the Shell: The Transformation from Kid to Teenager


It all began on a warm, bright morning in early August. Sixteen-year-old Emily sat on her porch, silently sipping her freshly brewed coffee, lost in thought. Just a year ago, she couldn’t contain her excitement over the idea of turning into a teenager. But now, faced with the reality of her metamorphosis, she couldn’t help but feel overwhelmed.

Emily had everything going for her as a child. Her brimming enthusiasm for life, coupled with an infectious giggle, had always endeared her to everyone around her. Having won a plethora of beauty pageants and her school arts and science exhibitions, she undoubtedly epitomized accomplishments and confidence. However, fleeting moments of happiness seemed inadequate nowadays, shadowed by uncertainties of adolescence.

Her reflection in the mirror bombarded her with a constant reminder of the daunting milestone of adolescence. Emily, once with a round, happy face, had suddenly sprouted facial hair and deepening voice. Tingly sharpshooters beneath her skin hinted at puberty’s approach. She furrowed her eyebrows in contemplation about what lay ahead.

The evening breeze lulled the rustling leaves, as Emily sought refuge in nature’s tranquility. Her delicate fingers traced the outlines of her favorite sketchbook, an instrument of solace and escapism when gloominess embraced her. Embarking on a journey that quelled her seemingly infinite existential questions, Emily flipped through untitled sketches.

On one of the pages, she captured the everyday innocence of her past self. Effortless twirls of hair within a colorful crown of flowers. Emily closed the sketchbook as tears trickled down her cheeks. Her last hope was to find solace by connecting with her best friend, Sam, who had always been an unwavering force in her life.
