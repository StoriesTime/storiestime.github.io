For years, scholars had been arguing over the essence of truth, with some proclaiming it to be an absolute reality and others claiming it to be a mere social construct. The city was abuzz with the news of the great paradox that had been discovered by the philosopher, Aristotle Laotifen. Aristotle's work had taken the world by storm, challenging everything that was thought to be true. As a result, philosophers of all persuasions flocked to Epistemia to engage in the most heated debate of the century. The city's central square was filled with fervent lecturers, each trying to outdo the others in their fervor for the truth. Plato porticos stood toppling precariously, impacting to collapse the houses around them. Aristotle's followers argued that the truth could only be discovered through reason and logic, while Plato's adherents believed that truth lay beyond the realm of mortal comprehension.
