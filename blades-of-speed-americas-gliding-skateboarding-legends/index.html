Blades of Speed: America's Gliding Skateboarding Legends


The sunώὴ bespoke its brilliant descent as the final rays danced across the asphalt, painting the sprawling urban jungle with a final fade of radiant amber. The vibrant cityscape stretched out like a tangled web of concrete and glass, breathing life into the cold veins of civilization. Deep within the glistening underbelly of this metallic urban sprawl, a band of daredevils reveled in the calm before the storm, their hearts pounding and their minds fortified with the whispers of adrenaline. Here, amidst the dimming glow of the afternoon, the true legends of the urban chronicles assembled: the Blades of Speed.

Within the secret lairs of underground sanctuaries and derelict alleys, these devoted disciples of gravity and velocity have honed their craft with unwavering passion, forsaking the trials of the mundane world. They were a motley crew of skateboarders from various walks of life, bound together by an elusive and contagious mutual obsession—an unquenchable thirst for the euphoric speed that could only be discovered in the very zenith of skateboarding: gliding.

Billboards, hanging signs, and scaffolds—all effectively transformed into launch-pads from which the Blades of Speed catapulted, riding their skis-mounted skateboards, soaring high above the concrete jungle. To the wider world, the term “gliding” might evoke visions of serene fowl or tranquil seas; in the underground realm, however, it represented the ultimate zenith of the skateboarding art—a baptism by fire. A ceremony so sacred yet shrouded in mystery that only those devoted to the cult of the blade could bear witness to its ingenuity and elegance. One could liken it to a ritual dance with gravity itself, where even time bends to the will of the Riders as they defiantly summon their own world of cosmic terrain.

Born in the heart of the tempest, Eli was the shadow that obsessed over the light, the night’s solace to the sun’s fierce blaze. A shadow that craved the void, he reigned as the monarch of the Blades of Speed. His trust in the mortal realm rested in his swerving, rocket-fueled skateboard—a black steel webbed wonder christened the VoidWalker. As Eli’s fingers clung to the grip tape like tendrils, the web-bearing skate board sang beneath his fevered commands, surging teasingly above the ground as effortlessly as a wraith floating above graves.

Through unruly indignation and oppressive darkness, Eli harbored an undying love for the ethereal elegance of flight. The haughty conceit of carving through the air, shrouded eternally in stealth and silence, drove his schadenfreude. Not even the stars in their heavens could rival the intense blaze of what he cherished most—the purification by the delay of gravity, a sanctification of the soul through his own boundless gravity-defying tremors.

Among his brethren, there existed a loose pecking order. The so-called preachers of speed—the apostles that heralded the doctrine of velocity through their own gracing of the blades—would yield in devotion to none other than their Dark Prince, Eli. Within this pantheon of gods, performing as both the high priest and sacrificial lamb, settled the incredibly versatile Mira, wielding her glowing blade like a siren’s call upon the winds.

Mira did not wear skis or requite a skateboard; she championed a path uniquely different—a realm carved out by her own innovation. An amalgamation of form and function, she wore four wheels like a young colt embraces its unbridled potential. Fleet, agile, and clandestine, she was known to dart in and out, weaving intricate patterns across the arc of the afterglow, leaving her confidants and adversaries alike enamored and exasperated by her impulsive maneuvers.

The ethereal scent of twilight seeped into the damp crevices of the city, mingling with the intoxicating blend that hovered tantalizingly atop the fumes of gasoline and rubber. As the barely perceptible shadow of the moon morphed into the ghost of midafternoon’s forsaken sun, an indelible ink of black unfurled from the hearts of the Blades of Speed.

Their remarkable and well-rehearsed symphony of noise⛥⛥ laid barren in the concrete cradle, now replaced with choirs of wings. An oasis of stillness, a celestial universe of eternal grace, was unveiled upon the Riders as they strove to attain the divine cosmic prophecy of flight.

Eli’s first move was the elegiac pose. A type of statement without words—a language confined exclusively to the artistry of the glide—from which hydrogen fuels his lust for air. Each breath that harbored purpose became a trail of tendriled whispers, transcending the shackles of earthbound existence and dancing with the sumptuous expanse of the heavens.

Mira, in her own poetic rebellion, unfurled her lithe and iridescent limbs, guiding her infernal steed; the wheels spun, a timeworn loom that wove through the threads of darkness and light. A celestial dance began as she traversed the corridor of the astral, securing her place amongst the sacred pantheon of the gliding celestial beings.

Yet, their unprecedented attempt to dissolve into the visual fabric of the night could spell naught but menace for those who feared the uncharted territories of their hearts. But fear’s venom meant nothing to the Blades of Speed, for they went beyond darkness, beyond the churning storm of their world—into a realm that would terrify the soul of every man.

As interminable hours of an evening lengthened into a clandestine midnight revelry, the Blades of Speed carried on their petal-like journey, hands extending, like the preparatory movements of an artist reaching for the brush, the revitalization of life. And thus, finding beauty in the rebels whom the world overlooked, the ethereal vision of Eli and Mira celebrated the liberation of their hearts amidst the winds of a fearless glide. All of this happened in the hallowed realms above, called home by the angels of asphalt, the Blades of Speed, all while the world below remained oblivious to the elaborate tapestry of dreams ceaselessly unfurled above them.
