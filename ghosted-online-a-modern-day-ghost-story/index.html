Ghosted Online: A Modern-Day Ghost Story


It was an ordinary Friday evening in San Francisco, the kind where the fog from the bay crept silently into the alleys of the city, twisting and wrapping itself around the homes of the unsuspecting. As the sun dipped below the horizon, Lucy Santos sat in her cozy apartment, furiously typing her heart out. An online dating profile wasn’t exactly her idea of fun, but here she was, trying to find love in a world blurring the lines between reality and the virtual realm - a modern-day ghost story.

“Meeting new people is so cliché,” she murmured to herself as she stared at the 180-character limit for her bio. It was a riddle she had to solve before the clock clicked to midnight and the new date, as it were, disappeared.

Delving into the abyss of online romance, Lucy underlined, crossed out, and backspaced. She forgot the clock was against her, and the chilly breeze from the open window felt like a gentle reminder. The apparition, a captain’s ghostly laughter sailing into the room, both exhilarated and terrified her.

“Here goes nothing,” Lucy typed, then hit enter. The cursor blinked, awaiting judgment, and so was the ghostly silhouette in her window.

As she logged off, Lucy was greeted with a thumbs-up emoji on her profile from her sister, Maria. Always the motivator of siblings, Maria had strongarmed her into creating this digital persona.

A beep, coming from her phone, snapped the air in the room. “Looks fantastic, Lucy! Time to swipe right!” Maria messaged, excitement oozing from her online thumbs.

Maria had been resolute that online swiping was the future of romance. After all, her latest encounter had been from a fishing app, where they had exchanged catfish jokes for weeks. Little did they know, danger lurked not in the water but beneath the screen.

With her bio complete and her confidence crystals and feathers all aligned, Lucy went back to the pool. The pool was everything her neighborhood community pool wasn’t: small, cramped, and freezing, with an eerie neon glow. But like a moth to the flame, she surreptitiously kept her eye on the glowing screen.

Before long, her phone let out a soft ping, like a ghostly whisper. Lucy’s heart skipped a beat—it was a match! Then, another. And then another.

Intrigued, she opened the chat, only for her eyes to widen when a name flashed back at her: Christian – a name both regal and, unbeknownst to Lucy, ominous.

As she navigated her new chat page, the need to connect overwhelmed Lucy, a byproduct of her crumbling confidence and lonely heart. She learned about Christian’s travels, his love for cheese, and his inexplicable fascination with her cat, Maximus. As predicted, she was ghosted by her other potential partners, leaving the conversation open between her and Christian.

Nights stretched into weeks, and weeks into months. Christian’s screen continued to light up with messages, leaving a warmth in Lucy’s heart – a warmth she’d never experienced with anyone, or anything, before.

Until, one day, the ghosts came calling. Lucy arrived home one evening to find her apartment in disarray. Papers and belongings strewn about, Maximus missing. Panic clawed at her throat, and she took to the only place she knew to seek solace – the virtual realm.

Christian, of course, was the first to check on Lucy’s well-being. As the search for her missing cat—now returned but frightened—intensified and the fog from the bay seemed thicker than ever, the connection between the two only intensified.

But it wasn’t until a ghostly knight, bejeweled in armor, emerged from the fog that Lucy realized the true nature of her connection to a supposed stranger. She felt Christian’s gaze through her window, watching her through the eyes of the ghost as the phantom knight vanished.

With the return of her cat, the flutters in her chest turned to alarm; she had stumbled into the abyss, and the more she tried to face it, the darker it grew. The flame died on her screen, and then, in a space of eerie blue light, so did her connected world.

Heartbroken and disoriented, Lucy fled into the fog, chasing reality. She felt frozen in time, gasping for air between memories of her digital love–gone-awry. The fog swallowed her existence, and when the sun peeked through, she awoke in an unfamiliar room, bathed in daylight.

In her dream, she had see a reflection of the ghostly knight cross over to the xylophone, as its soul left the earth. The weight of reality hit her as she looked around her room; a new cast of digital characters moved past her window, emboldened and bright smiles on their screens.

Lucy confronted her fear of the online abyss and the reality of the ghostly shadows lingering there. As she typed messages into the void, she knew that she had to cure herself from this twisted love story.

She grinned and faced her screen, fingers poised. At the bottom of the dark, abyssal space, she wrote a message to Christian – one she hoped would last a lifetime.

“Don’t be a ghost,” she declared, digital fingers chasing the ethereal haze within her online surroundings. “Let’s find our path to reality together.”

And as she sent the message into the depths, Lucy whispered a thank you to the ghostly knight from her dreams. Her heart, once weighed down by uncertainty, would heal with newfound hope and anxiety intertwined within the hollow confines of her newfound online love.
