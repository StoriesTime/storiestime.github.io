It was a crisp winter morning when Dr. Maria Hernandez stepped off the rickety bus that had brought her to one of the most remote and war-torn areas of the world. As a renowned aid worker and emergency medicine specialist, Dr. Hernandez had spent years working in some of the most challenging environments globally. But nothing could have prepared her for what she was about to witness. Word had spread quickly about a new aid distribution center emerging at the southern end of the camp. The dedicated team of volunteers, armed with intentions of connection, guidance, emergency response plans, and problem-solving, were waiting for the aid containers and food supplies.
