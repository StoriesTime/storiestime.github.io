Maria pondered the phrase "actions contemplate," contemplation through actions. Her inner voyage had reached a critical point. She thought about the times she had felt a sense of awe and wonder, when the beauty of the world seemed to transcend the ordinary. Those moments had been sacred, she realized, and they had been created by her own choices. She sat down on a bench, taking a deep breath as the silence enveloped her. In that moment, Maria felt a sense of clarity wash over her. She realized that her doubts were not a reason to abandon her faith, but rather an opportunity to deepen it.
