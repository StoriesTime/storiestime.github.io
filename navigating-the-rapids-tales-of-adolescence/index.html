Navigating the Rapids: Tales of Adolescence


Early mornings were nothing the Miller’s family hadn’t dealt with since Timmy, their youngest of three, was born. The tiny village of Parson’s Creek was nestled along the swift-flowing river, providing every generation with life’s necessities - not only in food, but in education and employment, too.

As the sun gently rose behind the distant forest, mother Charlotte stirred the children gently, whispering the auld words her own mother had whispered to her.

“Timmy, remember,” she’d say, her warm breath creating clouds in the cool air, “The river teaches us many things. It’s swift, unpredictable, and can be harsh, but it also nourishes and brings us great gifts.”

For Timmy, now nearing his fourteenth year, the river held a special fascination; it was a feeling other boys in the village couldn’t understand. Timmy’s dreams dreamt of the river, his thoughts ebbed and flowed like the currents itself.

On this particular morning, Timmy rose earlier than usual, inebriated with anticipation, and decided to leave a few steps before his usual walking trail, wandering into an area he had never explored. Crossing over a squishy marsh beneath his worn, sturdy boots, he quickly discovered an entrance to a hidden cave along the riverbank. As the daylight dimmed to a soft murmur and echoed off the chalky cave walls, Timmy could feel his heart beat faster.

His foot caught on a rock, and he tumbled forward. In the dim light, he saw multiple glints flash from a vast array of objects scattered across the cave floor. As his eyes adjusted, he found himself awestruck by the collection of pirate-cherry-wood flute instruments nestled in the depths of the once-looted den. Borrowing every parent’s guidance but his own father’s, Timmy gingerly picked up a flute with intricate carvings and began gently playing an impromptu tune – to which, the once-quiet cave responded with echos most melodic.

Time seemed to stand still as he immersed himself in the moment; only the pulsating daylight from the cave’s entrance caught his attention. Through the mouthpiece of the wooden flute, he heard a sound entirely out of place.

Gravel and rocks skittered onto the muddy cave floor. Turning around, Timmy’s face was met with a wide-beamed, spindly-legged flashlight wielded by his older brother, Henry. Timmy, who’d thought he had found solitude, now braced himself for at least ten minutes of older brother ridicule.

“Why would you wander off our usual trail?! Are you trying to impress the girls?” Henry mocked, eliciting a suppressed grin on Timmy’s face. He knew full well the significance girls held for Henry.

This particular exchange, between animosity and banter, was interrupted by the faint tremble of the earth and the sound of spraying water. As the brothers stared, gaping with mouths agape, the once-narrow cave widened reflexively; the ceiling of the cave cracked open, allowing in the magnificent azure river. What once was an empty cave was now the entrance to a natural rapid.

In a rush of water, vibrant with life, fish swam playfully. Timmy couldn’t help but think that this was his destiny, standing here between one world and the next. Watching as the water grew impatient, waiting to meet the rhythmic current of the river.

Henry, ever the protector, urged his younger sibling onto the newfound boulder protruding into the river. Timmy hesitated, feeling the strong current trying to tug him into the flow. But the pull grew too strong… He took a breath, closed his eyes, and leapt.

The ripples of the water greeted him welcome as he found himself nestled delicately over the undulating surge. His heart roared louder than the churning waters as he found his balance and rhythm. His body, moving in harmony with the river, felt like nothing he had ever experienced – even his flute-playing in the cave.

Timmy, now a natural navigator, charged his way through rapids more treacherous than he initially imagined. Those agile muscles, honed by years of farming and chasing ducks through Parson’s meadows, served him well as he braced and positioned himself over changing waves, proactively diving and arching in-tune with each turn.

His brother, an expert kayaker, seemed an unlikely companion. Yet, the pressure of watching Timmy perform frantically and forcefully provided Henry with an unexpected opportunity: He swiftly navigated to a large rock not far from the new river mouth. There, he tied a rope securely. Timing his jump against the momentum of the cascading water, he successfully grabbed Timmy’s outstretched hand in a firm grip.

As the sun refused to break free from the forest’s shadow, Henry and Timmy deftly secured the rope around a sturdy tree and hoisted the impromptu raft they had never planned. Over boulders. Through waves, up rivers. Their unspoken trust in one another grew visibly stronger with the river’s strength.

In spite of the rivulets coursing down their faces, Timmy and Henry shared knowing smiles, laughter, and tears, navigating the rapids of adolescence that day. They allowed their lives to follow the swift and unpredictable river, but ultimately they found moments of harmony and nourishment; their own personal and precious treasures.

Through weathering countless rapids, trials, and tribulations, the brothers emerged even stronger in their bond than they were those years ago. We are like the river, Timmy thought, guiding one another through life, emerging onto the open seas of adulthood, with gifts of wisdom and memories that nourish us still.
