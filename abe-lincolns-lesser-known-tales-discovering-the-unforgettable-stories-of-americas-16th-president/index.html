Abe Lincoln's Lesser-Known Tales: Discovering the Unforgettable Stories of America's 16th President


Abe Lincoln had a story for every corner of his life, and these were the lesser-known tales that molded the 16th president into the man he became. From his childhood days exploring the wilderness of Indiana to his friendships with prominent figures of his time, these stories paint a vivid picture of Lincoln’s life before and during his presidency.

A Young Stranger in New Salem

New Salem was a small village in Illinois, a place where simple yet meaningful stories unfolded. In his early adulthood, Lincoln arrived in this village seeking a new beginning. He worked as a clerk in a general store and later as a surveyor. During his time in New Salem, he met the love of his life, Ann Rutledge, who sadly passed away before they could marry. But despite the tragedy, his time in New Salem profoundly shaped his life.

One day, a group of young men bet that Lincoln couldn’t flat-sealed a barrel of whiskey in two days. If he could, they would have to give him a cigar, a bottle of wine, and the confidence of 217 young ladies. Lincoln embraced the challenge and sealed the barrel by himself, gaining respect and admiration from his fellow New Salemites.

The Unique Friendship with Joshua Speed

Lincoln’s friendship with Joshua Speed, a wealthy dry goods merchant from St. Louis, was unlike any other. Their bond started when Speed offered Lincoln a place to sleep in his store, since he had no place to rest his head. Lincoln accepted Speed’s offer, and the two soon became inseparable.

Speed would often visit Lincoln in his cozy space above the store, where they would discuss politics and life. When the two parted ways, Speed presented Lincoln with a last gift - a pocket watch with a gold chain, to which Lincoln gratefully replied, “I deplore any talk which shall be given me in this visit}

{You’ve got me thus far.”

Speed’s generosity and support meant a great deal to Lincoln, and this friendship remained one of the most unique aspects of his life.

Riding the Circuit

After leaving New Salem, Lincoln continued his law practice by riding the circuit. He would bike from courthouse to courthouse around the Sangamon County, offering legal advice to the people residing in remote areas.

One day, while riding the circuit, a customer at a country store stopped Lincoln to ask him for his opinion on the ongoing political debates. Lincoln wrote out his thoughts and handed them over to the customer, who happened to be Stephen A. Douglas. This chance encounter between two future rivals laid the groundwork for the intense political relationships that would unfold in the years to come.

These stories are just a small glimpse into the life of Abraham Lincoln. Beyond his role as the 16th president, he was a man who experienced love, tragedy, friendship, and ambition. These stories serve as a reminder of the human side of America’s most revered leader, and they help to preserve the rich history of the man who led the nation through its most challenging times. From his childhood days in Indiana to the friendships he forged along the way, these lesser-known tales of Lincoln’s life offer a much richer understanding of the man himself.
