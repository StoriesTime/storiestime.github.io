Shadows Within: Psychological Short Stories


The first step they had taken into this rundown old house, and the echoes reverberated ominously around them. It was if the place was alive. Like a palpable force, an aura of impending dread crept up on them, and even the bright daylight couldn’t stop the darkness from enveloping everything.

The Shadows Within

Samantha and Mark laughed nervously as they entered the shabby little abode. They were best friends since childhood, a bond that fortitude only, and now after many years of friendship, they have decided to embark upon the unjustifiable journey of exploring the supposedly haunted house in the outskirts of the city.

“Finally, we’re doing it,” Samantha giggled, half in excitement, half in fear. She had a rousing spirit about her and was ready to take in any ordeal.

Mark simply nodded, determined yet weary, “Let’s go.”

As they stepped through the worm-eaten door and into the hollow shell of a house, a decrepit stench hit them, wasting no time in welcoming them. Dull paint chipped off the walls, and dusty webs blanketed the corners that seemed to be acuminate with foreboding.

Suddenly, out of nowhere, a cold gust of wind swept past them, causing the worn-out wooden windowpanes to rattle. Margery, Samantha’s hand, tightened, emitting a faint shriek, “I don’t like this,” she whispered.

Mark patted her reassuringly, “It’s alright. Let’s just have a look around.”

And they did. They wandered through the empty chambers, older than the city itself, haunted by memories that defied time. Dusty furniture stood neglected in the rooms, as if awaiting the final occupants. In the center of the house, a staircase tapered into a black hole.

While they stared at it, a strange, unnatural darkness became more prominent in one of the rooms upstairs. It appeared odd because the sunlight pouring through the broken windows seemed unable to touch it. The darkness seemed daringly to defy the day, whispering in chilling resonating waves, “Come closer.”

“Samantha, do you see that shadows dancing around over there?” Mark questioned as he pointed eagerly at a shadow seemingly performing an act of its own accord on the opposite end of the hallway.

Samantha looked, and with a splash of light from the setting sun, that darkness spun with sinister force and sailed through the air toward them.

“Run!” Samantha screamed, breaking free from the grip of Pariah’s illusion.

Neither of the friends realized what had occurred. Their laughter dissolved into a spine-chilling scream. Instinctively reactionary reflexes fueled their escape as the inky shadow pursued in hot, suffocating gusts, its unnatural allure terrifying.

Shadow and sunlight collided in a explosive fury when Samantha yanked open the front door. Briefly there was a sound like distance and mind itself turned inside out, a rushing waitress-until egress vocalized in defeated surrender. And then, an abrupt silence, suffocating and ominous.

Mark and Samantha lie on the ground, panting heavily in short, ragged breaths. Every so often, Samantha would turn to Mark, fear brimming in her wide eyes. Trembling, she whispered, “Why did we come here?”

Mark drank in the sight of her, and reasoned softly, “Because we were able to run.”

He reached out a steadying hand, and she took it, strong in necessity. Together, they climbed to their feet, and into the enveloping sear and sizzle of the setting sun, they walked away from the haunted house.

An eerie evening haze filled the air as they disappeared in the distance. What had transpired in the forsaken house would remain a mystery, but of one thing Mark and Samantha were sure - there were shadows within, but not all were willing to stay in the darkness.
