Echoes from the Past: A Paranormal Horror


The small town of Eldermoor had always been a place steeped in mystery, even for those who lived there. Nestled deep in the woods, it was as if time itself had forgotten about the town, leaving it a relic of an older era. The houses stood in rows, weathered and sagging, their paint peeling off in strips like old skin. The streets, often quiet, echoed with the occasional creak of wooden shutters and the rustle of leaves stirred by a chilling breeze.

People who lived here, most of them elderly or long-time residents, spoke in hushed tones about the past—stories of strange occurrences, sightings of apparitions, and unexplained sounds that would drift across the town at night. But none of them dared to speak too loudly, and none of them ever went into the woods after dark.

For Emily, the town had always felt like a place frozen in time. She had grown up here, playing in the overgrown fields and abandoned barns, unaware of the darker whispers that haunted the town’s history. But that was before she had left for college, before she had lived a life outside Eldermoor. Now, after several years away, Emily had returned to settle her late grandmother’s affairs.

She had never been close to her grandmother. The old woman had always seemed distant, her sharp eyes piercing through any attempt at conversation, as if she could see things that others couldn’t. The house Emily had inherited was an old Victorian, perched on the edge of town, just at the border of the forest. It was a house that had been passed down through generations, and Emily couldn’t help but feel a sense of unease when she stepped over the threshold for the first time in years.

The air inside was thick with dust and the smell of aged wood. The floors creaked under her feet as she moved through the rooms, each one more silent and still than the last. The furniture, although old, was carefully maintained, but it was clear that this was not a home meant for living. It was more like a shrine, a relic of something long gone.

Emily’s grandmother had never been one to keep much company, and there were no photographs of her family on the walls. Instead, the house was filled with strange trinkets—objects that looked out of place, as though they didn’t belong in this time at all. Old books with faded covers lined the shelves, and the faint smell of incense lingered in the corners of the rooms.

As Emily began the process of sorting through her grandmother’s belongings, she stumbled upon something that she had never expected to find—an old journal, hidden beneath a floorboard in the attic. It was a leather-bound book, its pages yellowed with age. There were no markings on the cover, and as Emily opened it, she found that the handwriting was not her grandmother’s, but something far older.

The first entry was dated in the early 1800s.

November 1st, 1823

The whispers have begun again. They start in the dead of night, drifting through the cracks in the walls and the floorboards. At first, I thought it was nothing more than the wind, but then I heard them clearly—voices, speaking in a language I do not recognize. I have heard them for weeks now, and I can feel their presence, pressing against the air around me.

Tonight, they were louder than ever before. They came from the forest, just beyond the edge of the house. There is something in those woods—something that watches us, waiting. It is only a matter of time before it comes for us.

The entry chilled Emily to the bone. It was as though the writer had been speaking directly to her. She couldn’t explain it, but she could almost hear the echoes of the words in her mind. The hairs on the back of her neck stood up, and she quickly flipped to the next page.

November 2nd, 1823

I can no longer deny the truth. The whispers are not of this world. They are something else—something ancient, something that has been trapped in these woods for centuries. I have seen it, too—just a glimpse, a shadow moving in the trees. It is tall, far taller than any man, with long, gnarled fingers and eyes that burn like embers. I cannot say what it is, but I know it is not of this earth.

I fear I am losing my mind. But I cannot ignore the truth any longer.

Emily slammed the journal shut, her heart racing. What had her grandmother been involved in? What was this thing in the woods? She felt an overwhelming urge to leave, but something deep inside her compelled her to stay. There were answers here—answers that she had to find.

As the sun dipped below the horizon, Emily’s curiosity pulled her toward the forest. The trees stood tall, their twisted branches reaching toward the sky like skeletal hands. The wind howled through the canopy, a low, mournful sound that echoed through the woods. She knew the path—she had walked it countless times as a child, but tonight, it felt different. The air was thick with tension, as though the forest itself was holding its breath.

The deeper she ventured, the more the shadows seemed to stretch and twist, taking on shapes that were impossible to explain. She tried to convince herself that it was just the darkness playing tricks on her eyes, but she couldn’t shake the feeling that something was watching her.

And then, she heard it—the whisper. Faint at first, like the rustling of leaves, but then it grew louder, more distinct. A voice, calling her name. Emily froze, her heart pounding in her chest. The voice was soft, yet filled with a strange urgency.

“Emily… come closer…”

She couldn’t explain why, but she felt an overwhelming compulsion to follow the voice. It was as though it was calling to her from the very depths of the forest, urging her to come closer, to discover the truth.

As she moved deeper into the woods, the trees parted, revealing a small clearing. In the center of the clearing stood an ancient stone structure, covered in moss and vines. It looked out of place, as though it didn’t belong in this world at all. The stones were carved with strange symbols, and as Emily approached, she felt a surge of energy—a pulse that seemed to resonate deep within her chest.

The whispering grew louder, and Emily could hear the voice clearly now. “It is time, Emily. Time to remember…”

Suddenly, the ground beneath her feet began to tremble, and the air grew thick with an unnatural energy. The stones of the structure began to glow, faint at first, but growing brighter with each passing second. Emily stepped back, her heart racing in terror, but she couldn’t tear her eyes away from the pulsing light.

And then, she saw it.

A figure emerged from the shadows, tall and thin, with eyes that glowed like fire. Its face was a grotesque mask of twisted features, its mouth hanging open in a silent scream. It reached out toward her, its long, gnarled fingers stretching toward her with an unnatural speed.

In a panic, Emily turned to run, but the forest had changed. The path that she had once known so well was now a labyrinth of twisting trees and shifting shadows. She ran blindly, her heart pounding in her chest, the sound of the creature’s footsteps echoing behind her.

But no matter how fast she ran, she couldn’t escape the whispers. They filled her mind, drowning out all other thoughts, urging her to remember. She stumbled and fell, her hands scraping against the rough bark of the trees.

Then, she saw it—the clearing. She had returned to the stone structure, but it was different now. The stones were no longer glowing, and the air felt heavy with a sense of finality. The creature was gone, but the whispering remained, echoing through her mind.

As she stood there, trying to catch her breath, she realized that she had not been alone in the forest. The whispers were not just from the past—they were echoes, fragments of something that had once been. The thing in the woods, whatever it was, had been waiting for her. Waiting for her to return.

Emily didn’t understand why, but she knew that her grandmother had known. She had left her a message, a warning. The echoes from the past were not just memories—they were real. And they would not let her go.

With a final, terrified glance at the darkening woods, Emily ran back toward the safety of her grandmother’s house, knowing that the whispers would follow her.
