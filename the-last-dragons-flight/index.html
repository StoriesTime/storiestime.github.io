The Last Dragon's Flight


The wind howled as the towering peaks of the Jindara mountains stood like silent sentinels beneath the pale moonlight. A storm had been brewing for days, and yet it wasn’t the rain or the thunder that people feared. It was the prophecy, whispered for generations, that the last dragon was preparing for its final flight.

For centuries, dragons had soared through the skies of Myrrh, bringing both wonder and terror. They were creatures of legend, their wings like vast sails of midnight cloth, their scales shimmering with the fire of a thousand sunsets. Yet, even the mightiest of creatures were bound by time. The last of them, an ancient dragon named Ysara, had lived longer than most could remember, her presence a symbol of both strength and the inevitable passage of time.

Ysara had never been seen by the common folk—she had long since retreated to the highest of the Jindara peaks, where she built her lair amid the jagged rock faces. Few dared to approach her, for the dragon’s fury was well-known. Yet, despite her reclusiveness, the legend persisted, and as each year passed, her wings grew weaker. It was said that when the last dragon finally took flight, it would signal the end of an era—an end to both dragons and magic itself.

A prophecy of such magnitude could not go unnoticed. Throughout the land, scholars, adventurers, and those with hidden agendas alike had begun to gather near the mountains, all in search of Ysara’s final journey. But they were not the only ones who sought her out.

Eliora was one of the few who had spent her life studying the ancient texts and prophecies. The daughter of a renowned historian, she had grown up immersed in the lore of dragons and the ever-fading magic of Myrrh. While others saw the dragon’s final flight as the end of something precious, Eliora saw it as an opportunity. It was said that the dragon’s death would bring forth a surge of power, one that could restore the fading magic of the land, and perhaps, even grant immortality to whoever harnessed it.

But Eliora had never been one to seek power for its own sake. There was something else in the dragon’s tale that called to her—an unspeakable truth buried beneath the centuries of myths and legends. She had to find Ysara, not for the magic or the glory, but for answers. Answers to questions that had haunted her family for generations. Answers to the origins of the dragons themselves.

Chapter One: The Journey Begins

The climb to Jindara’s peak was treacherous, and Eliora had prepared for every possible hazard. Her boots crunched through the snow, her breath forming clouds in the frigid air. A blizzard was closing in, but she pressed on, her determination unwavering. She was alone on this journey, as her father’s warnings had kept her from bringing anyone along. “No one will be able to help you when the time comes,” he had said, his voice grave with the weight of his knowledge. “The dragon’s fate is bound to the world’s, and no one can change it.”

But Eliora refused to believe that. She couldn’t. She had always questioned the boundaries of the known world, always reached for the forbidden truths hidden in dusty tomes. And so, she would not be deterred by fear or caution.

As she ascended the mountain’s jagged cliffs, the storm intensified, and the winds threatened to push her back into the valley below. But then, just as the storm seemed unbearable, she saw it. A silhouette against the dim glow of the sky—an enormous shape, moving with the grace of a storm cloud. A great winged figure, with scales like silvered glass, glistening despite the darkness.

Ysara.

Eliora’s heart raced as she watched the dragon glide effortlessly through the air, her wings cutting through the wind with a fluidity that defied her age. The dragon’s flight was slow and deliberate, as if she was savoring each moment of it. The weight of centuries rested on her, but still, she was magnificent.

And then, for the first time, Ysara’s eyes met hers.

There was no roar, no grand gesture of acknowledgement. The dragon simply hovered, her massive form suspended in the air as she studied the young woman below. Eliora, despite the overwhelming awe that filled her, did not flinch. She knew that this moment, this connection, was not just the meeting of a mortal and an immortal being—it was the convergence of two destinies.

“You have come,” Ysara’s voice echoed in her mind, deep and ancient. “I have been waiting.”

Eliora opened her mouth to speak, but no words came. Her mind was filled with a thousand questions, but she did not know where to begin. Instead, she simply nodded, her gaze never leaving the dragon.

Ysara continued, her voice like a distant thunderclap. “You seek answers, little one. But know this—the truth is a heavy burden, and some questions are best left unanswered.”

Eliora’s heart skipped a beat. The dragon knew her purpose. She had never told anyone of her quest, yet Ysara had seen through her, as if she were a book with pages open.

“I have no choice,” Eliora replied, her voice steady despite the turmoil within. “The truth is my inheritance. I must know why the dragons came to be, and why they must fade.”

Ysara’s wings shifted, causing the air around her to hum with power. “You will know,” she said. “But be warned—the knowledge you seek will come at a price.”

Chapter Two: The Dragon’s Legacy

Ysara led Eliora into the heart of the mountain, where the cave’s walls glowed with a strange, ethereal light. The air grew warmer the deeper they ventured, until the cold of the outside world felt like a distant memory.

The dragon’s lair was vast, filled with treasures and relics from eras long gone. The walls were adorned with intricate carvings, ancient symbols that pulsed with a soft, golden light. There, among the forgotten relics, were the remnants of a time when dragons ruled not just the skies, but the very fabric of magic itself.

“Do you see?” Ysara’s voice whispered, reverberating through the cave. “The dragons were once the keepers of this world’s balance. But over time, the land began to wither, and so did we. Our magic, once pure and boundless, began to fade.”

Eliora stepped closer to one of the carvings, her fingers tracing the symbols. “Why?” she asked, her voice a mixture of awe and confusion. “What caused the dragons to fade?”

Ysara’s form shimmered, and for a moment, Eliora saw a vision—a vision of a time long before her birth. The world was lush and vibrant, and dragons soared through the skies, their scales glittering like the stars. But as the vision shifted, the skies darkened. The land grew barren, and the dragons began to disappear.

“A curse,” Ysara murmured. “A curse cast upon us by the very forces we once protected. We were bound to the magic of the land, and when the land began to die, so did we.”

Eliora’s heart tightened as she pieced the story together. “But if the dragons die, then so does the magic of Myrrh?”

Ysara nodded solemnly. “Yes. My death marks the end of an era. When I take my final flight, the magic of this world will vanish. The land will become like any other—empty and forgotten.”

Eliora took a step back, her mind racing. “Is there nothing that can be done? Can’t the dragons be restored?”

The dragon’s eyes softened. “It is too late for that. The magic has already begun to dissipate. My flight will mark the final act of our legacy.”

Eliora’s heart ached, but she knew that she could not accept this ending. She had to do something. She had come for answers, but she needed more. She needed a way to save the dragons—and the land.

Chapter Three: The Final Flight

The time had come. Ysara had said her farewells, and Eliora stood alone at the precipice of the highest peak, watching as the last dragon prepared for her final flight.

The storm had cleared, and the skies were eerily calm. The air felt heavy, as though the world itself was holding its breath. In the distance, Eliora could see Ysara’s massive form, silhouetted against the rising sun. With a final, mighty roar, the dragon spread her wings wide, her body trembling with the weight of centuries.

Then, with a powerful beat of her wings, Ysara took flight.

Eliora’s heart pounded as she watched the dragon soar into the heavens. There was no sadness in the dragon’s flight, no regret. It was a release—a return to the stars from which they had once come.

But as Ysara ascended, a strange phenomenon occurred. The land beneath her began to stir. The trees seemed to breathe, the ground pulsed with energy, and the air shimmered with a newfound vitality. The magic, it seemed, was not dying. It was shifting.

The final flight of the dragon did not mark the end—it marked a new beginning.

Ysara’s wings began to glow, her body becoming a living constellation of light. And with that, the last dragon disappeared into the sky, leaving behind a world transformed.

Eliora stood there for a long time, watching the dragon’s light fade into the stars. The magic of Myrrh had not died. It had evolved, changed, and found a new form.

And for the first time in her life, Eliora understood. Some endings were not endings at all. They were simply the beginning of something new.
