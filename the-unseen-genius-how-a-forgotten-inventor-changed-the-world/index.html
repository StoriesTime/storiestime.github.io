In a small, cluttered workshop nestled in the heart of 19th century London, a brilliant inventor toiled in obscurity. His name was forgotten, and his works lay ignored. Yet, it was this very individual who would irrevocably alter the course of history. The Neglected Inventor Godfrey Wynton, a man of unassuming appearance and humble demeanor, spent most of his days tinkering with discarded machinery and experimenting with unconventional materials. His obsession was not fame or fortune but the pursuit of innovation and the quest to improve the human condition. Wynton's fascination with the intricacies of mechanics and thermodynamics led him to develop a revolutionary system of atmospheric ignition. This breakthrough innovation paired the use of flammable gases with precise temperature control, paving the way for the modern internal combustion engine.
