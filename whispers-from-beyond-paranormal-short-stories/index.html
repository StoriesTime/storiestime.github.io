Whispers from Beyond: Paranormal Short Stories


The night air was thick with silence, broken only by the occasional rustle of leaves in the old forest that bordered the town of Eldridge. It was a place that many locals avoided after sunset, weaving legends of spirits that lingered between the trees and shadows that watched from the darkness. For those who had experienced the whispers from beyond, Eldridge held stories that defied explanation—tales where the veil between the living and the spectral grew thin.

The House on Raven’s Hollow

Mara had never believed in ghosts until she inherited the sprawling, dilapidated house on Raven’s Hollow. It had belonged to her great-uncle, a recluse who disappeared without a trace decades ago. The townsfolk had whispered that the house was cursed, haunted by restless souls who never crossed over.

When Mara first stepped inside, the air was cold and stale, thick with dust and memories. The floorboards groaned beneath her feet as she explored the long corridors, past peeling wallpaper and shuttered windows. As twilight sank in, a whispering wind seemed to breathe through the broken glass panes. Then she heard it—soft voices, almost imperceptible, calling her name from the shadows.

Determined to understand, Mara set up a voice recorder, hoping to capture any paranormal activity. Over the next several nights, the recordings revealed faint murmurings—fragments of conversations, echoes of sorrow and warning. One night, among the ghostly fragments, a clear message emerged: “Find the locket beneath the willow.”

Compelled by the mysterious instruction, Mara walked to the ancient willow tree on the edge of the property. Digging with bare hands, she unearthed a tarnished silver locket, engraved with initials she didn’t recognize. As she opened it, a sudden rush of cold air swept past her, and an apparition took form—a sorrowful woman in faded Victorian attire.

The spirit revealed herself as Eliza, a young woman who had lived in the house a century ago, bound to the mortal realm by heartbreak and betrayal. The locket was the key to setting her free. Mara promised to return it to the rightful heir, piecing together a family connection lost to time.

With the locket in hand, Mara crossed town to the nearby cemetery, placing it on Eliza’s neglected grave. The air shimmered briefly, and Eliza’s spirit finally found peace, whispering a heartfelt thank you before dissolving into the night.

The Silent Phone Calls

Jonas, a night-shift paramedic, began receiving silent phone calls at precisely 3:15 AM every Thursday. At first, he assumed it was a prank—no one on the other end, just the unnerving sound of breathing and static. But week after week, the calls persisted, each one draining a bit of his energy and filling him with a growing sense of dread.

One night, curiosity outweighed fear. Jonas answered, staying on the line to hear if anything would happen. This time, amidst the silence, he heard a faint, desperate whisper: “Help me.”

Unable to shake the voice from his mind, Jonas investigated old dispatch records, tracing the origin of the calls to a house abandoned years ago after a tragic fire. Legend had it that a woman named Sarah Hart perished in the blaze, trapped and calling out in her final moments.

Gathering his courage, Jonas entered the charred remains of the house. Among the rubble, a small, blackened diary caught his eye. Flipping through its scorched pages, he found entries describing Sarah’s torment and her final pleas for rescue.

That night, Jonas placed a recording device among the ruins and waited. The next call came, but this time the voice was clearer, less desperate, as if relieved: “Thank you.”

From that day forward, the phone stopped ringing. Jonas never forgot the experience—a haunting reminder that sometimes the echoes of the past reach out for connection, even from beyond.

The Lighthouse Keeper’s Secret

Perched on the cliffside overlooking Eldridge Bay stood the abandoned lighthouse, a relic from a bygone era when ships relied on its steady beam to navigate treacherous waters. The last keeper, Thomas Gray, vanished mysteriously in 1926. Locals said the lighthouse harbored a dark secret, guarded by a restless spirit that watched over the sea.

Evelyn, a local historian fascinated by Eldridge’s forgotten tales, decided to explore the lighthouse, hoping to uncover clues about Thomas Gray’s disappearance. Inside, the air was thick with salt and neglect. The winding stairs creaked as she ascended to the lantern room, where the great Fresnel lens once cast its guiding light.

As dusk approached, Evelyn noticed strange reflections dancing within the lens, like ghostly ships sailing across the sea. Whispers carried on the wind grew louder, telling a tale of betrayal and sacrifice.

That night, visions flooded Evelyn’s mind—Thomas Gray standing on the cliff during a violent storm, signaling ships away from submerged rocks that claimed many lives. He had made a devastating choice, diverting a cargo vessel full of townspeople to save a larger passenger liner, sacrificing himself in the process.

Evelyn chronicled the story, ensuring Thomas Gray’s heroism would be remembered. She left the lighthouse with a sense of peace, the whispers fading into a gentle murmur, as if the keeper finally rested.

Unseen Guests at the Inn

The Rosewood Inn had hosted travelers for over a century, its aged walls steeped in stories and secrets. The current owner, Clara, noticed unusual happenings shortly after reopening for the season—chairs shifted on their own, footsteps echoed when halls were empty, and cold spots gathered near Room 13.

Guests reported waking up feeling watched, sensing a presence nearby that couldn’t be explained. Clara, intrigued and not easily spooked, decided to investigate with the help of a paranormal research group.

Setting up cameras and sensors throughout the inn, they recorded eerie phenomena: shadow figures glimpsed briefly in corridors, disembodied voices captured at night, and mysterious cold drafts.

The investigation revealed the inn had once been a safe haven during a pandemic decades before, where a nurse named Margaret cared for the ill until her untimely death. Her spirit lingered—not malevolent, but tethered by a desire to protect others.

In a moving séance, Clara and the researchers communicated with Margaret, reassuring her that the inn was a place of warmth and healing. From that night on, the strange occurrences diminished, replaced by a gentle, comforting presence that many guests now felt as a silent guardian watching over them.

These tales from Eldridge remind us that the paranormal isn’t always about terror or malevolence. Sometimes, it’s about lingering memories, unfinished stories, and whispers seeking understanding. The unknown offers a gateway to histories untold, emotions unexpressed, and connections beyond the mortal coil.

If you happen to visit Eldridge or stumble upon the old house on Raven’s Hollow, the silent phone calls, or the lighthouse’s fading beam, listen closely. The whispers from beyond might just have something to tell you.
