The Unspoken Stories of Shanghai's Bund: A Historical Fiction


As the sun dipped below the horizon, the sky over the Bund turned a deep shade of pink. It was the end of the 19th century, and the British colonial period in Shanghai was in full swing. The bund, a waterfront promenade, boasted of Victorian architecture, and the sound of English music filled the air. However, there was one man who found himself out of place amidst the Western chaos.

Guangzhou Liang strode confidently along the pavement, his gait reflecting his loneliness. His eyes gazed at the şehre, the foreign buildings his home was no longer. Liang’s childhood was spent in a low-roofed, ornate Qing-dynasty house, a stone’s throw away from where he now found himself. But times have changed and so had he. Dressed in a Western suit, Liang had fully assimilated into his adopted identity – Richard Lee.

Liang’s life changed when he was introduced to Mr. Richardson, a successful British businessman. As Liang worked his way up the ranks, the world around him changed. His home was no longer a part of China but a commodity to be traded. In this world of commerce and politics, Liang found himself torn between two worlds.

His eyes rested on a group of Western women laughing together, he averted his gaze quickly. Liang felt the weight of his transformation. Forced into the world of deception and a new identity, he longed for the old days of simplicity. His heart yearned for the streets of old Shanghai and the stories that lay tucked away in the corners of his mind.

Chapter Two: A Fateful Encounter

During one of his trips to the bank, Liang found himself lost in a labyrinth of memories. Gazing out of the floor-to-ceiling windows, he saw an elderly woman weaving her way through the pedestrians. This time, instead of a Western garment, Liang recognized her traditional Chinese dress. In his mind, he saw her as a child, playing in the bylanes of Old Shanghai, her hair in pigtails, scurrying away from him as he chased her.

A sudden gust of wind made Liang’s reflection in the window shiver. Turning around, he saw a girl, her eyes mirroring the past. It was her, the little girl from his childhood whom he had almost forgotten. Time had not aged her well, it had merely polished her. In her arms, she carried a worn, leather-bound book.

As Liang approached her, the girl’s eyes locked onto his, and he saw the recognition. A Western suit, silver hair, and a pair of glasses later, he found himself in the presence of the past. As they exchanged pleasantries, Liang discovered that the girl, now Mrs. Hu, was a collector of old stories from the Bund. She had inherited her mother’s love for the tales their city whispered through its history. Listening to her, Liang felt approval, a gratification for his role in making this world what it was today.

Chapter Three: The Stories Unfurl

Over tea and fireworks, the duo delved into the murky depths of Shanghai’s history. Each narrative Mrs. Hu recited seemed to unravel the city’s fabric like a beautiful ballerina, pirouetting and twirling her way through time.

The story of the " xiaolongbao “, or soup dumplings, and how Liang’s grandfather, a street vendor, had transformed the culinary landscape of Shanghai with his invention, brought tears to Liang’s eyes. The tale of the " red-person book " mesmerized him with its intelligence, as he found his roots in the person who peddled those books in the streets of Old Shanghai.

Yet, one tale struck a chord that Liang had long forgotten. Mrs. Hu narrated the story of the “Unspoken Stories of the Bund,” a narrative of how the whispering walls of the Bund had tales etched into its bricks. Stories so potent, so meaningful that they refused to be voiced, living silently in the enigmatic streets of Shanghai. Liang’s heart beat faster as he realized the significance. If these stories remained unspoken, he was their mouthpiece – a man living in two worlds.

Chapter Four: The Meeting of the Worlds

The sun was preparing to say its final goodbyes to the Bund when Liang and Mrs. Hu came face to face with the future. Mrs. Hu’s son, a young man compelled by modernity and Western ideals, rejoiced in their conversation. The two worlds that Liang had lived between found themselves present in this moment. The traditional woman from Old Shanghai intertwined with the Western man of the 19th century, creating a new strand of reality.

Liang felt alive, his heart overflowing with stories that could only be his. For years, he’d kept these tales bottled up. But, in that moment, he realized his past and his present had to meet – and through this convergence, set his spirit free. Liang looked up, his eyes glistening under the moonlight.

And so, the past and present embraced, sharing the stories that the Bund had whispered for decades. For Liang, the once silent walls finally found a voice, and the “Unspoken Stories of the Bund” echoed through the hearts of those who heard them.

This was not just a tale of a man sandwiched between two worlds; it was a story of the heart’s relentless yearning for truth, of human spirit’s undying courage, of life’s endless dance of the past and the present.
