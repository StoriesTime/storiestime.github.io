Beyond the Coins: The Humanity in Philanthropy


In a sky-blue dawn, the city stirred gently to life. The streets hummed with the melody of morning activity; storekeepers unlocked the iron fronts of their shops, while street vendors embarked on their daily journeys carrying wicker baskets brimming with fresh produce. Amidst the cacophony of tangled voices, hearts beating with countless desires, one impactful name resonated through the cobblestone streets like a tide - Moncton Charity Foundation.

Just beyond the shimmering coins of charity, past the corridors and hallways of this imposing institution thrives a world that few dare speak of - the humanity in philanthropy.

In the dim basement of a once-swanky Victorian-style building, a young, inspired man named Evan worked tirelessly assembling intricate mech suits. His hands were the architects of invention, weaving a dream into existence.

The creation he hoped would change the world bore the heavy moniker, “Project Century.” Brushing a rebellious strand of chestnut hair away from his eyes, Evan turned the controller in his hand. The metallic arms of the suit awakened, the gears hissing and whirring in unison. A stream of sunshine haloed the genius at work, casting shadows over the towering sculptures that surrounded him - products of his relentless pursuit for change.

Evan had been laboring unfalteringly on this invention for the better part of a decade. His father, a renowned neurosurgeon, never quite let go of his son’s dream of revolutionizing society – a humanitarian riddled dream that perhaps stemmed from the same tender flower that had blossomed into the Moncton Charity Foundation.

The good doctor passed away a few years back, his last breaths bottled up within the corridors of Moncton, never getting the chance to witness the dawn of Project Century. And so, it became Evan’s solemn duty to carry on their legacy, air-dancing on the heels of his father’s once-domineering footsteps.

“The world needs more than just charity,” his father had said, sun glinting in his feverish eyes. “It needs solutions.”

Hence came the beguiling beauty of Project Century – to fill the unmet needs that charity could nuzzle but never truly solve. The suit was not designed for war or sport, but for compassion; a tool of life, with a heart pulsating alongside every gear. Whether navigating through complex urban terrains or soaring over colossal structures, the mech suit could extend love, nourishment and healthcare in places deemed unreachable.

With Project Century, Evan opens the window to a world where humanity and philanthropy dance hand-in-hand, tugging the strings that elevate life to unexplored heights.

In the confining walls of Moncton, beneath the tall portraits of past titans now silently gazing down upon him from above, Evan reflected on the meaning of his work. Such progress could not happen overnight or in isolation.

Indeed, countless were the meetings held with pristinely-coiffed individuals seated around polished wood tables – encounters that often concluded in tight-lipped nods. Yet, synergy was elusive; interlocking the continuum of alms and action seemed like an insurmountable hurdle.

In swift strides, Evan had left the world of cold, calculated professionals, the frigid environment breeding doubt and unease. He sought another outfit – a realm of aviation poets and steadfast engineers, seeking to create the vehicle that would traverse the metal roof of the world, the vanguard of humanitarian impulses.

As he cranked up the old machine in the basement, the whirring of gears synchronized with his pounding heart, a sound rose – the chorus of the city, of those who today counted on charity to see them through to the morrow. These people, undoubtedly, were also dollar signs in the echelons of Moncton, reminder of the invisible thread that tethered him.

But theirs was the story that true philanthropy should seek to erase, the chronicle of struggle that Project Century aimed to rewrite. Was Evan to remain ensnared in the boundaries of Moncton’s generosity, or dare to transcend its walls into the realm of what could be?

Taking a deep breath, he switched off the mech, the silence engulfing the space as the final gears came to a grind. Evan walked out, leaving the prototype untouched – a day off rest erected between him and the dreams that he dared to dare.

People across the city lived their lives as if Project Century was no more than the figment of a restless mind. Grateful nods of well-fed children clutching at the brief reprieve the Moncton Charity Foundation provided, some even lost in daydreams ignited by stories of fantastical flying machines.

They remained oblivious to the untold stories that had molded the forming of this invention, the trials of a relentless man bound between the privilege of philanthropy’s heavy drops and the igniting terror of responsibility.

As he disembarks from one world into another unseen, Evan’s eyes glitter with unspoken hope. The misadventures that reveal the humanity in philanthropy are but a narrative still unwritten, awaiting the intrepid wordsmith of tomorrow, the harvester of dreams unchained from the invisible albatross that hovers by the monolithic coin.

The world, in all its complexity, still lies ahead, waiting with bated breath for our next daring leap beyond the coins.
