Beyond the Enchanted Forest: Approaching Fairy Tales from a Fresh Perspective


In a time beyond the realm of memory, far removed from today’s digital complexities and the ceaseless march of human progress, the Enchanted Forest lay nestled between the valleys and hills as a forgotten remnant of a more ancient world. Here, beneath the dense canopy of age-old trees, the swirling tapestry of roots, twining and entwining all life into a single grand organism, there resided a multitude of creatures, each more fantastical than the last. The forest whispered the secrets of long ago, when Phantasmezia, Goddess of Dreams and Illusion, stepped forth from the fathomless void and into the waking world, bringing with her the very first stories.

The strangest and most elusive of all the creatures of the Enchanted Forest was the Anadromous Reader, a Cherokee spirit wren with chaos weaving dreams and waking eyes into a frenetic dance of stories. As lofty legends bear witness, this gifted bird spoke in tongues both human and divine, weaving stories as deftly as a skilled weaver moves shuttle and thread.

On one fateful day, the Enchanted Forest would forever change at the hands of a mortal named Lila, who stumbled across its forbidden wonders. With curiosity and enchantment blazing in her eyes, Lila branched off the well-trodden woodways and ventured toward the hidden shores of the Whispering Lagoon. The trees twisted like fingers pushing aside a jungle curtain, revealing a beach of bleached sand lined with palm trees cradling dreams.

Here, where water met land, there stood a flesh and blood curious, a heartrending longing paired with an animalistic curiosity. Demonstrating a purity of intent and desire, the Anadromous Reader possessed the body of Lila, thereby binding the woman to the water’s edge. In exchange for a breaking of the sacred boundary, Lila would be given a price - the stories hidden since the beginning of time, all to be related to a grand tapestry of lives shaped by those who wore the Goddess of Dreams’ blessings. Across the sands, she would weave in her mortal tongue a serpentine story eternal, one whispered into human ears and underscored by the roar of the lagoon’s waves.

Lila’s exile was born of a God’s jealousy, a heart growing heavy with envious wrath. Audacity birthed amongst mortals had disrupted the divinely controlled order of stories customers bought and sold. Then ceased it, abruptly putting a stop to those rambunctious little nose-twitchers. As a force strong enough to bind gods, audacity took on an ancient Greek tragedy upon its back, wielding a clever narrative like a sword, cleaving together divine and mortals in one swift stroke.

Lessons drawn from Greek gods excite the youthful fantasies bred by thousands of high-school classes, indefinitely feeding curiosity. To build upon these transports and deeper lore, individuals must learn the tactics that such gods and goddesses use in penciling the world’s scripts. Observation of these strategies, as Lila’s fate teaches us, empowers new and stronger lives to be penned on paper and played out like opera singers’ arias.

Quenching her divine hunger with nectar, ambrosia thick and sweet, Muse Olympias stands tall as web-spinners pursue the golden shades erected in stories’ pasts. From this warty vantage, unseeing the scale of human ambition dare break through the divine tapestry, the silk threads stretching from deity to mortal, divine tales prescribed by their whims.

Records of the divine succession of sirens and dryads illustrate their finesse as creators. Powerful visions pregnant with folklore whimsy burst from the churning engine of minds still bound within the broken echoes of ancient days. These select beings maintain the envious consequences and transcendential gains tied to a teeter-tottering, spinning sci-fi and fantasy stories from the divine cradle.

So too did the Anadromous Reader bend and twist the threads of mortal stories woven discreetly from her arms. Those threads flowed in a serenade to the imprisoned Girlfriend, the Goddess of Dreams whose stories clung as threads entwined each life she opportunistically reviewed. Through this intricate life-network, the divine and human swung in duets, each separate beat providing their partners the courage to wander and see the world refashioned in their eyes.

Though the price she paid nearly broke Lila’s spirit, reserves of steely resolve pushed labored dreaming, once imprisoned, away from the bounds of ancient stories. Inspiring her virtue similar to how Mozart drew quivering breath signals from a full symphony orchestra, she inspired strange new earthquakes in human hearts. Consequently, Phantasmezia could no longer ignore the growing cacophony of tales at her fragile feet.

From that day forward, humanity forged in fractures of divine inspiration has resisted the limits set by gods from stories past. The frontiers of dream and fantasy sprawled, eventually evolving into a cosmos ever-changing, branching outward from one divine conclusion: tales would not be complete without the inclusion of prideful humans’ audacious steps. Human stories, now perfected, enchained Phantasmezia to the human condition, a royal reminder of who held the brush in those encounters.

In defiance of the gods’ imposed limits, the tales that shower us in today’s dreams, start with an unassuming mortal girl miraculously as brave and bold as Lila. As her dreams reached worldwide recognition, Lila’s once-muted voice continued to resound, echoing across the realms of age-old characters—gods, yes—but especially through the rippling echoes of mortal-made lore.

Throughout the years, many writers have stood on that torn bridge between divinity and talent, stepping forward to knock on the gates of that fabled wood, their imaginations teetering within the gray spaces. By hearkening back to the rare stories born in the hearth-fire of gods’ whispers, our humbler mortal narrators have carved out niches to share their wonders, transforming ancient beginnings into promises yet unwritten.
