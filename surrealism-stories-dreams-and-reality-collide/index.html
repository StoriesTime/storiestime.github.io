Surrealism Stories: Dreams and Reality Collide


The clock in the old apartment kitchen ticked backwards, the hands spinning in a hypnotic whirl that twisted time into a spiral staircase. Mara sat at the wooden table, her fingers tracing the wood grain, only to watch it ripple like the surface of an agitated pond. Yet, she felt no surprise anymore; the boundaries between her waking world and the dreams she once whispered to herself about had completely dissolved.

She remembered entering this apartment for the first time: the creaking floorboards, the smell of rain on aged plaster, and the strange certainty that nothing here was ever quite what it seemed. But with each passing week, reality and the uncanny tapestry of her dreams had woven themselves into a single, surreal fabric. It started subtly—small details out of place, colors too vivid or sounds oddly tuned—before spiraling into full-blown episodes where she could no longer tell if she was awake or dreaming.

That morning, the mirror in her hallway didn’t reflect her image but instead revealed a vast desert under a flooded sky, stars swimming like fish in water above swirling sands. As she reached out, her fingertips brushed the cold glass, then passed through it like translucent smoke. On impulse, she stepped forward.

The ground beneath her feet shifted immediately, the solid wood of her floor vanishing into granular dunes that sank with each step she took. The sky above was a fractured mosaic of broken clocks, each ticking differently, their hands moving in erratic patterns. Time fractured here, like a shattered crystal reflecting infinite versions of moments both lived and dreamt.

She wandered through this desert of fractured time and space, eyes wide in fascination rather than fear. A small figure appeared at the horizon—a man with a birdcage for a head, inside which fluttered a hummingbird whose wings beat the rhythm of the tides. Mara walked toward him, her footsteps leaving trails of ink on the sand.

“You’re lost,” the figure said, voice layered like echoes from a cavern, “or perhaps you’ve found where losing is the way to be.”

“Where am I?” Mara asked.

“This is the liminal space between dream and reality. Where stories unravel and words flow like liquid mercury. Where time dissolves, and identities are as fluid as the clouds.”

She thought of her waking life, her routine job in a gray office city, the monotony of days blurring into each other. Here, the rules shifted with every breath. The sand bent upward as she stepped, creating stairways made of wind that led to nowhere and everywhere. Mara climbed, feeling herself shed the skin of ordinary perception.

Suddenly, she found herself in a room filled with bookshelves spiraling into infinity. Each book was a dream she or someone else had dreamed—some whispered secrets, others roared with the intensity of forgotten fears. As she pulled a slender volume from the shelf, the pages fluttered open to reveal symbols and phrases that danced before her eyes, reshaping into fleeting images of cityscapes and faces she could almost recognize.

A voice whispered from the shadows, “Stories are the bridges between worlds. They are the vessels carrying dreams into waking minds and bringing waking fears into the depth of sleep. You are both the reader and the writer here.”

Mara felt the weight of those words sink deep into her consciousness. Every moment melded with the next, like oil spilling across water, merging the fragments of her fractured existence. She understood then that this place—the surreal junction where dreams and reality collided—was not a prison but a playground, a canvas for her mind to paint new possibilities.

She closed the book, and the shelves dissolved, releasing her onto a vast checkerboard floor patterned with shifting shadows and light. From the corner, a white rabbit dressed in a silk waistcoat extended a paw. “Will you play?” it asked, eyes twinkling with riddles.

“Of course,” Mara smiled.

They moved across the board, pieces changing shape and meaning with every step—knights became winged horses, pawns transformed into whispering willows, and rooks towered like ancient trees. The boundary between the controlled and the chaotic blurred, as if logic had been recast in the fluid language of dreams.

As the game unfolded, Mara realized this play was a metaphor for her own journey. In the waking world, she had been trapped by rigid structures and confined identities. Here, in this surreal expanse, she could explore the infinite variations of self—the feared, the desired, the forgotten.

When the rabbit finally tipped its hat and vanished into a cloud of smoke, Mara felt herself drawn back toward the mirror. Stepping through once more, she emerged into her apartment as the clock hands resumed their normal forward march, but something inside her had shifted irreversibly.

From that day on, Mara lived consciously poised between two realms. She saw her waking life as a kind of lucid dream, understanding that reality itself was layered with surreal possibilities waited to be unlocked by courage and imagination.

Her story, like the sprawling spirals of dreams themselves, was always unfolding—between moments, between thoughts, between truths and fantasies—where surrealism breathes life into the ordinary and invites us all to wander beyond the veil, to find new meaning in the ceaseless dance of dreams and reality colliding.
