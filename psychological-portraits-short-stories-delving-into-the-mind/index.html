Psychological Portraits: Short Stories Delving into the Mind


Amelia loved her extraordinary Mind – a place she could run away to when life got too real. And so, with her eyes tightly shut, she painted the world from her bedroom corner with vibrant splashes of thought.

The house on the hill had a secret room, filled with shadows from old paintings and the faint scent of turpentine. Amelia sat at a small wooden table, carefully painting a portrait of her friend, Fiona, whose obsession with oranges matched Amelia’s own kaleidoscopic imagination.

As Amelia’s brush danced on the canvas, Fiona’s features lost their earthly confines and embarked upon a thrilling, bewildering existence. Fiona’s light-filled eyes were suddenly doors to the vast universe; her wavy hair was bouquets of interwoven sunbeams; and her dimples became imprints of enigmatic laughter.

As the afternoon sun filtering through Amelia’s bedroom window stretched and shimmered across the canvas, it dawned on Fiona that the only way to truly capture the person before her was to let go of the desire to frame her in conventional beauty. Instead, a portrait was a recipe of deeply observed moments, memories, habits, and eccentricities – a splendid, inconvenient, scrupulous mind.

Fiona blew out the candles on the birthday cake Amelia had orchestrated for this very moment. The encircling friends clapped and cheered. Fiona beamed, feeling exquisitely seen and applauded by admirers of her complexities. Yet, in the moments after the candles were blown out and the crowd dispersed, a new force began to emerge in Fiona. The veil of vulnerability lifted as she noticed the brush strokes where the mind of another had guided and changed the course of a simple, framed portrait.

Curiosity tapping at her heart, Fiona wanted nothing more than to return Amelia’s favor and give her a portrait in return. Each girl gave the other an envelope with a set of instructions for their self-portrait, an algorithm if you will, that would lead them on an artistic journey.

Amelia examined her instructions, eyebrows furrowed in concentration. The assignment declared that she had to create a portrait of Fiona from a memory. Escape reality, explore Fiona’s memory palace, and expose it through the lens of Amelia’s artistic vision. But which memory would tell the story best? Which would reveal Fiona’s heart and soul in a way that said, “This. This is what you see when you look into the mind of Fiona”?

As Amelia started her task, she realized it wasn’t just any memory that would fulfill this task but the memory of a piece of furniture they had discovered together – that box of lost and found objects Fiona had stumbled upon as a little girl. The nostalgia hit Amelia like a gust of wind, leaving her seeing not only the memory but the mind itself.

Amelia carefully sculpted the memory into the canvas. Fiona stood before the box, legs slightly parted, her fingers grazing over the treasure as if a sudden gust of wind had gifted her the ability to hear the stories of each discarded trinket waiting to be found. Amelia captured the subtle complexities of Fiona’s eyes – downcast but twinkling with anticipation.

As Fiona took the finished portrait, she felt a rush of warmth and familiarity. Amelia had captured not only the trinkets of her memory but the essence of who Fiona was – a young girl enchanted by the discovered world in a forgotten box, and a woman now assuming a place where once undiscovered objects yearned to be appreciated.

When Amelia finally saw the portrait Fiona had painted of her, she was struck by the accuracy of Fiona’s gaze. Amelia’s fear of being seen was beautifully captured – the vulnerability of her soul laid bare on the canvas, but the unveiled charm of Amelia’s mind was unmistakable. Two worlds, different yet entangled, thriving and yearning for more.

Occasionally, life requires imaginary walls to be shattered, reality to be bent, and expectations to be redefined. Remarkably, these transformations happen within the minds of individuals, narrating the invisible essence of humanity through colors and brush strokes. Fiona and Amelia’s stories, intertwined on canvas, give testament to the irrepressible power of creativity, love, and seeing the world through someone else’s eyes.
