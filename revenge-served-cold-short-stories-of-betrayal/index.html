Revenge Served Cold: Short Stories of Betrayal


It was late afternoon when Olivia paced the narrow hallways of her apartment, the silence enveloping her like a thick fog. She hadn’t heard from her husband, Gabriel, all day. He was usually home by now, his coat tossed carelessly on the couch, his shoes scattered by the door, his presence filling the space. But today was different. Today, she felt the sting of betrayal heavier than the weight of the world pressing down on her chest.

Two months ago, she discovered a message on his phone. It had been nothing more than a flicker, a momentary glance as he left his phone unattended. It was a message from someone named “Lena,” but it wasn’t the name that caught her attention. It was the tone of the words, the unmistakable intimacy that flooded her with a gut-wrenching sense of betrayal.

“I miss you. I wish we could have dinner tomorrow, just the two of us. Maybe we can tell him soon?”

At first, Olivia thought she might be imagining things. Her mind was playing tricks on her, she reasoned. After all, she had been stressed lately, juggling work, the house, and their two young children. But deep down, she knew the truth. Gabriel was having an affair.

The days that followed were an emotional whirlwind. She confronted him once, only to be met with lies and empty promises. His words were smooth, too smooth, and Olivia could see the cracks in his facade growing larger with every word. But she said nothing. She smiled, nodded, and carried on with her life. For two months, she said nothing to him, but the weight of his infidelity was like a cancer eating away at her from the inside.

And now, here she was, standing in their home, waiting for the moment when she would strike back. The moment when the silence would no longer be enough.

Chapter 2: The Plan Unfolds

Olivia had never been the vengeful type. In fact, she prided herself on her calm demeanor and her ability to handle any situation with grace. But this… this was different. Gabriel’s betrayal cut deeper than any wound she had ever known. It was not just the affair—it was the lies, the deceit, and the arrogance with which he had dismissed her. He had taken her trust, shattered it, and trampled on it without a second thought.

As Olivia sat on the couch, the shadows of the evening creeping in, she thought about Lena. She had seen her once, at a charity event. The woman was striking, with sharp features and a smile that could melt anyone’s resolve. She had no doubt that Gabriel was infatuated with her. But what Olivia couldn’t understand was how Lena could betray another woman like that. How could she knowingly participate in the destruction of a family?

The thought of Lena’s face brought a surge of anger to Olivia’s chest. But as much as she wanted to confront Lena, it wasn’t her that Olivia needed to confront. It was Gabriel. It was Gabriel who had crossed the line, Gabriel who had broken the vows they made to each other. Gabriel who had dishonored their life together.

And so, Olivia devised her plan. It would not be a quick strike. No, this would be a slow burn. Gabriel would not know what hit him. He would think he was winning, all the while unaware that Olivia was meticulously orchestrating his downfall, piece by piece.

Her first move was simple: a quiet conversation with their closest friends, the ones who had always seen Gabriel as the perfect husband, the perfect father. She would plant seeds of doubt, subtle hints about his behavior, small whispers about his late nights and absences. She knew that eventually, the truth would spread like wildfire.

The second step was even more delicate. Olivia knew that Gabriel’s business dealings were not as clean as he led everyone to believe. There were rumors of shady investments, of under-the-table deals that would tarnish his reputation if the right people found out. She had learned this through the grapevine, through quiet conversations with a few trusted colleagues. But now, it was time to make sure those whispers reached the right ears.

And finally, Olivia’s pièce de résistance: the letter. It would be an anonymous letter, detailing every sordid detail of Gabriel’s affair with Lena. It would include everything—dates, times, places. It would be a cold, hard reminder of the man he had become. She would send it to Gabriel’s colleagues, to their friends, to the people who mattered most in his life. The people who respected him, who admired him.

Gabriel would never see it coming.

Chapter 3: The First Strike

Two weeks after she had set her plan into motion, Olivia watched as Gabriel walked into their home, a familiar smile on his face, his coat still not removed. She had been watching him for days now, studying his every move, waiting for the perfect moment to make her next move. She had already done her part with their friends—seeds of doubt had been planted, and now they were taking root. She knew it was only a matter of time before the truth would come out.

“How was work?” Olivia asked, her voice steady, her eyes locked on him.

“Busy. You know how it is,” Gabriel replied, dropping his briefcase by the door. “But I’m glad to be home. How were the kids?”

“They’re fine,” Olivia said, watching him carefully. “I was thinking about taking them to the park tomorrow. Just the three of us.”

Gabriel nodded absentmindedly. “Sounds good.”

Olivia knew that the guilt was starting to gnaw at him. He had been avoiding her, avoiding their children, and every moment he spent at home was a reminder of his betrayal. She could see it in his eyes—he was unsettled. The calm, collected facade he had worn for so long was starting to crack.

And then, as he sat down on the couch, reaching for his phone, Olivia’s hand tightened around the envelope she had prepared earlier that afternoon. She had already made sure it was sealed, ready to be sent. It was time for the final blow.

She stood up, walking over to him slowly, her heart pounding in her chest. “I think you should read this.”

Gabriel looked up, confusion flickering across his face. “What is it?”

“It’s a letter,” Olivia said, her voice as cold as ice. “A letter from someone who knows all about you and Lena.”

Gabriel froze. His eyes widened, his face paling. He had no idea how Olivia had found out, but in that moment, he realized that the truth was no longer something he could control.

Olivia watched as Gabriel’s fingers trembled slightly as he opened the envelope. She could see the moment his eyes scanned the words, the shock, the realization that everything he had worked so hard to protect was crumbling before him.

“You—” Gabriel began, but his words faltered.

“You did this to yourself,” Olivia said softly, her voice cold and emotionless. “I just gave you the opportunity to feel the weight of your actions.”

Chapter 4: The Cold Aftermath

The days that followed were a blur. Gabriel’s world came crashing down in a way he never anticipated. The letter had been sent out to his colleagues, friends, and business partners. The whispers spread like wildfire, and before long, Gabriel’s reputation was in tatters. His business deals were scrutinized, and soon, the shady investments Olivia had hinted at came to light. People who once admired him began to turn away, leaving him to face the consequences of his choices.

But it wasn’t just his professional life that fell apart. Gabriel’s personal life unraveled as well. Lena, once the center of his world, disappeared. Olivia never got confirmation of their final confrontation, but she didn’t need to. The coldness that Gabriel now wore was enough to tell her everything.

Olivia didn’t gloat. She didn’t seek revenge in a way that required a public spectacle. No, her revenge was far more potent. She had stripped Gabriel of his pride, his security, and most importantly, his delusion that he could have it all without consequences.

As for Olivia, she found solace in the silence that followed. She had reclaimed her dignity, her strength, and her sense of self-worth. Gabriel may have broken her heart, but she was no longer the woman he once knew. She was stronger, wiser, and no longer willing to tolerate betrayal, no matter how cleverly disguised.

And so, in the end, Olivia’s revenge was served cold—silent, calculated, and far more devastating than anything she could have done in the heat of anger. It was a lesson learned the hard way, one that Gabriel would never forget.
