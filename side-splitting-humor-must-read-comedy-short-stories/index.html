Side-Splitting Humor: Must-Read Comedy Short Stories


Frank Murphy was, by all accounts, a man with a serious problem: he hadn’t laughed in seven years. It wasn’t a subtle absence of mirth either—it was a stark, ironclad daily reality. No chuckles at sitcoms, no snickers at dad jokes, no bursts of hilarity over prank videos. His friends called it the Great Giggle Gambit, a personal challenge Frank had unknowingly set himself after a particularly bad joke fell flat at a party one day, shattering his funny bone like a dropped ceramic plate.

That was seven years ago.

On a drizzly Tuesday morning in the quaint town of Cleverdale, Frank’s life took a turn as unexpected as stepping on a banana peel mid-run. He shuffled down Main Street to the small, oddly named shop “LOL Emporium,” a quirky little bookstore specializing in comedy literature, stand-up DVDs, and curiously, a small shelf dedicated to joke manuals dating back to the early 1900s.

At the counter stood Olive, a sprightly woman whose laugh was so infectious it could make a stone grin. Olive caught sight of Frank’s solemn face and decided today was the day. Today, Frank would laugh.

“Looking for the cure to the serious face syndrome?” she joked, handing him a battered book titled Side-Splitting Humor: Must-Read Comedy Short Stories.

Frank blinked. “Haven’t laughed in years. Heard this might help.”

Olive’s eyes twinkled. “Only one way to find out.”

That evening, back in his modest apartment, Frank cracked open the ancient tome. The first story began about a hapless tailor who mistakenly sewed pockets in a pair of pants that were meant to be pocketless—causing people’s coins to scoop out like magic—and it wasn’t long before Frank felt a tug at the corners of his mouth. A twitch. An almost imperceptible smile. And then, something more remarkable—the first chuckle in seven years escaped his lips.

“Progress,” Frank muttered, flipping to the next story.

The tales were wide-ranging: a baffled cat chasing its own reflection, a group of senior citizens forming a rock band called “The Wrinkled Wonders,” and a spoof about a detective who keeps solving cases by accident. The humor was clever, tongue-in-cheek, and sprinkled with bursts of slapstick hilarity that made Frank snort with laughter.

Encouraged by this breakthrough, Frank began to frequent the LOL Emporium. Olive introduced him to an eclectic mix of comedic styles: dry British wit, satirical essays, stand-up routines frozen in time on VHS, and modern meme culture explained in humorous essays. Each evening brought a new deluge of jokes and punchlines, feeding his hunger for laughter.

Curious to test the power of this rediscovered humor, Frank devised a little experiment. He signed up for an open mic night at the town’s notoriously serious library café. His plan? To read aloud the funniest excerpts from Side-Splitting Humor and see if others were as tickled as he was.

The night of the open mic arrived, and the café was nearly empty, save for a few scattered regulars sipping their black coffee in hushed tones. Frank adjusted the microphone and began reading the story about a forgetful magician who kept losing his rabbits—sometimes on stage, sometimes in the grocery store.

Almost immediately, chuckles surfaced, doubling into outright laughter. One by one, people leaned in, captivated by the absurd scenarios and clever wordplay. By the time Frank finished the tale of the “Great Spaghetti Mishap,” the whole café was roaring with laughter. Even the grumpy librarian cracked a reluctant but genuine smile.

Frank realized something profound: Laughter was contagious. It nourished bonds, erased worries, and transformed mundane moments into memorable ones.

Word spread quickly. Frank became the unofficial town jester, a role reversal for someone who had once been engulfed in silence and sobriety. He started organizing weekly comedy reading sessions, inviting locals to share their favorite humorous stories or try their hand at stand-up. The town of Cleverdale transformed into a beacon of laughter, a lively hub where humor was not just entertainment but a celebrated way of life.

Frank’s journey harvested an unexpected lesson: that the best comedy often lies in the shared experience, the togetherness of human imperfection and joy. Side-splitting humor, after all, isn’t just about jokes; it’s about connection.

Years later, Frank authored his own collection titled The Great Giggle Gambit: A Celebration of Comedy. It chronicled not just the stories that made him laugh but the people, places, and moments that rekindled his lost laughter. It became a must-read for anyone seeking the profound joy found in a good belly laugh.

The town of Cleverdale, once sleepy and somber, flourished into the joyous epicenter of comedic culture, proving that humor isn’t just in the punchlines but in the heartbeats we share when we laugh until our sides ache.

And so, if ever you find yourself in need of a laugh that will ripple through your soul, remember Frank and his Side-Splitting Humor—because sometimes, the best medicine doesn’t come in a bottle, but in the timeless, contagious joy of a well-told joke.
