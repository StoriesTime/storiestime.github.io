Resilient Hearts: Short Stories of Social Justice Warriors


Once upon a time in a small village called Elmanor lived a spirited young woman named Priti. She was known for her tranquil heart and adamant determination to fight against social injustice and marginalization.

A Global Missions Organization had heard of Priti’s resilience and tagged her as one of the Social Justice Warriors who fights courageously for a better world. They offered her an opportunity to travel to the most marginalized regions of the world and bring transformative change.

Priti embarked on her mission, leaving behind the familiarity and comfort of her village. As she stepped onto the first plane of her journey, her heart brimmed with trepidation and anticipation, filled with the possibility of creating a positive difference in people’s lives.

Priti’s first destination was a remote village located far in the wilderness of India. Here, she encountered a unique group of people known as the Banjara tribes. Banjara women were subjected to juvenile marriages, limited mobility, and restricted access to resources and opportunities. The rampant practice of early marriage led to a higher illiteracy rate amongst Banjara girls.

Priti knew she had to help. The women here were hardworking, resilient, and exceptionally skilled in making intricate crafts like embroidered footwear and pottery. Priti realized that if these handcrafted local products reached global markets, the Banjara women could gain economic independence. However, lack of awareness and a proper distribution channel hindered the growth of their talent and resourcefulness.

Priti organized an art workshop for young and older women members of the tribe, with workshops teaching modern design and skills. Introducing samples of pottery, intricate embroidery, and wooden carvings sourced from other regions, Priti encouraged the Banjara women to experiment with new and original designs. Within weeks, this proved successful, as more and more tourists and travelers started purchasing their products.

Feeling victorious after this accomplishment, Priti proceeded to the earthquake-stricken regions of Iran. Women survivors from the impoverished regions of Bam had fled to the outskirts of other villages, lacking basic amenities and often left without an identity. Priti was deeply moved by their resilience despite the odds that their lives offered.

Understanding that education could bring significant change for these women, she started a non-formal education center for girls of all ages in Koya. Here, Priti taught them to sew, weave, and cook, but also encouraged them to question society’s norms and expectations. The village elders didn’t think that the girls required education or would benefit from work that wasn’t physical labor. They opposed Priti’s efforts.

Priti knew she had to be relentless in her pursuit for social justice. She involved the local authorities in her mission and emphasized the positive effects of education on the community. It wasn’t long before the awareness became contagious, and the local community began to accept the idea of education for their daughters and young girls.

Finally, in a far-off, impoverished area of Elmanor, Priti encountered the ultimate challenge. She met a community riddled with child trafficking. She cautiously infiltrated the region to gain insight into the problem. Priti noticed that children were being sold into child labor by their devoted family members.

This time, Priti’s primary weapon was street theater – an engaging platform that she believed could effectively reach the audience in these remote places. She introduced thought-provoking performances about child trafficking, bringing the culture of exploitation into the limelight.

Sadly, during her last tour through her own village, Priti was struck by an unexpected fatal illness. As she lay in her bed, Priti realized that she had accomplished what she had set out for and felt triumphant, allowing her to smile through her pain.

Despite her untimely and tragic passing, Priti’s legacy was lived on by her heart of resilience and her ongoing quest for social justice. The mobile libraries, the vocational centers, and the powerful Street performances encouraged, empowered, and transformed lives continued to drive others to follow in her footsteps.

Vivid and hard-fought journeys, kindled by the immeasurable courage of Priti, have changed the world forever.
