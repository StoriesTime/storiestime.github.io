Scariest Ghost Stories to Read Before Bed


It was said that the Hollow House had been abandoned for decades. Its crumbling walls and sagging roof were covered with ivy that seemed to crawl across the surface like a living entity. Some villagers even claimed the ivy moved by itself at night, but no one dared to investigate.

Maria, an adventurous young woman, had always been fascinated by the stories of the Hollow House. She’d grown up hearing the whispers of her grandmother, who often warned her to never go near it. But curiosity had a way of gnawing at her, especially as she grew older. As Halloween approached, she made up her mind—she would finally see the Hollow House for herself.

One crisp evening, she gathered a few friends—Tom, Sarah, and Ben—to accompany her. Armed with nothing but flashlights and their bravado, they approached the house. The wind howled as they crossed the overgrown yard, their footsteps crunching on the dry leaves beneath their feet. Every shadow seemed to watch them as they made their way to the front door, which creaked open as though inviting them inside.

The air in the house was thick with dust and mildew, and the floorboards groaned with every step they took. The silence was unnerving, broken only by the occasional gust of wind rattling the broken windows. As they explored the dimly lit rooms, each one was more oppressive than the last. Portraits of long-dead ancestors hung crookedly on the walls, their eyes seeming to follow the group’s every move.

The deeper they ventured into the house, the colder it became. The temperature dropped noticeably, and an eerie, almost unearthly presence seemed to settle in the air. Sarah, the most superstitious of the group, started to feel a strange sensation at the back of her neck, like someone was standing just behind her. She turned to look, but there was no one there.

“Did you feel that?” she whispered to Tom, but he only shrugged, unconcerned.

Then, the unmistakable sound of footsteps echoed from upstairs.

Tom grinned nervously. “Probably just the house settling,” he said. But even he couldn’t hide the fear that crept into his voice.

They stood still, listening. The footsteps grew louder, descending from the upper floor. It sounded like something—or someone—was coming down to meet them. The air became thick with tension.

“Let’s go,” Ben said suddenly, his voice shaky. “This is too much.”

But as they turned to leave, the door they had entered through slammed shut with a deafening crash. They scrambled to open it, but the handle wouldn’t budge. The house seemed to be alive, its walls closing in on them.

That was when the whispers began.

Faint at first, they grew louder, seeming to emanate from the walls themselves. A guttural, unintelligible language filled the air. The group huddled together in terror, trying to make sense of the growing sound. It felt as though the house was speaking to them, warning them, or perhaps trying to welcome them into its dark embrace.

Suddenly, a shadow appeared at the top of the stairs, a figure draped in tattered clothing. Its face was obscured by a veil of long, dark hair, but its eyes—empty, hollow, and staring—locked onto Maria.

Without warning, the figure lunged toward them, its voice now a scream that rattled the very bones of the house. Maria froze in place, unable to move as the shadow came closer, its form twisting and contorting like smoke.

“Run!” Ben screamed, and in that instant, the door flew open. The group bolted from the house, not daring to look back.

They never spoke of the incident again, but the memory of the Hollow House haunted them for years. Some say that the house still stands, waiting for the next group of curious souls to venture too close.

The Lantern in the Woods

In a small village nestled on the edge of a vast forest, an old legend was passed down from generation to generation. It told of a lost lantern that appeared in the woods at night, guiding the souls of the departed to the afterlife. But the lantern wasn’t always a peaceful guide. It was said that those who followed the light without caution would never return.

The legend was a warning, but some didn’t heed it. One such person was James, a young man who had recently moved to the village. He found the story intriguing, dismissing it as nothing more than a fairy tale meant to keep children from wandering into the woods after dark. But curiosity, as it often does, got the better of him.

One evening, as the full moon hung high in the sky, James set off into the woods, eager to see the lantern for himself. The trees loomed above him, their branches stretching like skeletal hands, casting long, twisted shadows on the forest floor. He walked deeper into the woods, his footsteps muffled by the soft, damp earth beneath him.

After what felt like hours, he spotted it—just a faint flicker at the edge of his vision. The lantern. It was held by an invisible hand, its glow pale and eerie. It floated just above the ground, bobbing gently as though carried by a breeze that didn’t touch the trees.

James felt a shiver of excitement mixed with fear. He followed the light, keeping a cautious distance as it led him further into the heart of the forest. The farther he went, the stranger the woods became. The trees were thicker now, their trunks twisted into unnatural shapes, and the air felt charged with an unspoken tension.

Suddenly, the lantern stopped moving. It hovered in place, its flickering light casting an unsettling glow on the surrounding trees. James approached it, heart pounding in his chest. As he drew closer, the light seemed to grow dimmer, the shadows around it lengthening.

And then he heard it—whispers. Low and soft, like voices carried on the wind. They seemed to come from all directions, as if the forest itself were speaking to him.

“Turn back…” the whispers urged.

But James, determined to prove the legend wrong, ignored the warning and reached out to touch the lantern.

As his fingers brushed against the glass, a sharp, icy cold shot through him. His vision blurred, and the forest around him twisted. The trees grew taller, their branches reaching down like grasping hands. The whispers grew louder, now screaming in desperation.

“Turn back! You shouldn’t have come!”

But it was too late. The lantern flickered one final time before extinguishing, plunging the forest into darkness. James tried to move, but his body was frozen in place. The air grew heavy, and a shadow fell over him.

When the villagers searched for him the next morning, they found no trace of James. The only thing they found was a single lantern, glowing faintly on the edge of the forest, waiting for the next soul to follow.

The Woman in the Mirror

Maria had always been skeptical of old superstitions, but there was one tale that unsettled her to her core. It was about a woman who was cursed to appear in mirrors, forever trapped between the realm of the living and the dead.

The legend spoke of a beautiful woman named Isabella, who had been wronged by her lover and died under mysterious circumstances. Her spirit was bound to mirrors, unable to escape the glass that held her imprisoned. On certain nights, she would appear in mirrors, her eyes hollow with grief and rage, seeking to drag anyone who gazed upon her into the dark world she inhabited.

Maria dismissed it as a myth until one night when she was alone in her apartment. The moonlight streamed through the windows, casting long shadows across the room. She stood in front of her bedroom mirror, brushing her hair, her thoughts lost in the mundane task.

But then, as she caught a glimpse of movement from the corner of her eye, she froze.

In the reflection, there was someone else—standing behind her. The figure was a woman, her pale face twisted in an expression of sorrow and fury. Her eyes were dark and hollow, like two endless pits of despair.

Maria gasped and spun around, but there was no one there. She turned back to the mirror, and the woman remained, her face now pressed against the glass as though trying to escape.

Panic surged through Maria, but her feet felt heavy, as though the mirror itself was pulling her toward it. She wanted to look away, but her gaze was fixed on the woman’s hollow eyes, and she couldn’t tear herself free.

The whispers began, soft at first, then growing louder.

“Help me…”

Maria’s heart raced. She backed away, stumbling, but the woman in the mirror remained, her eyes pleading, her fingers tapping softly on the glass.

The next morning, Maria was found in her apartment, staring into the mirror, her expression frozen in terror. The mirror had cracked, but there was no sign of any struggle.

The woman in the mirror had claimed another victim.

The Unseen Neighbor

Emily had always lived a quiet life in her small, secluded house on the outskirts of town. Her neighbors were few and far between, and she liked it that way. She enjoyed the solitude, the peace that came with it.

But one evening, she began to hear strange noises coming from the house next door. It was an old, decrepit home, long abandoned, with broken windows and overgrown ivy crawling up the walls. The sounds started softly at first—scraping noises, like furniture being moved around, followed by soft whispers, as though someone were talking just out of reach.

Curious but unsettled, Emily decided to investigate. She walked over to the neighbor’s house, her footsteps crunching on the gravel path. As she neared the door, she felt a chill in the air, and the hairs on the back of her neck stood up.

The door creaked open as if waiting for her.

Inside, the house was empty—eerily so. There was no furniture, no sign of anyone having lived there for years. Yet the air was thick with the sound of whispering, like a hundred voices speaking in unison, their words unintelligible.

Emily took a step forward, drawn to the back of the house. There, in the dim light, she saw a figure—tall, thin, and shrouded in darkness.

It turned toward her, its face hidden in shadow, but Emily could feel its gaze on her, cold and unblinking. The whispers grew louder, now a cacophony of voices urging her to leave.

But before she could turn to flee, the figure reached out, its long fingers brushing her shoulder.

Emily never returned to her house. When authorities investigated, they found the house empty, save for the strange marks on the floor where the figure had once stood.

The neighbor’s house was never seen again.

These tales, steeped in the shadows of the unknown, remind us of the fine line between reality and the supernatural. When the veil between the two is thinned by darkness, the scariest things aren’t always what you can see, but what you can’t.
