Dark Dimensions: Supernatural Shorts to Bewitch Your Mind


The fog clung thick to the edge of Hollow Creek like a whispered secret no one dared reveal. On nights like this, the veil between reality and something deeply elseworldly seemed to thin, frail as spider’s silk. It was in these shadows that the unexplainable thrived, twisting minds and bending truths. Hollow Creek would say it’s all superstition, stories meant to scare children, but those who wandered too far into the dark dimensions found those tales never told the whole story.

The House with Whispered Walls

Sarah had always been drawn to the old Harper house at the forest’s edge. Its shutters hung crooked, paint peeling in long ribbons like old scars. Her friends warned her not to go near, whispering about things that lurked within—but curiosity, that ancient witch, pulled her closer.

On a dare, Sarah stepped inside, the floorboards creaking complaints beneath her weight. The air felt colder here, thinner, as if she had slipped into a seam in reality. Walls seemed to pulse, breathing faint murmurs just beneath hearing. Words she couldn’t quite catch, a voice neither alive nor dead.

Suddenly, the portraits lining the hallway flickered to life—their painted eyes tracking her every move. Shadows pooled and stretched, forming grotesque shapes that writhed around her feet. Sarah’s breath caught in her throat when a cold hand grazed her shoulder. She spun, but found only empty air and the scent of faded jasmine lingering.

Despite terror rooting her in place, fascination chained her forward. The house was a portal, an anchor to the other side—a realm where memories took shape and souls whispered secrets buried deep within the dark dimensions.

The Mirror’s Reflection

Ethan was a skeptic. Tales of spirits and hauntings were just superstitions, nothing more. But the antique mirror his grandmother left him challenged everything he thought he knew.

The first time Ethan looked into it, his reflection blinked on its own. At first, he thought it was a trick of the light or fatigue. But night after night, the mirror’s surface rippled like disturbed water, revealing glimpses of places Ethan had never been—twisted forests under blood-red moons, cities dissolving into shadow, faces contorted in both agony and ecstasy.

One evening, a voice echoed from the glass—a soft, seductive whisper promising forbidden knowledge. Against all instincts, Ethan reached out. His hand passed through the mirror like mist, fingers brushing a cold void beyond.

He pulled back, heart pounding. The mirror shimmered and, in the glass, the eyes staring back were no longer his own.

The Watcher in the Woods

Timothy was the kind of boy who listened when the forest spoke. The elders said the woods were alive, ancient and hungry. Hunters vanished while chasing deer, their screams swallowed without a trace.

On the fringes of Hollow Creek, Timothy’s grandfather had warned him about the Watcher—a creature neither animal nor spirit, but something older and far more dangerous.

One night, drawn by an inexplicable urgency, Timothy ventured deeper than ever before. As moonlight fractured through the canopy, a figure emerged—tall, draped in tattered shadows, eyes glinting like shattered stars.

The Watcher said nothing but extended a bony finger toward the path ahead. Against all fear, Timothy followed.

What he found was a clearing where time folded upon itself: fragments of memories hung like torn pictures, moments captured from past, present, and futures not yet lived. The Watcher slid aside, revealing a doorway stitched into the fabric of the night—a threshold into the dark dimensions, where souls wandered and destinies intertwined.

The Enchantress’s Bargain

Lena was a scholar of the occult, fascinated by legends of witches who could bend fate. Deep in Hollow Creek’s library, she uncovered a dusty grimoire that pulsed with unnatural energy.

The book told of an enchantress who thrived between worlds, a weaver of spells that could alter minds and twist reality itself. Desperate to break her own chains of loss and pain, Lena read the blackened incantations aloud under the eclipse’s shadow.

The air shimmered, and she appeared—mysterious, radiant yet terrifying—her gaze a galaxy of lost stars.

“I will grant you what you desire,” the enchantress whispered, “but every gift demands a price.”

Lena accepted, and with the pact, her world warped. She glimpsed hidden truths, saw the souls tangled in the web of the supernatural, but each revelation chipped away at her own sense of self. The line between curse and blessing blurred into a dark dimension of existence where madness and enlightenment danced an endless waltz.

The Clockmaker’s Secret

At the heart of Hollow Creek stood an old clock tower, its hands frozen at midnight for decades. Local legend said the clock was broken by the clockmaker after a trip into the dark dimensions changed him forever.

Ellie, a rising journalist hungry for a story, sneaked into the tower one stormy night. Inside, rusted gears and shattered glass littered the floor. Then, a soft ticking began—not from the clocks, but somewhere inside her own mind.

She found the clockmaker’s journal, entries filled with warnings and strange diagrams, speaking of worlds folded within time, places where moments looped endlessly and men became shadows of themselves.

Ellie’s pulse quickened as the air thickened, seconds folding into eternity. The clockmaker’s final note chilled her to the core: “To fix the clock is to unseal the dark dimensions; to unseal them is to lose one’s very self.”

Epilogue

Hollow Creek thrived in the balance between the seen and unseen, a crucible of dark dimensions whispering through every crack and shadow. Those who dared to enter its stories—whether through haunted houses, cursed mirrors, or mystical bargains—found their minds forever bewitched, dancing at the edge of a reality that promised everything and nothing all at once.

Some returned changed, carrying pieces of those supernatural realms like scars or gifts. Others vanished without a trace, swallowed by the folds of those dark dimensions where nightmares and dreams entwined.

But all bore witness to one truth:

In Hollow Creek, the supernatural isn’t just a story. It’s a living, breathing force that beckons the curious, the brave—and the irreparably lost.

Dark dimensions are not just figments of imagination; they exist in the spaces we overlook, the moments we dismiss, and the fears we bury. They challenge our understanding of reality and invite us to step beyond the ordinary into realms that disturb yet fascinate in equal measure.

If you ever find yourself in Hollow Creek—or anywhere the shadows deepen—remember: the dark dimensions don’t just lurk outside. Sometimes, they awaken inside.
