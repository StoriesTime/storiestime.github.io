Le~Qu~R~tr: A Calculated Dance of Betrayal and Retribution


In the vast and labyrinthine city of LeQuR~tr, nestled between winding alleys and secretive corners, a Calculated Dance of Betrayal and Retribution was about to unfold. The city, a sprawling metropolis of ex-architects, ex-painters, and ex-writers, had long succumbed to the whims of its inhabitants, whose every gesture was a performance.

At the city’s heart lay an infamous square, throbbing with life and death, a place where secrets festered and bloomed. Underneath its decaying carousel, unspoken promises echoed among the peeling murals.

Perched on the square’s edge was a documenter, a keen observer with a keen eye for the city’s enigmatic inhabitants. They were paid in secrets and whispers to chronicle the city’s complex history and the stories of its colorful characters. Today was no different.

In the square’s dim, nearly crippling light, one man approached the documenter. His clothes were torn and tattered, and his features were obscured behind a veil of mud-caked hair. His voice, barely a whisper, revealed names beneath the documenter’s inkwells.

“Lev, Velma, and Rulon,” he murmured, almost inaudibly. “It’s been years since they vanished. They sought power, and obscurity found them instead.”

The documenter listened, no expectation in their gaze. The whisperer leaned closer.

“Do you know,” he inquired, “the true story of LeQuR~tr’s inception? For that is the tale of Lev, Velma, and Rulon, and how their betrayal sculpted the very streets beneath our feet.”

The Coins of the Betrayal

A dozen years previous, Lev, Velma, and Rulon professed to be architects of the future. Their dreams, they claimed, would reshape the city to their vision’s will. They preached of a city suspended between two blades of a twisted balance—hierarchy and chaos—and they promised salvation to all who would follow.

As engineers of this promised metropolis, it was Lev, Velma, and Rulon who conceived of the square. It would be the heart of their grand design, a place imbued with the city’s spirit and fleshed out with its people. But unbeknownst to the city’s denizens, there was another, far more sinister motive. For in that public space, the architects planned to unearth the deepest, darkest desires of the city’s dwellers and exploit them to assert their control.

But their betrayal didn’t stop there. As the square came to life, so too did whispers and rumors of a fourth conspirator, half-remembered and shrouded in mystery. This elusive figure, known only as R, left a calculated path of deception, guiding Lev, Velma, and Rulon toward a treacherous end.

The Unraveling

As the years passed, the architects’ schemes crumbled. The city they sought to control only continued to grow in its defiance. Out of the shadows emerged unexpected heroes, unexpected enemies, and the unlikeliest of architects.

Unbeknownst to the city’s inhabitants, the hearts of Lev, Velma, and Rulon were on the cusp of being purged of deceit and betrayal. Their souls bore the weight of their treachery, and the horrors they inflicted on the innocent. Fleeing from the uncomfortable truth of their crimes, they vanished into the city’s chaotic embrace.

The mysterious R, driven by a thirst for vengeance, had grown tired of the city’s cheap games. Having played the puppet master for too long, they sought to confront those who’d been duped and twined in their calculated dance of treachery. They betrothed themselves to revenge in the service of liberating the city from the architects’ cruel dominion.

Another Betrayal

The documenter listened eagerly to the whispered tale, the tale of Lev, Velma, and Rulon. The whisperer spoke of how R, the elusive orchestrator of that treacherous trio, sought to reveal the deepest desires of the city’s inhabitants and twist them to both betray and control.

And yet, as they listened, the documenter realized that they were the ultimate betrayer. For in their work, they too had betrayed the trust of the city and its inhabitants. They had honed in on the whispers of its dwellers, exploiting their stories for their gain, never considering the consequences of writing stories for the voyeuristic sake of the world beyond.

The Calculated Dance of Betrayal

Lev, Velma, and Rulon disappeared, and the city bloomed anew in the chaos of their betrayal. But R would never enjoy the promised land of their vengeance. For R and the documenter were one and the same. The secrets they pursued, the whispers they chased—they were only their own.

The dance of betrayal and retribution, of control and chaos, continued in the city, where the line between the observer and the observed blurred in the dim light of the square. And there, in the labyrinth of LeQuR~tr, betrayal would live on, ever shifting and shifting the ground beneath their feet.
