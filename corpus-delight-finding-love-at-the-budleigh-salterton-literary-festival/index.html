Corpus Delight: Finding Love at the Budleigh Salterton Literary Festival


Budleigh Salterton, a quaint village in the county of Devon, came alive every September as it hosted its popular literary festival. The event drew inquisitive minds, authors, and readers alike, creating an atmosphere woven with the intoxicating scent of freshly printed paper, enticing talks, and unique conversations. Amidst the sea breeze and the sound of chattering attendees, a young bibliophile named James turned yet another page in the quest for his life’s true story.

James, 25, a book lover from London, had stumbled upon the mythical Budleigh Salterton Literary Festival by accident only a year before, while seeking a calming change from the city’s cacophony. As he walked along the festival’s vibrant lanes, escaping into the pages of newly released tomes, a sudden curiosity about finding love in such an unconventional setting stirred within him.

Now back for his second year, James hoped to pen the perfect chapter in his name – one starring a love that would be an untold love story. On the first day, as he navigated the maze of marquees, James unexpectedly found himself at a book-signing event for famous author, Evelyn de St. Clair, a romance novelist with a dozen New York Times bestsellers under her stylish belt. He couldn’t resist the temptation, so, with a deep breath, he forged his way into the fold of adoring fans and, in a flash, found himself face-to-face with the dazzling storyteller.

Evelyn’s wit and poise captivated him as she signed a personalized copy of her latest novel. James forced a heartening tone to hide the tremor in his voice, asking her about her favorite love stories at the festival. Perched on her stiletto heels, Evelyn dished out a myriad of experiences, and James listened, captivated. As the last fan left, a charming smile graced her lips, directed at James now alone before her. “It’s moments like these, Mr. . . . ?” she prompted, a linger of curiosity in her voice.

“James,” he uttered, brief and softly spoken. An awkward silence hugged them until, in one beautiful moment, a gust of laughter spilled out as Evelyn exclaimed her great admiration for the classic British novel “Wuthering Heights.” There, on a small, unassuming bench ahead, in the cool shade of a sycamore tree, they settled, discussing literature, love, and life’s peculiar crossroads.

Each day of the festival saw more shared moments. James, perhaps miraculously, grew adept at merging into her schedule, joining the conversations she partook in, and basking in the brilliance of her intellect. As the days dwindled, so did the certainty that this love story might still come undone, fearing their paths would diverge just as quickly as they had intertwined.

In the final moments before the festival’s close, under the setting sun casting its amber hues upon Budleigh Salterton, James brought the idea to Evelyn’s lips; it had spewed from his heart unbidden - a love story that transcended his wildest dreams, one inspired by the festival itself. A novelization of their journey, of how they found love at the Budleigh Salterton Literary Festival.

Her eyes sparkled, and the soft gasp fluttered away like the delicate petals of a flower at twilight. A shared silence spoke volumes, and they surrendered to it, the passing sea breeze a gentle foil to their elation. That secluded corner by the river behind the bookshops, where they shared stories and pages, had become their safe haven. The literati and attendees of Budleigh Salterton whispered about the budding romance and titled it the most enchanting love story of the season.

Together, they set pen to paper, weaving a tale about love, convergence, choice, and destiny in the context of their encounters at the Budleigh Salterton Literary Festival. The creative juices that had hurled them together fused like never before, resonating in the depths of each paragraph, proving love’s unyielding power.

Months later, in the afterglow of the harvest moon, James and Evelyn became man and wife, paying homage to their story, their love, and the picturesque Budleigh Salterton Literary Festival. In a rustic ceremony at the venue that had started it all, they exchanged their vows—an ode to a fateful autumn rendezvous that unearthed a love story like none other.

Their first dance, a waltz, was to the tunes of “Corpus Delight,” a composition so exquisite that it silenced the world, and their hearts intertwined as the lyrics reverberated in sync with the beats of their love story. To the world, it would forever be known simply as their song, a cherished testament of how finding love at Budleigh Salterton’s Literary Festival could transform two fates and forever bind their odyssey together.

The sea breeze carried their laughter to the star-lit skies, as James and Evelyn, hand in hand, stepped into their future, each leaf whispering tales of the love found, lost, and found again, at the mesmerizing Budleigh Salterton Literary Festival.
